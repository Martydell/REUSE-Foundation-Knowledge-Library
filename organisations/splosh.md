## Organisation Overview

Splosh Limited is a UK private limited company, incorporated 23 January 2009 (Companies House no. 06800040), that sells refillable household cleaning and personal-care products directly to consumers by post. Founder Angus Grahame has described the idea originating from observing plastic-bottle waste in a supermarket car park in Hay-on-Wye; company factory operations are based in Newtown, Wales. Customers buy a durable bottle once, then order concentrated refill pouches by post, mixing with water at home; used pouches are returned to Splosh for reuse or reprocessing, forming what the company describes as a closed-loop system.

## Business Model

Splosh operates a B2C direct-to-consumer subscription/reorder model for laundry liquids, fabric conditioners, dishwashing gels, hand soaps and surface cleaners. Customers purchase a permanent bottle and receive concentrated refill pouches by Royal Mail; some products require dilution with tap water at home, reducing the water and plastic shipped compared with ready-diluted products. Once customers have accumulated at least 20 empty pouches, they can request a return pack, photograph the pouches for a returns form, and post them back via a ParcelShop or arranged collection. Returned pouches are either washed and resold at a discount as "Crinkly" (reused) pouches, or sent to reprocessing partners who shred and wash unusable pouches into plastic pellets, which are then manufactured into products such as plastic "ply board" used as a timber alternative in UK construction. The company states this model cuts CO2 emissions by roughly 75% versus conventional packaged equivalents.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Splosh is a clear, purpose-built reuse business: its entire model is a closed-loop refill-and-return system for everyday cleaning products, with an explicit reprocessing pathway for pouches that cannot be reused directly. It is a small, independently owned UK operator without the scale of Algramo or Loop-style networks, and it discloses very little quantified impact data (no audited plastic-saved or customer-count figures were found), which limits verifiability. The seed data's 2009 founding year is corroborated by Companies House, though some press references (e.g., a 2014 feature and a "Domestic cleaning product, Splosh (Hay-on-Wye, 2013)" article title) suggest the consumer-facing refill product may have launched a few years after incorporation — this nuance is flagged for the tracker.
