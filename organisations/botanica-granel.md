## Organisation Overview

Botánica Granel is a Mexico City-based zero-waste retailer described in local press as the city's first zero-waste mini-supermarket, operating physical bulk stores in the Condesa and Nápoles neighbourhoods alongside an e-commerce site. Founded by Silvana Huicochea, who was inspired by Germany's early zero-waste supermarkets, the business allows customers to bring their own containers to purchase more than 400 package-free products, spanning groceries, personal-care, and cleaning items. The company states that roughly 95% of the products it sells are sourced from Mexican producers, positioning itself as a supporter of local, low-packaging supply chains.

## Business Model

Botánica Granel operates a bulk/refill retail model: customers bring reusable containers to physical stores to purchase unpackaged food staples (grains, pasta, spices, oils) and personal-care/cleaning items (shampoo bars, menstrual cups, toothbrushes, detergents) by weight or volume, avoiding single-use packaging at the point of sale. The company has extended this into e-commerce, offering direct-to-consumer online ordering with delivery (free shipping above a stated order threshold), which for online orders necessarily involves packaged/boxed shipment rather than in-store container refill. The business emphasizes sourcing roughly 95% of its catalogue from Mexican producers, framing local sourcing as part of its sustainability proposition alongside package-free retail.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems (customers bring their own containers for bulk food, personal-care, and cleaning products)

- Consumer Education (in-store model that teaches package-free shopping practices)

No deposit-return, take-back, rental, or repair activity was identified; the online/delivery arm of the business was not confirmed to use reusable shipping packaging.

## Products & Services

Bulk/unpackaged groceries (cereals, pastas, infusions, superfoods, condiments, oils, seeds, legumes), personal-care items (shampoo bars, menstrual cups, eco toothbrushes, razors), and cleaning products, sold both in physical stores and via online ordering.

## Customers

Environmentally-conscious consumers in Mexico City, served through two physical retail locations (Condesa and Nápoles) and online/delivery customers nationally within Mexico.
