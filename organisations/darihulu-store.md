## Organisation Overview

Dari Hulu is a Balikpapan, Indonesia-based zero-waste initiative founded by Ronald and Oda, combining a bulk-refill store with broader waste-reduction education. The name is reported to mean "From Yourself" (per one source), reflecting a philosophy of individual responsibility for waste reduction. The initiative began through social media before expanding into two physical facilities: Halte Sampah (a waste-sorting shelter/facility) and Dari Hulu Bulk Store, which operates a bring-your-own-container refill model by appointment.

## Business Model

Dari Hulu combines community waste-education outreach (initially built via social media) with two physical facilities: Halte Sampah, a waste-sorting shelter, and the Dari Hulu Bulk Store, a zero-waste refill shop where customers bring their own reusable containers to purchase everyday goods without single-use packaging. Operating by appointment (rather than fixed retail hours) suggests a smaller-scale, community-oriented operation rather than a high-volume commercial retail format, consistent with Indonesia's broader grassroots zero-waste/bulk-store movement.
