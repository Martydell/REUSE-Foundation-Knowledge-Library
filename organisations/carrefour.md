## Organisation Overview

Carrefour is a French multinational retail group operating hypermarkets, supermarkets, and convenience formats globally, with roughly 298,000–325,000 employees depending on source and year. As one of the world's largest retailers, its relevance to reuse comes from acting as a distribution channel for reuse systems rather than being a reuse operator itself: it hosts TerraCycle's Loop reusable-packaging platform at commercial scale in France (its largest market globally for Loop) and has piloted bring-your-own-container schemes in French hypermarkets since 2018.

## Business Model

Carrefour's core business is multi-format grocery and general-merchandise retail. Its reuse-relevant activity is a secondary, pilot-scale layer on top of that core business: it stocks roughly 40 returnable products via the Loop system (mainly beverages — Coca-Cola, Suntory brands including Orangina and Schweppes) across 300+ French stores, with Suntory products specifically in around 100 locations. Customers pay a deposit included in the product price, refunded digitally or in cash when containers are returned; Carrefour's chief sustainability officer has described the model's appeal as driving repeat store visits. Separately, Carrefour piloted a "bring your own container" scheme in ~15 hypermarkets in France's Centre region starting 2018, allowing customers to fill dry/clean/transparent containers with bulk goods. Reported product performance is uneven: beverages (beer, soda, water) perform well, while personal-care items (shampoo, perfume) show lower reuse adoption.

## Measurable Impact

Not publicly available as independently audited figures. Reported operational facts (not impact metrics): ~40 returnable Loop products stocked; Loop present in 300+ Carrefour stores in France; Suntory products in ~100 locations (Trellis, 2020s reporting, undated to a specific year).

## Technology

Deposit-return via Loop's tracking/refund system (digital or cash refund on container return); no further detail publicly available on Carrefour-specific technology.

## Challenges

Reported adoption is uneven by product category — beverages perform well, personal-care items (shampoo, perfume) show weaker uptake (Trellis reporting). Loop's UK/European e-commerce operations wound down around 2023, though France retail distribution (including Carrefour) has continued — worth noting as sector-wide context for reuse-system fragility.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Carrefour is relevant to the REUSE Foundation library primarily as a major distribution channel proving that reuse/deposit-return systems (via Loop) can scale to hundreds of mainstream retail locations rather than remaining niche — a meaningful signal for reuse-system viability at retail scale. However, reuse is a small pilot layer within an overwhelmingly conventional retail business, not a core model, which limits its depth as a case study compared to organisations built around reuse from the ground up.
