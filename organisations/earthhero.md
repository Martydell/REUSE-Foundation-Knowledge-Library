## Organisation Overview

EarthHero is a Colorado-based online marketplace launched in 2017 that curates sustainably made consumer products from vetted, independent brands. Founded by Ryan Lewis (CEO), the company positions itself as a "one-stop shop" for eco-friendly alternatives across home, beauty, cleaning, baby, pet, apparel and tech categories. EarthHero screens vendor brands through a proprietary "5-Pillar Sourcing Methodology" before listing them, is a Climate Neutral Certified company, and is a member of 1% for the Planet.

## Business Model

EarthHero operates a curated multi-vendor e-commerce marketplace: rather than manufacturing products itself, it vets and lists items from 500+ independent sustainable brands, taking a retail/marketplace margin on sales. Its differentiator versus general marketplaces (e.g. Amazon) is an explicit sustainability screening process (the "5-Pillar Sourcing Methodology") applied before any brand or product is listed, covering areas such as materials, packaging, manufacturing practices and company values. Revenue comes from direct-to-consumer sales across broad categories (home & kitchen, beauty, bathroom, cleaning, baby & kids, pet, clothing & footwear, tech & electronics) plus a corporate/bulk gifting arm (EarthHero Gifting) serving businesses seeking sustainable client and employee gifts. The company has pursued third-party accreditations (Climate Neutral Certified, 1% for the Planet membership) as trust signals rather than developing its own reuse infrastructure — it is fundamentally a retail curation and distribution business, not a reuse-system operator.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

EarthHero is a sustainability-curation retailer rather than a reuse-system operator: it sells greener versions of disposable/single-use consumer products rather than enabling refill, reuse, repair or take-back. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's core reuse/circularity mission is limited, though it has some value as a consumer-education and market-aggregation model for sustainable goods, and its brand-vetting methodology could be of secondary interest.
