## Organisation Overview

Gjenge Makers Ltd is a Nairobi-based Kenyan company founded by mechanical engineer and entrepreneur Nzambi Matee, who quit her job as a data analyst in 2017 after being struck by the volume of plastic waste littering Nairobi's streets. Working initially from a small lab in her mother's backyard, she developed a process — and eventually a self-built production machine (completed 2019) — for combining recycled plastic waste with sand to manufacture paving bricks ("pavers") that are stronger than standard concrete equivalents, with compression strength reported at roughly three times that of conventional concrete pavers. Matee was named a UNEP Young Champion of the Earth (Africa) in 2020 for this work.

## Business Model

Gjenge Makers operates an industrial reuse/recycling model: it sources discarded plastic waste (including from garbage collectors, community collection networks, and industrial off-cuts), shreds and combines it with sand under high heat (melting point over 350°C) to produce durable paving bricks, which are then sold to schools, homeowners, and construction/landscaping customers as a durable, cost-competitive alternative to standard concrete pavers. The company generates revenue through direct paver sales while creating downstream employment for waste-collection workers, women and youth groups who supply plastic feedstock — Gjenge reports having generated 112 job opportunities in this way. Its value proposition rests on both affordability and superior technical performance (durability, compression strength) versus conventional paving materials, differentiating it from purely philanthropic or subsidy-dependent recycling operations.

## Products & Services

Recycled-plastic paving bricks/pavers for pathways, driveways and outdoor flooring, sold to schools, homeowners and construction customers; plastic-waste processing and brick manufacturing at company facilities in Nairobi.

## Customers

Schools, homeowners, and construction/landscaping customers in and around Nairobi, Kenya, purchasing durable, cost-effective paving materials.

## Partnerships

## Measurable Impact

- Approximately 500–1,500 plastic pavers produced per day, recycling roughly 500 kg of plastic waste daily (multiple press sources, circa 2021).
- Approximately 20 tonnes of waste plastic recycled cumulatively as of 2021 (per Wikipedia, sourced from contemporary press coverage).
- 112 job opportunities generated for garbage collectors, women and youth groups (company/press-reported).
- Compression strength reported at roughly 3x that of conventional concrete pavers, with a melting point over 350°C (company/press-reported technical specification).
- Nzambi Matee named UNEP Young Champion of the Earth, Africa region, in 2020.
- No independently audited figures for total lifetime tonnage recycled or current (2026) production volumes were found; the above figures reflect 2021-era reporting and may understate current scale.

## Technology

Proprietary self-built machinery (developed by Matee, completed 2019) for shredding, heating and combining recycled plastic with sand to form high-compression paving bricks.
