## Organisation Overview
Fill it Forward is a Guelph, Ontario-based company founded by CEO Matt Wittek that created what it describes as the world's first scannable reusable products. Customers or organisations attach QR-coded tags/stickers to reusable water bottles, cups and bags; scanning the tag on each reuse logs the action in an app, unlocks a charitable donation, and lets individuals and organisations track cumulative environmental impact (single-use items avoided). The company is a certified B Corporation and 1% for the Planet member, and works with corporate, hospitality, education and event clients to run branded reuse programmes.

## Business Model
Fill it Forward's core revenue comes from selling branded reusable products (bottles, cups, bags) embedded or paired with scannable QR tags to organisational clients — hotels, corporations, universities, and event organisers — who use the programme as an ESG/engagement initiative. Clients pay for the branded reusables and app/reporting platform; end users scan tags for free, generating usage data and triggering charity donations funded by the client or Fill it Forward. The company reports serving 1,500+ organisations globally, with case studies including a hotel achieving 207,000+ tracked reuses and a corporate client eliminating 350,000+ single-use bottles. Real-time reporting dashboards let client organisations demonstrate quantified plastic-reduction outcomes, which is central to its B2B sales pitch (ESG reporting and engagement value, not just the physical reusable product).

## Customers
Corporations (e.g. KPMG), hotels (e.g. The Westin Bayshore, Vancouver), universities (e.g. Hamilton College, Thompson Rivers University), and event/conference organisers (e.g. Connect Marketplace, Cvent), globally.

## Partnerships
Charity partners include charity: water, DIGDEEP, Wine to Water, and Second Harvest. Certified B Corporation; 1% for the Planet member; Climate Pledge signatory. Client/case-study organisations include KPMG, The Westin Bayshore, Hamilton College, Marriott St. Louis, HPE Aruba Networking.

## Measurable Impact
Company reports more than 6.7 million single-use products kept out of landfills/oceans since its 2012 launch (cumulative, self-reported). Case-level metrics: The Westin Bayshore surpassed 207,000+ reuses; KPMG eliminated 350,000+ single-use bottles; Hamilton College achieved a 98.5% bottle return rate and $7K+ savings. Survey data: 94% of users would recommend the programme; 82% feel encouraged to reuse more frequently. No independently third-party-audited figure located.

## Technology
QR-code scannable tags/stickers on reusable products; consumer mobile app for scan logging and donation unlocking; organisational analytics/reporting dashboard for tracking reuse volumes and impact.
