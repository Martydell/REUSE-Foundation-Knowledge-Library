## Organisation Overview

Izifill is an Indonesian startup, founded 2 February 2020 by CEO/co-founder Ichsan Mulia Pertama, providing IoT-based, touchless public water refill stations accessible via smartphone. The service lets tumbler users refill their own containers hygienically, without physically touching the dispenser, at automated water-station machines — reducing single-use plastic bottle consumption while also addressing hygiene concerns around shared touchpoints (a design consideration that became especially relevant during COVID-19). The company was incubated through LPIK ITB (the Bandung Institute of Technology's business incubator).

## Business Model

Izifill operates IoT-enabled refill-station hardware in public locations, allowing users to refill personal reusable water bottles/tumblers by interacting with the machine via smartphone rather than direct touch — addressing both plastic-waste reduction and hygiene. The specific revenue model (station sales, hosting fees, per-use charges) was not confirmed in sources accessed during this research pass. The company has been recognised in Indonesian environmental-startup roundups (e.g., Telkomsel's blog on environmental startups) and was profiled by Dietplastik Indonesia, an anti-plastic-waste NGO.
