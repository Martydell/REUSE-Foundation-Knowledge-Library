## Organisation Overview

A New Earth is an organic and eco-living retail store in Achrafieh, Beirut, co-founded in 2009 by Layane Makarem and Sabine Kassouf. It is widely reported as Lebanon's first certified organic store. The shop sells organic food, health and wellbeing products, personal care and beauty items, pet care products, and a "Green Lifestyle" category of eco-friendly household goods, delivered across Lebanon. Since around 2019 it has expanded to include products from small producers and family enterprises following fair-trade principles.

## Business Model

A New Earth operates as a bricks-and-mortar organic grocery and eco-goods retailer with home delivery across Lebanon and cash-on-delivery payment. Its catalogue spans seven categories: Food and Drinks, Fresh and Chilled, Health and Wellbeing, Personal Care and Beauty, Pet Care, Green Lifestyle, and a curated "Shop by Lifestyle" selection. The business positions itself around consolidating hard-to-find organic and eco-friendly products in one location, reducing the friction consumers face sourcing sustainable alternatives individually. It does not appear to operate a refill, deposit, or reusable-packaging system in the sense used elsewhere in this library — its "eco-friendly" positioning is centred on selling organic/non-toxic products and household items marketed as safer for the environment, rather than a return-loop or refill infrastructure. No evidence was found of a formalised plastic-waste-reduction program (e.g., quantified diversion, refill stations, or take-back scheme); this appears to be a general product-selection commitment rather than a measured initiative.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

A New Earth is a long-running (since 2009), independently owned organic and eco-living retailer that predates much of Lebanon's more recent zero-waste retail movement, giving it some historical significance as a market pioneer. However, it functions as a conventional retail store selling organic and eco-branded products rather than operating reuse-specific infrastructure such as refill stations, deposit-return, or reverse logistics.
