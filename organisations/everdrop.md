## Organisation Overview
everdrop GmbH is a Munich-based start-up founded in 2019 by David Löw, Daniel Schmitt-Haverkamp and Christian Becker. It develops dissolvable cleaning and laundry tablets, dishwasher tabs, and laundry powder designed to be used with reusable spray bottles and containers, cutting the plastic and water/weight footprint of conventional liquid cleaning products. everdrop is a certified B Corporation and has been recognised (including by the Ellen MacArthur Foundation) as a circular-economy product example.

## Business Model
everdrop sells direct-to-consumer via e-commerce and increasingly through retail partners across Germany, Austria, Switzerland, France, Italy and the Netherlands. The core model is a "tablet + reusable bottle" system: customers buy a reusable spray/bottle once and then repeatedly purchase compact dissolvable tablet refills instead of replacement plastic bottles of liquid cleaner, reducing packaging and shipping weight/volume. The company has raised substantial venture funding (reported around $102M total from investors including TriplePoint Capital, Lombard Odier and Felix Capital) to scale manufacturing and market expansion across Western Europe. As of a recent count (May 2026), the company reported 87 employees, consistent with an SME/scale-up stage.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment
everdrop is a solid, well-documented example of refill-based waste reduction in household cleaning, with real scale (87 employees, six-country distribution, ~$102M raised) and formal B Corp certification. It fits the Refill Systems category cleanly. Its relevance is somewhat bounded by being product-substitution (bottle bought once, tablets refill) rather than a closed-loop return/logistics system, but it remains a strong, verifiable European example worth including.
