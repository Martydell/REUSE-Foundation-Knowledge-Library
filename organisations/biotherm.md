## Organisation Overview

Biotherm is a French skincare brand founded in 1952 by biochemist Jeanine Marissal and collaborator Jos Jullien, who identified a "thermal plankton" microorganism in the spring waters of Molitg-les-Bains in the French Pyrenees and developed it into a cosmetic ingredient under owner Adrien Barthélémy. L'Oréal acquired Biotherm in 1970 and built a research and production centre for the brand in Monaco. Biotherm remains part of L'Oréal's Luxe division today, selling face and body skincare internationally, including in Spain via a localised biotherm.es site — the seed data's "Spain" listing reflects a market site, not the brand's true origin or HQ.

## Business Model

Biotherm operates as a L'Oréal Luxe division brand, developing and marketing premium skincare products (serums, creams, body care) built around its proprietary "Life Plankton" thermal-spring-derived ingredient, sold through department stores, perfumeries, duty-free and e-commerce globally. Its relevant circular-economy initiative is the Life Plankton Elixir "Blue Fountain," an in-store refill device developed with innovation studio Possible Future: a customer's glass Life Plankton Elixir bottle (itself made with 25% recycled glass) is purified (rinsed, dried, UV-sterilised), then refilled from a reservoir at the fountain, with each bottle laser-engraved with a wave motif to mark refill cycles. The pilot launched in February 2021 at El Corte Inglés in Madrid, Spain — a single-location trial rather than a broad rollout, reflecting an experimental approach to packaging reuse within a much larger conventional single-use product catalogue.

## Measurable Impact

## Technology

The Blue Fountain is a purpose-built in-store refill device: it purifies returned glass bottles (rinse, filtered-air drying, UV-light sterilisation) before refilling from an internal reservoir, and laser-engraves a wave pattern on the bottle to visually track refill cycles — a proprietary hardware system developed specifically for this pilot rather than a generic refill dispenser.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Biotherm/L'Oréal's Blue Fountain is a technologically interesting refill pilot — with genuine engineering (sterilisation, tracked refill cycles) — but it remains, as far as public sources show, a single-store trial (El Corte Inglés, Madrid, from February 2021) with no confirmed multi-site rollout, ongoing operation status, or impact data. As a large multinational's small-scale prestige-innovation pilot rather than a core reuse business model, its relevance to the REUSE Foundation library is primarily as an illustrative luxury-sector refill-technology case study rather than a scaled reuse operator.
