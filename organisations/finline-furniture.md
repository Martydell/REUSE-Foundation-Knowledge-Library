## Organisation Overview

Finline Furniture is a family-run Irish sofa, chair, and footstool manufacturer founded in 1979 by Kieran and Bridget Finane in Emo, County Laois, to fill a gap in the market for stylish, high-quality, Irish-made upholstered furniture. The company sells through its own showrooms in Dublin, Cork, and Galway as well as its headquarters. In addition to new furniture manufacturing, Finline runs "Revive," a take-back and reupholstery scheme that recovers worn Finline sofas from customers, restores them in partnership with a community organisation, and resells them at a discount — positioning the company as a circular-economy case study within the Irish furniture sector.

## Business Model

Finline's core business is manufacturing made-to-order sofas, chairs, and footstools sold through its own showrooms and website, targeting residential and commercial customers who value durability (products are sold with a 20-year guarantee). Its Revive scheme adds a circular revenue stream: customers returning an old, worn Finline sofa receive a €100 voucher toward a new purchase; the returned sofa's frame is stripped back by the "Loved Back to Life" programme run in partnership with Aiséirí, a community organisation supporting people in addiction recovery, and then reupholstered by Finline's own team using end-of-line and recycled fabrics. The restored sofas are resold as "Revive by Finline" pieces at roughly 20% below the company's lowest standard retail price point, giving cost-conscious customers a sustainable option while diverting furniture from landfill and creating work experience opportunities for the Aiséirí programme's participants. Revenue therefore comes from both new furniture sales and discounted resale of restored Revive pieces, with FSC-certified timber and recyclable packaging used across the manufacturing process.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Take-back (customer sofa buy-back via €100 voucher incentive)
- Repair (professional reupholstery/frame restoration)
- Reverse Logistics (collection of returned sofas for refurbishment)
- Material Recovery (reuse of sofa frames; use of end-of-line/recycled fabrics)
- Waste Reduction (diversion of furniture from landfill)

## Products & Services

- New made-to-order sofas, chairs, and footstools (residential and commercial)
- "Revive by Finline" restored/reupholstered sofas (resold at ~20% discount, 20-year guarantee)
- Sofa take-back and €100 voucher trade-in programme
- Showroom retail in Dublin, Cork, Galway, and Emo (Co. Laois) headquarters

## Customers

Irish residential and commercial furniture buyers; existing Finline sofa owners who participate in the Revive take-back scheme; budget-conscious customers purchasing discounted restored Revive pieces.

## Partnerships

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Finline's Revive scheme is a well-documented, socially-embedded furniture take-back and repair model that combines genuine circular-economy activity (buy-back, reuse of frames, reduced-price resale) with a meaningful social partnership (workforce development with an addiction-recovery community organisation). Its long operating history as a manufacturer (since 1979) and case-study recognition from Circulate Ireland lend it credibility, though publicly available impact figures are limited to modest first-year targets rather than audited results, and the scheme remains small relative to Finline's overall furniture output. It is a strong niche example of manufacturer-led take-back/repair in the furniture sector, of most relevance to reuse research on product life-extension and social-enterprise partnerships rather than large-scale systemic infrastructure.
