## Organisation Overview

Miya Cosmetics is a Polish natural cosmetics brand founded around 2014-2015, producing skincare, haircare, lip care, and intimate hygiene products marketed around low environmental impact and recyclable packaging. In 2021 the company (operated under Kanani Europe) was acquired by investment firm TDJ together with beauty-industry veteran Leszek Klosinski, who became board chairman, as part of a strategy to scale cosmetics brands with export and consolidation potential. Since the acquisition, Miya has expanded its portfolio to roughly 100 SKUs and grown distribution into markets including the UK, Spain, and Ukraine.

## Business Model

Miya operates as a conventional consumer cosmetics brand sold through drugstore chains and online retail in Poland and expanding across Europe (UK, Spain, Ukraine, and other markets), rather than a dedicated refill-service operator as the seed data characterized it. Its sustainability positioning centers on formulating natural-ingredient products and using recyclable/ecological packaging, marketed under a "zero waste"-adjacent brand ethos. Growth since its 2021 acquisition by TDJ has been driven by portfolio expansion (doubling to ~100 SKUs), international distribution deals with drugstore chains, and digital/social-media marketing. No dedicated in-store or online refill program specific to Miya was found in public sources at the time of this research; the seed description of it as a "cosmetics refill service" could not be verified and appears to overstate its circular-economy model, which is more accurately eco-packaging-focused product retail.
