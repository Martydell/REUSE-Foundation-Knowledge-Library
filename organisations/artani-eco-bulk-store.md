## Organisation Overview

Artani is a small, independently owned zero-waste bulk store based in Makassar, South Sulawesi, Indonesia — described in local press as the city's first store of its kind. Founded in May 2021 by Ria Lestari Baso, an agriculture graduate, the business was inspired by her own difficulty sourcing package-free baby food. Operating under the tagline "Beli secukupnya, pakai semuanya" ("Buy only what you need, use it all"), Artani sells agricultural, household and personal-care products by weight/volume without single-use plastic packaging, and has since expanded into a tea bar and creative workshop space.

## Business Model

Artani operates a physical bulk/refill retail store in Makassar plus online sales via Tokopedia (an Indonesian e-commerce marketplace) and Instagram, with no independently owned e-commerce website identified. Customers bring their own containers or purchase reusable ones, buying dry goods (natural powder detergent) and personal-care items (wooden toothbrushes, Lerak-based natural soap and body care) measured to their own needs rather than in pre-packaged units, avoiding single-use plastic. The business has diversified revenue by adding a tea bar (serving herbal tea, described as Makassar's first of its kind) and hosting creative workshops. As a small independent retailer, no revenue, staff size, or investment information is publicly disclosed.
