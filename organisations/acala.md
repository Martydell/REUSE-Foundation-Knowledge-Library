## Organisation Overview

Acala is a UK-based online retailer of zero-waste, plastic-free beauty and personal-care products, founded by Hanna Pumfrey in 2018 after she identified a lack of convenient, affordable package-free personal-care options in the UK market. Widely described in press coverage as one of the UK's first dedicated zero-waste cosmetics retailers, Acala sells both third-party sustainable beauty brands and its own-brand DIY beauty ingredients (oils, butters, waxes, clays, dried herbs and flowers) that let customers formulate products at home rather than buying pre-packaged items.

## Business Model

Acala operates a curated e-commerce model selling plastic-free and low-waste health, beauty, and DIY-beauty products (shampoo bars, soaps, skincare, haircare, dental products, and reusable accessories such as brushes and cloths) direct to UK and international consumers, alongside supply relationships with independent eco-retailers. A distinguishing element of its model is an own-brand reusable-packaging service: customers return used containers via postbox for professional cleaning and reuse, moving packaging from single-use to a durable, closed-loop format — described by the company as working toward a circular economy "where we write trash out of existence." The company has also discussed plans for a refill subscription service delivering products in durable, reusable packaging. Acala reports tree-planting per order and carbon-positive shipping via a partnership with Ecologi, and applies supplier codes of conduct requiring sustainability commitments from brand partners.
