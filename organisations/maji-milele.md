## Organisation Overview

Maji Milele is the Kenyan brand of Water Forever, a for-profit social enterprise founded in 2013 that installs and operates smart, prepaid water ATMs (communal water meters) across East Africa. The system lets communities and households pay via mobile money for water dispensed on demand, typically used to refill jerrycans, replacing costly or unreliable alternatives such as bottled/trucked water or unsafe informal sources. Water Forever's network extends into Uganda, South Sudan and Rwanda in addition to Kenya, with real-time monitoring for usage, payments and leak detection.

## Business Model

Water Forever/Maji Milele installs prepaid, automated water ATMs (communal water points and household/institutional smart meters) that dispense metered volumes of water in exchange for mobile-money or card-based prepayment, targeting underserved and low-income communities across East Africa, including arid/semi-arid counties in Kenya such as Wajir. The model is designed as a sustainable, self-financing alternative to donor-dependent water infrastructure: revenue from water sales funds operations and maintenance of the ATM network, while remote/online monitoring tracks daily usage, payments, and detects leaks to keep systems running with minimal on-site staffing. The company positions itself as a "social enterprise" bridging humanitarian water access and commercial sustainability, and has partnered with humanitarian and development organisations (e.g., Oxfam GB, Mercy Corps) to deploy systems in refugee-hosting and drought-affected areas, as well as with fintech/impact-finance partners (e.g., UNTAPPED) to provide financing to local water service providers who operate the ATMs.

## Products & Services

Prepaid communal water ATMs/kiosks (jerrycan refill points), prepaid water meters for individual household or institutional connections, smart water meters with mobile-payment integration, and an online monitoring platform tracking usage, payments and leak detection.

## Customers

Households, communities and institutions across Kenya (including Wajir County), Uganda, South Sudan and Rwanda, particularly in underserved, arid or refugee-hosting areas; local Water Service Providers who operate ATM infrastructure under Water Forever's technology and support model.

## Partnerships

Oxfam GB and Mercy Corps (supported deployment of prepaid water ATM in Wajir County); UNTAPPED (financial aid partnership for Water Service Providers, via Kenya Climate Innovation Center reporting).

## Measurable Impact

Company-reported figures (undated, per official website): over 800,000 children and adults accessing water through the network; over 1,200 prepaid water ATMs installed; over 100 million litres of water dispensed through its systems. No independent third-party audit of these figures was identified.

## Technology

Smart, prepaid water ATM hardware with mobile-money payment integration; online/remote monitoring system tracking daily water usage, payments, and automated leak detection across the meter network.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Maji Milele/Water Forever is a mature, multi-country water-access operator whose prepaid ATM model directly supports reuse behaviour by making refillable jerrycans a practical, affordable daily option instead of single-use bottled water or unsafe alternatives. Its combination of humanitarian partnerships (Oxfam, Mercy Corps), a commercially sustainable revenue model, and substantial reported reach (800,000+ users, 1,200+ ATMs) make it a strong example of refill-system infrastructure at scale in a low-income-country context, though reported impact figures are company-sourced and undated.
