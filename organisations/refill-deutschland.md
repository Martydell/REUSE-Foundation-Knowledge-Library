## Organisation Overview

Refill Deutschland is a German non-profit initiative, part of the international "Refill" movement originating in the UK, that maps and promotes free tap-water refill points nationwide via the "Karte von morgen" (Map of Tomorrow) to reduce single-use plastic bottle consumption.

## Business Model

Refill Deutschland operates as a non-profit community network rather than a commercial business. Cafés, shops, and public venues volunteer to become free "Refill stations," signposted with stickers, where anyone can refill a reusable bottle with tap water at no cost. The initiative is sustained through partnerships, donations, and integration with the broader "Karte von morgen" sustainability-mapping platform rather than transaction revenue.

## Measurable Impact

Reported around 8,000 Refill stations listed nationwide (per company website, not independently audited).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Refill Deutschland is a large-scale, low-cost community refill-station network that meaningfully reduces single-use bottle demand across Germany; a strong reference model for national refill-mapping initiatives.
