## Organisation Overview

Zero Waste Harare is a group of women, led by Barbara Tiki, who since 2021 have operated a plastic-waste buying and sorting station beside Willowvale Road in Harare. Community members bring plastic waste to the station, where it is graded and sold on to recycling companies, generating income for the women involved, many of whom are widows, divorced or single mothers.

## Business Model

The group buys plastic waste brought in by residents and businesses, grades and sorts it by type and colour, then resells it in bulk to recycling companies operating in Harare. The women were trained in plastic sorting and grading as part of the Zimbabwe Sunshine Group (ZSG), a programme established jointly by Harare City Council and the Zimbabwe Environmental Management Agency (EMA). The model shifted over time from the women actively collecting waste at dumpsites to the community bringing plastic directly to their roadside station.
