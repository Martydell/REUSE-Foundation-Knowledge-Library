## Organisation Overview

Réseau Envie is a French federation of social-insertion enterprises founded in Strasbourg in 1984, specialising in the collection, repair, refurbishment and resale of used household appliances (and, more recently, medical equipment), alongside collection, sorting and recycling of waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE). As of 2024 the network comprised 53 insertion enterprises across more than 100 sites in France, all holding ESUS ("Entreprise Solidaire d'Utilité Sociale") accreditation, combining environmental and social-inclusion missions by employing people in employment-insertion contracts.

## Business Model

Envie's member enterprises collect used and end-of-life household appliances (washing machines, refrigerators, TVs, small appliances) and, increasingly, medical devices, primarily through take-back arrangements, donations and WEEE collection points. Appliances suitable for reuse are repaired, tested and refurbished by employees on insertion contracts (a workforce-development model providing job training and re-entry into employment for people facing social/economic exclusion), then resold through Envie retail stores typically 30-60% below the price of equivalent new appliances, with warranties (commonly cited as two years). Appliances beyond repair are dismantled for parts and material recovery, feeding into the network's WEEE recycling activity. Envie states its network collects roughly one-third of all WEEE gathered in France, combining environmental circularity (extending appliance lifespans, recovering materials) with a social mission funded through retail resale revenue and applicable insertion-scheme public support.

## Products & Services

Collection, repair and resale of refurbished household appliances (major and small) and medical equipment with warranty; WEEE collection, sorting, dismantling and material recycling; job-insertion/training programs for employees.

## Customers

Budget-conscious consumers purchasing refurbished appliances through Envie retail stores; households and businesses depositing end-of-life appliances for collection/recycling.

## Partnerships

Not publicly available (specific named corporate/manufacturer partnerships were not confirmed in this research, though Envie operates within France's regulated WEEE producer-responsibility ecosystem).

## Measurable Impact

As of 2024, the network comprised 53 insertion enterprises across more than 100 sites in France; Envie states it collects approximately one-third (1/3) of all electrical and electronic waste (WEEE) gathered in France; refurbished appliances are typically sold 30-60% cheaper than equivalent new products (Source: envie.org official site, 2024 figures/press materials).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Envie is a mature, large-scale and well-documented French network combining appliance repair/reuse with a strong social-insertion mission and meaningful WEEE-collection scale (roughly one-third of national volume). Its 40-year track record, national footprint (100+ sites), and dual environmental/social model make it a strong reference case for repair-based reuse enterprises, particularly for the REUSE Foundation's interest in models that combine circularity with employment inclusion.
