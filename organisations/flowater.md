## Organisation Overview

FloWater is a US company operating purified-water refill stations built around mostly-stainless-steel construction, deployed in offices, gyms, schools, and other business/institutional settings (including at least one confirmed US school district, Oakland Unified School District). Its stations use a "7x Advanced Purification" filtration system claimed to remove up to 99.9% of contaminants including microplastics, lead, PFAS, chlorine, pharmaceuticals, and heavy metals, holding seven gallons of purified water on tap at all times with automatic refill and activated-oxygen tank sanitisation.

## Business Model

FloWater deploys and presumably services/leases refill stations to business and institutional customers (offices, gyms, schools, warehouses), replacing bottled or lower-quality tap water with an on-site purification and refill system — customers bring reusable bottles/cups to fill rather than purchasing single-use bottled water. The stations' stainless-steel fill areas incorporate injection-molded materials described as reusing scrap material, and front panels use high-strength polycarbonate. The specific commercial model (sale vs. lease, service contract terms) was not confirmed in sources accessed during this research pass.
