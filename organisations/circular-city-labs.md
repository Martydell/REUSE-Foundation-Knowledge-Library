## Organisation Overview

Circular City Labs is not an independent company but a German government-funded development-cooperation project. It is implemented by GIZ on behalf of BMZ, funded through the BMZ Initiative for Climate and Environmental Protection (IKU), with ICLEI – Local Governments for Sustainability acting as the main partner for municipal engagement. The project runs accelerator programmes for local, often female-led, small and medium enterprises (SMEs) in four locations — Tirana, Tbilisi, Medellín, and South Africa — to help them design and pilot reusable packaging systems that reduce waste and greenhouse gas emissions while strengthening women's economic participation.

## Business Model

Circular City Labs operates as a grant-funded accelerator/capacity-building programme rather than a commercial business. In each partner city, GIZ and ICLEI recruit 6–14 local SMEs (with a stated preference for women-led businesses) into an acceleration phase involving training on circular economy principles, reuse systems, and gender inclusion, plus one-on-one coaching. Participating enterprises develop individual pilot implementation plans for reusable packaging systems relevant to their local context, which they pitch at the end of the programme; select pilots then receive further support to test their models in the local market. Revenue does not flow to CCL itself — the project is funded entirely by BMZ through GIZ, and its "customers" are the municipal governments and local businesses it supports rather than paying clients. Local implementing partners (e.g., Co-PLAN in Tirana) assist with in-country delivery.

Excluded categories such as Deposit Return, Repair, and Rental are not identified as core CCL activities in public materials, though individual local SME pilots may touch on adjacent practices.

## Products & Services

Not a product/service company. CCL delivers: SME accelerator training and coaching; toolkits and guidance materials on reusable packaging system design; city-level circular economy diagnostics and reports (e.g., "Circular economy in Albania"); pilot funding/support for participant enterprises; and public webinars and knowledge resources.

## Customers

Beneficiaries are local SMEs (6–14 per city cohort, with a focus on women-led businesses) and partner municipal governments in Tirana, Tbilisi, Medellín, and cities in South Africa. There are no paying customers in the commercial sense.

## Partnerships

- GIZ (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit) — lead implementer
- BMZ (German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development) — funder, via the BMZ Initiative for Climate and Environmental Protection (IKU)
- ICLEI – Local Governments for Sustainability — main partner for municipal collaboration
- Co-PLAN (Tirana, Albania) — local implementing partner

## Measurable Impact

Not publicly available. Project materials describe programme design (e.g., 6–14 SMEs per city cohort) but no independently verified quantitative outcome metrics (e.g., tonnes of waste avoided, packaging reuse cycles achieved) were found as of the last verification date.

## Technology

No proprietary technology identified; the project provides training, coaching, and advisory resources rather than a technology platform.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Circular City Labs is a relevant but non-commercial reference point: it is a bilaterally funded (German government) accelerator programme, not a company, cooperative, or NGO with its own balance sheet. It is directly on-topic for reusable packaging systems and provides useful city-level case studies (Tirana, Tbilisi, Medellín, South Africa) and a growing library of practitioner guidance. However, because it is a time-limited project (2023–2025) rather than a permanent institution, its long-term relevance depends on whether GIZ/ICLEI publish durable outcome data or spin the model into an ongoing programme.

**Priority Rating: ★★★ Medium**
