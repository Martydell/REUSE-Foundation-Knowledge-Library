## Organisation Overview

DPD Deutschland is one of Germany's largest private parcel-delivery companies, founded in 1976 and now a subsidiary of Geopost (La Poste Group), operating roughly 81 parcel-sorting centres and around 8,000 employees domestically (with Geopost group-wide headcount considerably larger internationally). While DPD's core business is conventional single-use parcel shipping, this profile focuses on two specific reuse-relevant initiatives it has piloted: a reusable shipping-packaging partnership with Berlin startup hey circle, and clothing-donation collection through its delivery network. Both represent incremental circular-economy additions to an otherwise conventional logistics operation, not a wholesale reuse-model business.

## Business Model

DPD Deutschland's core business model is standard parcel logistics: collecting, sorting and delivering shipments for e-commerce and B2B customers via a national network of sorting centres, depots and delivery drivers, monetised through per-parcel shipping fees paid by retailers and senders. Its reuse-relevant initiative is a partnership with hey circle, a startup providing reusable shipping boxes and bags rated for up to 50 use-cycles; DPD integrated hey circle's reusable packaging into its shipping process, beginning with a pilot moving 200 XL reusable boxes between clothing manufacturer Trigema's production facility and its retail stores. Reusable packaging can be returned by customers either empty (folded) or filled with new outbound goods, dropped with a DPD driver or at designated drop-off points; DPD/hey circle then inspect, clean and recirculate the packaging for further shipments — without requiring a customer deposit, since a return label is included. Separately, DPD has also supported clothing-donation collection via its delivery/collection network, though details on how this specific programme operates are less thoroughly documented in the sources reviewed. These initiatives sit alongside, not in place of, DPD's core single-use-adjacent shipping business.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging — reusable shipping boxes/bags (via hey circle partnership) rated for up to 50 cycles
- Reverse Logistics — return of empty/filled reusable packaging via driver handoff or drop-off points, followed by inspection/cleaning/recirculation
- Take-back — clothing-donation collection through DPD's delivery network (details of specific programme not fully documented in sources reviewed)

## Products & Services

Core parcel collection, sorting and delivery services for e-commerce and B2B shippers; pilot reusable-packaging shipping option (via hey circle) for business customers such as clothing manufacturer Trigema; clothing-donation collection service.

## Customers

E-commerce and B2B shipping customers nationally; specific reusable-packaging pilot customer named in sources is clothing manufacturer Trigema (production facility to retail store shipments).

## Partnerships

- hey circle (Berlin-based reusable packaging startup; core partner for DPD's reusable shipping-box/bag pilot) — heycircle.com, packagingeurope.com
- Trigema (clothing manufacturer; named pilot customer for the 200-box reusable shipping trial) — dpd.com
- Geopost / La Poste Group (parent company)

## Measurable Impact

Pilot involved 200 XL reusable boxes shipped between Trigema's production facility and its stores, reportedly showing "less waste, lower costs, and seamless integration into existing logistics processes" (dpd.com/geopost press materials); reusable boxes/bags are rated for up to 50 use-cycles each. No independently audited company-wide figures for total reusable shipments, tonnes of single-use packaging displaced, or CO2 savings were found in verified public sources.

## Technology

hey circle's reusable box/bag system, including no-deposit return-label logistics and a cleaning/inspection/recirculation process at DPD facilities. No specific RFID/QR/app-based tracking system for the reusable packaging was confirmed in sources reviewed.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

DPD Deutschland is a large conventional logistics company for which reuse is a genuine but small-scale, pilot-stage addition (the hey circle reusable-box partnership) rather than a core business model — the seed data's framing as a company that "tests reusable shipping boxes" is accurate but should not be mistaken for a company-wide shift to reusable packaging. The hey circle partnership is a credible, verifiable, named pilot with a real customer (Trigema) and is potentially significant given DPD's shipping volume (350+ million parcels/year at Geopost/DPD group level) if scaled, but as of sources reviewed it remains an initial pilot rather than standard practice.
