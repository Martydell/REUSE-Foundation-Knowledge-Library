## Organisation Overview

Lizee is a Paris-based software company founded in 2019 that provides a Rental Management System (RMS) enabling brands and retailers to launch rental and resale business models within a few weeks rather than building the capability in-house. Its platform manages the complete lifecycle of a rental or resale item — in-store and online booking, shipping, returns, refurbishment, restocking and eventual resale — through a single system. Lizee has worked with major retail and apparel names including Decathlon, Adidas, Maje and Kiabi, positioning itself as infrastructure for the fashion and retail industry's shift from purely linear (sell-once) models toward rental and circular resale.

## Business Model

Lizee operates a B2B Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) model, licensing its Rental Management System to brands and retailers rather than operating consumer-facing rental services itself. The platform lets a retail partner offer subscription, one-off, or on-demand rental options to end consumers, while Lizee's software (and associated logistics coordination) handles the operational complexity: booking, shipping, returns processing, quality inspection, refurbishment, restocking, and — at end of a rental item's life — transition into resale. This "circular-economy-as-a-service" approach lowers the barrier for established retailers to test and scale rental/resale without building bespoke reverse-logistics and inventory systems from scratch. Its most detailed public case study is with Decathlon's "Location Decathlon" outdoor-equipment rental service, where Lizee's system supported pricing such as EUR 8/day to rent a tent versus a EUR 130 purchase price, and delivered documented profitability and environmental improvements. Other named clients (VF Corp, Adidas, Maje, Kiabi) span sportswear and fashion retail, indicating a multi-sector rather than single-vertical customer base.
