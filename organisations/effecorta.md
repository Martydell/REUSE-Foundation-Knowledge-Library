## Organisation Overview

Effecorta is an Italian bulk-grocery retail concept that opened its first shop in Capannori (Lucca) in August 2009, offering over 100 products — wine, oil, flours, legumes, detergents and more — sold loose ("alla spina") in customer-supplied or reusable containers, sourced through short supply chains. The concept was recognised by the Province of Lucca as an innovative start-up and by the Tuscany Region in 2010 as an "eco-efficient" enterprise. A related but separately incorporated shop, Effecorta Milano S.r.l., opened in Milan in 2013, extending the model to a second Italian city with both in-store and online/delivery shopping.

## Business Model

Effecorta operates as neighbourhood retail shops built around bulk dispensing rather than pre-packaged goods: customers bring their own containers or reuse purchased ones and pay only for the quantity of product taken, reducing single-use packaging waste. The original Capannori venture combined this with a "filiera corta" (short supply chain / km-zero) sourcing ethos, prioritising local producers of wine, oil, pasta, flours, legumes, cheese, meat and household/cleaning products, and was set up with social-cooperative values alongside a provincial start-up credit line and support from Cassa di Risparmio di Lucca. The Milan shop, a separately registered limited company, replicates the bulk/short-chain retail format for an urban customer base and has added e-commerce with in-store pickup or home delivery (including cargo-bike delivery), only offering a subset of in-store products online. Revenue comes from direct retail margins on bulk food, beverage and household products; there is no evidence of franchising beyond the two known locations.

Bulk-dispensed pasta, cereals, flours, legumes, preserves, craft beer and wine, olive oil and honey, home cleaning products, personal hygiene items, plus fresh bread, cheese, cold cuts and fruit/vegetables (Milan); online ordering with in-store pickup or home/cargo-bike delivery.

## Customers

Local residents and neighbourhood shoppers in Capannori/Lucca province and Milan seeking package-free or reduced-packaging grocery options; online customers within Milan's delivery area.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Effecorta is a small but historically significant early mover in Italy's bulk/refill retail movement, predating much of the wider "negozi alla spina" trend by several years and receiving regional recognition for eco-efficiency. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation is mainly as a long-running proof-of-concept for community-scale refill retail rather than as a scalable model — it has not expanded beyond two cities in 15+ years, and no impact metrics (packaging avoided, volume sold) are publicly disclosed. Useful as a case study of durability and local-sourcing integration in refill retail.
