## Organisation Overview

Meu Copo Eco was created in Brazil in 2011 with the goal of replacing disposable cups across everyday settings — events, shows, festivals, schools, offices, and cafeterias — with a reusable, personalized "Copo Eco" cup. The company manufactures, customizes, sells, rents, and sanitizes its polypropylene cups, which are BPA-free, dishwasher/microwave/freezer safe, and treated with an antimicrobial additive. It markets itself as having eliminated billions of disposable cups from circulation since launch, primarily through the events and corporate hospitality sectors.

## Business Model

Meu Copo Eco operates a combined manufacturing, rental, and hygiene-service model. Clients (event organizers, festivals, corporations, and venues) can purchase customized reusable cups outright for branding/promotional purposes, or rent cup stock for single events, with the company handling collection and washing/sanitizing between uses. Cups come in multiple sizes (200ml, 400ml, 550ml) and color/print options, allowing customers to use them as branded merchandise as well as a waste-reduction tool. Revenue is generated from direct cup sales (including bulk custom-branded orders for corporate clients) and from rental/washing service fees tied to event cycles. The 100%-Brazil-made production model keeps the supply chain local and supports faster turnaround for event clients.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Meu Copo Eco is a long-running (since 2011), Brazil-based reusable cup company with a scalable rent-wash-return model well suited to the events sector, a high-volume single-use hotspot. Its claimed billions-of-cups-avoided figure, while directionally credible given over a decade of operation, is a self-reported company statistic rather than an independently verified metric. As a domestic Latin American reuse operator with a proven business model (sales plus rental plus washing services), it offers a useful case study for event-based reuse systems. Priority: ★★★ Medium.
