## Organisation Overview

HiBAR was founded in 2018 in the Minneapolis–Saint Paul area by parents whose children attended school together, with a mission to eliminate single-use plastic bottles from personal care. The company makes waterless, solid-format shampoo, conditioner, deodorant, face wash, and body wash bars designed to perform like conventional liquid products while removing the plastic bottle entirely. HiBAR products are sold online and reportedly stocked in over 11,000 retail locations in the US, positioning the brand within the broader plastic-free/zero-waste personal care movement rather than a refill-station or packaging-reuse model per se.

## Business Model

HiBAR sells solid personal-care bars — shampoo, conditioner, deodorant, and body/face wash — as direct substitutes for bottled liquid products, eliminating the plastic bottle rather than reusing or refilling one. Each shampoo bar is marketed as equivalent to roughly a 24 oz bottle of liquid shampoo, and conditioner bars to a 32 oz bottle or more, so the waste-reduction case rests on concentrated, water-free formulation rather than a packaging-return loop. Products are sold direct-to-consumer via the company website and wholesaled to a large network of independent and specialty retailers (the company states over 11,000 stockists), rather than through big-box mass retail partnerships that could be independently confirmed in this research pass. Revenue comes from one-time and repeat bar purchases; the company also sells reusable tins/containers as accessories for storing bars, which is the closest element to a reusable-packaging component in its model. No refill-station, deposit-return, or take-back program was identified.

HiBAR is a genuine plastic-waste-reduction company but operates on a "reformulate to eliminate packaging" model (solid bars) rather than a reuse, refill, or deposit-return system — it does not close a packaging loop so much as remove the packaging need.
