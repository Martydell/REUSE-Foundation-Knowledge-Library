## Organisation Overview

fritz-kola is a German soft drink brand founded in a Hamburg student dorm in 2002 (some sources say 2003), built from its outset around returnable glass bottle packaging at a time when the beverage industry was trending toward single-use containers. The company distributes cola and other soft drinks in glass bottles designed to be returned and refilled up to 20 times before recycling, working with regional bottlers and suppliers to keep the returnable-glass logistics loop geographically efficient.

## Business Model

fritz-kola sells branded soft drinks (cola and other flavours) primarily through a returnable glass bottle system: bottles are collected, cleaned, and refilled through a deposit-return loop, reused up to approximately 20 times before being recycled, and the company deliberately works with regionally-located bottlers/suppliers to minimise the transport footprint of the returnable-glass system. The brand has also explored canned packaging for some products (per industry press), suggesting the returnable-glass model may not cover its entire product range. Revenue comes from conventional beverage retail/wholesale distribution in Germany and other markets.
