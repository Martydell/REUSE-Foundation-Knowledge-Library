## Organisation Overview

Mission Reuse is a Dutch transition program, not a standalone commercial B2B packaging company as the seed data suggested — it is a collaborative non-profit initiative launched around 2020 by social enterprise Enviu together with Recycling Netwerk Benelux and Natuur & Milieu (under the Fair Resource Foundation umbrella). Based in Rotterdam, its mission is to make reusable products and packaging "the norm" in daily life across the Netherlands and Belgium, working through pilots, policy advocacy, and knowledge-sharing rather than selling a proprietary packaging product itself.

## Business Model

Mission Reuse operates as a non-profit "transition program" rather than a for-profit vendor of reusable packaging. It identifies promising reuse innovations (reusable e-commerce shipping packaging, food-service containers, beverage cups for events/education, hospitality "horeca" reuse systems), tests them at scale through collaborative pilots involving reverse logistics, collection points, and industrial cleaning processes, and then works to connect the entrepreneurs behind successful pilots with larger market players (retailers, logistics firms, hospitality chains) to help them scale commercially. Alongside pilots, it publishes research (e.g. on reverse logistics for reusable packaging in the Netherlands) and organizes industry convening events such as the Reusable Packaging Fair. It also engages in policy advocacy, lobbying Dutch government bodies to support enabling regulation and funding for reuse infrastructure. Funding comes from Dutch government ministries, foundations (including Stichting DOEN), and partner organizations rather than product sales.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Mission Reuse is a significant ecosystem-building actor in the Dutch/Belgian reuse space, functioning as a neutral convener and pilot-incubator rather than a commercial reusable-packaging vendor — an important correction to the seed data's "B2B reusable packaging system" framing. Its work spans e-commerce, food service, and hospitality reuse pilots plus policy advocacy, making it a valuable knowledge and network node for REUSE Foundation, though it does not itself operate reuse infrastructure at scale.
