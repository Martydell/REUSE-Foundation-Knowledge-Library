## Organisation Overview

Friendlier is a Guelph, Ontario deposit-return reusable food and beverage packaging system founded by Kayli Dale and Jacqueline Hutchings, both University of Waterloo chemical engineering graduates. Customers pay a $0.50 deposit when receiving a meal in a Friendlier container, then scan a QR code (via app or online) after returning it to a collection bin to reclaim the deposit via e-transfer 1-2 weeks later. The company has grown to 200+ partner businesses across Ontario, including major names like Scotiabank, Loblaws, and Compass Group Canada, and reports diverting 400,000+ plastic containers from landfill.

## Business Model

Friendlier operates a B2B deposit-return infrastructure model: restaurants, cafeterias, and food-service operators (including major corporate clients like Compass Group Canada, under its "Planet Promise" net-zero-by-2050 commitment) pay to use Friendlier's reusable containers instead of disposable packaging. End consumers pay a small refundable deposit ($0.50) per container, return it to a collection bin, and reclaim the deposit via QR-code scan and e-transfer. Friendlier's partnership footprint spans Guelph, Waterloo, Hamilton, London, Stratford, St. Marys, Ottawa, Collingwood, and the Greater Toronto Area, and includes grocery deployment (49 Farm Boy locations across Ontario).
