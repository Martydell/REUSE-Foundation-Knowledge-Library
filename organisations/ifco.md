## Organisation Overview

IFCO is the world's leading pooler of reusable plastic containers (RPCs) for fresh food, founded in Germany in 1992. It pioneered an outsourced, closed-loop pooling model — branded "SmartCycle" — that replaces single-use cardboard and plastic packaging in fresh produce, protein, dairy, egg and bakery supply chains. IFCO operates a pool of over 370 million RPCs, used for roughly 2 billion shipments annually, serving grocery retailers and suppliers across Europe, the Americas and Asia-Pacific. It has passed through several private-equity owners since spinning out of Brambles in 2019.

## Business Model

IFCO's core model is business-to-business RPC pooling: it owns and manages a shared, standardized fleet of durable plastic crates that move fresh food from growers/packers to retailers. Instead of retailers or suppliers buying and disposing of single-use packaging, they rent capacity in IFCO's pool. After use, crates are collected from retail backrooms, transported to IFCO's regional service centers, washed/sanitized, inspected, and redistributed ("recollection" and "SmartCycle"). Revenue comes from per-trip or subscription-style pooling fees paid by participating suppliers and retailers. The model depends on dense logistics networks and wash-center infrastructure to keep crates cycling efficiently, and IFCO has grown partly through acquisitions (e.g., expansion into Chile via Rentapack) to extend geographic reach. The company reports employing roughly 2,200–2,500 people across some 30+ countries and six continents.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

IFCO is a flagship, at-scale exemplar of B2B reusable packaging pooling — one of the largest reuse systems in the world by volume, directly displacing single-use fresh-food packaging across dozens of countries. Its closed-loop pooling and reverse-logistics model is a reference case for reusable packaging system design. Ownership has changed hands repeatedly (Apax to Brambles to Triton/ADIA to Triton/Stonepeak), which is worth monitoring for strategic continuity, but the core reuse operation has continued uninterrupted since 1992.
