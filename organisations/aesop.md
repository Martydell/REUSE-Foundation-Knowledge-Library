## Organisation Overview

Aesop is a premium skincare, haircare and body-care brand founded in 1987 by Dennis Paphitis in Melbourne, Australia, originally as an offshoot of his hairdressing salon "Emeis." The brand grew into a global retailer of roughly 395 stores across some 27 countries, known for design-led signature stores and plant/lab-derived formulations. Aesop was majority- and then wholly-acquired by Brazilian cosmetics group Natura & Co between 2012 and 2016, then sold to L'Oréal in August 2023. Aesop is a certified B Corporation (certified 2020, re-certified 2024) and publishes sustainability commitments including packaging and decarbonisation roadmaps.

## Business Model

Aesop sells premium personal-care products (skincare, haircare, body care, fragrance) primarily through its own directly operated "signature" retail stores, complemented by department-store counters, online sales and a growing store network across Asia-Pacific, Europe and North America. Design-forward store architecture is a distinctive part of its brand identity. As part of its sustainability programme, Aesop operates two circular-economy mechanisms that should not be conflated: (1) a small "in-store refill" trial — customers buy a reusable glass bottle and refill it from dispensary machines with select formulations, returning empty bottles for exchange — piloted first at its South Yarra store (Melbourne) in 2021 and expanded to a second store, Aesop QV (also Melbourne), in December 2022; and (2) "Rinse and Return," a much broader in-store packaging take-back scheme (for recycling, not reuse) offered in over 150 stores globally. Aesop states a goal of offering refill solutions for 50% of its beauty packaging by 2030. Since the L'Oréal acquisition, Aesop's stand-alone financials are not separately disclosed in a form found in this research.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems — in-store bottle refill trial (South Yarra and Aesop QV, both Melbourne, as of last verified sources)
- Take-back — "Rinse and Return" packaging take-back scheme in 150+ stores worldwide (recycling-oriented, not reuse)
- Consumer Education — public sustainability/circularity reporting on brand websites

## Products & Services

Skincare, haircare, body care and fragrance products sold through signature retail stores, counters and online, plus in-store refill and packaging take-back services in select locations.

## Customers

Retail consumers of premium/luxury personal-care products, purchasing in-store or online across Aesop's ~27-country footprint.

## Partnerships

Not publicly available for the refill programme specifically (no named third-party partner for the dispensary/refill mechanism was found). Corporate ownership relationships: Natura & Co (former parent, 2012–2023) and L'Oréal (current parent, since August 2023).

## Measurable Impact

No independently verified quantitative figures were found for the refill trial itself (e.g., bottles refilled, plastic avoided). Company-stated goals: 50% of beauty packaging refillable by 2030; at least 50% recycled content across its plastic packaging range by 2025 (per its Sustainable Packaging Roadmap). B Corp certification (2020, re-certified 2024) is independently assessed by B Lab. Historical revenue reference: R$1.9 billion reported for 2020 under former parent Natura & Co; current revenue is not separately disclosed since the L'Oréal acquisition.

## Technology used

Custom-made in-store dispensary/refill machines used in the two-store refill trial. No consumer-facing app or digital tracking system for the refill programme was identified in public sources.

## Challenges

Aesop's own communications describe the refill programme as a "trial" that will "expand into more stores... over the coming years," indicating it remains small-scale (two stores identified) years after its 2021 launch, with no confirmed timeline for wider rollout found in public sources.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Aesop is a large, well-resourced global beauty retailer with genuine but currently limited reuse infrastructure: a refill trial confined to two Melbourne stores several years after launch, alongside a much larger but recycling-focused (not reuse) take-back scheme. Its scale, brand influence and stated 2030 refill target (50% of packaging) give it potential to meaningfully shift consumer refill behaviour in premium beauty retail if the pilot expands, but as of the most recent verified sources the reuse activity itself is small relative to the company's size. Corporate ownership by L'Oréal also means sustainability commitments are increasingly set at group level, and stand-alone impact data is not separately disclosed.
