## Organisation Overview

Bio-home is a household cleaning product brand owned by Lam Soon Singapore, a heritage Singapore FMCG manufacturer founded in 1929. The brand offers dishwashing liquid, laundry detergent, floor cleaner, multi-surface cleaner and fruit-and-vegetable wash formulated with plant-based, biodegradable active ingredients. Several bio-home products carry Singapore Green Labelling Scheme (SGLS) certification. In 2025, bio-home partnered with sustainability platform Ecoworks to pilot an automated refill station at a Singapore campus, allowing consumers to dispense cleaning products into reused containers rather than buying new plastic bottles each time.

## Business Model

Bio-home is a manufacturer-brand model: Lam Soon Singapore formulates, manufactures and packages the bio-home range and sells it through conventional FMCG retail channels — supermarkets (NTUC FairPrice), pet and household retailers (Kohepets, Good Dog People), and e-commerce marketplaces (Lazada, Amazon Singapore, Grocery Owl). Revenue is generated primarily through wholesale/retail sale of bottled cleaning products to Singapore consumers. The company has begun piloting a refill/dispensing model in partnership with third-party operator Ecoworks: an automated vending-style refill station was installed on a Singapore campus in 2025, letting users refill their own containers and pay only for product dispensed, reportedly saving up to 37% versus buying new bottles. As of the source reviewed, this refill distribution model was described as a pilot with potential expansion to residential estates and other locations, rather than bio-home's core national distribution model, which remains conventional pre-packaged retail with recyclable bottles.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Bio-home is primarily a conventional bottled-cleaning-product brand, but its 2025 refill-station pilot with Ecoworks represents a genuine, verifiable step toward reuse infrastructure — enabling consumers to refill and reuse containers rather than purchasing new plastic bottles each time. The initiative is still at pilot scale (single campus deployment as of the source reviewed) with no confirmed nationwide rollout or independently audited impact data. As a heritage FMCG manufacturer with established retail distribution, bio-home has meaningful potential to scale refill infrastructure across Singapore if the pilot expands, which would materially increase its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's mission.
