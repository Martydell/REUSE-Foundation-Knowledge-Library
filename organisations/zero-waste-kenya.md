## Organisation Overview

Zero Waste Kenya is a national non-profit initiative promoting integrated sustainable waste management across Kenya. It runs educational campaigns on waste segregation, reduction and recycling, engages communities and volunteers, and supports reporting mechanisms for illegal dumping, aiming to empower Kenyans to take responsibility for the waste they generate.

## Business Model

The organisation operates as an advocacy and education non-profit rather than a commercial waste operator: it runs public awareness campaigns, community engagement and volunteer programmes on the reduce-reuse-recycle hierarchy, and references Kenya's Sustainable Waste Management Policy 2021 in its messaging. It also promotes deployment of third-party waste-processing technology (e.g. plasma thermal decomposition) among its partners rather than operating such technology itself.

## Measurable Impact

Cites national statistics (per Kenya's Sustainable Waste Management Policy 2021): average Kenyan generates ~0.5 kg of waste daily; national waste composition ~60% organic/compostable, ~30% recyclable, ~10% other; less than 20% currently recovered. These are national policy figures cited by the organisation, not its own operational impact data.
