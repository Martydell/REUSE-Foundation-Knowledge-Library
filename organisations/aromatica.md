## Organisation Overview

AROMATICA is a South Korean clean-beauty and aromatherapy company founded in 2004 by Jerry Kim, who set out to replace synthetic fragrance with natural essential oils in cosmetics. Beyond product formulation, AROMATICA has built a multi-year sustainable-packaging programme spanning refill pouches (since 2016), an in-store bottle refill network beginning with "House of AROMATICA" (2020), a subscription refill-and-return service (2022), and — as of 2024 — the first Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi)-validated greenhouse gas reduction target held by a Korean beauty brand.

## Business Model

AROMATICA sells scalp-care, skincare, hair, body, and home-care products formulated with essential oils and herbal extracts, through e-commerce, retail partners, and its own physical stores in South Korea, with international expansion into Southeast Asia and other markets. Alongside standard retail, the company operates a layered reuse/refill strategy: single-material PE refill pouches (introduced 2016, refined to mono-material in 2021) let customers replenish core products with less packaging; an "Empty Bottle Collection" campaign (from 2020) and in-store refill stations let customers bring back or refill containers; and a 2022 "Returns and Refills" subscription lets customers receive refills via a recyclable delivery box. Manufacturing is centralised at AROMATICA's Osan "Smart Factory," which the company states is partly solar-powered and holds ISO 14001 certification (2023). The company does not disclose revenue or headcount figures.

## Measurable Impact

- Cumulative elimination of approximately 34 tons of plastic through container reuse and recycling initiatives, as of Q1 2024 (source: company/LinkedIn coverage of SBTi announcement, June 2024).
- SBTi-validated target to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 42% by 2030 against a 2023 baseline of 484 tCO2eq (source: SBTi validation announcement, June 2024).
- Solar panels (315 panels, 455W each) installed at the Osan facility in 2022, supplying a reported ~26–30% of the factory's annual electricity (sources vary slightly between company materials, see Sources).
- Up to 97% of products reported to use 100% post-consumer recycled plastic and 90% recycled glass packaging (company-stated, via press coverage of Zero Station launch, 2021).

## Technology

Standard e-commerce "Track Your Order" account portal (no evidence of a specialised reuse-specific package-tracking system beyond routine shipment tracking); solar energy generation and ISO 14001-certified environmental management system at the Osan manufacturing facility.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

AROMATICA is a well-documented, multi-year example of a beauty brand integrating refill systems, take-back, and science-based emissions targets into its core business, with independently verifiable milestones (SBTi validation, ISO 14001 certification) rather than only marketing claims. Its refill infrastructure is concentrated in South Korea and impact figures, while sourced, are largely self-reported. It is a strong candidate case study for refill-systems and consumer-education content in the knowledge library.
