## Organisation Overview
Happi Earth is an Australian-owned company, manufacturing from a factory in Molendinar on the Gold Coast, Queensland, that produces a certified-organic (food-grade certified), concentrated laundry liquid delivering up to 400 washes per pouch. Founded by Alf Orpen, the company was already established and being covered in industry media by May 2019. Its packaging is a kraft-paper pouch with a fine plastic lining and integrated handle/spout, designed explicitly for return and refill rather than single use, and the company has also promoted an environmental initiative planting mangrove forests via a partnership with the Worldview International Foundation in Myanmar.

## Business Model
Happi Earth sells direct-to-consumer via e-commerce, with its core product a concentrated organic laundry liquid in a large-format kraft pouch (400 washes per pouch, priced at roughly 25 cents/wash). Its circularity mechanism is a genuine take-back and refill program: customers can return used pouches to the company's Molendinar, QLD facility, where they are sterilised and refilled, and customers ordering a "Return & Refill" pouch receive a 20% discount versus a new pouch — directly incentivizing the reuse loop rather than just recyclable packaging. The company states one pouch replaces approximately 13 single-use plastic bottles per household annually. Independent lab testing (8 tests cited) found the product performed 61% better on average than Australia's leading laundry brand. Revenue is generated through direct product and refill-pouch sales rather than subscription or B2B licensing.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment
Happi Earth operates a genuine, well-defined take-back and refill loop — used pouches are physically returned, sterilised, and refilled, with a clear financial incentive (20% discount) driving customer participation — which is a stronger reuse mechanism than simple recyclable or compostable packaging claims.
