## Organisation Overview

Ekopulloyhdistys ry (branded Ekopullo) is a Finnish non-profit association established in 2004 that manages the deposit-based return system for refillable glass bottles and associated transport/crate units used by Finnish beverage producers. While closely affiliated with Suomen Palautuspakkaus Oy (Palpa) — from whom it sources staff and operating premises — Ekopullo is governed as a distinct, separately constituted membership association funded by entrance and membership fees from participating beverage companies. Its system is officially approved and monitored by Finland's Pirkanmaa ELY Centre (regional environment authority).

## Business Model

Ekopullo coordinates a shared, multi-brand refillable-glass-bottle logistics pool: member beverage companies contribute standardised refillable bottles and reusable transport crates into a common circulating stock, and Ekopullo manages inventory allocation, tracking, cleaning/inspection scheduling and repair coordination across the supply chain so that member companies always have sufficient bottle and crate stock available. Revenue comes from membership and entrance fees paid by participating beverage producers, distributors and wholesalers rather than consumer-facing retail margins. Returned bottles are washed and inspected after every collection before being redeployed, with the system engineered to maximise the number of reuse cycles per bottle before final recycling, distinguishing it from Finland's separate one-way container deposit scheme (also linked to Palpa) which is oriented toward material recycling rather than refill.

## Products & Services

Shared pool management for refillable glass beverage bottles and reusable transport crates; return-logistics coordination, cleaning/inspection scheduling, and inventory optimisation services for member beverage companies.

## Customers

Finnish beverage producers, distributors and wholesalers who are members of the association; end-consumers interact indirectly through in-store bottle returns.

## Partnerships

Suomen Palautuspakkaus Oy (Palpa) — provides staff, premises and operational support while Ekopullo remains a separate legal entity; Pirkanmaa ELY Centre — official regulatory approval and compliance monitoring body for the return system.

## Measurable Impact

Approximately 97% of refillable glass bottles placed into circulation are returned by consumers; bottles are refilled an average of 33 times before being recycled (Source: ekopullo.fi, official site, undated but current as of 2026 verification).

## Technology

Shared bottle/crate pooling and inventory-tracking system across member companies and the supply chain (specific software/technology platform not publicly detailed).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ekopullo is a strong, well-documented example of a functioning multi-brand deposit-return system specifically for reusable (refillable) glass packaging, with credible, sourced impact figures (97% return rate, ~33 refill cycles) that are directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's reuse mandate. Its governance model — an independent association operating alongside but distinct from Finland's larger recycling-focused deposit scheme (Palpa) — is also a useful structural case study for how refill-specific logistics pools can be organised apart from general recycling infrastructure.
