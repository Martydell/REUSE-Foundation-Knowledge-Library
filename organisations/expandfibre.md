## Organisation Overview
ExpandFibre was a large-scale Finnish research and development ecosystem launched in 2020 by Metsä Group and Fortum, with major public co-funding from Business Finland, to accelerate development of sustainable bioproducts from renewable pulp fibres, hemicellulose and lignin sourced from straw and northern wood. It was structured as a "leading company" (Veturi) programme comprising 35 publicly funded projects and 108 participating organisations — companies, universities, research institutes, associations and venture capital investors. The programme concluded in its original form in August 2024 when Business Finland's leading-company funding to Metsä Group and Fortum ended.

## Business Model
ExpandFibre was not a commercial company but a collaborative R&D ecosystem: it pooled roughly €140M of R&D investment (over €70M of it public funding from Business Finland) across dozens of member organisations to jointly develop fibre-based materials — including the Kuura textile fibre and Muoto fibre-based packaging products — intended to substitute for fossil-based plastics in textiles, packaging and paperboard. Rather than generating revenue directly, the consortium functioned to de-risk early-stage material science so that member companies (led by Metsä Group, a forestry industry group, and energy company Fortum) could commercialise resulting products independently. The programme formally ended in August 2024, though resulting technologies and products (e.g. Kuura, Muoto) may continue to be developed by the corporate members.

## Products & Services
R&D outputs including Kuura (textile fibre from wood pulp), Muoto (fibre-based packaging products), and related paperboard/fibre product development; the programme itself did not sell consumer products.

## Customers
Not a direct-to-consumer or direct-to-business commercial entity; "customers" were effectively the 108 member organisations and their downstream commercial customers for resulting bioproducts.

## Partnerships
Metsä Group, Fortum, Business Finland (public funder); 108 total member organisations including companies, universities, research institutes and VCs (individual members not exhaustively listed in sources reviewed).

## Measurable Impact
Programme-reported (undated/aggregate): approximately €140M total R&D investment, over €70M of it public funding from Business Finland; 35 funded projects; 108 participating organisations. No independently audited environmental-impact metric (e.g. tonnes of plastic displaced) located.

## Technology
Fibre-processing R&D across multiple projects; notable outputs include Kuura textile fibre and Muoto fibre-based packaging material.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment
ExpandFibre is a large, credible, publicly co-funded industrial R&D consortium relevant to plastic-substitution/material-recovery research rather than to reuse logistics directly. Its programme has formally concluded (August 2024), which materially changes its "Status" from the seed's implied ongoing consortium — this should be reflected wherever it is cited as an active initiative. Its main future relevance to the REUSE Foundation is via its commercialised outputs (e.g. Kuura, Muoto) rather than the consortium itself.
