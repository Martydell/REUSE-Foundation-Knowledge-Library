# L'Occitane

Global cosmetics group that pioneered refill pouches in 1980 and now operates in-store refill fountains and reusable aluminium "Forever Bottles" across dozens of markets, including the Philippines via local retail partners.

## Organisation Overview

L'OCCITANE en Provence is a French-origin, Switzerland-headquartered cosmetics and personal-care group selling body, hair, skin and home fragrance products in roughly 90 countries. Founded in 1976, it began experimenting with refillable packaging in 1980 and has since built out an eco-refill product line, in-store refill fountains, and a bottle take-back/recycling programme. The Philippines is served as a retail market rather than as a distinct operating subsidiary; products are sold online at ph.loccitane.com and through local retail partners rather than a wholly-owned Philippine entity with independently reported financials.

## Business Model

L'OCCITANE en Provence is one of several brands within the L'OCCITANE Group (alongside ELEMIS, Sol de Janeiro, Erborian, Melvita, L'OCCITANE au Brésil and Dr. Vranjes Firenze), selling premium body, face, hair and home-fragrance products through roughly 4,910 owned and franchised retail locations plus e-commerce, in about 90 countries. Its core reuse mechanism is the "eco-refill": a lightweight pouch or recycled-plastic tub containing concentrate that is used to top up a durable original bottle/jar, sold at a discount to the equivalent single-use product. Since 2021 the group has piloted in-store "Refill Fountains" — machines that dispense shampoo, shower gel and conditioner into a reusable 100%-recycled-aluminium "Forever Bottle," using a sterilising flash-cleaning system (Claranor technology) between fills — deployed in over 100 boutiques across roughly 33 countries. In the Philippines specifically, products (including a "Refills" product category) are sold via ph.loccitane.com and third-party retail partners such as Rustan's, Adora.ph and Zalora, rather than through a separately reported Philippine subsidiary. Revenue and reuse-specific financials are reported only at the group level.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging (refillable jars/tubs, reusable aluminium "Forever Bottle")
- Refill Systems (eco-refill pouches since 1980; in-store Refill Fountains since 2021)
- Take-back / Reverse Logistics (Pact/beauty-empties recycling collection programme in several markets)
- Consumer Education (in-store refill demonstrations, sustainability communications)
- Material Recovery (partnership trials using 100% recycled PET/PP in packaging)

## Products & Services

Body care, hair care, skincare, and home fragrance products, many available in eco-refill format (liquid soaps, shampoos, conditioners, shower gels, oils, hand washes). In the Philippines, the product range and refill SKUs available online largely mirror the group's global refill catalogue, sold under the ph.loccitane.com storefront.

## Customers

Retail consumers purchasing premium personal-care products in-store and online; in the Philippines, customers order via ph.loccitane.com or partner retail platforms (Rustan's, Adora.ph, Zalora, Lazada's Beauty by Lazmall).

## Partnerships

- Loop Industries — pilot (announced 2022) to produce bottles from 100% recycled PET using Loop's depolymerisation technology (2,000-bottle trial of shower oil bottles).
- Claranor — sterilisation technology supplier for in-store Refill Fountains.
- Ellen MacArthur Foundation — signatory to the New Plastics Economy Global Commitment; named an "engaged brand" in Foundation reporting.
- TerraCycle — referenced in some market recycling/collection programmes (verify per-market; not confirmed specifically for the Philippines).
- Philippines retail distribution: Rustan's, Adora.ph, Zalora (verified via product listings; formal distributor agreement terms not publicly disclosed).

## Measurable Impact

- Group-wide: eco-refill pouches use on average 85% less plastic than an equivalent standard bottle, and refill fountains are stated to use 94% less plastic than conventional packaging (L'OCCITANE Group communications, cited via Packaging Europe/Group L'OCCITANE news release).
- The group reports having 25 eco-refills in its range as of September 2020 (Group L'OCCITANE / industry press).
- The group's share of recyclable or reusable plastic packaging reportedly fell from 37% in FY2024 to 27% in FY2025, and L'Occitane's own Asia sustainability lead has publicly stated refill fountains "are not as effective as we had hoped" at reducing waste (Eco-Business podcast, 2024/2025) — indicating self-reported challenges rather than a straightforward success metric.
- No Philippines-specific reuse or refill volume metrics were found in verified public sources.
- Group ESG data are published annually; see L'OCCITANE Group ESG Report FY2024 and FY2025 for detailed environmental metrics (full breakdown not extracted in this research pass).

## Technology used

Refill Fountain in-store dispensing machines with Claranor flash-sterilisation technology for reusable "Forever Bottle" cleaning between fills. No app-based or RFID/QR reuse-tracking technology was identified in verified sources for this specific reuse programme.

## Challenges

L'Occitane's own Asia sustainability chief has publicly acknowledged that in-store refill fountains "may not be effective at reducing waste" as hoped (Eco-Business, 2024/2025 podcast), and the group's reported share of recyclable/reusable packaging declined year-on-year (37% FY2024 to 27% FY2025) — both documented in company/press sources rather than independent audits.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

L'Occitane is a large, well-resourced global cosmetics group with a genuinely long track record on refill (dating to 1980) and a scaled in-store refill-fountain and reusable-bottle programme spanning dozens of countries — making it a relevant reference case for reuse infrastructure in premium retail. However, its Philippines presence appears to be a retail market served via third-party distributors/e-commerce partners rather than a distinct operating entity with its own sustainability reporting, and the company's own recent statements suggest its refill fountains have underdelivered against expectations. Priority should reflect the global brand's relevance to reuse research, while treating "Philippines" as a market-access note rather than a standalone circular-economy initiative.
