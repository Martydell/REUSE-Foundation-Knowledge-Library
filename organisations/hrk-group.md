## Organisation Overview

HRK Group is a Vietnamese sustainable-packaging manufacturer founded in 2019 to develop alternatives to conventional plastic packaging. It operates through its Turn Green brand and platform, and has invested in dedicated production facilities — including GPS Plas (biodegradable/compostable resins) and PVA Pro (water-soluble PVA resin and flexible film packaging), the latter described as Vietnam's first factory producing water-soluble PVA resin. The company positions itself as a "one-stop sustainable packaging" supplier to F&B and retail brands across Vietnam.

## Business Model

HRK Group is a B2B materials and packaging manufacturer, not a consumer-facing retailer. It supplies raw materials, semi-finished goods, and fully customized packaging products across the sustainable packaging value chain, primarily to food & beverage and retail businesses seeking to replace single-use plastic. Its core technologies include AquaFlex (water-soluble PVA plastic that dissolves in water), BioFlex (starch-based biodegradable plastic), GRS-certified post-consumer recycled plastic packaging, and natural fibre/paper-based packaging, alongside finished formats such as flexible films, takeaway containers and eco-friendly utensils. Revenue comes from manufacturing and supplying these materials/products to brand customers rather than from direct-to-consumer reuse services; the "circularity" value proposition is centered on compostability and biodegradation rather than reusable packaging loops.

## Products & Services

Home-compostable packaging (e.g., "iBAG Compo," described as 0% plastic and compostable in home compost within 3-6 months), water-soluble PVA plastic film (AquaFlex), starch-based biodegradable plastic (BioFlex), GRS-certified recycled-content plastic packaging, natural fibre/paper packaging, and eco-friendly single-use utensils/takeaway containers.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

HRK Group is principally a compostable/biodegradable materials manufacturer rather than a reuse-system operator — its packaging is designed to degrade after single use, not to be reused or refilled. It is relevant to the REUSE Foundation as an example of Vietnam's broader plastic-alternative manufacturing sector, but its activities sit closer to "material substitution" than genuine reuse infrastructure, limiting direct alignment with reuse-focused priorities.
