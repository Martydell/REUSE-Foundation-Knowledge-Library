## Organisation Overview

Bottle None is a Canadian personal-care company based in Kelowna, British Columbia, selling solid, package-minimal haircare, skincare, and body-care products (shampoo and conditioner bars, deodorants, facial and body sticks) under the tagline "more living...less waste." Its distinguishing reuse feature is a line of travel cases and soap dishes that are 3D-printed in Kelowna from 100% recycled Canadian plastic, paired with a stated end-of-life return program in which worn-out cases can be sent back to the company to be reprocessed into 3D-printer filament for new products.

## Business Model

Bottle None sells solid-format personal-care products (bars rather than liquids in plastic bottles) direct-to-consumer online, reducing packaging at the product level. Its 3D-printed travel cases and soap dishes are manufactured locally in Kelowna using recycled plastic feedstock, and the company operates a closed-loop mechanic: customers can return broken or unwanted cases to be reprocessed into 3D-printing filament and remanufactured into new cases or bottles, rather than being discarded. The company sells direct-to-consumer via its own site and is also stocked through third-party Canadian eco-retailers (e.g., Portia-Ella, Simply Natural Canada, Baltic Marché, COBIA Beauty, Better Clean Canada, All Things Being Eco).

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging (3D-printed travel cases and soap dishes made from recycled plastic)
- Take-back (return program for worn/broken cases to be reprocessed into new products)
- Material Recovery (recycled plastic reprocessed into 3D-printing filament for remanufacture)
- Waste Reduction (solid bar-format products eliminating liquid plastic bottles)

No refill-station, deposit-return, rental, or reverse-logistics network beyond the direct take-back-and-remanufacture case system was identified.
