## Organisation Overview

Woola manufactures protective shipping packaging — envelopes, bottle sleeves and cushioning inserts — from waste sheep wool that would otherwise be discarded (an estimated 200,000 tonnes go unused in Europe annually). Founded by Anna-Liisa Palatu with Jevgeni Širai and Katrin Kabun, the company positions its "Bubble Wool" product as a reusable, water-repellent, compostable replacement for fossil-fuel-based bubble wrap.

## Business Model

Woola sells wool-based protective packaging (envelopes, sleeves, cushioning inserts) direct to e-commerce brands and fulfilment operations as a drop-in substitute for plastic bubble wrap and mailers. Revenue comes from B2B sales of packaging materials rather than a consumer-facing refill or take-back scheme; the wool is sourced as an upcycled byproduct of European sheep farming (wool graded too coarse or contaminated for textile use). The company is B Corp-certified and has received EU/Climate-KIC support and grant funding, alongside recognition including the 2023 LVMH Innovation Award (Sustainability & Greentech category).
