## Organisation Overview
Fill Up Buttercup is a non-toxic, zero-waste refill shop founded in August 2020 in Costa Mesa, California, by Jamie Lake and business partner Kristina Guccione. It sells bulk household and personal-care products (shampoo, conditioner, body wash, all-purpose cleaner, laundry detergent and more) that customers can refill using their own containers, alongside a curated online store of plastic-free, non-toxic branded products. The company grew from a single Costa Mesa location to a second store (Huntington Beach, opened around January 2023) and was recognised as the 2024 Small Business of the Year for California's 73rd Assembly district.

## Business Model
Fill Up Buttercup operates a dual model: a physical refill shop where customers bring their own containers to purchase bulk household and body-care products by weight/volume (reducing packaging waste per purchase), plus an e-commerce store selling third-party non-toxic, plastic-free branded products (e.g. bamboo toothbrushes, mineral sunscreen) shipped nationally. Revenue comes from in-store refill sales, online product sales, and local delivery services in its California market. The founder's personal motivation — researching links between household product ingredients and health/toxic body burden — shapes the company's non-toxic/ingredient-transparency positioning alongside its plastic-reduction mission. The business has expanded from one to two physical locations since 2020, indicating a small but growing bricks-and-mortar + e-commerce hybrid.
