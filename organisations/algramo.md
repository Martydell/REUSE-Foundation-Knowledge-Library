## Organisation Overview

Algramo is a Chilean social enterprise founded by José Manuel Moller that developed a "Packaging-as-a-Wallet" refill model: RFID-tagged reusable containers linked to a mobile app let customers buy household staples such as detergent, rice and pet food "by the gram" at neighbourhood stores or via home delivery, avoiding the higher per-unit cost typically paid by lower-income households buying small single-use packets. Algramo began as a bulk-dispenser concept aimed at Santiago's poorer neighbourhoods and has since scaled into a B Corp-certified platform working with multinational FMCG brands and international retailers, with pilot operations extending beyond Chile.

## Business Model

Algramo operates a B2B2C refill infrastructure model. It partners with FMCG manufacturers (Unilever, Nestlé's Purina, Colgate-Palmolive, Clorox reported among brand partners) and retailers (including Walmart and, in a UK trial, Lidl) to install RFID-enabled smart dispensers in neighbourhood ("almacén") stores and, in some markets, on home-delivery routes using electric tricycles. Customers pay per gram/unit of product rather than for pre-packaged volumes, and reusable containers are tracked and refilled repeatedly — one reported Unilever detergent pilot saw containers refilled up to 15 times each. The model directly targets a `poverty penalty' in packaged-goods pricing, where lower-income consumers historically paid more per unit for small-format packaging. Algramo is a Certified B Corp and was an early participant in Sistema B Chile's certified-enterprise network; UNEP and Closed Loop Partners have both profiled it as a leading refill-economy case study.

## Products & Services

RFID-enabled refill dispensers and reusable containers for household staples (detergent, cleaning products, rice, pet food and other packaged goods); a consumer mobile app for tracking purchases, refills and environmental impact; B2B refill infrastructure licensed to retail partners and FMCG brands.

## Customers

Lower-income households in Santiago neighbourhood stores; expanding retail-chain customers (e.g., piloted with Walmart in the US and Lidl in the UK); FMCG brand partners distributing products through the refill network.

## Partnerships

- Walmart — US retail partnership/pilot
- Lidl — UK trial

## Measurable Impact

- UNEP reports Algramo customers have reused 900,000+ pieces of packaging since 2020, equivalent to 100,000+ kg of plastic avoided (UNEP feature, unep.org).
- Operating in 2,000+ (per UNEP/Closed Loop Partners) to 1,700+ (per other sourcing) family-owned stores, reaching 275,000–325,000+ end customers in Santiago, figures vary by source and reporting date.
- Sales reportedly surged 356% between April and June 2020 during Santiago's COVID-19 lockdown (Ellen MacArthur Foundation case study).
- Recognition: "Most Innovative Company in Latin America" (2015, 2020); 1st place, Ocean Plastic Innovation Challenge (2019); B Corp "Best for the World" award (Chile cohort).
- No independently audited (non-company/non-NGO) impact figures were found; cited metrics originate from UNEP, Closed Loop Partners and Ellen MacArthur Foundation case studies, which draw on company-reported data.

## Technology used

RFID-tagged reusable packaging ("smart packaging"); IoT-enabled dispensing machines; consumer mobile app for purchase tracking, refill history and environmental-impact reporting; electric-tricycle-based home delivery in some markets.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Algramo is one of the most well-documented and frequently cited refill-economy case studies globally, with substantive third-party validation from UNEP, the Ellen MacArthur Foundation, the World Economic Forum and Closed Loop Partners, plus B Corp certification. Its dual focus on affordability and plastic-waste reduction — targeting the "poverty penalty" faced by low-income consumers — gives it a distinctive social-equity angle relevant to the REUSE Foundation's mission beyond pure environmental metrics. Its named partnerships with major multinational FMCG brands (Unilever, Nestlé/Purina, Colgate-Palmolive, Walmart, Lidl) indicate genuine commercial traction beyond a single-market pilot. The main gap in the record is a firmly resolved founding year (sources genuinely conflict between 2011 and 2013) and independently audited (rather than NGO/company-reported) impact figures.
