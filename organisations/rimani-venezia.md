## Organisation Overview

Rimani is a packaging-free bulk shop in Venice's Castello district, founded in 2020 by Lisa and Orsetta, selling bulk pasta, biscuits, teas and other dry goods alongside refillable detergents and cut soaps, plus local artisanal products. Its name plays on "ri-mani" ("recycled" + "hands"), reflecting a philosophy of reuse and handmade craft.

## Business Model

Rimani operates as a small independent bulk-refill retail shop: customers bring their own containers (especially for liquids) to purchase dry goods, detergents, and soaps in the quantity they need, reducing packaging waste. The shop also stocks locally made Venetian artisanal goods, positioning it as a community-oriented, sustainability-focused retailer rather than a chain or franchise.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems (bulk dry goods, detergent and soap refill)
- Reusable Packaging (customer-supplied containers encouraged)
- Waste Reduction (core store mission)
- Consumer Education (promotes plastic-free, low-waste shopping habits)

## Products & Services

Bulk dry foods (pasta, biscuits, teas, infusions), refillable detergents, cut soaps, and locally made Venetian artisanal goods and accessories, sold from a single physical storefront.
