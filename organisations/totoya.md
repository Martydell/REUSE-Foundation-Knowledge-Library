## Organisation Overview
Launched July 3, 2021 in Kyoto, Totoya is billed as Japan's first zero-waste supermarket, selling fresh produce, cleaning supplies, noodles and nuts entirely package-free through a bring-your-own-container (BYOC) bulk-refill system, with jars and stainless-steel lunch boxes available on a deposit basis.

## Business Model
B2C zero-waste grocery retail: customers weigh and fill their own (or store-provided/deposit) containers for bulk goods; an RFID label system developed with technology partner DIGI (Teraoka Seiko Co.) tags each container's tare weight once, streamlining repeat self-service weighing at checkout.
