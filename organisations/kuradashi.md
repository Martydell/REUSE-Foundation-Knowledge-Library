## Organisation Overview

Kuradashi Co., Ltd. is a Tokyo-based e-commerce company founded in 2014 by Tatsuya Sekito after he witnessed large volumes of food waste at processing facilities while working for a Japanese trading firm in China. Its "Kuradashi.jp" marketplace sells surplus, near-expiry or overstocked food, beverages, health and beauty items, and daily necessities at discounted prices, framed around the Japanese "mottainai" (regret over waste) ethic. A portion of proceeds is donated to non-profit organisations spanning disaster relief and environmental protection. The company received the Japanese Ministry of the Environment's Food Loss Reduction Promotion Award in 2020, achieved Benefit Corporation certification in 2022, and listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange in June 2023.

## Business Model

Kuradashi operates a business-to-consumer e-commerce marketplace that buys or intermediates surplus inventory — food nearing best-before dates, overproduced goods, packaging-changed items, and other saleable-but-hard-to-move stock — from manufacturers and retailers, then resells it to registered members at a discount. As of 2024 the platform reported roughly 1,600 partner companies (including major manufacturers such as Nestlé and Coca-Cola) supplying surplus goods, and approximately 515,000 registered members purchasing them. Kuradashi's revenue comes from the margin/commission on these transactions; part of proceeds is directed to partner non-profit organisations as a social-contribution component embedded in the purchase, giving customers a dual value proposition of discounted goods plus donation impact. This model converts what would otherwise be discarded or destroyed surplus stock into sold, consumed product — a food-loss and waste-reduction mechanism rather than a reuse/refill packaging system. The company's 2023 Tokyo Stock Exchange listing reflects a shift to conventional public-company governance and capital-raising.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Waste Reduction — resale of surplus/near-expiry food and goods that would otherwise be discarded, reported at over 20,000 cumulative tons diverted (as of 2024)
- Consumer Education — "mottainai" waste-consciousness messaging embedded in marketplace branding and marketing
- Other (Social Contribution / Redistribution) — donation of a portion of proceeds (money and/or product) to non-profit organisations, including disaster relief and environmental causes

## Products & Services

Online marketplace selling discounted surplus food, beverages, health and beauty products, and daily necessities sourced from manufacturer/retailer partners; associated social-contribution donation mechanism tied to purchases.

## Customers

Japanese consumers (approximately 515,000 registered members as of 2024) purchasing discounted surplus goods via the Kuradashi.jp platform.

## Partnerships

## Measurable Impact

More than 20,000 cumulative tons of food loss reduced as of 2024 (company-reported); approximately 515,000 members and 1,600+ partner companies as of 2024 (company-reported); Benefit Corporation certification obtained 2022; Tokyo Stock Exchange listing June 2023; Ministry of Environment Food Loss Reduction Promotion Award, 2020. Figures are company-reported/press-reported; independent third-party audit of the tonnage figure was not located.

## Technology

E-commerce marketplace platform matching surplus-inventory supply from manufacturer/retailer partners with consumer demand; holds ancient-goods trading, alcoholic-beverage retail, and wholesale licenses to support diverse product categories. No RFID or physical reuse-packaging technology identified — the platform is a digital resale marketplace, not a physical reuse-logistics system.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Kuradashi is a well-documented, publicly listed company with a substantial, verifiable scale (515,000 members, 1,600+ partners, 20,000+ tons of food loss reduced) and clear alignment with waste-reduction goals, though its core activity is surplus-goods resale/redistribution rather than reuse, refill or return of packaging/products in the strict sense the REUSE Foundation prioritises. It is a strong, credible example of the adjacent "food loss and waste" circular-economy category and worth including for its scale and verifiable public-company disclosure, even though it sits at the edge of the Foundation's core reuse-infrastructure focus.
