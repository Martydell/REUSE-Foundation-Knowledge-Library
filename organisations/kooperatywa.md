Warsaw's first grassroots food cooperative, running member-owned shops selling organic, seasonal and bulk/refill goods direct from small farms.

## Organisation Overview

Kooperatywa Dobrze is Poland's first grassroots consumer food cooperative, founded as an association in Warsaw in July 2013 before opening its first member-run shop in 2014 and a second in 2016. The cooperative sources organic, seasonal food directly from around 40 small-scale Polish farms annually, cutting out conventional retail intermediaries. As of 2023 it comprised roughly 206 member households (around 500 individual members), employed 18 staff, and operated two Warsaw shops (Andersa 27 and Polna 18/20). Members contribute both membership fees and monthly work hours in exchange for lower food prices, governed collectively through general assemblies under international cooperative principles. In 2022 the cooperative converted its legal structure into a formal multi-stakeholder cooperative.

## Business Model

Kooperatywa Dobrze operates a member-ownership consumer cooperative model rather than a conventional retail business. Member households pay a membership fee and commit to contributing roughly three hours of unpaid work per month (e.g., stocking shelves, staffing tills, coordinating orders) in exchange for access to lower-priced organic and seasonal groceries, many sold in bulk/refillable format (dry foods, oils, detergents) to reduce packaging. The cooperative buys directly from approximately 40 small-scale organic farms each year, shortening the supply chain and supporting local agriculture. Governance is democratic, with major decisions made through general member assemblies rather than top-down management, and the organisation operates debt-free, funded through membership fees, donations and crowdfunding campaigns (including a PolakPotrafi.pl campaign to help open its second shop). This model prioritises affordability, community ownership and reduced packaging waste over conventional profit-driven retail.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems — bulk/unpackaged sale of dry foods, oils and detergents reducing single-use packaging
- Sharing — cooperative/collective ownership model with shared member labour and shared purchasing
- Consumer Education — reported 70+ educational and community events on sustainable/local food systems
- Waste Reduction — direct-from-farm sourcing shortening supply chains and reducing associated packaging/transport waste

## Products & Services

Organic, seasonal groceries including bulk/refillable dry goods, oils and household products, sourced directly from approximately 40 partner small-scale farms; two member-run retail shops in Warsaw.

## Customers

Cooperative members — approximately 206 member households (around 500 individuals as of 2023) in the Warsaw area who pay membership fees and contribute work hours to access the shops.

## Partnerships

- Approximately 40 small-scale Polish organic farms (annual direct-sourcing partners)
- PolakPotrafi.pl (Polish crowdfunding platform) — funded the cooperative's second shop opening

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Kooperatywa Dobrze is a well-documented, long-running (since 2013) grassroots cooperative that embeds bulk/refill purchasing and direct farm sourcing into a genuinely democratic, member-owned governance model — a strong example of community-scale reuse and waste-reduction infrastructure. Its impact is necessarily local (two Warsaw shops, ~500 members) rather than nationally or internationally scaled, and no independently verified environmental impact metrics are available. It is a credible, verifiable case study of cooperative-based refill retail relevant to REUSE Foundation's interest in alternative ownership/governance models for reuse systems.
