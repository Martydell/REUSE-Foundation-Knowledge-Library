## Organisation Overview
Founded in 2010 by Daphna Nissenbaum and Tal Neuman, TIPA develops compostable, flexible films and laminates for food and apparel packaging that mimic conventional plastic's durability and barrier properties but decompose within roughly 180 days. The company has raised over $130M across funding rounds.

## Business Model
B2B materials supplier: TIPA licenses/sells its patented compostable polymer-blend films and laminates to brand owners and packaging converters as drop-in replacements for conventional flexible plastic packaging, monetising through material sales and industry partnerships (e.g. with Google Express).

## Products & Services
Compostable flexible films and laminates for fresh/dry food packaging and apparel bags.

## Customers
Food brands, apparel companies and packaging converters worldwide.

## Partnerships
Google Express (compostable packaging supply); multiple VC investors including Meitav Dash, Millennium Food Tech.

## Measurable Impact
Company claims its material decomposes in ~180 days vs. ~1,000 years for conventional plastic (company-stated figure, not independently verified).
