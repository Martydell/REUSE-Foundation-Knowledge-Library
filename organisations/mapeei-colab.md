## Organisation Overview

Mapeei Colab describes itself as Brazil's first "zero waste" retail space, opened in late 2018 in São Paulo (initially on Rua Augusta) as "Mapeei — Uma Vida Sem Plástico" by co-founders Lívia and designer Lori Vargas, before relaunching under an expanded curated-marketplace format, Mapeei Colab Sustentável, at a new Pinheiros address. The store/online platform curates and sells products from a large number of Brazilian sustainable brands, artisans and small businesses spanning home goods, cosmetics, wellness, and accessories, with a stated mission of reducing waste and promoting conscious, low-impact consumption.

## Business Model

Mapeei Colab operates as a curated multi-brand retail marketplace, both as a physical store in São Paulo's Pinheiros district and via an e-commerce platform, selling products from a network of roughly 70-85+ Brazilian sustainability-focused brands, artisans and small producers. Rather than manufacturing its own goods, Mapeei's business model is curation and distribution: it vets and hosts third-party brands across categories such as home goods, wellness, cosmetics and accessories, taking a retail margin or commission on sales, while positioning itself as a community and educational hub for low-waste, plastic-free living. The venture originated as a crowdfunded project (via Catarse) under the name "Uma Vida Sem Plástico" before evolving into the current "Colab Sustentável" format, reflecting a shift from a single storefront to a broader collaborative-marketplace model that supports small sustainable businesses in reaching customers.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Mapeei Colab is a notable early mover in Brazil's zero-waste retail space, credited in Brazilian media as the country's first dedicated "lixo zero" store, and has since evolved into a broader curated marketplace supporting dozens of sustainable small brands. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's library is as a retail-aggregation and consumer-education model rather than a reuse-infrastructure operator (no refill dispensers, deposit-return, or packaging-reuse system of its own was verified). Given the lack of published impact data and its focus on retail curation rather than direct reuse operations, it is a useful illustrative case but not a top research priority.
