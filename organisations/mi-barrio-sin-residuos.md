## Organisation Overview

Mi Barrio Sin Residuos ("My Neighbourhood Without Waste") is a household recycling pilot launched in Asunción, Paraguay on World Recycling Day 2021, under the UNDP-led Asunción Sustentable Project. It links residents in five Asunción neighbourhoods directly with the San Francisco Neighbourhood Recyclers Association, a cooperative of informal waste pickers, via a differentiated (source-separated) collection service. The initiative pairs technology (a simple registration platform) with in-person collection to build trust between households and recyclers, aiming to formalize and dignify waste-picker livelihoods while increasing household recycling participation.

## Business Model

This is a donor/government-funded pilot program rather than a commercial enterprise. Funding and coordination come from UNDP Paraguay, the Municipality of Asunción, and Paraguay's environment ministry (MADES), with Coca-Cola Paraguay as a private-sector partner. Operationally, residents register through a platform to participate; they separate recyclables at home; collection is carried out by the social enterprise Soluciones Ecológicas working alongside the San Francisco Neighbourhood Recyclers Association, an organized waste-picker cooperative. The project's stated theory of change — validated through an impact evaluation — is that direct household-to-recycler interaction builds interpersonal trust, which increases sustained participation in waste separation more than anonymous collection systems would. A second edition of the program is underway, expanding to additional waste-picker communities and neighbourhoods.

## Products & Services

Household recyclables registration platform; differentiated (source-separated) door-to-door recyclables collection service; capacity-building programs for waste-picker cooperatives; impact evaluation/research documentation of the trust-building model.

## Customers

Not a commercial service — "customers"/participants are households in five (initially) Asunción neighbourhoods, plus waste-picker cooperative members as service-delivery partners.

## Partnerships

UNDP Paraguay; Municipality of Asunción; Ministry of Environment and Sustainable Development (MADES); Coca-Cola Paraguay; Soluciones Ecológicas (implementing social enterprise); San Francisco Neighbourhood Recyclers Association (waste-picker cooperative).

## Measurable Impact

Pilot recovered more than 4 tonnes of recyclable material from participating households (UNDP-reported figure, pilot phase). An impact evaluation found that direct interaction between residents and waste pickers increased household trust and participation in source separation, though no separate quantified participation-rate figure was found in public sources.

## Technology

Simple household registration/participation platform (details of the underlying software not publicly available); no advanced digital tracking system identified.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Mi Barrio Sin Residuos is a modest-scale but methodologically interesting UNDP pilot notable for its explicit focus on social trust as the mechanism driving recycling participation, and for integrating informal waste pickers into formal municipal recycling infrastructure. It is not a reuse/refill business but a material-recovery and consumer-education program; its relevance to REUSE Foundation's reuse-focused mandate is secondary, though its inclusion of waste-picker livelihoods and household behaviour-change data may be useful for broader circular-economy policy research. Priority: ★★ Low.
