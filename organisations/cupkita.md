## Organisation Overview

Cupkita was a Jakarta-based reusable-cup deposit-return pilot, developed as a venture within Enviu's Zero Waste Living Lab programme and explicitly described in sourcing as "a replication of Muuse," a similar reusable-cup company operating in San Francisco, Singapore, and Hong Kong. The service let coffee-shop customers receive drinks in reusable cups against a refundable digital deposit paid via QR code, returning cups to participating shops or smart bins to reclaim the deposit, with Cupkita handling collection, cleaning, and redistribution. As of this research, the venture's status is listed as inactive/pilot phase.

## Business Model

Cupkita's model followed a standard QR-deposit reusable-cup mechanism: customers pay a refundable digital deposit via QR code when receiving a drink in a Cupkita reusable cup, then return the cup to a participating coffee shop or smart collection bin to get the deposit back, with Cupkita responsible for the collection-cleaning-redistribution loop. As an Enviu Zero Waste Living Lab venture, it was likely structured as an incubated pilot rather than an independently scaled commercial operation, targeting Jakarta coffee shops and cafés as its initial market — a sector-context figure cited that a typical Jakarta coffee shop sells ~125 single-use cups daily (major chains up to 1,000/day), with an average store able to avoid an estimated 525-2,100 kg of waste annually by switching to reusable cups.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Cupkita is a relevant example of the Enviu Zero Waste Living Lab's incubation approach to piloting reuse systems in emerging markets, explicitly modelled on the more established Muuse system, but its confirmed inactive/pilot-only status significantly limits its current relevance as an operating case study — it should be catalogued as a historical pilot rather than an active reuse operator.
