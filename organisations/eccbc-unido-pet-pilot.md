# ECCBC / UNIDO PET Pilot (Morocco)

Three-year industrial pilot in Morocco building a financially viable bottle-to-bottle PET collection and recycling system, led by Equatorial Coca-Cola Bottling Company with UNIDO and a multi-partner consortium under the EU/Finland-funded SWITCH2CE programme.

## Organisation Overview

The ECCBC/UNIDO PET pilot is a three-year industrial project in Morocco aiming to build a scalable, financially viable system for collecting and recycling PET bottles back into new food-grade bottles (bottle-to-bottle recycling), rather than downcycling into lower-value plastic. It is led by Equatorial Coca-Cola Bottling Company (ECCBC) in partnership with the United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO), under the EU- and Finland-funded SWITCH to Circular Economy Value Chains (SWITCH2CE) programme. The pilot combines household- and point-of-sale-level PET collection with technical and financial feasibility work to validate rPET use in beverage packaging.

## Business Model

This is a donor- and corporate-funded pilot project, not a revenue-generating company, so it has no independent business model in the conventional sense. Funding and technical support come from the EU and Government of Finland (via UNIDO's SWITCH2CE programme) and from ECCBC as the lead industrial partner; the International Finance Corporation (IFC) separately announced a loan to ECCBC in April 2023 to support resource-efficient production and plastic recycling investment across its North and West Africa footprint, including Morocco. Operationally, the pilot is structured as a value chain: waste pickers and household collectors (organized by Roots for Sustainability) gather PET at the household level; Right Execution Daily (RED) collects packaging at points of sale and transports it to centralized collection centres; recycling partner La Générale du Recyclage (LGR) processes collected PET; and ThirdWay Partners coordinates the pilot and develops the business case and financial models needed to make the recycling loop commercially self-sustaining beyond the pilot/donor-funded phase. The intended long-term model, if validated, is a self-financing local rPET supply chain that ECCBC (and potentially other Moroccan beverage producers) could draw on for food-grade recycled content in new bottles.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Material Recovery — household- and point-of-sale-level PET bottle collection and centralized sorting
- Reverse Logistics — organized collection network (waste pickers/collectors, POS collection, transport to collection centres)
- Industrial Reuse — bottle-to-bottle rPET recycling feeding recycled content back into new PET preforms/bottles
- Waste Reduction — reducing plastic waste and leakage from Morocco's packaging value chain
- Consumer/Community Engagement — "upskilling" of waste pickers and collectives (Roots for Sustainability)

## Products & Services

Not applicable in a conventional product sense — the pilot's "output" is a validated, scalable PET collection-and-recycling process and a financial/business model for bottle-to-bottle rPET use in food-grade packaging, plus expanded PET collection infrastructure in retail and residential areas of the Casablanca-Settat region.

## Customers

Not applicable in the conventional sense; beneficiaries/end-users include Moroccan waste pickers and collector communities engaged in the collection network, ECCBC as the industrial off-taker of recycled PET, and (indirectly) Moroccan consumers of Coca-Cola-system beverages sold in bottles with recycled content.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

This pilot is a material-recovery/recycling initiative (bottle-to-bottle rPET) rather than a reuse programme in the strict sense of extending a product or package's life through repeated reuse — it recycles PET into new bottles rather than reusing bottles directly. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's mission is therefore adjacent rather than core: it demonstrates infrastructure-building for closed-loop packaging value chains, formalizes informal waste-picker livelihoods, and could complement (not substitute for) genuine reuse/returnable systems, such as Coca-Cola's separate returnable-bottle programs in the region. Given its lack of published impact metrics and its focus on recycling rather than reuse, its direct relevance to a reuse-focused knowledge library is moderate.
