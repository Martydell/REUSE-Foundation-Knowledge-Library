## Organisation Overview

Le Vrac is a package-free grocery store ("épicerie sans emballage") located at 4 Rue de la Préfecture in Besançon, France. It sells organic-leaning bulk food items — grains, legumes, spices, nuts — alongside seasonally sourced fresh fruit and vegetables, packaged foods, beverages, and household/hygiene and cosmetics products, all designed to be purchased without single-use packaging. The store offers both in-person and online ordering with delivery options.

## Business Model

Le Vrac operates a single-location bulk retail store, selling the large majority of its catalogue package-free: customers weigh out dry goods (grains, legumes, spices) and other products into their own reusable containers or store-provided bags, avoiding pre-packaged formats. The product range spans fresh produce (weekly-selected organic fruit and vegetables), grocery staples, cleaning products and cosmetics sold loose, positioning the store as a full alternative to a conventional packaged supermarket rather than a specialty add-on shop. The business supplements in-store sales with an e-commerce/delivery channel, widening its reach beyond walk-in customers in central Besançon. As an independent single-site retailer, its business model is direct retail rather than franchise-based expansion, differentiating it from national or multi-city bulk-store chains.
