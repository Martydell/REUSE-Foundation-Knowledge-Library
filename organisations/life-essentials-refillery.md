## Organisation Overview

Life Essentials Refillery is an independent refill shop and organic bulk-goods store founded by Sheila Haque, an entrepreneur with an MBA and over three decades of business experience. It operates from a South Tampa storefront (4317 W El Prado Blvd) with a second location in Wesley Chapel, Florida, plus an online store. The business was inspired by Haque's observations of plastic waste in Tampa Bay and her personal search for safe skincare products for her daughter's severe eczema. It sells bulk herbs, teas, spices, personal care and home-care products refillable in customer-supplied or store containers, alongside local raw dairy and grass-fed meats.

## Business Model

Life Essentials Refillery runs a direct-to-consumer refill retail model across two Florida storefronts and an online shop. Customers bring their own jars or purchase reusable containers in-store, then refill them with bulk herbs, loose-leaf teas, whole and ground spices, personal-care items, and eco-friendly home-care goods, paying by weight or volume rather than for pre-packaged units. The store differentiates itself by pairing the refill model with a curated selection of local, farm-sourced products — including real/raw milk and grass-fed meats sourced from regional producers — positioning itself as a hub for both package-free shopping and local/organic food sourcing. Revenue is generated through in-store and online retail sales, and the business also hosts workshops, tastings and community events to build customer engagement and educate on sustainable shopping. There is no public evidence of franchising, external investment, or corporate partnerships; it operates as an independent small-business retailer.
