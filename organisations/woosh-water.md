## Organisation Overview

Woosh Water designs internet-connected, self-service "smart" water stations that purify (via ozone) and dispense drinking water, including a bottle-cleansing function for reusable bottles. Piloted on Tel Aviv streets from 2013, the concept expanded to US sites including a Dayton, Ohio college campus (2016) and Miami Beach (from around 2019).

## Business Model

Woosh installs computerised, remotely-monitored water refill kiosks in public spaces, campuses and cities, generating revenue through station sales/leasing to municipalities and institutions and/or per-use or subscription charges to end users. Stations are equipped with sensors reporting usage and estimated plastic-bottle/ecological savings back to operators. The company's core proposition is replacing single-use bottled water with free or low-cost refill infrastructure for reusable bottles.

Networked smart water refill/purification stations for public spaces, campuses and cities, with reusable-bottle cleansing and real-time water-quality monitoring.

## Measurable Impact

Early Tel Aviv pilot reported to have saved over 100,000 plastic bottles and reduced carbon footprint by more than 200,000 kg (source: Times of Israel/ISRAEL21c coverage, unverified against a primary company disclosure).
