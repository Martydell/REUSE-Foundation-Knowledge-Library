## Organisation Overview

Anew is a New Zealand-founded consumer brand producing reusable, plant-based water bottles as an alternative to single-use and conventional reusable packaging (plastic, aluminium, glass). Founded by Waiheke Island-based Jayden Klinac in 2015, the company positions its bottles as a "circular mindset" product: made from renewable plant-based material rather than petroleum-based plastic, mono-material (no separate plastic lining, unlike aluminium bottles/cans), and designed to be either household-recycled or returned to company collection points to be remade into new bottles in New Zealand.

## Business Model

Anew sells reusable, plant-based, mono-material bottles direct-to-consumer via its own e-commerce site (chooseanew.com and a US storefront, us.chooseanew.com) and through retail stockists in New Zealand, Australia, and the USA. The stated circular mechanism is twofold: bottles are designed to be reused long-term by the original owner ("rinse, reuse, repeat"), and at end-of-life can either go into standard household recycling or be returned to company collection points to be remanufactured into new bottles domestically in New Zealand. ## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Anew is a materials-innovation consumer brand rather than a reuse-infrastructure operator — its core contribution is a mono-material, plant-based bottle intended for long-term personal reuse plus an end-of-life remanufacturing loop, rather than a shared/returnable packaging system. It is a reasonable example of product-level design-for-reuse and material circularity, but the absence of any published impact data or named partnerships limits its usefulness as a benchmarked case study.
