## Organisation Overview
HAVU Cosmetics is a Finnish natural cosmetics brand founded by Lumi Maunuvaara, who developed the original concept — a natural lipstick in a simple wooden case — as a chemical engineering student at Aalto University, together with classmate and co-founder Tatu Fontell who encouraged bringing the product to market. The brand's name derives from the Finnish word for an evergreen sprig or needle. HAVU produces its lipsticks in its own lab in Espoo, using PLA (polylactide, a corn-starch-derived biodegradable polymer) mechanisms inside cases made of Finnish wood (aspen and alder), positioning the product as 100% biodegradable including packaging.

## Business Model
HAVU sells direct-to-consumer via its own e-commerce site and through retail/salon partners (e.g., Ruohonjuuri, Lyko, Glasshouse Helsinki), monetizing primarily through lipstick and refill sales. Its key circularity mechanism is a universal lipstick refill: the refill mechanism is sized to fit any standard 12.7mm lipstick case — including cases from other brands, not just HAVU's own — explicitly designed to extend the life of existing packaging (plastic or otherwise) rather than requiring customers to buy a new case each time. Refills are packaged in cardboard and paper only. The company has raised early-stage funding (reported at approximately EUR 110,000) to support production and international expansion, and has won recognition including a Nordic Natural Beauty Award for Best Lipstick and Best Package Design.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment
HAVU Cosmetics offers a genuinely distinctive reuse innovation: a refill system deliberately engineered to be cross-compatible with other brands' lipstick cases, which is a rare and notable design choice that maximizes packaging reuse regardless of original purchase source. Its scale is small (single-country cosmetics brand with an at-times-closed online shop) and impact data is unverified, but the product design itself is directly aligned with reuse-system principles. Priority rating: ★★★ Medium.
