## Organisation Overview

Minus Zero Waste Store is a female-founded, Johor Bahru-based online retailer selling zero-waste personal care, feminine hygiene, and kitchen products. Founded in September 2019 by Cassey Goh, the store built its model around eliminating single-use plastic packaging entirely, relying instead on upcycled and reused cardboard for shipping. It is recognised within Malaysia's zero-waste community and has been profiled as a case study by Zero Waste Malaysia.

## Business Model

Minus Zero Waste Store operates as a direct-to-consumer e-commerce business, selling low-waste and package-free alternatives to everyday personal-care and household items (e.g. shampoo bars, reusable feminine hygiene products, kitchen essentials). Its differentiator is logistics: since launch it has reportedly never purchased new packaging material, shipping all orders in upcycled/reused boxes rather than plastic mailers. For local (Johor Bahru) customers it offers deliveries in reusable boxes that customers return for reuse, and it launched a mobile refill station around its first anniversary (September 2020). The store incentivises waste reduction directly at checkout through "bring your own bottle/container" (BYOB) discounts (e.g. discounted laundry detergent when a customer supplies their own container), and it collects customers' waste paper for reuse as receipts/documentation. The business relies heavily on repeat custom and community engagement rather than mass-market advertising, positioning itself as an educational as well as commercial actor in Malaysia's zero-waste movement.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems (mobile refill station; BYOB discounts)
- Reverse Logistics (reusable delivery boxes returned by local customers)
- Waste Reduction (upcycled/reused shipping packaging; no new packaging materials purchased)
- Consumer Education (zero-waste community engagement via Zero Waste Malaysia case studies)
- Material Recovery (collects customer waste paper for reuse)

## Products & Services

Zero-waste personal care products, feminine hygiene products, kitchen essentials, and package-free household goods, sold online with delivery in reused/upcycled packaging; local mobile refill station in Johor Bahru.

## Customers

Individual consumers in Malaysia, primarily in and around Johor Bahru, with online orders shipped more widely; reported approximately 80% of orders come from returning customers.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Minus Zero Waste Store is a small but genuine grassroots example of packaging-free retail and reuse-based logistics in Southeast Asia. Its consistent policy of never purchasing new packaging, combined with a reusable-box return system and refill station, makes it a useful case study of low-cost, community-scale circular retail rather than a large-scale infrastructure player. Its impact is modest in absolute terms but demonstrates a replicable model for small e-commerce zero-waste retailers in emerging markets.
