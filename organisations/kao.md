## Organisation Overview

Kao Corporation is a major Japanese household and personal-care products manufacturer, founded in 1887, publicly listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange (4452), with 33,000+ employees globally. Kao released its first refill packs in 1991 and has since built one of the largest and longest-running refill programmes in the consumer goods industry, encompassing 423 distinct refill products as of December 2023 spanning shampoo, liquid detergent, and other categories, alongside its proprietary "Smart Holder" system for the Raku-raku Eco Pack Refill line.

## Business Model

Kao's refill business is fully integrated into its core household/personal-care product lines rather than a separate initiative: customers buy a durable bottle once, then purchase refill packs (flexible pouches, using substantially less packaging material per unit than a new rigid bottle) for repeat purchases. The proprietary "Smart Holder" container installs in about 10 seconds and uses a custom pump mechanism enabling complete, waste-free product transfer from the refill pack. Refill products now represent approximately 80% of Kao's relevant unit sales, with fabric softener refills specifically reaching a ~90% refill ratio — indicating refill has become the default purchasing pattern for Kao customers in Japan rather than a niche alternative.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Kao is one of the most significant refill-system operators globally by sheer scale and duration — a 30+ year track record (since 1991) that has made refill the majority default purchasing behaviour (~80% of unit sales) for a major mass-market manufacturer, not a niche premium option. This is a highly relevant benchmark case demonstrating that refill can be normalised at true mass-market scale when integrated deeply into core product design rather than offered as a side option.
