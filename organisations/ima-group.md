## Organisation Overview

IMA Group is a large Italian multinational, founded in 1961 and headquartered near Bologna, that designs and manufactures automatic processing and packaging machinery for pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, food, tobacco, tea and coffee. It employs roughly 6,000 people worldwide (over 2,800 outside Italy) and reported consolidated revenues above €1.6 billion in recent years. IMA is overwhelmingly a machinery manufacturer, not a reuse-system operator; its connection to reusable packaging is narrow and recent, stemming from a 2024 collaboration between its IMA E-Commerce division and a third-party reusable-packaging specialist, Ocean Package.

## Business Model

IMA's core business model is B2B capital-equipment sales: it designs, manufactures and sells automated packaging and processing lines to pharmaceutical, cosmetic, food and beverage manufacturers across dozens of subsidiary brands and product divisions. It does not itself operate a reusable-packaging pooling or refill system. The specific reuse-relevant angle identified in this research is a 2024 partnership in which IMA's E-Commerce division validated the "machinability" of reusable e-fulfillment cases — made from recycled ocean-bound plastic (per partner Ocean Package, at least 30% ocean-sourced recycled content, reusable up to ~20 times) — on IMA's E-CO FLEX packaging-line machines. In this arrangement, Ocean Package designs and supplies the reusable case product; IMA's role is to ensure its packaging machinery can process that reusable case on customers' automated lines, targeting e-fulfillment and intralogistics markets. IMA does not manufacture or own the reusable cases itself.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

IMA Group is fundamentally a packaging-machinery manufacturer, and the seed brief's caution about verifying the "reusable packaging" angle is well warranted: IMA does not operate a reuse or pooling system itself. Its only substantiated reuse connection is enabling its machines to process a partner's reusable, recycled-content case — a machine-compatibility validation, not a reuse program IMA owns or scales. This is a legitimate but narrow and indirect contribution to the reuse ecosystem (equipment-side enablement), not a reuse business model in its own right.

## Verification Notes

Seed data's caution was justified: IMA Group's primary identity is as a packaging-machinery OEM (founded 1961), not a reusable-packaging provider. The "B2B reusable packaging from recycled material" description in the seed most likely refers to IMA's 2024 partnership with Ocean Package, where IMA validates machine compatibility for a partner-made reusable case rather than producing reusable packaging itself.
