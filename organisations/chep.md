## Organisation Overview

CHEP is the world's leading pallet and container pooling company, operating as the core brand of Australian-listed Brambles Limited. Its origins trace to 1945, when the Australian Federal Government took ownership of a pool of pallets left behind by US forces after WWII, naming it the Commonwealth Handling Equipment Pool; Brambles acquired the business from the government in 1958. CHEP now manages a pool of roughly 348 million pallets, crates, and containers through a network of 750+ service centres across around 60 countries, employing approximately 11,000 people (out of Brambles' 12,000+ total).

## Business Model

CHEP's model is B2B pooling: rather than selling pallets and containers outright, CHEP retains ownership of reusable equipment and rents it to manufacturers and retailers on a share-and-reuse basis, collecting, inspecting, repairing, and redistributing units after each use cycle through its service-centre network. This "pool" model means the same physical assets circulate repeatedly among many different customers across a supply chain, reducing the need for single-use or company-owned packaging equipment. Revenue comes from rental/leasing fees paid by manufacturers, distributors, and retailers who use CHEP's pooled pallets and containers instead of purchasing and maintaining their own.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

CHEP is one of the most significant industrial-scale reuse operators in the world by physical volume — its pooling model is a textbook example of "reuse as a service" applied to supply-chain logistics, at a scale (hundreds of millions of units, tens of thousands of employees) far beyond typical consumer-facing reuse brands. It is highly relevant as a benchmark case for how reuse can be embedded structurally into B2B logistics rather than retrofitted onto single-use defaults.
