## Organisation Overview

Cupable is a Mumbai-based startup, founded in 2019, that designs and manufactures reusable cups and food containers from crop-waste materials such as rice husk and bamboo fibre, as an alternative to single-use plastic at restaurants, cafés, and events. Each unit is designed for up to roughly 250 uses. The company offers both outright purchase (including customised branded cups for corporate events) and a rental model where clients pay only washing charges, with Cupable handling delivery, collection, and sanitation logistics and tracking at each cycle stage.

## Business Model

Cupable operates a dual commercial model: event and corporate clients can either purchase branded reusable cups outright (as event souvenirs) or enter a rental contract, paying only for washing rather than the full product cost each cycle. Cupable itself manages the reverse-logistics loop — delivery of clean cups, collection of used ones, sanitation, and tracking at each stage — positioning it as a reuse-system operator rather than purely a product manufacturer. The crop-waste material base (rice husk, bamboo fibre) gives the product itself a lower-impact material profile compared to plastic alternatives, independent of the reuse cycle.
