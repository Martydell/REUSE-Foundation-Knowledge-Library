## Organisation Overview

Eco Panplas is a Brazilian industrial recycling company based in Hortolândia, São Paulo, specialising in the recycling of post-consumer plastic lubricant-oil containers — a packaging stream that is typically hard to recycle due to residual oil contamination. Using a patented, odourless, water-free degreasing and grinding process, the company converts contaminated HDPE containers into high-quality recycled resin suitable for new packaging manufacture. It is a Certified B Corporation and has received national and international recognition, including from WIPO, for its patented technology. Public sources do not confirm a founding date.

## Business Model

Eco Panplas operates a reverse-logistics and industrial-recycling business built around Brazil's mandatory reverse-logistics requirements for lubricant-oil packaging. The company collects used, oil-contaminated HDPE containers, processes them through a proprietary grinding and decontamination technology that uses no water and produces no waste, and sells the resulting recycled HDPE resin to packaging manufacturers — reportedly at up to 10% lower cost than virgin resin, supporting a genuine closed-loop market for this packaging category. Revenue also comes from recovery and resale of residual lubricant oil extracted from the containers, and from licensing the underlying decontamination technology to other operators in Brazil and internationally, with plans (per WIPO reporting) to scale to six processing units across multiple Brazilian states, targeting 24,000 metric tons of packaging recycled annually by 2026. No evidence was found during this research of a consumer-facing "Refillcycle" reusable-container programme; the company's model is business-to-business industrial material recovery, not reusable packaging for businesses or consumers.

Recycled HDPE resin (black, blow-moulding grade) derived from lubricant-oil containers; recovered/resold residual lubricant oil; licensing of proprietary container-decontamination and recycling technology.

## Customers

Packaging and plastics manufacturers purchasing recycled HDPE resin; lubricant-oil companies and distributors subject to Brazil's reverse-logistics packaging obligations; licensees of the company's recycling technology.

## Measurable Impact

Per company-reported figures (via WIPO Magazine): for every 500 tonnes of plastic recycled, the process recovers approximately 17,000 litres of residual oil and avoids approximately 800 tonnes of CO2 emissions (exact date of these figures not specified). The company's stated goal, per the same source, is to recycle 24,000 metric tons of lubricant-oil packaging annually by 2026 across an expanded six-site network. These figures come from a single secondary source (WIPO Magazine) rather than a dated primary company impact report, so should be treated as indicative rather than fully independently verified.

## Technology

Patented, odourless, water-free degreasing and grinding technology for decontaminating oil-soaked HDPE plastic packaging, enabling production of food/industrial-grade recycled resin without a water-based washing stage.
