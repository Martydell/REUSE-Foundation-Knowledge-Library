## Organisation Overview
Tingi Station is a zero-waste store in Baguio City, Philippines, offering pantry staples, household goods, loose-leaf teas, roasted nuts and pet products refillable by weight — allowing customers to buy exactly the quantity needed (the "tingi" tradition) using their own containers, cutting single-use sachet waste.

## Business Model
B2C bulk/refill retail: customers bring their own containers (BYOC) and pay per weight/volume for dry goods and household products, avoiding pre-packaged sachets that dominate Philippine retail; part of a wider "Kuha sa Tingi" movement (with Greenpeace Philippines) promoting refill hubs nationally.

## Measurable Impact
Early "Kuha sa Tingi" movement data cited up to ~2x cost reduction vs. sachet purchases (movement-wide figure, not Tingi Station-specific).
