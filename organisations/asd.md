## Organisation Overview

ASD (Ambalaj Sanayicileri Derneği), known in English as the Packaging Manufacturers' Association, is Turkey's principal packaging industry trade association, founded in 1992 by 39 founding members and now representing over 200 member companies. Based in Istanbul, ASD represents manufacturers across paper/cardboard, glass, metal, flexible packaging, plastic bottles and containers, labels, wooden packaging, and packaging machinery sub-sectors. As confirmed during verification, this is an industry association and non-profit membership body, not a commercial packaging company, consistent with the seed-data note flagging this distinction.

## Business Model

As a non-profit trade association, ASD does not sell products or generate commercial revenue from packaging sales; it is funded through member dues and represents its members' collective interests. Its stated core purposes are to foster growth of the Turkish packaging industry, give the sector a public identity, represent it nationally and internationally, and build scientific, technical, and social cooperation among members. Activities include organising industry events (the Eurasia Packaging Fair Istanbul, the "Crescents and Stars for Packaging" design competition, and student design competitions), publishing trade journals (Ambalaj Dünyası, Pack Converting), running a "Packaging Academy" training programme, and disseminating regulatory and industry analysis. ASD holds ISO 9001:2015 certification for its own operations and is a member of international bodies including the World Packaging Organisation (WPO), the African Packaging Federation (APF), IAPRI, and the World Design Organization (WDO).
