## Organisation Overview

DSF L&I is a South Korean company, established in 2017 and headquartered in Seoul, that develops and commercialises a reusable-container and cup return system using near-field communication (NFC) technology for restaurants and food-service businesses. The company expanded its reuse-infrastructure capacity by acquiring Happy Bridge in August 2021 — a firm specialising in washing and cleaning reusable cups and containers for clients including Starbucks — and completed a processing plant in Yongin, Gyeonggi Province, in a semiconductor-industry cluster, to scale these services. In December 2023, DSF L&I won the top ESG award in the small-manufacturing-enterprise category from the Korean ESG Association and National Assembly ESG Forum, recognising its role in systemising reusable-container adoption at the point of consumption.

## Business Model

DSF L&I provides reusable-container and cup return infrastructure as a service to restaurants, cafés and food-service operators: it supplies durable containers/cups embedded or paired with NFC technology enabling tracking and management of the return cycle, then operates (via its Happy Bridge acquisition) the industrial washing and cleaning operations needed to sanitise and recirculate containers back into the supply chain. This positions DSF L&I within South Korea's broader push toward reusable food-service packaging, which includes government-driven initiatives such as Seoul's disposable-cup deposit scheme and city-level reusable-container rollouts to cafes, festivals and public facilities. By combining NFC-based tracking with washing/logistics capacity built through the Happy Bridge acquisition and new Yongin facility, DSF L&I positions itself as an end-to-end reuse-infrastructure operator for the food-service sector rather than a single-technology vendor, aligning its commercial growth with South Korea's regulatory direction on single-use plastic reduction.
