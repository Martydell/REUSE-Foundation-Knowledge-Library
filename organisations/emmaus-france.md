## Organisation Overview

Emmaüs France is the French national federation of the Emmaus movement, founded by Abbé Pierre in 1949 when he began housing and working alongside destitute individuals ("compagnons") at Neuilly-Plaisance, funding the community by collecting, repairing and reselling discarded goods ("chiffonniers"). The umbrella association Emmaüs France was formally established in 1985 (loi de 1901) to federate France's Emmaus-affiliated groups. In 2023 the movement encompassed dozens of community and insertion structures nationwide, involving more than 38,000 people — companions, employees and volunteers — engaged in reuse-based social solidarity work.

## Business Model

Emmaüs France federates a network of member associations and "communautés" (communities) across France that collect donated used goods — furniture, clothing, books, household items, electronics — repair or sort them, and resell them cheaply through Emmaus shops (bric-a-brac/charity shops), generating revenue that funds community housing, meals and support for companions (many of whom are formerly homeless or socially excluded individuals) as well as broader poverty-relief and solidarity programs. Since 2008 many communities operate under France's "Organisme d'Accueil Communautaire et d'Activités Solidaires" legal status, combining not-for-profit community living with work-based social insertion. Beyond direct reuse-retail, Emmaüs France's network includes structures focused on international solidarity, housing advocacy (via the affiliated Fondation Abbé Pierre, created 1988) and social insertion enterprises. Revenue is overwhelmingly generated from resale of donated second-hand goods rather than external funding, reflecting the movement's founding "chiffonniers" self-financing model.

## Products & Services

Collection, sorting, light repair and resale of donated second-hand goods (furniture, clothing, books, household goods, electronics) through community-run shops and "bric-a-brac" sales; social insertion work programs; community housing for companions; affiliated international solidarity and housing-advocacy work.

## Customers

General public purchasing second-hand goods through Emmaus shops across France; companions and socially excluded individuals served through community housing/insertion programs; donors contributing used goods.

## Partnerships

Fondation Abbé Pierre — affiliated foundation (created 1988) focused on housing for the disadvantaged; Emmaüs International — global federation of which Emmaüs France's member associations are part.

## Measurable Impact

In 2023, more than 38,000 people were involved across Emmaüs France's network, including 6,903 companions (19.5% women, up 2 points since 2021), roughly 1,250 people under judicial supervision, over 11,730 employees (47% on insertion contracts), and approximately 16,500 volunteers (62% women) (Source: Emmaüs France 2023 Rapport d'Activité). The movement is commonly cited as collecting several hundred thousand tonnes of goods annually, though a precise, currently-sourced 2023/2024 tonnage figure was not confirmed within this research's scope.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Emmaus France is one of the largest, longest-established and best-documented reuse organisations in Europe, combining large-scale second-hand goods resale with a distinctive social-insertion and community-housing model dating to 1949. Its scale (38,000+ people involved in 2023), national reach, and strong public reporting (annual activity reports) make it an essential reference case for the REUSE Foundation, illustrating how reuse-based retail can be structured around social solidarity rather than pure commercial logic.
