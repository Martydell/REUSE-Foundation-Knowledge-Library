## Organisation Overview

Czyste Miasto Gdańsk ("Clean City Gdańsk," abbreviated CMG in seed data) is the City of Gdańsk's municipal waste-management programme, running public education initiatives under a "Less Waste" banner. Its core public-facing tools are a free mobile app teaching residents the "6R" principle (Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Repair, Rot, Recycle) and guidance on the city's 5-bin waste-sorting system, alongside broader conditions to support reduce/reuse/recycle practice across the city.

## Business Model

As a municipal programme rather than a company, CMG's "business model" is public-service delivery funded through the city government: it develops and maintains a free consumer-facing app that educates residents on waste reduction and the 6R framework (with Refuse and Reuse as explicit pillars — avoiding items like straws and excess packaging, and choosing reusable alternatives such as tap water over bottled water), supports correct use of Gdańsk's 5-bin sorting system, and runs public engagement activities such as the "Czyste Miasto Gdańsk Festival" promoting less-waste living. It is a public information/behaviour-change initiative rather than an operator of a specific reuse product or service.
