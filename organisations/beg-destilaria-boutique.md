# Beg Destilaria Boutique

A Brazilian boutique gin microdistillery near Campinas, São Paulo, notable for launching the first Brazilian gin refill bottle made entirely of aluminium as a lighter, more sustainable alternative to glass.

## Organisation Overview

BEG Destilaria Boutique — trading as "Beg Gin" — is a Brazilian gin producer founded in 2012 by four friends with a shared interest in spirits, who established a dedicated microdistillery in 2015 in the Joaquim Egídio district of Campinas, São Paulo, housed in a revitalised, historically listed former coffee-farm headquarters building dating to the mid-19th century. The brand name combines "B" for Brasil and "G" for gin. Beg produces artisanal, small-batch gins that have won over 60 medals in international spirits competitions, including recognition as 2nd-best gin in the world at the 2023 Bartenders Spirits Awards. Note: the seed data's name "BEG Distilaria Boutique" appears to be a minor misspelling of the verified official name, "BEG Destilaria Boutique."

## Business Model

Beg operates as a boutique/artisanal distiller producing gin in small batches at its own Campinas microdistillery, selling primarily through retail liquor distributors and its own online store, with an on-site visitor experience at its historic distillery building. Its distinguishing circular-economy feature is the "Garrafa Refil" (refill bottle) line: a 500ml gin refill sold in an all-aluminium bottle — roughly nine times lighter than an equivalent glass bottle — designed for consumers to decant into and refill their existing glass Beg bottle rather than buying a new glass bottle each time. The company markets aluminium as infinitely recyclable with high existing recycling rates in Brazil (citing a national aluminium beverage-can recycling rate of 97.4% in 2020) and states that a recycled aluminium bottle can return to the market in as little as 60 days. This is a refill-format product sold at retail (priced around R$59.99–R$69.90 for the 500ml refill, per retail listings) rather than a company-operated deposit-return or take-back loop — customers refill their own retained glass bottle rather than returning packaging to Beg.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems — "Garrafa Refil" aluminium 500ml refill bottles designed for consumers to top up their existing glass Beg bottle
- Reusable Packaging — lightweight aluminium refill format explicitly designed to reduce material use and carbon footprint versus repeat glass-bottle purchases
- Consumer Education — company communication around aluminium recyclability and the rationale for the refill format

## Products & Services

BEG Brazilian Boutique Dry Gin, BEG New World Navy Gin, BEG London Dry, and BEG Modern & Tropical gin variants, sold in standard 750ml glass bottles and as 500ml aluminium "Garrafa Refil" refill bottles; an on-site distillery visitor experience in Campinas.

## Customers

Individual consumers purchasing gin at retail (liquor stores, supermarkets and online retailers across Brazil) and via the company's own e-commerce store; visitors to the distillery's on-site experience.

## Partnerships

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Beg Destilaria Boutique is a credible, award-winning boutique spirits producer whose aluminium refill-bottle format is a genuine and relatively distinctive reuse-adjacent innovation in the spirits category — a category where reusable/refillable packaging is uncommon. However, the mechanism is a refill product sold at retail (consumer refills their own bottle) rather than a company-run deposit-return or take-back loop, and no Beg-specific quantified impact data (bottles refilled, glass avoided) was found. Its relevance to the library is real but narrow — a useful single-product example of refill innovation in an underexplored sector (spirits/beverages) rather than a broad reuse-system operator.
