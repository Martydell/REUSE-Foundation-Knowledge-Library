## Organisation Overview

Brabantia is a Dutch household-goods manufacturer founded in 1919 in Aalst, North Brabant, originally as Van Elderen & Co., a sheet-metal fabrication business making milk cans, jugs, sieves and funnels. The company has remained under continuous ownership of the van Elderen family across four generations, now employing roughly 1,000 people across four production units and selling in 85 countries. Brabantia is best known for durable, long-life household products — waste bins, laundry and ironing equipment, kitchenware — and has publicly committed to a goal of 100% circular design by 2035, becoming a Certified B Corporation in March 2023.

## Business Model

Brabantia designs, manufactures, and sells durable household products (waste/recycling bins, laundry and ironing equipment, kitchen tools, bathroom accessories) through a global wholesale and retail distribution network spanning 85 countries, operating four in-house production units. Rather than a reuse-service or sharing-platform model, Brabantia's circularity strategy is built around design-for-longevity: extended product warranties (2–10 years depending on product category) backed by a spare-parts availability guarantee of up to 20 years, intended to keep products in use and repairable rather than replaced. The company has structured its sustainability programme around six stated pillars — material health, renewable energy, clean wastewater, recycled production waste, recycled/biobased material content, and product recyclability — and has pursued third-party certification (Cradle to Cradle, B Corp) to validate progress against its 2035 circular-design target.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Repair (spare parts guaranteed available for up to 20 years per product)
- Waste Reduction (design-for-durability strategy, extended warranties of 2–10 years)
- Material Recovery (recycled and biobased material content in products, e.g., NewIcon Step On Trash Cans reported at 68% recycled material)
- Consumer Education (public sustainability/impact reporting on circular-design pillars)
- Industrial Reuse (recycling of production waste within manufacturing, reported at 93% in 2024)

No formal take-back, deposit-return, rental, or sharing-platform program was identified; Brabantia's circular strategy centres on product longevity and repairability rather than reuse-service infrastructure.

## Products & Services

Waste and recycling bins, laundry baskets and ironing boards/covers, kitchenware and food-storage products, bathroom accessories, and related household goods, sold through global retail and wholesale channels.

## Customers

Consumer households globally, sold via retail and wholesale partners across 85 countries.

## Partnerships

- WeForest — reforestation partner since 2014 (tree-planting programme in Ethiopia and Burkina Faso)
- Cradle to Cradle Products Innovation Institute — third-party product certification body
- B Lab — B Corp certifying body (certified March 2023)

## Measurable Impact

- Over 3 million trees planted since 2014 through the WeForest partnership (Brabantia sustainability reporting, as of 2024/2025).
- More than 700 products Cradle to Cradle certified at Bronze or Silver level, representing roughly 80% of self-manufactured products; more than 25% reaching Silver level as of 2023 (Brabantia Impact Report 2022/2023; company sustainability page).
- 2024 reported progress against Brabantia's six circular-design pillars: 99% material health, ~100% renewable energy (with noted gaps in Chinese facility gas infrastructure), 100% clean wastewater maintained, 93% production waste recycled, 43% recycled/biobased material content, 83% product recyclability (Brabantia sustainability page, 2024 data).
- Certified B Corporation since March 2023 (B Lab).
- NewIcon Step On Trash Cans cited as containing 68% recycled material and being 99.9% reusable/recyclable (company sustainability page).
- All figures above are company-published (Brabantia Impact Reports and sustainability webpage); B Corp and Cradle to Cradle certifications provide independent third-party verification of underlying standards, but the specific percentage figures themselves are self-reported.

Its published Impact Reports provide relatively detailed, dated metrics across a defined set of pillars, which is stronger sourcing than most organisations in this batch, even though the figures remain company-reported rather than independently audited.
