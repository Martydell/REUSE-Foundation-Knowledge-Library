## Organisation Overview
Re-Use Austria is a non-profit advocacy association founded in 2004 (originally as RepaNet, renamed in 2023) representing over 40 member organisations working in re-use, repair and resource conservation across Austria. It acts as a "lobby for re-use," connecting repair networks, repair cafes and social-enterprise second-hand businesses.

## Business Model
As a membership association, Re-Use Austria is funded by member contributions and public/institutional support, coordinating and advocating for social-enterprise re-use businesses (textiles, electronics, furniture, catering equipment) that create jobs for disadvantaged people and offer affordable goods. It also runs the online second-hand platform WIDADO (launched 2022) and supports repair cafe networks.

## Products & Services
Sector advocacy and coordination, the WIDADO online second-hand marketplace, and support for member repair cafes and re-use social enterprises handling textiles, electronics and furniture.

## Customers
Member re-use/repair organisations across Austria; indirectly, lower-income consumers buying affordable second-hand goods and disadvantaged workers employed by member enterprises.

## Partnerships
Network of 40+ member organisations; engages with Austria's Federal Ministry for Climate Action and Environment (BMLUK) on re-use policy.

## Measurable Impact
In 2021, member organisations at 244 locations handled over 29,000 tonnes of goods (60% textiles, 12% electrical appliances, 28% furniture/household items), saving an estimated 620,000 tonnes of CO2-equivalent.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment
A mature, sector-wide advocacy and coordination body with substantial verified impact data and strong social-enterprise integration, making it a key reference organisation for re-use policy and infrastructure in Austria/Europe.
