## Organisation Overview

JD Logistics is the logistics and supply-chain subsidiary of Chinese e-commerce giant JD.com. Originally built to serve JD.com's own delivery network, it became an independent, externally-facing logistics provider in 2017 and listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in 2021. As part of its "Green Stream Initiative" (launched 2017), JDL has developed one of the largest reusable-packaging programs in e-commerce logistics, replacing single-use cartons and tape with durable, trackable delivery boxes across its warehousing and last-mile network in China.

## Business Model

JDL operates as a for-profit, publicly listed logistics and supply-chain-as-a-service company, generating revenue from integrated supply chain services for JD.com and external enterprise clients (warehousing, fulfillment, freight, cross-border and cold-chain logistics). Its reusable packaging program is embedded within this core logistics business rather than sold as a standalone product: reusable totes/boxes replace corrugated cartons and tape for parcels moving through JD's own network and partner brands' e-commerce shipments. The company has stated a "DWOP" (durable, reusable packaging) goal to apply reusable packaging to over 80% of packages sold via e-commerce channels by 2030, in partnership with consumer brands such as P&G, Unilever and BRITA. Reusable totes are tracked via barcode/RFID through a proprietary circular packaging management system that monitors usage cycles, location and condition, enabling closed-loop return logistics between fulfillment centers, delivery stations and customers. Revenue is not separately broken out for the reuse program; it is a sustainability/cost initiative layered on JDL's core logistics revenue base.

## Products & Services

Reusable delivery totes/boxes (rated for 50-300+ reuse cycles), "slim tape" reduced-packaging technology, cold-chain reusable containers, and an integrated warehousing/fulfillment/delivery logistics platform for JD.com and third-party brands.

## Customers

JD.com's own e-commerce operations; external enterprise brand partners (e.g., P&G, Unilever, BRITA) using JDL's fulfillment network; third-party merchants on JD's marketplace.

## Partnerships

Procter & Gamble, Unilever, and BRITA are cited as partners in JDL's green/reusable packaging goal (per JD corporate communications).

## Measurable Impact

- JD Logistics reported deployment of over 500,000 reusable cold-chain delivery boxes (JD Logistics/packaging industry press).
- Upgraded reusable boxes rated for up to 300 reuse cycles, roughly double earlier models (JD corporate blog).
- Target: apply reusable packaging (DWOP) to over 80% of e-commerce packages by 2030 (JD corporate communications).

## Technology

Proprietary circular packaging management system using barcode and RFID tagging for real-time lifecycle tracking (usage count, location, condition) of reusable totes across the logistics network.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

JDL is a significant industrial-scale example of reusable packaging integrated into e-commerce reverse logistics, backed by real deployment numbers (500K+ boxes) and RFID-based tracking infrastructure — relevant as a model for large-scale B2B2C reuse logistics. However, it is a logistics subsidiary of a major public conglomerate rather than a reuse-focused enterprise, and detailed independent verification of impact claims is limited to company-published sources.
