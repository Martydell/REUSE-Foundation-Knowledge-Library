## Organisation Overview

Blueland is a US direct-to-consumer household-cleaning company co-founded in 2019 by CEO Sarah Paiji Yoo and John Mascari. Its core innovation is a refill system: customers buy a reusable bottle or container once (dispenser/tin/duraware), then reorder concentrated tablets, powders or paper-based refills that dissolve in water at home, eliminating repeated purchases of pre-filled single-use plastic bottles. The company reports $300 million in lifetime sales since launch and has expanded from an online-only DTC model into major US retail chains including Target, Costco, Kroger and HEB.

## Business Model

Blueland's model centres on a reusable "forever" container paired with a subscription/reorder cycle of low-mass refills (cleaning tablets, laundry tablets, dishwasher tablets and paper-based cartons), which are far lighter and less plastic-intensive to ship than pre-mixed liquid cleaning products in single-use bottles. The company sells directly via its own website and through expanding retail distribution (Target — nearly all US stores; Costco — all US locations; Kroger; HEB; Amazon; Whole Foods reported as a retail partner). Blueland holds Cradle to Cradle Certified®, B Corp ("Best for the Environment" recognition) and EWG Verified status, and in 2022 CEO Sarah Paiji Yoo publicly committed not to raise further venture funding — a position later revised, with the company reportedly closing a $55M minority-stake investment in June 2025. In 2025 the company also launched the "One Home Impact Fund," a related nonprofit initiative.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging (reusable bottles, dispensers, tins and duraware)
- Refill Systems (tablet, powder and paper-carton refills mixed with water at home)
- Consumer Education (impact reporting, sustainability messaging via annual impact report)
- Waste Reduction (product reformulation reducing carbon footprint, e.g. dishwasher tablets)

## Products & Services

Refillable cleaning-product starter kits and reusable containers across household cleaning categories: dish soap, hand soap, all-purpose cleaner, glass cleaner, bathroom cleaner, laundry detergent tablets and dishwasher detergent tablets; direct-to-consumer subscription refills and retail-shelf refill packs.

## Customers

## Measurable Impact

- Company reports diverting 1 billion+ single-use plastic bottles from landfills and oceans cumulatively since 2019 (Blueland 2025 Impact Report, blueland.com).
- 488 million+ square feet of plastic packaging saved (Blueland 2025 Impact Report).
- 7.5+ billion pounds of CO2 saved cumulatively — company states this is equivalent to taking 793,521 gasoline-powered cars off the road for a year (Blueland 2025 Impact Report).
- 2025 annual figures: 17,470,650 plastic containers prevented; 3,167,945 lbs of plastic waste avoided; 911,065,000 grams of CO2e emissions reduced (Blueland 2025 Impact Report).
- $300 million in lifetime sales reported (Fortune, June 2025).
- Revenue reportedly grew 80% in 2025 (aggregator-sourced; not independently confirmed via audited financials).
- All figures above are company-reported/self-published in Blueland's own impact report; no independently audited third-party verification was found in public sources.

## Technology used

Not a technology-driven reuse system in the RFID/IoT sense; core "technology" is product formulation (dissolvable tablets/powders, concentrated paper cartons) engineered to work with reusable dispensers rather than pre-filled liquid packaging.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Blueland is a well-established, financially substantial DTC refill brand with genuine national retail distribution (Target, Costco, Kroger) and third-party sustainability certifications (B Corp, Cradle to Cradle, EWG Verified), giving it stronger commercial validation than many smaller refill start-ups. Its impact figures, while significant in scale (1 billion+ bottles diverted, cumulative), are entirely self-reported through the company's own annual impact report rather than independently audited, which should temper confidence in the precise figures even though the underlying refill business model is clearly real and operating at scale. As a consumer-facing refill/reuse case study with strong retail traction, it is directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's documentation of scalable DTC reuse models.
