## Organisation Overview

Brush Naked is a small, privately held Canadian company based in Kelowna, British Columbia, that manufactures bamboo-handled toothbrushes for adults and families. The company markets itself as "Canadian-owned" and positions its products as an alternative to plastic toothbrushes, which it notes typically end up in landfill. Brush Naked offers bamboo toothbrushes with either biodegradable bristles or BPA-free nylon bristles, packaged in what the company describes as plastic-free packaging. Products are sold online direct-to-consumer with international shipping to several countries.

## Business Model

Brush Naked operates a direct-to-consumer e-commerce model, selling bamboo toothbrushes individually and in multi-packs through its own website (brushnaked.com, with a us.brushnaked.com storefront for US customers) rather than primarily through third-party retailers. The core product is a bamboo-handled toothbrush offered with a choice of biodegradable bristles or BPA-free nylon bristles, sold in natural, green, and pink handle colour options; a Canadian maple handle variant has been announced. The company states it is working toward increasing Canadian-made content in its supply chain, including local (BC) manufacturing and packaging. Products are Health Canada licensed as medical devices (MDEL 24984). The company's positioning is squarely on material substitution and biodegradability (bamboo/compostable materials replacing plastic) rather than a reuse, refill, or take-back model — the toothbrush itself is a single-use-replaceable consumable, with the sustainability claim resting on its end-of-life compostability rather than reuse or circularity in the traditional REUSE Foundation sense.
