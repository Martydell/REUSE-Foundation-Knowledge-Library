## Organisation Overview

Interzero is a German environmental-services group tracing its roots to Interseroh AG, founded in Cologne in 1991 by a consortium of waste-management companies. In 2022, following a split of the ALBA Group between brothers Eric and Axel Schweitzer, the plastics-recycling and related circular-economy businesses were carved out and rebranded as Interzero, under Axel Schweitzer's control. The group operates across roughly 61 subsidiaries in 10 European countries, employs about 2,000 people, and organizes its business into three main areas: circular/reusable-packaging solutions, plastics recycling, and Germany's "Dual System" packaging take-back scheme, with combined segment turnover exceeding €700 million in 2023.

## Business Model

Interzero generates revenue through fee-based environmental compliance and circular-economy services for producers and retailers, spanning three main lines: (1) "Dual System" services, where Interzero manages statutory extended-producer-responsibility packaging take-back and recycling compliance for German companies (a legacy Green Dot-style system dating to Interseroh's founding); (2) plastics recycling operations that process collected packaging waste into recycled material; and (3) circular/reusable-packaging solutions, where Interzero designs, implements and manages closed-loop reusable packaging systems for industrial and retail clients — including reusable transport packaging (developed since 2007) and newer reusable e-commerce shipping box initiatives developed with startup partners. Clients pay Interzero to design, supply, collect, clean and recirculate reusable packaging systems, similar in concept to pooling models but tailored/consultative rather than a single standardized crate product.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Interzero is a substantial, verifiable German circular-economy group with a genuine, longstanding reusable-packaging services division alongside its legacy recycling and compliance businesses. Its scale (2,000 employees, pan-European footprint, dedicated reusable-packaging offerings) and history developing reusable transport packaging since 2007 make it a credible reference organisation for B2B reusable/returnable packaging-as-a-service. It is worth noting reuse is one of three business lines rather than its sole focus, and it should not be confused with the similarly-scaled competitor PreZero (owned by Schwarz Group), which operates independently.
