## Organisation Overview
Launched by the Agricultural Credit Cooperatives of Turkey (Tarım Kredi) in July 2024, the first "Sıfır Market" (Zero Market) opened in Beytepe, Ankara, letting customers buy raw milk, legumes, dried fruits/nuts, liquid soap, detergent and sunflower oil using their own reusable containers.

## Business Model
Cooperative retail chain extension: an existing national agricultural-cooperative supermarket network (Tarım Kredi Koop Market) adds a zero-waste BYOC format alongside digital receipts and QR-code traceability, with plans to expand to 10 locations across major Turkish cities.

## Products & Services
BYOC bulk sales of raw milk, legumes, dried fruits/nuts, liquid soap, detergent and sunflower oil.

## Customers
Turkish consumers via the national Tarım Kredi cooperative retail network.

## Partnerships
Turkish Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry (QR traceability system).

## Measurable Impact
First location opened July 2024; expansion planned to 10 cities including Istanbul, İzmir, Bursa, Konya and Ankara.
