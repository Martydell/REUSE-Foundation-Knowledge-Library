## Organisation Overview

Het Goed is a long-running Dutch "kringloop" (circular) warehouse network that collects donated furniture, electronics, clothing, and household goods and resells them through around 30 stores across the Netherlands. Originating in 1982 in Emmeloord under the name Goedzooi, the network took the "Het Goed" name in 1992 when it expanded to Deventer. Individual locations are typically run by independent regional foundations rather than a single corporate owner, sharing branding, logistics, and sorting infrastructure. The organisation combines waste diversion with social employment for people distant from the labour market.

## Business Model

Het Goed operates a donation-and-resale model: consumers and businesses donate furniture, appliances, electronics, textiles, and household items either by dropping them at one of roughly 30 warehouse stores or via a free collection/pickup service (including trailer rental for self-transport). Donated goods are sorted, refurbished where needed, and resold at low prices through the retail stores, with textiles also processed through dedicated sorting centres. Revenue from resale funds store operations and, in many locations, subsidises social employment programmes that provide work experience and reintegration placements for people with distance from the labour market — a common feature of Dutch kringloopbedrijven. Because the network is a federation of separately incorporated regional foundations rather than a single legal entity, financial structure, ANBI (tax-exempt charitable) status, and precise non-profit/commercial classification vary somewhat by location; some sources describe the parent concept as run on more commercial lines than traditional charity-donation kringloops. The core model — free take-back of unwanted goods, sorting, resale, and reuse job creation — is consistent nationwide.

## Measurable Impact

One source (site-derived, undated but recent) reported network-wide figures of approximately 32.3 million kg of items collected annually, 10.7 million products reused, around 1,960 employees, and 3.1 million customers served. These figures could not be cross-verified against an official annual report during this research pass, so they should be treated as indicative rather than fully confirmed.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Het Goed is a long-standing, large-scale example of the Dutch "kringloop" model: a nationwide network of secondhand warehouses that extends product lifespans for furniture, electronics, and textiles while creating inclusive employment. It is a strong real-world reuse case study, though it operates as a federation of independently incorporated regional foundations rather than one company, which limits the availability of consolidated, verifiable financial and impact data. Its relevance to REUSE Foundation's knowledge base is high as a mature national reuse-retail network, though documentation of its exact legal structure and impact metrics needs further primary-source verification (e.g., individual foundation annual reports).
