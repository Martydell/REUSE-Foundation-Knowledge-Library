## Organisation Overview

Kleen Hub is a Copenhagen-based circular-economy technology company founded in 2019 by Simone Favorito, Robert Dichtl and Giuseppe Lanzafame after the founders were struck by the volume of single-use plastic generated by a Thai takeaway dinner. The company builds reusable-packaging infrastructure for the food-service sector, evolving from an early app-based cup/container borrowing system into "TAP&REUSE™," a contactless bank-card-tap system using RFID/NFC-embedded stainless-steel cups and containers. The system lets customers borrow and return reusable packaging without downloading an app or paying an upfront deposit outside of a card tap, aiming to remove the main behavioural barriers to reuse adoption in cafés, campuses, stadiums and airports.

## Business Model

Kleen Hub supplies reusable stainless-steel cups and containers embedded with RFID chips/QR codes, plus card-tap terminals (Android tablets), to venues such as cafés, offices, campuses, stadiums and airports. Under the TAP&REUSE™ model, a customer taps their bank card to receive a reusable cup; a small deposit-style charge (reported around $3.55) is authorised, and is not charged (or refunded) if the item is returned within a set window (reported as up to 7–10 days), with partial refunds for late returns to still encourage circulation. This removes the need for a dedicated app or cash deposit, addressing what the company calls the "barista's barrier" — reliance on staff to explain and encourage reuse. Revenue is generated by charging partner venues for the reusable packaging/hardware system and transaction processing, positioning Kleen Hub as a B2B circular-infrastructure provider (Packaging-as-a-Service) rather than a consumer-facing retailer. The company has piloted in Copenhagen, including a collaboration with Danish coffee chain Coffee Collective, and has described plans for international pilots and an investment round to fund expansion.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging — stainless-steel reusable cups and containers replacing single-use cups/takeaway packaging
- Digital Tracking — RFID/NFC chip and QR-code-embedded packaging tracked via card-tap terminals
- Packaging-as-a-Service — B2B system supplying reusable packaging and return infrastructure to café/venue partners
- Deposit Return — card-based deposit-and-refund mechanism tied to timely return of reusable items
- Consumer Education — in-venue staff-assisted promotion of reuse over single-use alternatives

## Products & Services

TAP&REUSE™ system: RFID/NFC-embedded stainless-steel reusable cups and food containers, card-tap terminals/tablets for venues, and the underlying deposit/tracking software; earlier-generation system used a mobile app for interest-free multi-day borrowing of reusable steel boxes and mugs.

## Customers

Cafés and coffee chains (e.g., Coffee Collective in Copenhagen), and stated target sectors including campuses, corporate offices, stadiums and airports; deployments reported as hyperlocal (Copenhagen) with pilot activity referenced internationally (including Brazil coworking spaces per company sources).

## Measurable Impact

Company-reported figures (not independently audited): each reusable cup is estimated to save approximately 58.7 kg CO2 over its lifetime; operating cost cited at approximately $0.16 per cup versus $0.24 for a comparable sustainable paper cup; company states "at least 80%" of customers opt for reuse when baristas explain the system (Forbes, October 2023). No independently verified, dated third-party impact audit was found.

## Technology

RFID- and NFC-chip-embedded reusable packaging; QR-code scanning for return-window tracking and push notifications; Android tablet-based card-tap payment/deposit terminals at point of service (TAP&REUSE™ system).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Kleen Hub represents a technically sophisticated, purpose-built reuse infrastructure company directly aligned with the REUSE Foundation's mission — using contactless payment technology to remove behavioural friction from reusable packaging adoption, a well-documented barrier in the sector. Its evolution from app-based to card-tap systems shows iterative, evidence-based product development. However, deployment remains hyperlocal (primarily Copenhagen) as of the most recent public reporting (2023), impact figures are company-reported rather than independently audited, and funding/scaling status beyond a sought 2024 investment round is unconfirmed. Strong conceptual and technological fit; scale is still limited.
