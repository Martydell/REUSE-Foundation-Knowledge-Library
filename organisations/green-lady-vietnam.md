## Organisation Overview

Green Lady Vietnam is a social enterprise established in 2017 by Bui Thi Minh Ngoc to produce reusable cloth menstrual pads designed specifically for Vietnamese women, addressing both plastic waste from disposable sanitary products and access to affordable, comfortable menstrual care. Ngoc developed the product after struggling to source suitable fabric — naturally made, absorbent, washable, and quick-drying — and built the venture around both product sales and educational courses on ecological living and reproductive health. The products have reportedly reached markets in Cambodia, the Philippines, Thailand, and export markets including the United States, Europe, India, Japan, and South Korea.

## Business Model

Green Lady Vietnam designs, manufactures, and sells reusable cloth menstrual pads made from naturally sourced, absorbent, washable, and quick-drying fabric, targeting the large disposable-sanitary-product replacement market in Vietnam and neighbouring Southeast Asian countries. Founder Bui Thi Minh Ngoc built the business around a dual value proposition: environmental (a standard disposable sanitary towel reportedly takes as long to decompose as four plastic bags, and an average woman may use up to 10,000 disposable pads across her lifetime) and social (a stated ambition to create jobs for disadvantaged women through pad production). Beyond direct product sales — via the company's Facebook page and Vietnamese retail site xanhsuot.vn — the enterprise also runs educational courses on ecological/zero-waste living and reproductive health awareness, positioning itself as an advocacy and education business alongside a product manufacturer. Distribution has expanded regionally into Cambodia, the Philippines, and Thailand, with export reach reported into the US, Europe, India, Japan, and South Korea; planned expansion into Myanmar and Indonesia was also reported.
