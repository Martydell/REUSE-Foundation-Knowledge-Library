## Organisation Overview

LOLA is an unpackaged, 100% vegan grocery store network in Bern, Switzerland, founded in 2017 as the first store in Bern to offer over 100 items package-free. It operates two neighbourhood locations — Lorraineladen and Mattenhof — plus an online shop and its own beverage line. Customers bring bags, tins and bottles to fill with grains, rice, nuts, detergent, cosmetics, and other bulk goods. The Lorraine store also hosts a community fridge for sharing surplus food, reflecting a community-embedded approach to package-free and waste-reducing retail rather than a purely commercial one.

## Business Model

LOLA operates a bring-your-own-container bulk retail model across two Bern neighbourhood stores, selling vegan and largely organic dry goods (grains, rice, legumes, pasta), cosmetics, cleaning and detergent products by weight, alongside fresh items like bread and croissants. It supplements in-store sales with an online shop (LOLA vegan) and its own branded beverage line, extending its retail footprint and revenue streams beyond the two physical locations. A distinguishing community feature is a shared community refrigerator at its Lorraine store, allowing surplus food redistribution among neighbours — positioning LOLA as embedded in local food-waste-reduction and mutual-aid practices rather than operating purely as a standard retail chain. There is no public evidence of external investment, franchising, or corporate partnerships; it functions as an independent, locally rooted small retail operation.
