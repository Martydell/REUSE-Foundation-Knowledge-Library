## Organisation Overview

Bower Collective is a UK direct-to-consumer company, founded by Nick Torday and Marcus Hill, that supplies natural household and personal-care products (laundry, home cleaning, body and personal care) in reusable, traceable "BowerPack" packaging delivered directly to customers' homes. The name references the bowerbird, known for repurposing colourful debris to decorate its nest. Marcus Hill previously founded and sold London Bio Packaging (a biodegradable food-packaging supplier) to Bunzl, while Nick Torday's background is in digital/technology consultancy for organisations including the UN, WWF and Amnesty International. The company is a Certified B Corp and reports having prevented millions of single-use plastic bottles from reaching landfill through its refill model.

## Business Model

Bower Collective operates a refill-at-home subscription and one-off purchase model: customers buy an initial product in a reusable outer container (e.g. an aluminium or durable bottle), then reorder concentrated refills shipped in lightweight BowerPack pouches, which are lower in plastic mass and shipping volume than repeat purchases of full pre-filled bottles. Over 90% of products are reported as manufactured in the UK, and formulations are marketed as free from parabens, bleach and synthetic fragrances. The company raised a £2.1 million seed round in 2021 led by Oxford Capital and Doehler Ventures, and reported rapid early growth (month-on-month revenue growth of 44% and an annual recurring revenue of £550k around the time of that raise). It publishes an annual impact report covering plastic waste, harmful ingredients and carbon emissions, and distributes both direct-to-consumer and through partners such as the Essential Trading Cooperative.

## Products & Services

Refillable household cleaning products (laundry detergent, home cleaning), body and personal-care products (soap, shampoo, etc.), delivered in reusable outer packaging (BowerPack) with concentrated refill reorders; direct-to-consumer subscription and one-off ordering via bowercollective.com.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Bower Collective is a genuine, moderately scaled UK refill-at-home operator with credible third-party validation (B Corp certification, named institutional investors, wholesale distribution partner) and a clear, well-documented reusable-packaging model (BowerPack). Its impact claims, while plausible and consistent with its stated growth trajectory, remain self-reported rather than independently audited, and precise company size/employee data is only loosely corroborated (estimates range from roughly 10 to 21 employees across different years of third-party aggregator data). It is a solid, if not exceptional-scale, example of DTC reuse-packaging innovation relevant to the REUSE Foundation's UK consumer-goods coverage.

## Verification Notes

Seed data listed "Year Established: 2018" — this is only partially correct. Companies House confirms the legal entity was incorporated 4 December 2018, but under the name "Monomer Ventures Ltd," not "Bower Collective Ltd" — the name change to Bower Collective Ltd occurred in August 2019. Independent press coverage additionally states the company was "founded in summer 2019" by Torday and Hill and did not commercially launch to customers until January 2020. This is a **correction/clarification** to the seed data: 2018 reflects the shell company's incorporation date, not the founding of Bower Collective as a business concept or its market launch — the profile above presents all three dates with sourcing rather than picking one. Seed data's employee count (11–50) is broadly consistent with a 2025 PitchBook-sourced figure of 21 employees, though older data (January 2022) showed as few as 10 — treat precise current headcount as approximate. Seed data's partner list ("Camden Town Brewery, Diageo, Tom Parker Creamery") was not found associated with Bower Collective in any verified source — those names in fact belong to the separate organisation Again (letsuseagain.com/Acet Circular Solutions Ltd), and appear to have been mixed up in the original seed tracker. This has been corrected here: Bower Collective's own verified partners are the seed investors (Oxford Capital, Doehler Ventures) and its wholesale distributor (Essential Trading Cooperative).
