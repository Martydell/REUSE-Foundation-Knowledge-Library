## Organisation Overview

Hygiene of Sweden, founded in 2012 by Philip Wilhelmsson and based in Bromma, Sweden, develops non-alcoholic disinfection and hygiene products for professional and everyday use. Its formulations are marketed as gentler on skin and materials than alcohol-based alternatives while remaining effective against viruses including coronaviruses and norovirus. The company supplies both branded products and private-label hygiene solutions, and participated in the IFMA Innovation award programme with its "BioPolymer+" alcohol-free hand hygiene product.

## Business Model

Hygiene of Sweden manufactures hygiene/disinfection formulations and the refillable dispenser hardware needed to deliver them, selling to workplaces, facilities-management companies and via private-label arrangements with other brands. Its automatic touch-free foam dispensers use a 1-litre refillable canister rated for roughly 1,000 doses, with battery-powered units (8 AA batteries) rated for up to 30,000 doses before replacement — a refill-cartridge model that reduces packaging waste compared to single-use bottles. Revenue is generated through direct B2B/B2C product sales and private-label manufacturing for other hygiene brands (e.g., referenced in Hemfrid's own-branded cleaning product line).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Hygiene of Sweden represents a straightforward but credible refill-system model in the commercial hygiene/disinfection sector: refillable dispenser hardware paired with concentrated formulations reduces single-use bottle waste in workplace settings. Its B2B/private-label orientation and lack of published impact data limit its visibility, but the underlying refill-dispenser mechanic is a relevant, replicable model for institutional reuse.
