## Organisation Overview

Carccu is a Finnish flexographic printing house and paper packaging manufacturer based in Sastamala, operating as EPP-Pack Oy. It is a family business, now in its fourth generation of ownership, with Lasse Borg as CEO and founder Olli Borg still involved. The company manufactures packaging papers, takeaway food containers, meat/fish/cheese wrapping papers, flower and gift wrapping papers, and paper bags, using flexographic printing with water-based inks. Its sustainability positioning centres on fossil-free manufacturing electricity, a high (99.9%) production-waste recycling rate, and certifications including ISO 9001, ISO 14001, PEFC, and FSC. Named clients include Kirami (hot tub manufacturer) and WollReich Haslital (Swiss agricultural business).

## Business Model

Carccu operates as a B2B contract manufacturer and printer of paper-based packaging, supplying custom-printed and stock packaging papers, takeaway/fast-food containers, and specialty wrapping papers (meat, fish, cheese, flowers, gifts) to food service and retail businesses, primarily in Finland and Europe. Its environmental positioning is built around production-side sustainability (fossil-free electricity, high recycling rate of manufacturing waste, water-based inks, forestry certifications) and the inherent recyclability/compostability of paper as a material, rather than around product reuse. Notably, the company's own website states that "according to product lifecycle studies, paper-based disposable packaging also has a lesser environmental impact than the kinds of reusable containers used in fast food restaurants" — explicitly positioning its single-use paper products as environmentally preferable to reusable alternatives, rather than offering or promoting a reuse model itself.
