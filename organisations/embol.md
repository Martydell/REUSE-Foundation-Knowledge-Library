## Organisation Overview

Embol (Embotelladoras Bolivianas Unidas S.A.) is Bolivia's exclusive Coca-Cola bottler, formed in 1995 through a merger of previously separate Bolivian bottling operations with the involvement of Chilean bottling entrepreneur Hernán Vicuña Reyes. It operates four bottling plants (La Paz, Cochabamba, Santa Cruz, Oruro) and states its distribution network covers roughly 75% of Bolivian territory, reaching about 95% of the national population. A core part of its operations is a returnable-bottle system for both glass and plastic containers, promoted heavily as a lower-cost, lower-carbon-footprint option for Bolivian consumers.

## Business Model

Embol manufactures, bottles and distributes Coca-Cola Company beverage brands across Bolivia under an exclusive bottling license, generating revenue through wholesale and retail beverage sales via its own distribution network reaching the large majority of the country's population. A significant share of its volume is sold in returnable/refillable containers — the company states glass bottles can be reused up to roughly 20 times and PET bottles up to roughly 12 times in Bolivia before being retired — with returned empties collected through retail points and reintroduced into the bottling cycle after washing/inspection, an established affordability strategy that also reduces per-unit packaging costs and environmental footprint versus one-way packaging. The company has publicly emphasised returnable-container promotion (including in 2021 messaging) as both a cost-of-living and sustainability strategy, positioned by Coca-Cola Bolivia's own communications as part of its circular-economy commitments.

## Measurable Impact

Company/Coca-Cola Bolivia communications state that in Bolivia a plastic (PET) returnable bottle can be reused up to approximately 12 times and a glass returnable bottle up to approximately 20 times before recycling (Source: coca-cola.com/bo, "Retornables y recuperación de envases," undated, current as of 2026 verification). The seed's claim of a "90%+ return rate" could NOT be independently verified in any public source found during this research; it should be treated as unverified.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Embol is a significant, large-scale example of a returnable-bottle system operating at national scale in a Latin American market, with genuine reuse-cycle figures (up to ~20 reuses for glass, ~12 for PET) disclosed via Coca-Cola's own regional sustainability communications. However, as a franchise bottler for a multinational beverage brand, its returnable-packaging program is primarily a cost/margin strategy inherited from long-standing regional bottling practice rather than a purpose-built circular-economy initiative, and the specific high-return-rate claim in the seed data could not be substantiated. Still a relevant large-scale deposit-return case study given its national reach.
