## Organisation Overview

Glassia is a Vietnamese social enterprise that began operations in Da Nang in March 2021, incubated by Evergreen Labs. It supplies purified drinking water in 100% reusable, refillable glass bottles, replacing single-use PET water bottles for hospitality and office customers. Its model closes the loop through a "re-collection – sterilization – refill" cycle: Glassia delivers full bottles, collects empties from the same customers on the next delivery run, and returns them to its decentralized bottling facility for washing, sterilizing and refilling — an ISO- and HACCP-certified process. The company positions itself within Vietnam's broader circular-economy movement and has been featured as a national circular-economy showcase case.

## Business Model

Glassia operates a B2B refill-and-return service: customers (primarily hotels, resorts, restaurants, banquet venues and offices) subscribe to receive purified water in reusable glass bottles on a rotating delivery schedule, with empty bottles collected at the point of each new delivery and brought back to Glassia's local bottling facility for cleaning, sterilization and refilling before redistribution. Each facility is designed as a decentralized, localized production unit, with reported capacity to fill up to 5 million bottles annually and an estimated carbon offset of 1,044 tons of CO2 per facility per year compared to single-use alternatives. An Ida C. & Morris Falk Foundation case study describes Glassia pursuing a "social franchising" growth strategy, aiming to replicate its decentralized facility model in new locations rather than centralizing production, keeping logistics costs and transport emissions low. Its reported average of 15 reuses per bottle to date, and independent lifecycle-assessment findings that its service has the lowest environmental impact in five of six categories versus single-use packaged water, underpin its sustainability positioning to prospective hospitality clients.

- Reverse Logistics (empty-bottle collection integrated into delivery routes)
- Take-back (customer return of empty bottles at each delivery)

Purified drinking water supplied in reusable, refillable glass bottles; scheduled delivery and empty-bottle collection service; ISO- and HACCP-certified bottling/sterilization facilities; social-franchising model for facility replication.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Glassia is a strong, well-documented example of a genuine reuse-economy business: a closed-loop reusable glass-bottle water service with credible B2B hospitality partnerships (multiple named international hotel brands), an independent lifecycle-assessment claim of favourable environmental performance, and a scalable "social franchising" growth model designed specifically to replicate reuse infrastructure in new markets. It directly matches several of the REUSE Foundation's core categories (reusable packaging, refill systems, reverse logistics, take-back) and should be considered a priority case study for the knowledge library.
