## Organisation Overview

Loopedin is a Singapore-based "eco-technology" packaging company that enables food & beverage retailers and corporate clients to replace single-use plastic takeaway containers with a fully reusable, food-grade, recyclable inventory system. Each container carries an RFID chip and QR code, allowing customers to scan, locate the nearest return station via the Loopedin app, and drop off used containers for collection, sorting, sanitising and redistribution. At end-of-life, containers are recycled back into food-grade polymers. The company positions itself as both a sustainability solution and a business-intelligence tool, giving F&B and corporate clients data on container usage and customer engagement.

## Business Model

Loopedin operates a B2B "reusable-packaging-as-a-service" model, supplying F&B retailers and corporates with reusable, RFID/QR-tagged food containers as an alternative to single-use plastic. Consumers who receive food or drinks in a Loopedin container use the Loopedin mobile app to locate the nearest Return Station and drop the container off after use; Loopedin then collects, sorts, quality-checks, sanitises, and redistributes containers back into circulation for reuse. When a container reaches the end of its usable life, it is collected and recycled into food-grade polymers rather than discarded, closing the material loop. The RFID/QR tagging gives Loopedin's business clients visibility into container circulation, return rates, and customer interaction data — an added value proposition beyond simple environmental compliance. Loopedin markets its offering as part of Singapore's broader push (e.g., referenced by The Matcha Initiative) to reduce single-use packaging in food service, indicating a business model built around servicing corporate and F&B clients seeking to meet sustainability and single-use-plastic-reduction goals.
