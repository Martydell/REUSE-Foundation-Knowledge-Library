## Organisation Overview

Bluewater is a Stockholm-founded water-purification technology company established in 2013 by Swedish entrepreneur Bengt Rittri, who previously founded and sold air-purification company Blueair to Unilever. Bluewater develops patented reverse-osmosis purification technology and sells hydration stations and purifiers for home, hospitality, workplace, wellness, and emergency-response use, aimed at reducing reliance on single-use bottled water. In April 2022 Bluewater merged with Denver-based FloWater to form a combined global group, with Rittri as CEO of the merged entity while retaining separate brand identities and market focuses.

## Business Model

Bluewater sells purification hardware — under-sink and countertop home purifiers, and larger-format "hydration stations" (e.g., Flowstation, Café/Restaurant/Hotel Station product lines) — that filter tap water to eliminate the need for bottled water, targeting B2B customers (workplaces, hospitality venues, schools, event organisers) as well as home consumers. Revenue comes from equipment sales/leasing and likely service contracts (filter replacement, maintenance), positioning Bluewater as an infrastructure provider that displaces single-use plastic bottle purchasing with refillable-bottle-and-tap-water use at scale. Its 2022 merger with FloWater combined Bluewater's R&D/global commercial base with FloWater's U.S. customer base and events/venues expertise, and the group has also engaged in emergency-response deployments (e.g., military/disaster relief water purification) as a secondary line of activity.

## Products & Services

Home water purifiers (e.g., Kitchen Station 1, Spirit/Cleone/Pro Purifiers); B2B hydration/dispensing stations for hospitality, workplace and public settings (Café/Restaurant/Hotel Station, Flowstation); emergency-response water purification stations for disaster relief, field hospitals and military use.

## Customers

B2B and institutional: schools, healthcare facilities, hospitality venues, offices, wellness centers, event organisers, military and emergency-response organisations; B2C: home purifier customers. Geographic reach per company materials spans Europe, the Middle East, China, Southeast Asia, the United States and South Africa.

## Partnerships

FloWater (Denver, Colorado, US) — merged with Bluewater in April 2022 to form a combined group, per Bluewater's own press materials and independent trade press (WaterWorld). Company materials also reference emergency-response/humanitarian deployments (e.g., support to Ukraine) and school/community hydration-station projects, though specific named institutional partners beyond FloWater were not independently verified in this research.

## Measurable Impact

Per Bluewater's own website (accessed 2026): company states "1 Billion Bottles Saved" through its purification systems and cites 15 million plastic bottles collected/removed via specific initiatives. These figures are company-reported without independent third-party audit found in this research; treat as unverified marketing claims pending external corroboration.

## Technology

Patented reverse-osmosis-based water purification technology, described by the company as removing pollutants including toxic metals, chemicals, microplastics and limescale from tap water; deployed in both small (home) and large-format (public/institutional) hydration station hardware.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Bluewater is a substantive, well-established (est. 2013, since merged with a US counterpart in 2022) water-purification company whose core business model — on-site filtration replacing bottled-water purchasing — is a clear and scalable refill/reuse mechanism with real B2B and institutional traction (schools, hospitality, emergency response). The main limitation for the knowledge library is that its headline impact figures ("1 billion bottles saved") are self-reported without visible independent audit. Its combined global scale post-FloWater merger and diversified public/institutional deployment (including humanitarian use) make it a relevant refill-systems case study.
