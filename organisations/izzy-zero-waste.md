## Organisation Overview

Izzy Zero Waste Beauty is a New York City-based direct-to-consumer cosmetics brand co-founded by industry veteran Shannon Goldberg (former leadership roles at Peter Thomas Roth Clinical Skin Care and Madonna's MDNA Skin) and engineer/inventor Chris Tarling. Marketed as "the first fully reusable, sustainable, direct-to-consumer beauty brand," its flagship product is a prestige mascara described as 100% reusable and 100% recyclable, addressing the cosmetics industry's typically disposable packaging model. The brand name derives from Tarling's young daughter, Izzy.

## Business Model

Izzy sells reusable cosmetics products — beginning with a mascara designed for long-term reuse via replaceable/refillable components rather than full product disposal after each use — directly to consumers online. The company positions itself explicitly against the cosmetics industry's disposable-packaging norm, aiming to demonstrate that "consumable does not have to mean disposable." Specific mechanics of the reuse/refill system (e.g. replaceable brush heads, refillable formula cartridges) were not independently confirmed in sources accessed during this research pass, beyond the "100% reusable, 100% recyclable" positioning.
