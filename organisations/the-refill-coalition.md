## Organisation Overview

A cross-sector UK industry coalition, run by reuse consultancy GoUnpackaged, developing an open-source industry standard for reusable bulk vessels. Partnered with Aldi, Ocado and logistics firm CHEP, and funded by Innovate UK, it launched its first real-world in-store/online trial in October 2023.

## Business Model

Consultancy-led, grant-funded (Innovate UK) collaborative R&D coalition rather than a direct-to-consumer business; develops and licenses an open-source reusable bulk vessel standard (14-litre HDPE container manufactured by Berry Global) for retail partners to adopt across in-store refill, online delivery, and future tareless-weighing use cases, aiming to reduce single-use plastic packaging industry-wide.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging (reusable bulk vessels, standardised design)
- Refill Systems (in-store and online refill delivery models)
- Reverse Logistics (vessel return via retailer/logistics partner)
- Industrial Reuse

## Products & Services

An open-source standardised reusable bulk vessel (14L HDPE container) for in-store refill stations and pre-filled online-delivery consumer vessels; supply-chain reuse/logistics model developed with CHEP.

## Customers

Retail partners (Aldi, Ocado) and their end consumers in the UK.

## Partnerships

Aldi, Ocado, CHEP, Berry Global (vessel manufacturer), Innovate UK (funder), led by GoUnpackaged.

## Measurable Impact

Each in-store reusable vessel reportedly replaces 24 single-use plastic packs; each online consumer-sized vessel replaces 5 single-use packs (per project reporting).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

A well-documented, multi-retailer industry initiative with credible funding (Innovate UK) and major partners (Aldi, Ocado, CHEP), directly targeting standardisation of reusable packaging at scale — high relevance to REUSE Foundation's infrastructure/systems focus, though it is a project/initiative rather than an independent company.
