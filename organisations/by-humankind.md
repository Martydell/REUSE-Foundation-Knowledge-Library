## Organisation Overview

by Humankind is a US direct-to-consumer personal care company, headquartered in New York, founded by Brian Bushell and Joshua Goodman. The company sells natural-ingredient deodorant, shampoo bars, and mouthwash tablets built around a reusable-container, subscription-refill model designed to reduce single-use plastic packaging. It raised a $4 million seed round led by Lerer Hippeau (announced February 2019), with participation from Red Sea Ventures, BoxGroup, SV Angel, Great Oaks, and individual investors including SoulCycle co-founder Elizabeth Cutler. As of 2026, the company continues to actively sell its refillable deodorant and other products via its website.

## Business Model

by Humankind operates a direct-to-consumer, subscription-based refill model: customers purchase (or receive free with subscription) a reusable container — most prominently for deodorant ("Kindfill") — and then receive recurring refills that snap or slot into the existing container rather than requiring a new full product package each cycle. The company also sells shampoo bars and mouthwash tablets, which reduce packaging through concentrated/solid formats rather than liquid refills. Marketing materials state the refill system can reduce single-use plastic waste substantially compared to conventional personal care packaging (one secondary source citing up to five pounds of plastic saved per consumer per year, and per-refill plastic-avoidance figures for deodorant); these are company/press claims and are noted as such rather than independently audited figures. The company also states it donates to ocean-plastic-removal initiatives on a customer's first purchase. Products are sold via the company's own e-commerce site with one-time purchase and subscription options.
