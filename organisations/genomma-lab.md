## Organisation Overview

Genomma Lab Internacional, S.A.B. de C.V. is a publicly listed Mexican pharmaceutical, personal-care and consumer-health company incorporated in 1996, headquartered in Mexico City and trading on the Bolsa Mexicana de Valores under ticker LABB. It develops and markets a broad portfolio of over-the-counter health, dermatological, hair-care and personal-care brands (including Tío Nacho) across Mexico and other Latin American markets. Since publishing its "Sustainability Strategy 2025," Genomma Lab has run a multi-year programme to incorporate recycled plastic into its packaging across several Latin American markets, and it has been included in ESG indices such as the Dow Jones Sustainability MILA Pacific Alliance Index and the S&P/BMV Total Mexico ESG Index.

## Business Model

Genomma Lab's core business is the manufacture and marketing of branded, largely single-use OTC health and personal-care products sold through retail pharmacies and mass-market channels across Mexico, Latin America and (via licensing/distribution) other regions — its revenue model is unrelated to reuse or circular-economy services. Its sustainability programme operates as a packaging-materials initiative layered onto this conventional manufacturing business: the company has progressively increased the share of post-consumer recycled (PCR) resin in its plastic packaging (reporting up to 30% PCR content in polyethylene packaging as of 2021) and has partnered with recycled-material and alternative-fibre suppliers (e.g., Braskem Idesa for recycled resin; Propal and Ingeniería Gráfica for paper/cardstock made from sugarcane bagasse and rice-straw waste fibre) to substitute virgin materials in its packaging supply chain. It has also run public plastic-collection events ("Plastianguis") in Mexico as a take-back/collection mechanism feeding recyclers, separate from its own packaging production. None of this constitutes "reuse" of packaging or products in the circular-economy sense (repeated use of the same item) — it is recycled-content substitution and end-of-life material collection/recycling.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Material Recovery (post-consumer recycled resin incorporated into new packaging; alternative-fibre paper/cardstock from agricultural waste)
- Waste Reduction (avoided virgin-plastic use through recycled-content substitution)
- Consumer Education / Take-back (Plastianguis public plastic-collection events feeding recyclers)
- Other (ESG/sustainability-index participation; carbon-neutral packaging line, Tío Nacho Sustentable)

## Products & Services

OTC health, dermatological and personal-care brands (including Tío Nacho hair-care line); packaging manufactured with increasing shares of recycled plastic resin and alternative-fibre paperboard; public plastic-collection events (Plastianguis) in Mexico.

## Customers

Mass-market retail and pharmacy consumers across Mexico and other Latin American markets purchasing Genomma Lab's branded health and personal-care products; not a B2B reuse/circularity service provider.

## Partnerships

- Braskem Idesa — supplier of recycled resin incorporated into Genomma Lab packaging
- Propal and Ingeniería Gráfica — alternative-fibre paper/cardstock (sugarcane bagasse, rice straw) suppliers
- S&P Global / Dow Jones Sustainability Index — ESG index inclusion (S&P Global Sustainability Yearbook 2022; Dow Jones Sustainability MILA Pacific Alliance Index; S&P/BMV Total Mexico ESG Index)

## Measurable Impact

- Cumulative total of more than 16,390 tons of recycled material incorporated into packaging manufactured across Colombia, Mexico, Argentina and Brazil (company-reported, as of reporting around October 2024). Note: this is recycled-content material used in manufacturing new packaging — not plastic "reused" (i.e., reused as-is) as characterized in the seed data.
- In 2021, avoided the use of 407 tons of virgin plastic by incorporating up to 30% post-consumer recycled material into polyethylene packaging (company-reported).
- In 2022, collected more than 190 tons of plastic waste through various editions of the "Plastianguis" public collection events, equivalent to an estimated 297 tons of CO2-eq avoided (company-reported).
- Included in the S&P Global Sustainability Yearbook 2022 as one of 10 recognised Mexican companies for sustainability practices.

## Technology

Not publicly available as a distinct reuse/circularity technology; standard consumer-goods manufacturing and supply-chain operations, with recycled-resin sourcing and alternative-fibre processing handled via named material suppliers.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

The seed data's claim that Genomma Lab "reused over 16,000 tons of plastic" is a mischaracterization that this research corrects: the verified, company-reported figure (16,390+ tons, as of ~October 2024) refers to recycled material incorporated into new packaging manufacturing across four Latin American countries — a recycled-content/material-recovery initiative, not literal reuse of plastic items or packaging. Genomma Lab is a large, publicly listed, well-documented conglomerate with genuine and independently reported sustainability metrics, but its programme sits squarely in recycling and sustainable sourcing rather than the reuse economy (reusable packaging, refill, deposit-return, take-back for reuse) that is this library's focus. It should be included, if at all, as a large-scale recycled-content case study rather than a reuse operator.

Priority: ★★ Low (verified but recycling/recycled-content focused, not a reuse-economy operator; included primarily to correct the seed data's mischaracterization)

## Verification Notes

This profile makes a material correction to the seed data: the claim "reused over 16,000 tons of plastic" does not match any verified source. Multiple independent Spanish-language sustainability news outlets (Cuál Es Tu Huella, Latam Green, Revista Economía, Corresponsables) consistently report the figure as "more than 16,390 tons of RECYCLED MATERIAL used in packaging manufacturing" across Colombia, Mexico, Argentina and Brazil — i.e., recycled feedstock content in newly manufactured packaging, not reuse of existing plastic items. This is a meaningful distinction for a reuse-economy library: recycling/recycled-content and reuse are different circular-economy strategies, and Genomma Lab's public claims and reporting relate exclusively to the former. Founding year (1996) and stock listing (BMV: LABB) are well corroborated across financial data providers (Investing.com, Google Finance, Yahoo Finance, Bloomberg, PitchBook).
