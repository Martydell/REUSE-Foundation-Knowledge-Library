## Organisation Overview

Siklus is a Jakarta-based startup founded in 2019 by Jane von Rabenau that provides an on-demand refill service for household staples — shampoo, liquid soap, cooking oil, detergent and dishwashing liquid — as an alternative to single-use plastic sachets, which dominate the FMCG market across Southeast Asia. Customers order via app, Instagram or WhatsApp and receive refills delivered by motorbike into their own reusable containers, or buy from roadside refill stalls stocked by Siklus. The model targets both lower-income and, increasingly, middle-income households in Greater Jakarta, combining cost savings for consumers with plastic-waste reduction.

## Business Model

Siklus operates a B2C/B2B hybrid refill-and-reuse supply chain. It purchases household and personal-care products in bulk from major FMCG manufacturers, then redistributes them in refillable formats via a network of motorbike delivery riders ("refill bikes") and partner roadside stalls (warungs), where customers bring or receive reusable containers rather than single-use sachets. Ordering is digital-first, through a mobile app, Instagram direct messaging and WhatsApp, with in-app functionality to show customers how much plastic and money they save per order. The company has received programmatic grant funding from the P4G (Partnering for Green Growth and the Global Goals 2030) platform — an initial start-up grant in 2018 and a scale-up grant in 2021 — alongside venture and angel investment, and has run community education initiatives on plastic waste in partnership with local environmental organisations.

## Products & Services

Refillable shampoo, liquid soap/body wash, cooking oil, laundry detergent and dishwashing liquid, ordered via app/WhatsApp/Instagram and delivered by motorbike, or purchased at partner refill stalls.

## Customers

Households in Greater Jakarta, Indonesia — originally targeted at lower-income communities, with reported uptake also among middle-income households; B2B relationships with FMCG brands supplying bulk product.

## Measurable Impact

- P4G-funded partnership reported reaching over 3,900 people through community outreach and stakeholder events, and educating 1,600+ women on waste issues across 15 villages/sub-districts, as of 2022 (source: P4G Partnerships, p4gpartnerships.org). Grant-recipient self-report, not independently audited.
- Media Outreach Newswire (Dec 2021) reported Siklus had prevented an estimated 27.65 tonnes of single-use plastic from being used/wasted since 2020. Company-reported figure, not independently audited.
- Products reported to average approximately 20% cheaper than conventional packaged equivalents (source: P4G Partnerships).

## Technology used

Mobile ordering app; WhatsApp and Instagram-based ordering channels; in-app calculator estimating plastic and cost savings per purchase (source: Jakarta Post, Aug 2021).

## Challenges

Not publicly documented in verified sources beyond general sector context (Southeast Asia accounts for a large share of the global single-use sachet market, and Indonesia is cited as a major source of marine plastic pollution — Greenpeace data cited via Jakarta Post).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Siklus is a strong fit for the REUSE Foundation library: refill and reusable packaging is the entire premise of its business, directly displacing single-use sachets — one of the hardest-to-recycle plastic formats globally — in a high-need market (Indonesia). It combines a genuine reuse/refill logistics model with measurable (if self-reported) plastic-avoidance and community-education outcomes, and has attracted credible institutional grant funding (P4G) alongside major FMCG brand partnerships. Note the seed data's "Year Established: 2021" is not supported by independent sources (Crunchbase, Jakarta Post, and P4G materials consistently point to founding/launch in 2019, with P4G funding beginning in 2018); this should be corrected in the tracker.
