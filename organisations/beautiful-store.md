# Beautiful Store

A South Korean nonprofit foundation operating a nationwide network of volunteer-run charity shops that collect donated used goods and resell them to fund social and community programmes.

## Organisation Overview

Beautiful Store is a South Korean nonprofit charity-shop network launched in 2002 by People's Solidarity for Participatory Democracy, modelled in part on the Oxfam charity-shop concept, with its first store opening in the Anguk neighbourhood of Seoul. It became an independent nonprofit corporation in 2008. The organisation has since grown to more than 100 volunteer-run stores across some 16 cities in Korea, plus a small number of shops abroad. Stores collect donated secondhand goods — clothing, books, bags, kitchenware and appliances — and resell them, directing a share of proceeds to community and social-innovation grant programmes.

## Business Model

Beautiful Store's core model is donation-based resale: the public donates used household items and clothing to volunteer-run retail shops, which sell the goods at affordable prices to the public, generating revenue used to fund social programmes. The organisation reports directing more than 10% of sales profits to grassroots community projects and social entrepreneurs through its "Beautiful Fellow" support programme, active since 2011. Beyond straightforward resale, the organisation also runs "Eco Party Mearry," an upcycling design brand launched in 2006 that transforms donated materials such as used clothing, banners, sofa fabric and leather into new designed products, entirely from donated inputs. It additionally operates flea markets (including a long-running partnership with the Seoul Metropolitan Government at Ttukseom, begun in 2004) and has a separate fair-trade import arm supporting small producers in developing countries.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Take-back — nationwide network of stores collecting donated secondhand goods from the public
- Material Recovery — resale of donated items and the "Eco Party Mearry" upcycling brand converting donated textiles/materials into new products
- Consumer Education — flea markets and public programming promoting reuse and donation culture
- Waste Reduction — diversion of household goods, clothing and materials from disposal via resale and upcycling

## Products & Services

Resale of donated secondhand clothing, books, bags, kitchenware and appliances through 100+ physical stores; "Eco Party Mearry" upcycled design products made from donated materials; public flea markets; fair-trade imported goods from small producers in developing countries; "Beautiful Fellow" grant/support programme for social innovators.

## Customers

General public donors and shoppers across South Korea (100+ store locations in 16 cities); community organisations and social entrepreneurs receiving Beautiful Fellow grant support; small producers in developing countries participating in the fair-trade import programme.

## Partnerships

Seoul Metropolitan Government (Ttukseom flea market, since 2004); People's Solidarity for Participatory Democracy (founding organisation); small producer/fair-trade partners in developing countries (specific named partners not publicly available).

## Measurable Impact

Reported (source: Wikipedia, citing underlying reporting): by 2009, approximately 95 branches had opened; as of 2007, stores received roughly 97 tons of donated secondhand goods annually per store; since 2011, the Beautiful Fellow programme has supported 62 social innovators with approximately 3.4 billion Korean won in funding (figure as of November 2025, per the same source).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Beautiful Store is a large, long-established (2002–present) and well-documented nonprofit reuse organisation with a genuinely large physical footprint (100+ stores across Korea) and independently reported impact figures spanning nearly two decades. Its combination of donation-based resale, an upcycling product brand, and a social-innovation grant-funding mechanism represents a mature, diversified take-back and material-recovery model that is well outside the "young startup" profile of most organisations in this batch. Its scale, longevity and multi-faceted circular activities make it a strong, high-relevance addition to the library.
