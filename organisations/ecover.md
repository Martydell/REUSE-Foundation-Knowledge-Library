## Organisation Overview

Ecover is a Belgian manufacturer of ecological cleaning products, founded in 1979 by Frans Bogaerts in response to concerns about phosphates in conventional detergents damaging aquatic ecosystems. Headquartered in Malle, Belgium, where it built what it described as the world's first "ecological factory" (with a green roof) in 1992, Ecover has long been recognised for sustainability leadership, including a 1993 UNEP Global 500 Roll of Honour listing and a 2008 Time magazine "Hero of the Environment" award for CEO Mick Bremans. In 2012 Ecover combined commercially with US biodegradable-cleaning brand Method (as "People Against Dirty," each retaining separate identity/R&D), and in 2017 both brands were acquired by S. C. Johnson & Son.

## Business Model

Ecover manufactures and sells plant-based and mineral-based cleaning products — washing-up liquid, laundry detergent, fabric conditioner and multi-surface cleaners — through mainstream and health-food retail channels across Europe and internationally, with manufacturing sites in Malle (Belgium, since 1992), Boulogne-sur-Mer (France, since 2007) and Steffisburg (Switzerland, via the 2003 Held AG acquisition), alongside Method's Chicago factory (2015) under shared corporate ownership since the 2017 SC Johnson acquisition. Ecover has long run a "container refill service" allowing customers to reuse original bottles, historically available mainly through independent health-food stores and small local/cooperative refill schemes rather than as a mainstream supermarket-wide rollout — the seed data's framing of large-scale "refill pouches" as a current core offering should be treated cautiously, as independent sourcing found refill availability described as historically limited in reach rather than ubiquitous. As an SC Johnson subsidiary, Ecover benefits from a large multinational's manufacturing, distribution and R&D resources while retaining its own brand identity and formulation focus.

Plant- and mineral-based washing-up liquid, laundry detergent, fabric conditioner, multi-surface and other household cleaning products; container refill service for reusing original bottles at participating retail locations.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ecover is a long-established (1979), well-documented pioneer of ecological cleaning products with a genuine multi-decade history of sustainability leadership and a real, if historically limited-reach, container refill service — making it a valuable case study of an early-mover reuse model that predates the current refill/PaaS wave by decades. Its acquisition by SC Johnson (2017, not 2019 as in seed data) and past commercial link to Method are well documented in mainstream business press, giving this profile solid corporate sourcing. However, current-day quantified refill/impact metrics were not found, and the seed's characterisation of "refill pouches" as a current core activity is not strongly corroborated — this should be revisited with a direct look at Ecover's current retail refill footprint.
