## Organisation Overview

Babyloop is a Portuguese circular-economy platform, launched in January 2019, that lets families buy and sell second-hand baby equipment — strollers, cots, bassinets, car seats and related gear. It originated as a challenge set by retailer Sonae MC (parent of Continente supermarkets) and was developed by Book in Loop, a Coimbra startup that had previously applied the same reuse model to second-hand school textbooks, with television presenter Carolina Patrocínio involved as the public face of the concept. Sellers can arrange free collection of items or drop them off at any Continente supermarket; products are sanitised and professionally photographed before resale.

## Business Model

Babyloop operates a consumer-to-consumer resale marketplace with logistics and quality-control support: parents list used baby equipment for sale, Babyloop (via Book in Loop's operations) collects the items — either through free pickup or drop-off at Continente supermarket locations nationwide, leveraging Sonae MC's retail footprint — then sanitises, inspects and professionally photographs each item before listing it for resale to other families. This combination of a retail partner's physical network (Continente) with a specialist reuse-logistics operator (Book in Loop/The Loop Co.) is the same model the founders previously built for second-hand textbooks (grades 5-12), extended to baby gear such as strollers, cots and car seats. The stated goal is to cut the cost burden on families of buying baby equipment, with the company citing a target of reducing related family expenses by up to 80%, while extending the usable life of durable baby products that are otherwise used for only a short window before being outgrown.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reverse Logistics (free collection service and Continente drop-off points for used baby items)
- Take-back (structured intake, sanitisation and inspection of used goods before resale)
- Waste Reduction (extending useful life of baby equipment, reducing new-purchase demand)
- Consumer Education (circular-economy messaging tied to family cost savings)

Not identified from public sources: refill systems, deposit-return schemes, or rental/subscription models — Babyloop's model is resale (ownership transfer), not rental.

## Products & Services

Resale marketplace for second-hand baby equipment (strollers, cots, bassinets, car seats and related durable goods), including free collection/drop-off logistics, sanitisation and professional product photography prior to resale.

## Customers

Families in Portugal buying and selling baby equipment, accessed via the babyloop.pt platform and Continente supermarket drop-off points nationwide.

## Partnerships

Sonae MC / Continente (Portuguese retail group; supermarket network used for drop-off collection points and the originating retail partner); Book in Loop / The Loop Co. (developer and operator of the platform).

Online marketplace platform (babyloop.pt) with integrated logistics coordination for collection/drop-off, plus a professional photography/listing workflow for resold items.

Babyloop is a well-documented, retailer-backed circular-economy platform addressing a clear reuse opportunity — baby equipment with a short usable life per family — through a credible collection-and-resale logistics model integrated with a major national supermarket chain's physical footprint. Its partnership structure (retailer + specialist reuse operator) is a replicable model worth highlighting for the REUSE Foundation library, though independently verified impact metrics (volumes, tonnes, revenue) are not publicly available, limiting quantitative assessment.
