## Organisation Overview

Bio c' Bon Japon is the Japanese operating company of the French organic supermarket brand Bio c' Bon, established in 2016 as a joint venture between Japanese retail group Aeon and the French chain's owner, Marne & Finance Europe. It operates organic supermarkets in the Tokyo area (with locations also reported in Kanagawa) offering more than 1,000 certified-organic product lines spanning fresh food, grocery, beverages, cosmetics and household goods, several of which include in-store bulk/refill "pick-your-own" stations for items such as nuts, dried fruit, grains and lentils.

## Business Model

Bio c' Bon Japon operates as a bricks-and-mortar organic supermarket chain, licensing the Bio c' Bon retail concept and brand from its French originator for the Japanese market. Revenue comes from in-store retail sales to consumers, supplemented by an online store (bio-c-bon.jp) operated in partnership with Aeon's e-commerce channels (Green Beans, Aeon Style Online). The company reports 236 employees (per PitchBook/aggregator data, not independently confirmed) and operated 24 stores in the Tokyo area as of reporting cited April 2026 (some sources cite 27 stores at various points since 2016). The core reuse-relevant element of the business model is the bulk/refill "pick-your-own" bar found at several stores, where customers dispense dry goods (nuts, dried fruit, wild rice, lentils) by weight into their own or provided bags, reducing packaging per unit sold compared to pre-packed equivalents. This sits alongside the company's broader organic-food positioning rather than being the primary business driver; the chain's core revenue model is conventional organic grocery retail, not a refill-first or reuse-first model.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems — in-store bulk/"pick-your-own" bar for nuts, dried fruit, grains and lentils at multiple Tokyo locations (e.g. Azabu Juban, Akasaka)
- Consumer Education — in-store promotion of organic and lower-packaging shopping choices

## Products & Services

Organic and natural food and beverages, fresh produce, meat, seafood and dairy, ready-to-eat/deli items, cosmetics and household non-food items, plus a bulk/refill section for select dry goods; online ordering via the company's e-commerce site and Aeon-affiliated platforms.

## Customers

Retail consumers in the Tokyo metropolitan area (and reportedly Kanagawa), primarily health- and environment-conscious shoppers seeking certified organic products.

## Partnerships

- Aeon Co., Ltd. (joint-venture partner and, from 2018, minority shareholder in the French parent) — aeon.info press release
- Marne & Finance Europe (French chain owner/co-founder of the Japan joint venture)
- Carrefour (acquired the Bio c' Bon banner in France in 2020–2021; relationship to the separately incorporated Japan entity not confirmed in public sources)
- Cross-listing/e-commerce ties to Aeon's Green Beans, Aeon Bike Mall, Aeon Body and Aeon Style Online

## Challenges

Japan's organic food retail sector remains a small niche; industry commentary (Foreign Correspondents' Club of Japan event, "the ordeals of an organic food retailer in Japan") frames Bio c' Bon Japon's expansion as occurring in a market with limited organic-consumer penetration compared with Europe. No further documented operational or reuse-specific challenges were found in public sources.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Bio c' Bon Japon is a conventional organic-supermarket operator whose refill/bulk offering is a secondary feature within a small number of stores rather than a dedicated reuse or refill business model. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's mission is limited to the "pick-your-own" bulk stations reducing packaging for a subset of dry-goods lines; there is no evidence of reusable-packaging schemes, deposit-return systems, or measurable reuse/circularity metrics. As a large-format retailer with Aeon backing, it has potential to scale refill infrastructure further, but current public information does not show this as a strategic priority.
