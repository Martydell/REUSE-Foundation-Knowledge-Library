## Organisation Overview

Loopfront is a Norwegian company founded in 2018 by architect Michael Anthony Curtis and four co-founders, born from frustration with the lack of effective digital tools for reusing building materials. It has built what it describes as Norway's largest digital platform for the reuse of building materials and interiors, using AI-assisted surveying tools to give buildings a digital material inventory within days, then managing the full process — mapping, documentation, internal/external marketplace listing, logistics coordination, and reporting on cost, CO2 and waste savings. The company now has around 80 paying customers across Norway, Sweden and Germany, spanning public sector bodies, property owners and large private companies.

## Business Model

Loopfront operates a B2B Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) model targeted at the construction, property and public sectors. Field specialists or client teams use Loopfront's AI-assisted surveying tool to inventory a building's reusable materials and fixtures within a few days, creating a "digital identity" for each item that can be tracked, listed for internal reuse (within a project or organisation) or externally on a marketplace, and coordinated for logistics/collection. The platform automatically generates reports quantifying reduced costs, CO2 emissions and waste diverted from disposal. Pricing is structured as a monthly subscription pegged to a small fraction (reported as 0.01% or less) of total project cost, making it scalable across projects of varying size. Its roughly 80 paying customers span Norway, Sweden and Germany, with growing interest particularly among public-sector clients, property owners and larger companies — reflecting increasing regulatory and ESG-driven demand for material reuse documentation in construction and demolition projects.
