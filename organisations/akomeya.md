## Organisation Overview

AKOMEYA TOKYO is a Japanese specialty retailer built around rice: brown rice is milled to the customer's preferred degree on-site and sold by weight in paper bags rather than pre-packaged. The brand, operated by AKOMEYA TOKYO, Inc. (a subsidiary/joint venture of The SAZABY LEAGUE, Ltd.), opened its first Ginza store in 2013 and has since expanded to multiple locations in Tokyo (Shinjuku, Shibuya, Kagurazaka, Nihonbashi) offering rice, dashi, seasonings, baked goods, kitchenware and curated gifts alongside an on-site restaurant format in some stores.

## Business Model

AKOMEYA TOKYO operates specialty retail stores plus e-commerce, generating revenue from the sale of rice, condiments, prepared foods, and kitchen/lifestyle goods to Japanese and international consumers, including tourists. Its distinguishing operational feature is an on-demand rice-milling service: customers select from around 18 varieties of specialty rice sourced from producers across Japan, choose a polishing grade (from brown rice through to fully white), and have it milled to order at an in-store "rice bar," which is then dispensed into simple paper bags rather than sold pre-packaged in plastic. This on-demand model inherently reduces packaging and spoilage waste compared to conventional pre-packaged rice retail, since product is processed and portioned only at the point of sale. Beyond rice, the company positions itself as a "food catalyst" connecting small-scale Japanese food and craft producers with consumers, and operates restaurant/café formats at some locations (e.g., Kagurazaka). The company does not appear to publish standalone revenue figures; it is one of several retail businesses under parent group The SAZABY LEAGUE, Ltd., which also operates Afternoon Tea and Ron Herman, among others, and reported ¥95 billion in consolidated sales for FY ending March 2023.

## Products & Services

Specialty rice (milled to order), dashi broth, seasonings and condiments, baked goods, prepared foods, kitchen utensils and tableware, and curated gift sets; some locations include an attached restaurant/café.

## Customers
