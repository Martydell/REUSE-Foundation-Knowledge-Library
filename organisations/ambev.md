## Organisation Overview

Ambev S.A. is a Brazilian brewing and beverage company formed in 1999 through the merger of Brahma and Antarctica, two breweries with 19th-century origins, and is today majority-owned by global brewer AB InBev while remaining separately listed on the NYSE and B3. With over 39,600 employees as of its most recent annual report and operations across 18 countries in the Americas, Ambev is one of the largest publicly documented operators of returnable glass bottle systems globally, reporting approximately 5 billion returnable bottles in circulation in Brazil and a long-standing target to shift its packaging portfolio toward returnable formats and recycled content.

## Business Model

Ambev's core business is the production, distribution and sale of beer, soft drinks, energy drinks, juices, teas and water across Brazil and other Latin American markets, but a structurally significant share of its beer volume moves through a returnable glass bottle system rather than single-use packaging: consumers purchase returnable bottles and receive them back into circulation via retail and informal return channels, with the company reporting roughly 5 billion such bottles active in the Brazilian market and design targets of up to 30 reuses per bottle before retirement. In 2024, Ambev extended this model by reintroducing a 1-litre returnable glass bottle for its Guaraná Antarctica soft drink and introducing returnable long-neck bottles for its Corona brand in seven Brazilian cities, alongside continued investment in glass manufacturing capacity — including a reported BRL 870 million (~US$154 million) investment in a new sustainable glass plant in Paraná designed to use recycled/broken glass sourced partly through reverse-logistics partnerships. The company has stated a corporate target (aligned with AB InBev's global "Circular Packaging" 2025 goal) for 100% of its products to be in returnable packaging or packaging made with majority recycled content by 2025, with company communications citing 40% of its beer volume already in returnable packaging as of 2020. As a subsidiary of AB InBev, Ambev's sustainability reporting is nested within AB InBev's broader global ESG framework, though Ambev publishes its own annual and sustainability report via its investor relations site.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging (returnable glass bottles, ~5 billion in circulation in Brazil)
- Deposit Return (informal/retail-channel bottle return system underpinning the returnable model)
- Reverse Logistics (partnerships supporting collection of broken/recycled glass for its new Paraná glass plant)
- Material Recovery (recycled PET content in packaging, reported at 42.9% in 2024, up from 40.3% in 2023)
- Consumer Education (communications supporting adoption of returnable formats, e.g. Guaraná Antarctica relaunch)

## Products & Services

Beer brands (including Brahma, Antarctica, Skol and licensed international brands such as Corona and Budweiser in Brazil), soft drinks (Guaraná Antarctica), energy drinks, juices, teas and bottled water — sold in both returnable glass and other packaging formats depending on brand and market.

## Customers

Mass-market retail and on-trade consumers across Brazil and other Latin American countries where Ambev operates (18 countries in the Americas); returnable-bottle purchasers specifically are retail and bar/restaurant customers participating in the deposit-style return channel.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ambev is a major, well-documented public company operating one of the largest returnable-packaging systems in the world by absolute volume (billions of bottles in a single national market), giving it high relevance as a large-scale, real-world proof point for reusable packaging at commercial scale. Its returnable bottle model long predates recent "reuse revolution" trends, and its 2024 moves (Guaraná Antarctica relaunch, Corona returnable long-necks, new glass plant investment) show continued, active investment rather than a static legacy programme. As a subsidiary of AB InBev with SEC-listed reporting obligations, its disclosures are more scrutinised than most organisations in this library, though this research pass relied on secondary press coverage of sustainability claims rather than the primary annual/sustainability report PDF, so several impact figures should be treated as Medium confidence pending direct citation to Ambev's Form 20-F or annual/sustainability report.
