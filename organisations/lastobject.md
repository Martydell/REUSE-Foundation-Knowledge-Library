## Organisation Overview

LastObject was established in August 2016 in Copenhagen, Denmark, by siblings Isabel and Nicolas Aagaard together with Kåre Frandsen, after research into ocean plastic pollution identified cotton swabs as a major contributor. The company developed LastSwab, a reusable cotton swab, launching it publicly in April 2019 and running a Kickstarter campaign the following month that raised over $700,000 from more than 19,000 backers. LastObject has since expanded its range to include LastTissue, LastRound and LastMask, selling globally online and through retail partners.

## Business Model

LastObject designs and manufactures reusable, durable substitutes for disposable everyday hygiene products — a reusable swab (LastSwab) replacing single-use cotton buds, a reusable cloth handkerchief system (LastTissue) replacing paper tissues, reusable/recyclable cotton pads (LastRound), and a reusable face-mask kit (LastMask). Its go-to-market model combined crowdfunding (Kickstarter for LastSwab in 2019, later an Indiegogo campaign raising over €1 million from 30,000+ backers) with direct e-commerce and international retail distribution. The company has raised institutional capital, including 6 million DKK (~$980,000) in seed funding from the Danish Green Investment Fund in April 2021, to scale manufacturing and distribution. LastObject also engages in cause-linked partnerships, such as its November 2020 tie-up with Plastic Bank, a Canadian social enterprise building recycling ecosystems in vulnerable coastal communities, aligning its commercial growth with broader plastic-pollution reduction goals.

## Products & Services

LastSwab (reusable cotton swab), LastTissue (reusable cloth tissue system), LastRound (reusable/recyclable cotton pads), LastMask (reusable face mask and spray kit), sold via direct e-commerce and retail partners worldwide.

## Customers

Global consumer market, purchasing via LastObject's own websites (lastobject.com, lastobject.co.uk) and retail/distribution partners across Europe and North America.

## Partnerships

Plastic Bank (partnership announced November 2020); Danish Green Investment Fund (seed investor, April 2021); crowdfunding backers via Kickstarter (2019) and Indiegogo.

## Measurable Impact

Kickstarter campaign (2019) raised over $700,000 from more than 19,000 backers; a subsequent Indiegogo campaign raised over €1 million from more than 30,000 backers; LastSwab was selected for the Beazley Designs of the Year exhibition at London's Design Museum (October 2020).
