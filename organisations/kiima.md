## Organisation Overview

Kiima is a Montreal-based startup co-founded by Jenny Essiambre and Samuel Lemire-Dupont, developing a refillable deodorant applicator designed to last 10 years, manufactured in Quebec. The applicator uses a unique mechanism enabling plastic-free refilling, and the company has built an ecosystem of cosmetics partners who design compatible refills for the applicator format. Within two years of launch, Kiima expanded to hundreds of points of sale, including Jean Coutu, Avril, and Simons in Canada, plus international distribution in Italy, Spain, and the US.

## Business Model

Kiima sells a durable, reusable deodorant applicator (designed for a 10-year lifespan) once, then customers purchase compatible refills from Kiima and its partner cosmetics brands rather than buying a new plastic deodorant stick each time. The applicator format was launched via a Kickstarter crowdfunding campaign and has since scaled into retail distribution across hundreds of points of sale in Canada and internationally (Italy, Spain, US), with Kiima developing partnerships with cosmetics companies to design refills specifically compatible with its applicator mechanism.
