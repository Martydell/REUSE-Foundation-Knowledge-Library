## Organisation Overview

ecostore papalagi is a small, independently run zero-waste retail shop in Fujisawa, Kanagawa Prefecture, Japan, launched in January 2019 by a professional diver and three colleagues as a form of climate action advocacy. It operates under cooperative governance via the Plastic Free Promotion Association (a worker cooperative, ワーカーズコープ) rather than as a conventional profit-driven company or franchise. Despite the name's resemblance to the New Zealand ecostore brand and its Japanese licensee ecostore JAPAN Co., Ltd., independent research found no corporate, licensing, or ownership relationship between them — the seed data's suggestion of a connection is not supported by available evidence.

## Business Model

ecostore papalagi runs a single physical retail location selling plastic-free household goods, biodegradable cleaning products, pesticide-free rice (sourced from a 50-year farming relationship), and personal-care items, with core products available "by weight" (量り売り) so customers can buy only what they need using their own containers. The store also functions as a free filtered-water refill point for the community. Reflecting its cooperative structure and advocacy origins, the business runs environmental education events and seminars alongside retail sales, positioning commerce as a secondary vehicle for climate/plastic-pollution advocacy rather than the primary purpose of the organisation.
