## Organisation Overview

Costa Brazil is a luxury beauty and personal-care brand founded in 2016 by Francisco Costa, former creative director of Calvin Klein Collection. The brand sells body oils, creams, fragrances, face serums, candles, and haircare products formulated around Brazilian ingredients — notably Kaya oil from sapucaia trees in northern Brazil — sold across 30+ countries, with sustainability positioning built around FSC-certified packaging, vegan/cruelty-free formulation, and partnerships with Conservation International and Brazil's Yawanawá tribe.

## Business Model

Costa Brazil sells premium beauty and personal-care products direct-to-consumer via e-commerce and presumably select luxury retail, with ingredient sourcing tied to Amazon-region partnerships (Yawanawá tribe) and conservation commitments (Conservation International). Seed data described a "refillable perfume cartridge" system, but this research pass found no evidence of a refill mechanism on the company's own site — the sustainability narrative centres on ingredient sourcing ethics, FSC-certified packaging, and formulation purity (free of parabens, phthalates, silicones, sulfates, etc.) rather than a documented reuse/refill system. A partnership with LATAM Airlines was also referenced but not detailed further.
