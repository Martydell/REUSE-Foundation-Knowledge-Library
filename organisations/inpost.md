## Organisation Overview

InPost is Poland's largest last-mile delivery and parcel-locker (Automated Parcel Machine/APM) network operator, founded in 2006 and listed on Euronext Amsterdam since January 2021. The company's core business — over 20,000 parcel lockers across Poland and expanding European markets — is not itself a reuse activity. Separately, InPost developed EkoBox, launched in December 2021 with fashion retailer MODIVO (CCC Group), Poland's first reusable secondary-packaging (returnable shipping box) system for e-commerce, developed with Danish packaging company ReZIP. This profile focuses on the EkoBox reuse initiative, not InPost's broader logistics business.

## Business Model

InPost's overall business is a last-mile parcel-locker and courier network monetised via delivery fees charged to e-commerce retailers and marketplaces (its core model, unrelated to reuse). EkoBox operates as an add-on service layered onto this existing infrastructure: participating e-shops (initially MODIVO) ship customer orders in durable, sized reusable boxes instead of single-use cardboard; customers return the empty box free of charge via any InPost parcel locker by scanning a label; InPost collects, inspects/disinfects, and recirculates the boxes back to the retailer. The service was offered free of charge to end customers during its pilot phase. InPost has stated an intention to open EkoBox to other e-commerce retailers beyond MODIVO, leveraging its existing 20,000+ locker network as the return infrastructure — the same asset that powers its core parcel business — rather than building separate reuse-specific logistics. No public information indicates EkoBox generates material standalone revenue; it functions as a sustainability/differentiation feature of InPost's broader delivery platform.

## Products & Services

EkoBox — a durable, FSC-certified, fully recyclable cardboard reusable shipping box, sized to reduce void fill, designed for approximately 10+ reuse cycles before recycling; returned by customers free of charge via InPost's Automated Parcel Machine (APM) network for cleaning/inspection and redistribution to participating retailers.

## Customers

- E-commerce retailers shipping via InPost's network (launch partner: MODIVO, the fashion e-tailer of CCC Group/eobuwie.pl)
- End consumers who receive and return EkoBox packaging through InPost lockers

## Partnerships

- **MODIVO (CCC Group)** — launch and, as of the most recent public confirmation, primary retail partner for EkoBox, shipping orders in EkoBoxes since the system's full-scale rollout in 2022
- **ReZIP (Denmark)** — packaging design/technology partner that developed the reusable box format used in EkoBox

## Measurable Impact

- InPost/MODIVO reported the pilot test phase (from December 2021) as "more than positive," with the reusable format able to reduce single-use cardboard box circulation "up to ten times" fewer boxes needed versus single-use (InPost/MODIVO press materials, 2022) — a company-stated design claim, not an independently audited figure
- No independently verified unit counts, tonnage diverted, or CO2 figures specific to EkoBox were found in public sources as of this review
- Not publicly available: current (2025-2026) number of participating retailers or active EkoBox units in circulation

## Technology

EkoBox packaging is tracked via label-scanning at InPost's existing Automated Parcel Machines (APMs), the same infrastructure used for InPost's core parcel-locker business; no additional public detail (e.g., RFID, unique unit serialisation) on box-level tracking technology was found.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

InPost itself is a very large last-mile logistics company for which reuse is a minor, pilot-scale side initiative (EkoBox) rather than a core activity — the seed data's framing as a "reusable packaging system for online retailers" over-describes what is, on current public evidence, a single-retailer pilot dating to 2021-2022 with no confirmed broader rollout. EkoBox is nonetheless a genuine, verifiable reverse-logistics reuse system built on unusually strong existing infrastructure (20,000+ collection points), giving it real scaling potential if InPost chooses to expand retailer participation. Given the narrow, unconfirmed-at-scale nature of the reuse activity within a otherwise non-reuse-focused conglomerate, this warrants a modest priority rating.

**Priority Rating: ★★ Low** — genuine, verifiably-launched reuse pilot with strong potential infrastructure, but confirmed real-world scale is limited to one retail partner with no independently verified current metrics; core company is not a reuse business.
