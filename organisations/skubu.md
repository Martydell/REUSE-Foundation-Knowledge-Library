## Organisation Overview

Skubu is South Africa's first fully automated refill store, launched in Diepsloot by tech startup Sonke in partnership with the CSIR and the Department of Science, Technology and Innovation under the Circular Economy Demonstration Fund. Customers refill staples like maize meal, rice, cooking oil and detergent using IoT-enabled dispensing machines.

## Business Model

Sonke designs, manufactures and operates automated, IoT-connected refill stations; Skubu is the retail brand operating these machines in low-income township malls. Revenue comes from per-litre/per-kg product sales priced up to 60% below conventional retail, targeting affordability and food security alongside waste reduction.

## Products & Services

Automated dispensing of maize meal, sugar, rice, cooking oil and detergent via customer-supplied containers; a smaller "Skubu Lite" unit in development for mobile clinic distribution of therapeutic food.

## Customers

Lower-income township consumers in Diepsloot, Johannesburg; planned expansion to health-clinic distribution in Limpopo.

## Partnerships

Developed with the CSIR and South Africa's Department of Science, Technology and Innovation (DSTI) under the Circular Economy Demonstration Fund; product partner Manzi Water.

## Measurable Impact

Eliminates up to 100% of single-use plastic packaging per transaction and enables consumer savings of up to 50% versus standard retail pricing, per CSIR and press reporting.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

A high-impact, publicly-backed automated refill model directly addressing plastic reduction and food affordability in underserved communities — a strong template for scaling refill infrastructure in emerging markets.
