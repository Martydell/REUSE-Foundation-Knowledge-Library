## Organisation Overview

Bite is a Los Angeles-based oral-care company founded in 2018 by Lindsay McCormick, who developed chewable toothpaste "bits" as an alternative to plastic toothpaste tubes after learning of the scale of tube waste going to landfill and ocean. The company sells toothpaste bits, mouthwash bits, whitening gel, deodorant and related personal-care products direct-to-consumer, packaged in a reusable glass jar refilled via compostable pouches. Bite is B Corp certified and gained mainstream visibility through a "Shark Tank" appearance (declining offers made) and features in outlets including Forbes, Oprah Magazine and Vogue.

## Business Model

Bite operates a direct-to-consumer e-commerce and subscription model: a customer's first purchase includes a reusable 4oz glass jar (and, for some product lines, other durable holders), and subsequent orders — available as one-off purchases or auto-ship subscriptions (company materials reference a roughly 4-month refill cadence) — are compostable refill pouches rather than new plastic containers. This design eliminates repeat purchase of single-use plastic toothpaste tubes, replacing them with a reusable jar plus a lower-material, compostable pouch format. The company states its refill pouches are designed to break down in home compost within a stated timeframe. Bite has expanded from toothpaste bits into a broader personal-care range (mouthwash, whitening, deodorant, floss, tongue scrapers) sold through its own site and, per company materials, wholesale/retail partnerships.
