## Organisation Overview

Aleph Beauty is a New Zealand-made clean, vegan, cruelty-free cosmetics and skincare brand founded in 2018 by makeup artist Emma Peters. It sells a curated range of multi-functional makeup and skincare products directly to consumers and through wholesale/stockist partners. The company is B Corp certified and operates "Re Aleph," a packaging take-back initiative that collects empty product containers via mail-in and stockist drop-off points for industrial cleaning/reprocessing, with damaged or non-reusable glass diverted to recycling. It is a member of New Zealand's Sustainable Business Network and holds a "Zero Waste" certification from Look For the Zero.

## Business Model

Aleph sells cosmetics (concealer/foundation, cheek and lip tints, eye pigments, mascara, skincare balms and serums) through a direct-to-consumer e-commerce channel and wholesale stockists (e.g., Sills + Co, Chapman Store, Skintopia) in New Zealand. Its circularity model centres on "Re Aleph," a take-back scheme: customers accumulate at least five empty containers and either mail them to the company or drop them at partner stockist locations (in the US, via PACT Collective drop-off points); Aleph states these are industrially sanitized and, where suitable, returned to manufacturing for refilling, while glass that cannot be refilled is crushed and diverted to other industries. Separately, the company's own FAQ page describes returned packaging being dismantled and separated into correct recycling streams rather than refilled — see Verification Notes for this discrepancy. No evidence was found of live in-store refill stations where a customer decants product themselves, contradicting the seed data's description.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Aleph Beauty is a small, values-led New Zealand cosmetics brand with a genuine, B Corp-certified take-back programme for empty packaging, but the seed data's characterization as "in-store cosmetic refill stations" is not supported by available evidence — the verified model is a mail-in/drop-off collection scheme, and the company's own materials are internally inconsistent about whether returned containers are actually refilled or simply dismantled for recycling. This inconsistency should be resolved with the company directly before publishing strong reuse claims. Given the small, single-country scale and unquantified impact, this is a lower-priority listing pending clarification and impact data.

**Priority Rating: ★★ Low** (genuine but small-scale, unquantified programme; core "refill station" claim in seed data not substantiated — internal company sources conflict on whether return = refill or recycle)
