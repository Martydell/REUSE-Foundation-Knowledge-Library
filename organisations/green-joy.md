## Organisation Overview

Green Joy is a Vietnamese company that manufactures compostable drinking straws and food-service utensils from grey sedge (Lepironia) grass and other natural materials native to the Mekong Delta, alongside handicrafts from bamboo, wood, and palm leaf. Founded by CEO Vo Quoc Thao Nguyen, it positions itself as a plastic-straw replacement for the hospitality industry and consumer market. The company sources raw material from smallholder farmers in Long An province and exports the majority of its production internationally, contributing to plastic-waste reduction in the food-service sector.

## Business Model

Green Joy manufactures single-use-alternative straws, utensils, and related products from natural grass and plant fibre, sold through two channels: direct-to-consumer e-commerce (its own site, Amazon, Shopee, Tiki) and B2B/wholesale supply to the Horeca (Hotel/Restaurant/Café) sector. It reports over 1,000 Horeca customers within Vietnam and export relationships covering 30+ countries, with roughly 80% of production exported to markets including the US, Germany, France, Belgium, Poland, Japan, and South Korea. It supplies international luxury hotel and resort supply chains in Vietnam and has listed products on Amazon US to reach Western B2C markets. Raw material is grown by partner farmers and cooperatives on an estimated 100 hectares in the Mekong Delta, giving the model an agricultural-supply-chain component alongside manufacturing.

## Products & Services

Dried grass drinking straws, grass-based utensils and tableware, and handicraft items made from bamboo, wood, and palm leaf.

## Customers

Food-service businesses (hotels, restaurants, cafés) in Vietnam (1,000+ reported) and internationally; individual consumers via e-commerce marketplaces (Amazon, Shopee, Tiki).

## Partnerships

- Amazon, Shopee, Tiki (e-commerce distribution)

## Measurable Impact

Figures are self-reported (official website) and via the UNDP-affiliated Equator Initiative Nature-Based Solutions Database — the two sources give different totals for the same underlying claim, both undated to a specific year (cumulative-to-date figures):

- Official site (greenjoystraw.com): 40 million plastic straws replaced; 20 tons of plastic mass reduced; 50 tons of CO2 emissions reduced; 100 farmers/local people supported.
- Equator Initiative / UNDP profile (2024): 40 million plastic straws replaced; 250 tons of plastic mass reduced; 200 tons of CO2 emissions reduced; ~100 hectares of grass cultivation area; 65% of workforce women.

No independently audited figures were found. See Verification Notes for the discrepancy.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Green Joy is a materials-substitution manufacturer rather than a reuse operator in the strict circular-economy sense — its core contribution is single-use-plastic avoidance through a compostable natural-fibre alternative, not reuse, refill, or return-loop infrastructure. It is nonetheless relevant to the REUSE Foundation library as an example of supply-chain-level plastic reduction with a genuine smallholder-farmer livelihoods component, recognised by UNDP's Equator Initiative. Its relevance to core "reuse" scope (as opposed to substitution/recycling-adjacent activity) is limited, which should inform its categorisation.

**Priority Rating: ★★ Low** (adjacent to reuse scope — materials substitution rather than reuse/refill/return systems)
