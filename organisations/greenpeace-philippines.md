## Organisation Overview

Greenpeace Philippines is the Philippine national office of the international environmental NGO Greenpeace, formally established in Metro Manila in March 2000 as part of Greenpeace Southeast Asia (alongside Thailand and Indonesia offices). Within its plastic-free-future campaign work, Greenpeace Philippines runs "Kuha sa Tingi" ("get it in small portions"), a reuse-and-refill initiative launched in November 2022 in partnership with the San Juan City local government and Impact Hub Manila. The project reintroduces the traditional Filipino "tingi" (small-portion) purchasing culture through refill stations for everyday products like detergent, dishwashing liquid, and shampoo installed in neighbourhood sari-sari (small convenience) stores, letting customers buy precise quantities into their own reusable containers instead of single-use sachets.

## Business Model

As a non-profit advocacy organisation, Greenpeace Philippines does not operate a commercial business model; it is funded through individual donations (Greenpeace's global policy is to refuse corporate and government funding to preserve campaign independence). Its "Kuha sa Tingi" project operates as a piloted public-private-NGO partnership: Greenpeace Philippines partnered with the San Juan City local government unit and Impact Hub Manila to install refill stations for everyday household products inside existing neighbourhood sari-sari stores, rather than building new retail infrastructure. Store owners are trained and equipped to sell products like detergent, dishwashing liquid, and shampoo by volume into customers' own reusable containers, replacing single-use plastic sachets. The pilot began in San Juan City in November 2022, later expanded to Quezon City, and by the end of 2024 had grown to reach over 2,000 stores across Metro Manila. The project's business case for store owners centres on improved profit margins from refill sales compared to sachet retail, alongside reported consumer cost savings, making the model self-reinforcing for small retailers once adopted.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems (small-portion refill stations for detergent, dishwashing liquid, shampoo, and other household products in sari-sari stores)

- Waste Reduction (direct displacement of single-use plastic sachet consumption)
- Reusable Packaging (customer-supplied reusable containers used at refill stations)

## Products & Services

"Kuha sa Tingi" refill stations for everyday household products (detergent, dishwashing liquid, shampoo, and similar goods) installed within existing sari-sari stores; associated store-owner training and community engagement/advocacy programming; broader Greenpeace Philippines plastic-pollution campaign and policy advocacy work.

## Customers

Filipino consumers, particularly in Metro Manila neighbourhoods (San Juan City, Quezon City, and other Metro Manila areas), purchasing refilled household products from participating sari-sari stores; sari-sari store owners as direct programme participants/beneficiaries.

## Partnerships

- San Juan City Local Government Unit — co-launch partner for the original "Kuha sa Tingi" pilot (2022)
- Quezon City Local Government Unit — expansion partner
- Impact Hub Manila — programme design/implementation partner

## Measurable Impact

- "Kuha sa Tingi" reached over 2,000 sari-sari stores across Metro Manila from its 2022 launch through the end of 2024.
- Over 3.9 million single-use plastic sachets avoided cumulatively (2022–end of 2024), per Greenpeace Philippines reporting.
- Earlier programme reporting (March 2024 publication) cited over 50,000 sachets avoided and 201% consumer savings in initial pilot phases — figures superseded by the larger 2022–2024 cumulative totals above as the programme scaled.
- A cited survey figure states 97% of Filipinos support switching from single-use plastics to reuse-and-refill systems (source survey not independently identified in this research pass).

## Technology

No advanced digital tracking or app-based technology identified; the model relies on physical refill dispensing equipment installed in participating sari-sari stores and manual store-owner record-keeping/training.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Greenpeace Philippines' "Kuha sa Tingi" programme is one of the more rigorously documented reuse/refill initiatives in this batch, with verifiable, dated, multi-year impact figures (2,000+ stores, 3.9 million+ sachets avoided by end of 2024) published directly by Greenpeace Philippines and corroborated by local press (Manila Bulletin) and city government sources (San Juan City press release). Its integration of traditional Filipino "tingi" purchasing culture with a modern refill-station model, delivered through existing informal retail infrastructure (sari-sari stores) rather than new dedicated shops, is a distinctive and highly scalable approach directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's mission, particularly for lower-income and emerging-market contexts. As a non-profit campaign rather than a company, its "business model" section reflects programme structure rather than commercial mechanics.
