## Organisation Overview

GAIA International (Private) Limited, trading as GAIA Bags, is a Pakistani company founded in 2017 and headquartered in Islamabad. It manufactures reusable, recyclable bags made from woven polypropylene yarn, positioned as a sturdier, longer-lasting alternative to single-use plastic shopping bags. The company frames its offering around the waste hierarchy's "3 Rs," arguing its bags reduce waste through durability, are reusable across many shopping trips, and are recyclable at end of life. GAIA operates primarily as a B2B supplier, producing customizable/branded bags for supermarkets, lifestyle retailers and clothing brands across Pakistan. LinkedIn company data lists it as an 11–50 employee packaging and containers business.

## Business Model

GAIA sells reusable woven-polypropylene bags on a B2B basis to retail chains — supermarkets, lifestyle stores and clothing brands — who purchase bags in bulk, often customized and branded to reflect the retailer's identity, for distribution to their own shoppers as an alternative to single-use plastic bags at checkout. Revenue is generated through direct manufacturing and sale of bags rather than a subscription, deposit or reuse-as-a-service model; the environmental case GAIA makes to prospective retail clients is a lifecycle argument — while a woven-PP bag requires more energy to produce than a single-use plastic bag (the company cites 3,736 GJ of non-renewable energy per 42 kg of bags at production), the impact amortizes down as each bag is reused, dropping to roughly 467 GJ once a bag has been used at least 8 times. The company markets durability, water-resistance and brand customization as its main competitive differentiators against thinner reusable-bag alternatives.
