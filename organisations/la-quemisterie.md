## Organisation Overview

La Quemisterie is a self-described "triple impact" (social, environmental, economic) company founded in Buenos Aires in 2016 by three women — chemist Graciela Oblitas (previously with multinationals including Clorox and Colgate), book editor Daniela Nobili, and technology teacher Inés Abbiati. Based in the Las Cañitas neighbourhood, it sells biodegradable, cruelty-free cleaning, fabric-care and hand-care products through a bulk refill model, using an automated LED-lit dispensing system called "Le Vrac®" to fill customer containers. The company formulates its own products in-house — house-brand lines called Maison (home cleaning), Blancherie (fabric care/perfuming) and Beauté (hand care) — free of animal-derived ingredients and animal testing, and frames its operations around compliance with Argentina's Zero Waste Law (Ley Basura Cero, Law 1854).

## Business Model

La Quemisterie operates a specialty bulk-refill retail model: customers make an initial purchase that includes a reusable, recyclable PET container (e.g., for laundry softener or cleaning liquid); on return visits, they pay only for the product refill dispensed via the in-store Le Vrac® automated station, not for new packaging. This deposit/reuse-container approach is designed to be repeated indefinitely, with containers described as reusable "for many years." The company differentiates through in-house formulation of biodegradable, neutral-pH, paraben-free cleaning and personal-care products across three branded lines (Maison, Blancherie, Beauté), combining environmental positioning with an owner-led artisanal/small-batch approach. Revenue is generated through direct retail sales at its physical store and online shop, targeting environmentally conscious urban consumers in Buenos Aires. The founders' explicit "triple impact" framing signals an intent to balance profit with social and environmental goals rather than operate as a purely conventional retailer.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems — automated Le Vrac® LED dispensing station for bulk refilling of cleaning, fabric-care and hand-care products
- Reusable Packaging — reusable, recyclable PET containers reused repeatedly across refill visits
- Deposit Return — first-purchase container cost with refill-only pricing on subsequent visits
- Waste Reduction — in-house biodegradable product formulation reducing chemical and packaging waste, aligned with Argentina's Zero Waste Law
- Consumer Education — positioning as one of Argentina's first package-free ("sin envase") retail stores

## Products & Services

Refillable biodegradable cleaning products (detergents, all-purpose cleaners, laundry soap, fabric softener) under the "Maison" and "Blancherie" lines, and hand-care products under "Beauté," all dispensed via the in-store Le Vrac® refill station into reusable PET containers; also sold via online shop.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

La Quemisterie is a well-documented, founder-led example of Argentina's early package-free retail movement, notable for combining automated dispensing technology (Le Vrac®) with in-house biodegradable product formulation and explicit triple-impact framing. It was identified by Argentine business press as one of the country's first package-free stores alongside Cero Market and Ecotown, giving it some standing as a pioneer in the local market. Public impact data and partnership information are limited, and the business appears to operate at a single-store, city-neighbourhood scale rather than nationally, but it is a credible, verifiable refill-system case study.
