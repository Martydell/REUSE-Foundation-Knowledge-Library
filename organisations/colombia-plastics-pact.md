# Colombia Plastics Pact

## Organisation Overview

The Colombia Plastics Pact is a national multi-stakeholder initiative launched in 2023, coordinated by CEMPRE (Business Commitment to Recycling, Colombia) and the UK-based Waste and Resources Action Programme (WRAP), with UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) support. It is the second Plastics Pact in Latin America and sets four 2030 targets: eliminate 100% of problematic/unnecessary plastics; make 100% of plastic packaging reusable, recyclable, or compostable; achieve a 50% effective recycling rate; and ensure 30% recycled content in new plastic packaging.

## Business Model

As a Plastics Pact, this is a non-profit, multi-stakeholder coordination initiative rather than a commercial business: it brings together major companies operating in Colombia (Nestlé, Coca-Cola, Carvajal, Plastisol, Jerónimo Martins, Grupo Plastilene, Xiclo, Resiter) alongside NGO and academic partners (Acoplásticos, The Consumer Goods Forum, WWF, University of Los Andes) around shared, time-bound national targets for plastic packaging reduction and circularity. It operates as part of the global Plastics Pact network (coordinated internationally by the Ellen MacArthur Foundation), publishing a national roadmap and baseline rather than operating consumer-facing products itself. Colombia's baseline context: roughly 700,500 tonnes of plastic packaging placed on the market annually, with only about 3% currently reincorporated into new packaging.

## Measurable Impact

Baseline (as of launch): ~700,500 tonnes/year of plastic packaging placed on the Colombian market; ~3% currently reincorporated into new packaging (CEMPRE/WRAP reporting). Targets (not yet achieved, 2030 horizon): 100% problematic-plastic elimination; 100% reusable/recyclable/compostable packaging; 50% effective recycling rate; 30% recycled content in new packaging.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

The Colombia Plastics Pact is a significant national-level policy and industry-coordination initiative directly relevant to reuse and circularity at scale, bringing major multinational and local companies under shared, measurable 2030 targets that explicitly include reusable packaging alongside recyclability. Its relevance is as a systemic enabling framework rather than an operating reuse service — useful context for understanding the regulatory/industry landscape reuse operators in Colombia work within.
