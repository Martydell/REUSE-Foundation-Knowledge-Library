## Organisation Overview

E-Mart is South Korea's oldest and largest discount-store chain, founded in 1993 by Shinsegae and now a publicly listed subsidiary (KRX: 139480) operating Emart discount stores, Emart Traders wholesale-style stores, and online retail (Emart Mall/Traders Mall). While E-Mart's core business is conventional grocery/discount retail, this profile focuses on its "Eco Refill Station" pilot: a laundry-detergent and fabric-softener refill vending system launched in September 2020 at E-Mart Seongsu and Traders Anseong, developed with detergent brand SugarBubble, South Korea's Ministry of Environment, and the Korea Environment Institute, then expanded to additional stores in early 2021.

## Business Model

E-Mart's core business model is large-format discount retail: it sells groceries, household goods and general merchandise through Emart and Emart Traders store formats plus e-commerce, generating revenue via conventional retail margins. Its Eco Refill Station initiative operates as an in-store vending-machine-style dispenser: customers bring a dedicated refill container and dispense SugarBubble-brand "Natural Bubble Laundry Detergent" or "Snow Cotton Fabric Softener" directly, paying a reduced per-refill price (reported at 4,500 KRW for 3L of detergent and 3,600 KRW for 3L of fabric softener) that represented a 35-39% discount versus buying pre-packaged full-size products. The pilot launched at two locations (E-Mart Seongsu, Traders Anseong) in September 2020 as a public-private collaboration involving the Ministry of Environment and Korea Environment Institute alongside product partner SugarBubble, then expanded to additional Emart and Traders locations (Wangsimni, Eunpyeong, Jukjeon, Yeongdeungpo, Suwon, Songrim) from January 2021. The refill process took roughly 70-80 seconds per 3L dispense. Reported monthly usage exceeded 1,000 customers on average, with particular uptake among household shoppers.

## Measurable Impact

Reported average of over 1,000 customer uses per month across Eco Refill Station locations (Korean press coverage, 2021); refill pricing represented a 35-39% discount versus full-size pre-packaged products (sedaily.com/etnews.com, 2021); refill process time of approximately 70-80 seconds per 3L dispense. Figures are as reported in Korean business press at the time of the pilot's 2020-2021 expansion; no more recent independently audited data on current scale or cumulative plastic-bottle reduction was found in verified public sources.

## Technology

Vending-machine-style automated dispensing units for the Eco Refill Station, requiring a dedicated 3L refill container; no RFID/QR/app-based tracking system for the refill programme was identified in public sources.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

E-Mart's Eco Refill Station is a well-documented, verifiable example of a major mainstream retailer piloting an actual in-store refill system (not just eco-marketing), in direct partnership with government environmental bodies — giving it credibility and policy relevance beyond a single company's CSR effort. Reported usage data (1,000+ monthly users) suggests genuine consumer uptake. However, sources reviewed date to 2020-2021 and describe an expansion to roughly 8 additional stores; no confirmation of nationwide scale-up or current (2026) operating status was found, so the durability/scale of this initiative beyond its initial pilot phase remains unverified. As instructed, this profile centres on the refill-station pilot rather than E-Mart's overall retail business.
