# Almang Market

Korea's first refill station and zero-waste shop, selling unpackaged goods by weight from a Seoul storefront since June 2020 and operating a wholesale supply arm for other zero-waste retailers nationwide.

## Organisation Overview

Almang Market (알맹상점) is South Korea's first dedicated refill station, operating a Seoul retail store where customers bring their own containers to purchase cosmetics, household detergents, food ingredients (teas, coffee, spices, oils) and other goods by weight, without single-use packaging. It grew out of a 2018 grassroots community campaign in Seoul's Mangwon neighbourhood formed in response to a national waste-collection crisis, and opened as a permanent store in June 2020. The organisation also runs a B2B wholesale supply arm and maintains a national online map of zero-waste shops.

## Business Model

Almang Market generates revenue primarily through direct retail sales at its Mangwon storefront (and previously a Seoul Station branch), where customers purchase refillable products — cosmetics, detergents, dish/body soap, and dry food items such as tea, coffee, spices, olive oil and balsamic vinegar — by bringing and filling their own containers, priced by weight/volume. It supplements this with a mobile zero-waste beverage cart service, a B2B wholesale mall supplying other zero-waste retailers, pop-up shops and bulk purchasers, and corporate/event order services. The organisation frames itself as "a platform of small hearts reducing waste," positioning its commercial retail activity within a broader mission of building zero-waste culture and infrastructure in Korea, including maintaining a crowdsourced national map (via almang.net) that helps consumers locate other zero-waste and refill shops across the country — extending its influence beyond its own direct retail footprint into a broader movement-building role.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Stations (core retail model: bring-your-own-container refill of cosmetics, detergents, food items)
- Waste Reduction (unpackaged/zero-waste retail model)
- Consumer Education (in-store guidance, national zero-waste shop map, public campaigns)
- Material Recovery (in-store collection/recycling of specific hard-to-recycle materials, per program pages)
- Reusable Packaging (bring-your-own-container system)

## Products & Services

Refillable cosmetics, household detergents and dish/body soap; bulk dry food and pantry items (tea, coffee, spices, olive oil, balsamic vinegar, dried fruits, vegan snacks); plastic-free personal-care items (bamboo toothbrushes, solid toothpaste, loofah/sponge-gourd scouring pads); a mobile zero-waste beverage cart service; B2B wholesale supply for other zero-waste retailers and pop-ups; corporate and event bulk-order services.

## Customers

Individual retail consumers in Seoul seeking package-free shopping; other zero-waste shops, pop-up retailers and bulk purchasers via its wholesale arm; corporate clients for bulk/event orders.

## Partnerships

Not publicly available as named commercial partners; Almang Market is documented as connecting/supporting a broader informal network of approximately 200 zero-waste shops nationwide via its online shop map, but specific named partner organisations were not identified in verified sources.

## Measurable Impact

- 299,543 plastic containers reduced, cumulative 2020–2024 (Almang Market official site).
- 20,489 kg of waste collected for recycling, cumulative 2020–2024 (Almang Market official site).
- 300+ refillable product types offered (Almang Market official site).
- Network connecting approximately 200 zero-waste shops nationwide via its shop map (Almang Market official site).
- 75,144 plastic containers reduced in 2022 alone (Korean government policy magazine 공감/Gonggam, cited via Korea.kr).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Almang Market is a well-documented, community-rooted pioneer of the refill-station model in South Korea, with a clear origin story (2018 grassroots anti-waste campaign), a durable physical retail presence since 2020, and self-reported cumulative impact figures (nearly 300,000 containers reduced, over 20 tonnes of waste collected between 2020–2024). Its dual role as both a direct retailer and a connector/mapper of a wider ~200-shop zero-waste network in Korea gives it outsized relevance as an infrastructure and movement-building node, not just a single storefront. As a small, independently operated business its reported metrics are self-published rather than independently audited, and detailed partnership and technology information is limited, but it remains one of the clearer, best-sourced refill-station case studies available for a market (South Korea) that is otherwise thinly represented in Western-language reuse literature.

Priority: ★★★★ High

## Verification Notes

Two slightly different impact figures exist for 2022: the organisation's own cumulative 2020–2024 figure (299,543 containers reduced) and a specific single-year 2022 figure reported via Korean government media (75,144 containers reduced) — both are plausible and not necessarily contradictory (the single-year figure could be a component of the cumulative total), but they should not be conflated. The legal entity name (주식회사 알리(망원), business registration 155-81-02836) is sourced from the company's own site footer/legal notice rather than an independent corporate registry lookup, so it is rated Medium rather than High confidence. Founding is treated as 2020 (store opening) per the organisation's own framing, though the underlying community campaign began in 2018.
