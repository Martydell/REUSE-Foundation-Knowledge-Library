## Organisation Overview

Benamôr is a Portuguese cosmetics and personal-care brand founded in 1925 in Lisbon, originally producing ointments and creams from a small laboratory. It became one of Portugal's largest cosmetics producers in its early decades before declining and being revived. In 2015 the brand was acquired by a group of investors (Pierre Stark, Filipe Serzedelo, Takehide Takasa) who relaunched and modernised it. Benamôr now sells face, body and hand-care products through its own retail stores in Lisbon, e-commerce, and international distribution in an estimated 40 markets.

## Business Model

Benamôr is a direct-to-consumer and wholesale cosmetics brand selling face, body, hand-care and fragrance products, largely through its own physical stores (including a flagship at LX Factory, Lisbon), online store, and third-party retail/export channels in roughly 40 markets. Its relevant circular-economy activity is an in-store refill programme launched in October 2019 at its LX Factory location, described by Benamôr and Portuguese press as the brand's first "refill" concept store. Customers purchase a reusable 500ml recycled-aluminum bottle (initial cost approximately €1.50) for shower gels, liquid soaps and body lotions (Alantoíne, Gordíssimo, Jacarandá and Rose Amélie product lines); on return visits the bottle is refilled at a price around 10% lower than buying a new bottled product. This model reduces packaging waste for a defined range of liquid body-care products but is a small part of the brand's overall product catalogue, which remains predominantly single-use tubes and bottles.

## Products & Services

Refillable shower gels, liquid soaps, and body lotions (Alantoíne, Gordíssimo, Jacarandá, Rose Amélie lines) dispensed into reusable 500ml recycled-aluminum bottles at the LX Factory store; broader non-refill product catalogue includes face creams, hand creams, body butters, soaps and fragrances sold in conventional packaging.

## Customers

Retail consumers (B2C), primarily in Portugal via physical stores and online, with export/wholesale distribution reported in approximately 40 international markets.

## Partnerships

Benamôr participates in a packaging take-back/recycling initiative with Sociedade Ponto Verde (Portugal's national packaging-recovery compliance scheme), referenced on the company website. No other named circular-economy partners were found in available sources.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Benamôr's refill station is a genuine, verifiable reuse initiative — a reusable-packaging refill model for a defined set of liquid body-care products at one flagship store — but it is limited in scale (one confirmed location), covers only a subset of the product range, and has no publicly reported impact metrics or scaling plans beyond the original 2019 launch. The brand's primary business remains conventional single-use cosmetics packaging. This makes it a useful small case study of refill retail in the personal-care sector rather than a systemic circular-economy operator.
