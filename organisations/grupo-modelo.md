## Organisation Overview
Grupo Modelo is Mexico's largest brewer, founded in 1925 and best known internationally as the maker of Corona, Modelo Especial and Victoria. It has been a wholly owned subsidiary of AB InBev since the completion of AB InBev's acquisition in 2013. Within Mexico, Grupo Modelo operates a large-scale returnable ("retornable") glass bottle system for its core domestic beer brands, in which consumers return empty bottles to retailers for a deposit-linked discount, supported by a network of recyclers and processing plants across the country.

## Business Model
Grupo Modelo sells the large majority of its beer volume for the Mexican domestic market in returnable glass bottles that customers bring back to points of sale in exchange for new bottled beer at a lower price (commonly cited as roughly 15% cheaper than non-returnable purchases). The company runs consumer engagement campaigns — including "Cada Botella Cuenta" ("Every Bottle Counts") — and an "ecolana" mobile app rewarding consumers (with prizes such as festival tickets or delivery-service perks) for returning empties. Logistically, Grupo Modelo works with a network reported at around 80 recycling/collection partners feeding roughly nine processing plants nationally, which wash and inspect bottles for reuse or send broken/damaged glass to recycling, supporting jobs across the collection value chain. This returnable-bottle model is distinct from AB InBev's broader global recycling initiatives and is specific to the domestic Mexican beer market structure, which has historically supported high bottle-return rates due to retail deposit incentives.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment
Grupo Modelo operates what is very likely one of the largest and most mature deposit-return/reuse systems for beverage packaging in the Americas, verified by a credible independent (96%) return-rate figure, at a scale (a top-3 global beer market) few reuse programs reach. As a wholly owned AB InBev subsidiary its returnable-bottle model reflects a market-specific retail structure rather than a distinct reuse "innovation," but the scale and verified performance make it a benchmark case study for deposit-return systems. Priority rating: ★★★★ High.
