## Organisation Overview

La Nature is a Mexico City retailer, opened in 2016, dedicated to selling healthy, locally sourced farm products — primarily dairy — in returnable glass containers or in bulk, explicitly aiming to value Mexican small-scale producers while reducing the city's carbon and packaging footprint. Its signature products are pasture-raised dairy items: free-range eggs from Querétaro, cheeses from Zacatlán de las Manzanas, fresh milk and cream from Ajusco, milk and goat cheese from Chimalhuacán, butter, ghee, and house-made yogurt and compotes. The store operates a deposit-return system for glass containers and uses biodegradable packaging (for items like cheese) where reusable containers are impractical, positioning itself within Mexico's "circular economy" and zero-waste retail movement.

## Business Model

La Nature runs a deposit-return retail model: customers pay a premium on their first purchase to cover the cost of a reusable glass container (reported deposit refund of 10–15 Mexican pesos depending on container size), and on return of the empty container receive that deposit back or exchange it for a freshly filled replacement, paying only for the product itself thereafter. The store sources directly from small Mexican farms and producers rather than requiring formal organic certification, using direct relationships and store-level verification of quality and ingredients instead — a deliberate choice framed as prioritising local producer support over the cost of certification. Producers now deliver goods to the store in pre-sterilised containers for on-site packaging, integrating reuse into the supply chain itself rather than only at the point of retail sale. Complementary waste-reduction tactics include bulk/unpackaged sales (customers bringing their own containers) and biodegradable packaging for products like cheese that are not suited to the glass-jar returnable format.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Deposit Return — glass container deposit-refund system for dairy and pantry products
- Reusable Packaging — returnable glass jars/bottles reused across multiple purchase cycles, including pre-sterilised container supply from producers
- Refill Systems — bulk/unpackaged sales for customers bringing their own containers
- Waste Reduction — biodegradable packaging alternatives (e.g., cheese wrapping) where reusable glass is not practical
- Consumer Education — messaging around circular economy, local sourcing and reduced-waste consumption

Farm-direct dairy and pantry goods sold in returnable glass containers or in bulk: eggs, artisanal cheeses, fresh milk and cream, goat milk/cheese, butter, ghee, yogurt (plain and flavoured with chai, maple, honey), fruit compotes, sauces and preserves.
