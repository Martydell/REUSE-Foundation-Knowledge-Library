## Organisation Overview

Circolution is a German startup offering "packaging-as-a-service": reusable stainless-steel packaging that grocery and FMCG brands rent instead of purchasing single-use containers, with circolution handling collection, cleaning, tracking, and redistribution of the containers. Founded by Max Bannasch, Kirils Jegorovs, and Alessandro Marchiaro, the company is small (reported 1-10 employees) but has secured a pilot partnership with Nestlé and recognition from Amcor's packaging innovation programme.

## Business Model

Circolution's core offering is a rental model for reusable stainless-steel food packaging: rather than brands buying and managing their own reusable containers, they rent capacity from circolution, who handle the full reverse-logistics loop — collection from retail points, industrial cleaning, quality inspection, and redistribution back into the supply chain, with digital tracking of container movement. This shifts reusable packaging from a capital cost to an operating expense for brand customers, an approach known as "packaging-as-a-service." The company has piloted this model with Nestlé for at least one product line and was recognised as a winner of Amcor's "Lift-Off" innovation initiative, which highlighted its packaging-as-a-service approach.
