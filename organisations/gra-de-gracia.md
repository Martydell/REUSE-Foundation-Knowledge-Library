## Organisation Overview

Gra de Gràcia is a zero-waste bulk grocer founded in 2012 in Barcelona's Gràcia district, among the earlier "botigues a granel" (bulk shops) in the city. Co-founded by Sara Ingelmo and Jordi Escoriza, it began as a single store on Carrer Puigmartí and expanded to three Barcelona locations (Puigmartí, Bonanova, and Cigne) as the concept proved popular. The shop sells roughly 600 organic, natural, and locally-sourced dry and prepared goods — rice, pasta, legumes, spices, oils, coffee, chocolate, and more — allowing customers to buy only the quantity they need without single-use plastic packaging.

## Business Model

Gra de Gràcia operates a direct-to-consumer bulk-retail model across three physical stores in Barcelona plus an online shop with home delivery (free above €60). Customers bring their own reusable containers and bags, or use the shop's paper bags, starch-based bags, or corn-starch tubs for moisture-sensitive products such as nut butters, honey, and olives. The company incentivises reuse directly at the point of sale: customers who bring their own containers or reuse bags receive a 3% discount on their total purchase. The product range spans roughly 600 SKUs of organic, natural, and "zero kilometre" (locally sourced) dry goods, oils, prepared/frozen meals, and dietary supplements. Revenue comes from per-weight retail sales rather than any subscription or platform-fee structure; the business has remained an independent, small-team operation (described on its own site as "a young company formed by a small human team") rather than franchising or scaling nationally.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems (bulk dispensing of dry goods, oils, and other groceries with customer-supplied containers)
- Waste Reduction (elimination of single-use plastic packaging across ~600 product lines)
- Consumer Education (promotion of "buy only what you need" conscious consumption)
- Reusable Packaging (reusable customer containers/bags incentivised via discount)

## Products & Services

Bulk organic and natural groceries including rice, pasta, cereals, legumes, spices and spice blends, coffee, chocolate, dried fruit, seeds, flour, oils, condiments, honey, dietary supplements, teas/infusions, and prepared/frozen meals; three physical stores in Barcelona (Puigmartí, Bonanova, Cigne) plus an online store with home delivery.

## Customers

Individual consumers in Barcelona (primarily the Gràcia and Bonanova neighbourhoods) shopping in-store, plus online customers across the wider delivery area eligible for free shipping over €60.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Gra de Gràcia is a genuine, long-running (14-year) example of the package-free bulk-grocery model, predating much of the "zero waste store" wave that followed in the later 2010s. Its three physical Barcelona locations and ~600-product range demonstrate real commercial durability rather than a short-lived pilot. Its main limitations from a research-standard perspective are a lack of any third-party impact reporting, partnership disclosures, or press beyond local city-guide and design-award coverage — typical for a small independent retailer of its type, but limiting for benchmarking purposes.
