## Organisation Overview

Incrediballs is a New Zealand consumer-goods startup founded by Brianne West (previously founder of plastic-free beauty brand Ethique) after she stepped down as Ethique's CEO in 2023. It makes small, concentrated, dissolvable "balls" of flavouring that turn a glass of tap or sparkling water into a flavoured drink, marketed as the "world's first plastic-free, waste-free drink." The product is designed to eliminate the need for single-use plastic beverage bottles and to cut the weight/emissions cost of shipping bottled liquid, positioning itself against New Zealand's roughly 2 billion single-use drink containers discarded annually.

## Business Model

Incrediballs sells small, effervescent, concentrated flavour tablets/balls direct-to-consumer (and via retail), designed to be dropped into a glass or bottle of water that the customer already has, rather than requiring any new bottle or packaging at time of consumption. By concentrating the flavouring and removing the water content from the product itself, Incrediballs reduces packaging size, product weight, and transport emissions compared to shipping pre-bottled drinks. Revenue is generated through direct online sales and retail distribution of the tablets, packaged in small, minimal (reportedly plastic-free) containers rather than single-serve bottles. This is fundamentally a source-reduction/product-substitution model — eliminating the need for a disposable drink container altogether — rather than a system for reusing or pooling existing packaging.
