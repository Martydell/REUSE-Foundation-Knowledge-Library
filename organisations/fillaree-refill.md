## Organisation Overview

Fillaree is a Durham, North Carolina-based maker of natural, vegan soaps and household cleaning products, founded in 2014 by Alyssa Cherry. It began as a home-kitchen operation making nontoxic refillable soap for the founder's family and grew into a small manufacturing operation with a retail storefront (opened around three years after founding). Fillaree positions itself as an early mover in circular-economy personal care, built around a "wash, sanitize, refill" reuse loop for its liquid soap and cleaning spray bottles, run by a small, mostly women-staffed manufacturing team.

## Business Model

Fillaree sells liquid soap, plant-based bar soap, and a household cleaning spray ("Clean Dream") produced at its Durham factory. Its core reuse mechanism is a refill subscription: customers receive a 32-ounce vessel of liquid soap, use it, and mail the empty container back in a prepaid envelope; Fillaree washes, sanitizes, and refills the same vessel before shipping it to its next customer, closing the packaging loop rather than using single-use bottles. The company also operates roughly 40 physical refill stations across the U.S. (concentrated in North Carolina's Triangle area) where customers can refill containers in person, plus direct e-commerce sales of packaged and refillable products. Fillaree has also used crowdfunding (a Kickstarter campaign for "Refillable Condensed Soap" that exceeded its $18,000 goal) to fund new refillable product lines. Revenue comes from direct-to-consumer subscriptions, one-time online orders, and in-store/refill-station sales; there is no evidence of wholesale, licensing, or franchise activity.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Refill Systems (mail-back "wash, sanitize, refill" bottle loop; ~40 in-person refill stations)
- Reusable Packaging (durable soap/cleaner vessels designed for 5-10 years of reuse)
- Reverse Logistics (prepaid return envelopes for used containers)
- Waste Reduction (near-zero-waste factory operations)
- Consumer Education (messaging around plastic-bottle avoidance)

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Fillaree is a small but genuinely pioneering reuse business, having built a closed-loop "wash, sanitize, refill" bottle system for liquid soap since 2014 — years before refill subscriptions became a common sustainability trend. Its scale is modest (a single factory, ~400 subscribers, ~40 refill stations, no disclosed partnerships), and impact figures are self-reported and unaudited, limiting confidence in quantitative claims. Still, its long operating history, functioning reverse-logistics loop, and durable-container design make it a useful small-scale case study for refill-subscription models in personal care, though it is not a scalable infrastructure provider comparable to larger reuse networks.
