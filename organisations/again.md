## Organisation Overview

Again (legal entity Acet Circular Solutions Ltd) is a UK B2B reusable-packaging infrastructure company founded in 2021 by CEO Matt Kennedy. It built a decentralised network of automated "CleanCell" facilities — compact, shipping-container-sized units — that collect, sort, de-label, clean, inspect and palletise returned glass, PET and PP packaging so FMCG and drinks brands can reuse containers at a cost comparable to single-use packaging. The company positioned itself as infrastructure-layer plumbing for the reuse economy rather than a consumer-facing brand. As of 2026, Companies House records show the company in liquidation, a material development not reflected in the seed data.

## Business Model

Again's core offering was a B2B "reuse-as-a-service" model: rather than brands building their own washing and logistics capability, Again's CleanCell units — developed with food-safety body Campden BRI — handled the full reconditioning cycle (collection, sorting, de-labelling, cleaning, drying, visual inspection, palletising and redistribution to filling lines) at a reported price of roughly £0.12 (US$0.15) per unit with no upfront cost to brand partners. Its "Again Tag" tracking system scanned and sorted packaging by brand, size and type while monitoring reuse counts, return rates, inventory and water/CO2e savings. CleanCells were designed to be portable and sited at retailer distribution centres, waste-management facilities or brand production lines, giving the company a decentralised, franchise-like scaling model. It raised a £2.55m pre-seed round in April 2022 (led by Eka Ventures) and was a partial winner of the £1.4m "Bring It Back Fund" (co-created by Starbucks and Hubbub). The 2026 liquidation filing indicates the model did not reach sustainable scale — consistent with wider sector commentary (see Challenges).

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging (glass, rPET, rPP bottle/container reconditioning)
- Reverse Logistics (CleanCell collection, sorting, cleaning, redistribution network)
- Take-back (bottle return schemes with retail/delivery partners)
- Digital Tracking ("Again Tag" — scans packaging by brand/size/type, tracks reuse counts and return rates)
- Industrial Reuse (B2B infrastructure-as-a-service for brand owners)

## Products & Services

CleanCell automated micro-cleaning facilities (container-based, ~500,000-units/month capacity per unit); Again Tag tracking technology; end-to-end reverse-logistics service (collection, cleaning, quality inspection, redistribution) offered to FMCG and drinks brands on a per-unit fee basis.

## Customers

FMCG and drinks brands, distribution networks, and hospitality/retail chains seeking to introduce reusable packaging without building their own cleaning/logistics infrastructure.

## Measurable Impact

- CleanCell processed approximately 100,000 units in 2022 (company-reported, via plasticfree.com feature).
- Company claims 60% lower CO2e emissions versus recycling, 90% less energy and 76% less water use per CleanCell cycle (self-reported).
- Average reported reuse rate of 6.8 times per item (self-reported).
- Two CleanCells operational as of 2022 (self-reported).
- No independently audited impact figures were found in verified sources.

## Technology used

Automated CleanCell reconditioning facilities (container-based); "Again Tag" packaging identification and tracking system (scans brand/size/type, monitors return rates, water use and CO2e savings).

## Challenges

Sector-wide commentary (Packaging Europe, 2026) reports that "at least half a dozen" reusable-packaging companies exited the market in the year to 2026, citing venture-capital withdrawal from the category and the high cost of scaling reuse beyond niche use-cases into mainstream grocery/to-go formats. While this article did not name Again specifically, it documents the broader financial pressure facing companies with Again's business model, consistent with the company's own 2026 liquidation filing at Companies House.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Again represented one of the more credible attempts at B2B reuse infrastructure in the UK — its CleanCell model and named brand partnerships (Diageo brands, Milk & More, Tom Parker Creamery, Camden Town Brewery) were genuine and well-documented through 2022–2024. However, the verified 2026 Companies House record showing the parent entity Acet Circular Solutions Ltd in liquidation, with a winding-up petition and overdue statutory filings, is a critical and disqualifying update: this is no longer an "Active" trading company in the sense the seed data described. The Foundation should document Again as a historically significant case study in reuse infrastructure — including as a cautionary example of the sector's financial fragility — but should not treat it as an ongoing active operator without further confirmation of its current trading status.

Priority: ★★★ Medium (high historical/case-study relevance; downgraded from seed data's implied "active operator" status pending confirmation of liquidation outcome)
