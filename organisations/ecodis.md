## Organisation Overview

Ecodis is a French distributor and multi-brand manufacturer based in Saint-Nolff, Brittany, founded in 2000 as a pioneer in designing and distributing ecological products to organic and specialty shops. The company supplies roughly 2,000 products across France and Europe, manufactured in France or Western Europe using natural mineral or plant-based materials and maximised organic ingredients, including refillable household cleaning capsules and personal-care items. It operates several in-house brands, including "La droguerie éco," Ah! Table!, Anaé, Venga, Kimayo, and Grand Beau, and reported a €5 million investment alongside hiring, according to regional business press.

## Business Model

Ecodis operates primarily as a B2B distributor, supplying independent organic shops, health-food stores, and specialty retailers across France and Europe with ecological household and personal-care products rather than selling directly to end consumers at scale. Its portfolio spans multiple owned brands covering different categories — general ecological household cleaners (vinegar- and baking-soda-based formulas), refillable capsules for cleaning products, kitchen/baking paper, and personal-care lines — allowing retail partners to stock a broad ecological assortment from a single supplier relationship. The company differentiates on certification and environmental commitment: it states that all products carry recognised, independently audited ecological labels, and that it returns 0.5% of annual revenue to ecological projects. A reported €5 million investment and associated hiring (per Le Journal des Entreprises) indicates recent business expansion.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ecodis is a long-established (25+ years), reasonably substantial French ecological products distributor with a genuine refill-format product line and independent certification claims, plus corroborating regional business-press coverage of a significant recent investment. However, its core function is B2B distribution/wholesale of a broad ecological product catalogue rather than a focused reuse or refill infrastructure system — refill capsules are one line among many conventional (single-use) ecological products. Useful as a supply-chain-level example of the ecological products sector but of secondary relevance to core reuse/refill system research.
