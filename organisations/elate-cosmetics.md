## Organisation Overview

Elate Cosmetics is a Canadian vegan and cruelty-free cosmetics brand founded in 2014 by Melodie Reynolds, a 20-year beauty-industry veteran, with a stated mission to redefine "low-waste" beauty and reject shame-based marketing common in the cosmetics industry. The brand is built around bamboo-based, refillable makeup compacts and packaging designed to be reusable, recyclable or plantable, with a free take-back program allowing customers to return empty packaging to the company for refilling or proper recycling rather than disposal.

## Business Model

Elate sells direct-to-consumer (and through select retail/online stockists such as EarthHero and Camomile Beauty) a range of vegan, cruelty-free makeup — foundations, eyeshadows, lip products and tools — packaged predominantly in FSC-certified bamboo compacts designed for refill rather than single-use disposal. The company states that over 75% of its packaging is reusable, recyclable or plantable, and it operates a no-cost packaging take-back scheme: customers mail back empty compacts/containers, which Elate either refills for resale/reuse or recycles through appropriate channels, reducing the volume of cosmetics packaging sent to landfill by conventional retail. Revenue is generated through direct product sales (initial compact purchase plus repeat refill purchases) and wholesale distribution to sustainability-focused beauty retailers; the company has articulated a public goal of becoming among the world's most sustainable cosmetics companies (per Forbes, 2021).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Elate Cosmetics is a credible, well-documented example of reusable-packaging design applied to color cosmetics, a category where reuse models are less common than in food/household refill. Its free take-back and refill program, bamboo compact design, and public sustainability commitments (including Forbes coverage of its ambitions) make it a useful case study, though independently verified impact metrics are lacking and the take-back program's actual uptake/volume is not disclosed.
