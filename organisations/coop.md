## Organisation Overview

Coop is one of Switzerland's two largest retail groups, structured as a cooperative with roots tracing to Switzerland's first consumer cooperative (founded 1864 in Schwanden, canton of Glarus) and the 1890 formation of the Verband Schweizerischer Konsumvereine (Federation of Swiss Consumer Cooperatives), the direct predecessor of today's Coop. With 90,000+ employees, Coop operates bulk-goods dispensers in some stores for products like nuts, grains, and pulses, encouraging customers to bring reusable bags or jars rather than buying pre-packaged equivalents.

## Business Model

As a large cooperative retailer, Coop's core business is conventional grocery and general-merchandise retail across Switzerland. Its reuse-relevant activity is a secondary, in-store feature: bulk dispensers for dry goods (nuts, grains, pulses) that let customers fill their own reusable containers rather than buying pre-packaged quantities, reducing single-use packaging for those specific product categories. As a cooperative, Coop is structured around member ownership rather than shareholder capital, though its retail operations function at conventional supermarket scale.
