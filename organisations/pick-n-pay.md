## Organisation Overview

Pick n Pay is one of South Africa's largest retail grocery chains, founded in 1967 and listed on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange (JSE: PIK) the following year. It operates roughly 2,300 corporate and franchised stores and has piloted zero-waste supermarket concepts and reverse vending machines for recyclables.

## Business Model

Pick n Pay operates corporate and franchised supermarkets across South Africa and neighboring countries, generating revenue through grocery and general merchandise retail. Its sustainability initiatives include a net-zero-waste pilot store (Stellenbosch Square) targeting a 50% cut in food waste by 2030, food donation partnerships, and in-store reverse vending machines (with provider Imagined Earth) that use AI to sort recyclables dropped off by customers.
