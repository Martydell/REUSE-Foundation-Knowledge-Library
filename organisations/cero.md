## Organisation Overview

Cero (branded "Cero Tucson") was a vegan, zero-waste retail store in Tucson, Arizona, describing itself as the city's first dedicated zero-waste shop. It began as a pop-up in May 2019, co-founded by Val Timin and Nelene Deguzman, and grew into a permanent retail and events space, later located along Tucson's Sunshine Mile (Broadway Boulevard) at 160 S. Avenida del Convento. The store centered on a refill bar for household and personal-care staples alongside local, handmade, and vintage goods. As of the current website, Cero has closed after roughly seven years of operation; the business notes closure directly on its homepage.

## Business Model

Cero operated as a bricks-and-mortar zero-waste general store built around a self-serve refill bar: customers brought their own containers to fill up on hand soap, dish soap, laundry soap, household cleaners, and hair and body products, paying by weight/volume rather than buying pre-packaged goods. Alongside the refill offering, the store sold local gifts, handmade items, and vintage/secondhand goods, positioning itself as a lifestyle retailer for low-waste living rather than a pure grocery/bulk-food operation. The business also hosted community events at its retail space. Founded by two Tucson-based environmentalists who had independently been drawn to the "trash-free" retail concept before it was widespread in the US market, Cero relied on direct retail sales and community engagement rather than a subscription or B2B model. The store relocated at least once during its operating history (moving in fall 2022) before ultimately closing.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

Cero was a small, community-scale zero-waste retail store illustrating the refill-store model common in the US zero-waste movement, but it has since closed, limiting its ongoing relevance as a case study of an active reuse system. Useful primarily as a historical example of a first-mover independent zero-waste retailer and the operational challenges (relocation, eventual closure) such small businesses face.
