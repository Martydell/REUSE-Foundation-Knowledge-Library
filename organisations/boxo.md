## Organisation Overview

BOXO is a Dutch start-up based in Leeuwarden that provides reusable shipping packaging ("Boxobag") for online retailers as an alternative to single-use cardboard boxes and plastic mailers. The company's packaging was originally developed from discarded big bags used by dairy producer FrieslandCampina, repurposing industrial textile waste into a durable, foldable, multi-use shipping bag. BOXO operates a deposit-based return system: consumers receive a €3.95 deposit refund via digital payment (Tikkie) or in-store when they return a used Boxobag to a network of drop-off points, after which the bags are cleaned and redistributed to participating webshops for reuse.

## Business Model

BOXO's model centres on a deposit-return logistics network for reusable e-commerce shipping bags. Participating online retailers ship customer orders in Boxobags instead of single-use cardboard or plastic packaging; the bags are collected at physical drop-off points (including a rollout in Maastricht), and the €3.95 deposit is refunded digitally or at checkout. Because the bags fold flat and can be stacked (reportedly up to ten folded bags fitting inside one bag), the company designed the system for efficient, low-volume collection logistics comparable to returning a beer crate. BOXO reports the bags can be reused up to roughly 500 times, and cites third-party research (Fashion for Good) claiming reusable shipping packaging can cut CO2 emissions by up to 90% relative to recycled cardboard boxes. The company also offers a distinct "BOXO Return" service aimed at business clients for return logistics.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Reusable Packaging (Boxobag, made from repurposed industrial big bags, reusable up to ~500 times)
- Deposit Return (€3.95 deposit refunded via Tikkie or in-store on return)
- Reverse Logistics (network of physical drop-off/collection points)
- Material Recovery (original bag material sourced from discarded FrieslandCampina dairy big bags)

No repair, rental, or sharing activity was identified; the company's core offering is the reusable-bag deposit system itself.

## Products & Services

Boxobag reusable shipping packaging for e-commerce orders; a deposit-refund collection network; "BOXO Return" business return-logistics service for participating online retailers.

## Customers

Participating Dutch online retailers/webshops and their end consumers, with drop-off points reported in multiple Dutch cities including Maastricht.

- Reusability: bags reported reusable up to approximately 500 times (company website).
- Emissions claim: reusable shipping packaging cited as achieving up to 90% lower CO2 emissions than recycled cardboard boxes, attributed to Fashion for Good research (company website) — the underlying Fashion for Good study itself was not independently reviewed in this research.
- Context figure: company website states more than 300 million single-use shipping boxes and bags become waste annually in the Netherlands (unattributed figure on company site).

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

BOXO is a small but distinctive Dutch reusable-packaging start-up notable for its circular sourcing story (repurposing FrieslandCampina's discarded industrial big bags into consumer shipping packaging) and its deposit-based return-and-reuse logistics model. Its verified public documentation is moderate — Dutch trade and local press (Duurzaam Ondernemen, RTV Maastricht, Vakblad Voedingsindustrie) corroborate the core model, but founding year, ownership structure, and named retail partners could not be confirmed from public sources. It is a relevant small-scale case study for reusable e-commerce packaging with an interesting industrial-upcycling origin story.
