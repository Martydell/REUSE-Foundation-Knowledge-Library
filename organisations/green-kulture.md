## Organisation Overview

Green Kulture is a Singapore-developed and -manufactured natural household cleaning brand built around proprietary "Multi-Enzyme Technology" derived from fruit and vegetable enzymes rather than harsh synthetic chemicals. According to the company, the brand was created by a founder (identified publicly only as "Roy") motivated by concerns over the chemical content of household products after becoming a father. Its core sustainability practice is sourcing unsold, disposal-bound fruit and vegetables from wholesalers as feedstock for its enzyme extraction process, alongside offering a 5-litre bulk refill bottle option for its cleaning products to reduce single-use packaging.

## Business Model

Green Kulture manufactures a line of natural, enzyme-based household cleaners (dishwashing liquid, hand soap, multi-purpose cleaner, floor cleaner, laundry liquid, delicate/lingerie wash) formulated using a proprietary "Multi-Enzyme Technology" that the company states is derived by collecting unsold fruits and vegetables destined for disposal from wholesalers, then sorting and processing them for enzyme extraction — combining a waste-valorisation input with a chemical-free product positioning. Products are sold in standard retail sizes (250ml–800ml) through the company's own e-commerce site and via retail partners including NTUC FairPrice (Singapore's dominant supermarket chain) and Lazada, as well as smaller platforms like The Green Collective SG and The Social Space. To reduce single-use packaging, Green Kulture also offers a 5-litre bulk refill pack that customers can use to top up existing bottles rather than purchasing new ones each time, with free delivery above a S$30 order threshold.
