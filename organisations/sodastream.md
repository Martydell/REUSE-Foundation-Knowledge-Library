## Organisation Overview

SodaStream makes home carbonation machines and reusable CO2 cylinders that let consumers make sparkling water and flavoured drinks at home instead of buying single-use bottled/canned beverages. The brand's roots trace to 1903, when Guy Hugh Gilbey of London gin distillers W & A Gilbey Ltd developed an early home carbonation apparatus; the business passed through Reckitt & Colman, a 1974 management buyout, and Cadbury Schweppes (1985) before being acquired in 1998 by Soda-Club, an Israeli company founded in 1991 by Peter Wiseburgh — the entity that became today's SodaStream International Ltd, headquartered in Kfar Saba, Israel. PepsiCo acquired SodaStream in August 2018 for US$3.2 billion; it now operates as a PepsiCo subsidiary sold in over 40 markets.

## Business Model

SodaStream sells a B2C razor-and-blades model: consumers buy a home carbonation machine and reusable CO2 cylinders, then return empty cylinders for exchange (via SodaStream's own retail/online exchange program or third-party retail exchange points) rather than buying new ones — each returned cylinder is inspected, sterilized and refilled through what the company calls its "Triple Quality Control" process before re-entering circulation. This cylinder-exchange loop is the company's core reuse mechanism and displaces both single-use beverage packaging (bottles/cans) and single-use gas canisters. The company also sells reusable bottles and flavour concentrates. Since the 2018 PepsiCo acquisition, SodaStream has been positioned within PepsiCo's portfolio partly as a sustainability/reuse showcase, with public commitments around renewable energy sourcing for production sites and recycled-content bottles.

## REUSE Foundation Assessment

SodaStream is one of the largest-scale, most consumer-visible examples of a deposit-return/reuse model in the world — its CO2 cylinder exchange program is a genuine, decades-old reuse loop operating at industrial scale across 40+ countries, directly displacing single-use beverage packaging. Its acquisition by PepsiCo gives it exceptional distribution reach but also means its sustainability narrative is corporate-controlled and its impact figures (e.g. "5.5 billion bottles") are self-reported without independent audit. On founding year: the seed data's "1903" is defensible as the brand's historical origin but is potentially misleading as a "company founded" date, since the entity that is today's Israel-headquartered SodaStream International only came into being via Soda-Club's 1991 founding and 1998 acquisition of the SodaStream brand — this nuance should be preserved in the tracker rather than collapsed to a single year.
