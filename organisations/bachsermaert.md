## Organisation Overview

BachserMärt is a Swiss grocery retailer that began in 2008 when Patrick Honauer took over and refurbished a village shop in Bachs, a rural community roughly 20km north of Zurich, aiming to secure local food supply and support organic and local farmers. The concept expanded to urban Zurich in 2009 with a second shop in Albisrieden, and the company now operates five shops plus a warehouse around Zurich, including its largest location at Kalkbreite (opened 2014), which is known for its wide selection of unpackaged bulk goods. BachserMärt sources from local farmers, cheesemakers, bakers and small-scale producers, and combines conventionally packaged goods with an unpackaged/bulk refill offering.

## Business Model

BachserMärt is a multi-location independent grocery retailer generating revenue through in-store sales across its five Zurich-region shops, sourcing directly from local and organic producers (farmers, cheesemakers, bakers, small manufacturers). Its refill system lets customers bring or buy glass jars/containers and fill them with bulk dry goods — grains, nuts, muesli and similar products — dispensed from bulk containers, reducing per-unit packaging versus pre-packaged equivalents; the company describes this as combining "the advantages of packaged and unpackaged food." Refilling of glass containers for some products takes place centrally at the company's Zurich warehouse rather than exclusively in-store. The business model blends a conventional neighbourhood-grocery format with the zero-waste/bulk-buying trend, differentiating itself on local sourcing and packaging reduction rather than being a pure zero-waste specialist retailer.
