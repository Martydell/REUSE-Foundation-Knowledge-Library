## Organisation Overview

rhinopaq develops and operates reusable shipping and transport packaging for B2B logistics, offering polypropylene boxes (partly recycled material) as an alternative to single-use cardboard. Founded in 2021 in Essen, Germany, it serves manufacturers, rental services, and logistics operators with a closed-loop collect-clean-redistribute system.

## Business Model

rhinopaq offers two commercial models: "Pay-per-Use Collect," where rhinopaq owns, tracks, collects, cleans and redistributes reusable boxes for a per-use fee, and "Pay-per-Box," where customers purchase the reusable boxes outright. Containers are reused at least 20 times and are tracked via unique container IDs with ERP integration. The company also highlights that its reusable system can exempt German customers from certain packaging-law (VerpackG) producer-responsibility obligations.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Packaging-as-a-Service (pay-per-use and pay-per-box reusable box models)
- Reusable Packaging (shipping and transport containers)
- Reverse Logistics (collection, cleaning, and redistribution network)
- Digital Tracking (unique container IDs, ERP integration)

## Products & Services

Reusable polypropylene shipping boxes (for samples, spare parts, rental items) and transport containers (for palletised goods and intralogistics); collection, cleaning, and redistribution logistics; ERP-integrated tracking.

## Customers

Named client: Riese & Müller GmbH (bicycle components); broader target base of manufacturers, rental services, and logistics operators in Germany and neighbouring markets.

## Partnerships

EU Circular Economy Platform (recognised good practice); goCircular Pass certification; Theodor Fliedner Foundation (container refurbishment, social employment); Circular Valley, FUTURY, and greentech.ruhr innovation networks.

## Measurable Impact

Reports up to 95% waste reduction and up to 40% shipping cost savings per 20-use cycle; states over 110,000 single-use packages eliminated to date.
