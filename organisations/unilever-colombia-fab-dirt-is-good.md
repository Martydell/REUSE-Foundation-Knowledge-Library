## Organisation Overview
FAB is one of Colombia's most established laundry detergent brands, historically owned by Unilever. Unilever introduced sustainable-packaging measures for FAB, including bottles made partly from recycled plastic, and separately piloted mobile refill stations for detergents (via an Algramo partnership) in Latin American markets. In January 2026, Unilever sold its Colombia/Ecuador home-care portfolio, including FAB, to Peruvian conglomerate Alicorp.

## Business Model
Historically, mass-market detergent retail through Colombian supermarkets, supplemented by Unilever's broader Latin American refill trials (e.g., Algramo-partnered mobile refill stations dispensing detergent into RFID-tagged reusable containers, piloted regionally). Following the 2026 sale, FAB's ongoing packaging/reuse strategy is now directed by Alicorp rather than Unilever.

## Products & Services
FAB powder and liquid laundry detergent; recycled-content plastic bottles; regional refill-station trials for detergent products (via Unilever-Algramo partnership model).

## Customers
Colombian retail consumers purchasing FAB detergent through supermarkets and pharmacies.

## Partnerships
Algramo (mobile refill-station technology partner for Unilever detergent brands in Latin America, including OMO/Quix pilots); Alicorp (acquirer of the FAB brand and Colombia/Ecuador home-care business as of January 2026).

## Measurable Impact
FAB's redesigned packaging was reported to avoid over 78 tons of virgin plastic annually, using 6.5 tons of recycled material monthly in bottle production; specific refill-station volumes for FAB itself were not confirmed in this research pass.

Relevant as a case of a legacy detergent brand undergoing both packaging-material recovery efforts and regional refill piloting, though the January 2026 sale to Alicorp means future reuse strategy for this brand is no longer under Unilever's direct control — flag for ownership follow-up.
