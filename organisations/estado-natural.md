## Organisation Overview

Estado Natural is a Mexican bulk-grocery retail chain that opened its first store in Roma Norte, Mexico City, in October 2017, selling nuts, grains, cereals, flours, superfoods, dried fruit, spices, and personal-care/household-cleaning products entirely in bulk ("100% a granel"). The company has since expanded to multiple neighbourhoods within Mexico City (Condesa, Del Valle Centro, Polanco, Álvaro Obregón, and others) as well as locations in Puebla and Querétaro, positioning itself around affordable, healthy food and reduced food/packaging waste.

## Business Model

Estado Natural operates bricks-and-mortar bulk-grocery stores where customers buy exactly the quantity of product they need from bulk bins/dispensers rather than pre-packaged units, reducing both food waste (by allowing precise portioning) and packaging waste (by enabling reusable-container refills). The company states its mission is to offer a healthy, affordable food option while fostering a culture of minimal waste, both of food discarded at home and of packaging that would otherwise go to landfill. Revenue is generated through direct retail sales across its multi-location store network in central Mexico, with home delivery offered in at least some markets to extend reach beyond walk-in customers. As a multi-city chain (rather than single shop), Estado Natural represents one of the more scaled bulk-retail operators in the Mexican market.

Estado Natural is a genuine multi-city bulk-retail chain operating since 2017, demonstrating that package-free grocery retail can scale beyond a single flagship store in a major Latin American market. However, public information is limited to the company's own website and lifestyle/tourism press coverage, with no independently verified impact metrics (packaging avoided, volume sold, customer counts) currently available.
