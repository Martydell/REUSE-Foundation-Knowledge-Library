## Organisation Overview

La BOB (Bibliothèque d'Objets de la Broye — "Broye Object Library") is a Swiss non-profit association operating a network of "object library" locations across the Broye region, in Avenches, Estavayer and Payerne, with a further location planned in Moudon. Modelled on a traditional book library but for physical objects, La BOB lets members borrow rather than buy over 700 items spanning cooking, celebrations/parties, gardening, DIY/repair, health and recreation categories. The organisation is run entirely by volunteers and is partly funded by Loterie Romande, a regional lottery that supports Swiss non-profit and cultural causes. It uses the myTurn platform (labob.myturn.com) for its online catalogue and lending management.

## Business Model

La BOB operates a community tool/object-lending library model funded through a mix of membership fees and philanthropic/lottery support rather than commercial retail. Members can access the collection through pay-per-item rental for occasional borrowing or annual subscriptions offered in tiers for individuals, businesses and associations, granting broader borrowing access. The object collection is built through a combination of public/member donations and purchases funded by grants from Loterie Romande, and grows continuously ("s'étoffe tous les jours"). Day-to-day operations — staffing library hours, managing the catalogue, and processing loans — are run entirely by volunteers rather than paid staff, keeping overhead low and membership costs affordable. Online inventory browsing and loan management are handled through the third-party myTurn lending-library software platform, which the organisation uses across all four Broye locations.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Sharing — community lending of tools, kitchen equipment, party/event items, gardening and DIY equipment in place of individual ownership
- Rental — pay-per-item and subscription-based access models for borrowing objects
- Waste Reduction — reducing the need for one-off individual purchases of rarely-used items
- Consumer Education — promotion of "borrow instead of buy" consumption alongside community/human-connection goals

## Products & Services

Lending of 700+ objects across six categories (cooking, celebrations, gardening, DIY/repair, health, leisure/recreation) via pay-per-item rental or individual/business/association subscriptions, accessed through an online catalogue (myTurn platform) and four physical locations in the Broye region.

## Customers

Individual residents, households, and reportedly businesses and associations in the Broye region (Avenches, Estavayer, Payerne, with Moudon planned) who take out subscriptions or per-item rentals.

La BOB is a genuine, verifiable community sharing-economy organisation directly aligned with the REUSE Foundation's interest in object libraries and sharing models, using an established digital lending platform (myTurn) across a small multi-site regional network. Its scale is modest (a single Swiss region, volunteer-run) and key facts — founding year, membership numbers, loan volumes — are not publicly documented, limiting the depth of verifiable impact assessment.
