## Organisation Overview

Salubata is a Nigerian-founded footwear company that designs and manufactures modular sneakers with interchangeable, patented components, made from recycled ocean plastic (PET). Founded by environmental scientist Fela Buyi Akinse alongside Yewande Akinse, each pair reportedly repurposes around 25 plastic bottles while using over 70% less manufacturing energy than conventional footwear.

## Business Model

Salubata sells direct-to-consumer modular sneakers designed for longevity: interchangeable components let customers replace worn parts rather than discard the whole shoe, extending product life versus fast-fashion footwear. Manufacturing is carried out ethically in Portugal and Italy. The company donates 5% of profits to programmes empowering children and women in underprivileged communities, and has grown into a global brand reported to have reached roughly $3 million in sales.

## Circular Economy Activities

- Material Recovery (ocean plastic/PET collected and converted into shoe material)
- Industrial Reuse (patented plastic-to-footwear manufacturing technology)
- Waste Reduction (~25 PET bottles diverted per pair; modular design reduces discard)
- Repair (interchangeable components allow part replacement instead of full shoe disposal)
