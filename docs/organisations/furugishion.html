Organisation Overview

Furugishion (ふるぎっしょん, "furugi" = secondhand/used clothing) was a Japanese-language content and media website covering secondhand/vintage fashion, retro style guides, and rankings of clothing resale and buyback ("kaitori") services. Search-engine indexing shows articles such as "80s Retro Fashion Styles," "Recommended Ranking of Used-Clothing Home-Pickup Buyback Services," and shopping guides pointing readers to third-party resale shops and apps. Based on available evidence, Furugishion functioned as an affiliate/editorial media property that directed readers to other companies' marketplaces and buyback services, rather than operating its own buy-and-resell transaction platform as described in the seed data.

Business Model

Furugishion's content (fashion guides, "best of" rankings of secondhand clothing shops and buyback services, sizing guides) is consistent with an affiliate-content media business model common among Japanese shopping-comparison sites: generating traffic through SEO-optimised articles and monetising via referral/affiliate links to the marketplaces, resale apps (e.g., Mercari, Rakuma-style platforms) and buyback services it reviews, rather than directly buying, listing or reselling secondhand garments itself. No evidence was found of Furugishion operating its own inventory, storefront, logistics or payment system for secondhand clothing transactions. As of this research, the domain furugishion.com returns an HTTP 301 redirect to an entirely unrelated website (mppscguide.com, which appears to be unconnected content), strongly suggesting the original domain has expired and been acquired by a different, unrelated party — a common fate for abandoned Japanese affiliate media sites.

Verification Notes

This is a significant correction to the seed data. The seed data characterised Furugishion as a "secondhand clothing buy-and-resell platform." Two independent WebFetch attempts to the seed URL (https://furugishion.com/) and a specific article path both returned HTTP 301 redirects to an unrelated website (mppscguide.com), which is not a fashion or resale-related site. Google-indexed search snippets (accessed the same day) still show the old Furugishion site structure and article titles, suggesting the redirect is recent — the underlying domain has most likely expired and been re-registered by an unrelated third party, a common occurrence for lapsed Japanese content-media domains. Even in its indexed/cached form, the content resembles an affiliate fashion media/blog (style guides, "best of" rankings linking to other companies' resale and buyback services) rather than a first-party marketplace where the company itself buys and resells clothing. Given both the unverifiable business-model claim and the apparent domain lapse, this profile should be treated as low-confidence and flagged for removal or re-investigation before being presented as an active REUSE Foundation entry.
