Organisation Overview

The Restart Project is a London-based charity founded in 2013 that empowers people to repair and use electronics longer, reducing e-waste. It runs community "Restart Parties" where volunteers help the public fix broken devices, and supports a wider UK network of repair initiatives.

Business Model

The Restart Project operates as a non-profit funded through grants, donations, and crowdfunding, running free community repair events ("Restart Parties") staffed by skilled volunteers. It also collects and publishes open data on repair barriers from ~600 citizen scientists, and since 2019 co-leads the European Right to Repair Campaign, a coalition of 100+ organisations advocating for right-to-repair legislation.

Community electronics repair events, repair skills training, open repair-barrier data collection, and right-to-repair policy advocacy.

Measurable Impact

Around 600 citizen scientists have recorded over 81,000 repair attempts, aggregated and published under an open licence.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A leading and long-running voice in the right-to-repair movement with substantial community reach, published open data, and direct policy influence in the UK and EU. Highly relevant as both a grassroots repair model and policy advocate.
