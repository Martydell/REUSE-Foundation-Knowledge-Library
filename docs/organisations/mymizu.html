Organisation Overview

mymizu was co-founded by Robin Lewis and Mariko McTier, launching as Japan's first free water-refill app in September 2019 under nonprofit Social Innovation Japan. It maps public water fountains and partner cafes/shops/hotels where users can refill bottles for free, reducing single-use PET bottle consumption.

Business Model

mymizu operates as a free consumer app connecting users to a crowdsourced and partner-verified map of refill points (public fountains plus partner businesses like cafes and hotels). Revenue/sustainability comes through partnerships with businesses and municipalities (e.g., Tokyo Metropolitan Government) rather than consumer fees, supporting the nonprofit's broader sustainability education mission.

Products & Services

Free mobile app (iOS/Android) mapping 200,000+ refill points globally, including 11,000+ in Japan; partner network for cafes, restaurants and hotels.

Customers

Individual consumers/travelers globally; partner businesses and municipalities (e.g., Tokyo) that host refill points.

Partnerships

Tokyo Metropolitan Government; over 1,000 registered business refill partners; expanding local chapters including a Singapore team.

Measurable Impact

200,000+ refill points worldwide across 100+ countries; 11,000+ refill spots in Japan alone.

mymizu is a well-documented, scaled refill-infrastructure app directly reducing single-use plastic bottle consumption, with strong partnership and impact data. Core relevance to the REUSE Foundation's refill-systems focus.
