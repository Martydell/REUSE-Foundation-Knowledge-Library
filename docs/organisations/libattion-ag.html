Organisation Overview

Libattion AG is a Zurich-based company founded in 2018 that designs and manufactures modular Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) for commercial, industrial and utility-scale applications. Its core proposition is upcycling: it sources battery modules and cells that are discarded from EV production lines or come from end-of-life vehicles — often due to minor cosmetic defects or overproduction rather than technical failure — and repurposes them into stationary storage systems (branded E-Rack, E-Cabinet and E-Container) ranging from 97 kWh to 60 MWh, managed with proprietary software. In 2025 it opened what is described as Europe's largest EV-battery upcycling facility, a 7,000 m² plant in Biberist, Switzerland, targeting 500 MWh annual capacity by 2026.

Business Model

Libattion operates a B2B industrial model, selling and installing complete stationary energy storage systems to commercial, industrial and utility customers rather than selling raw cells. Its differentiation is the "second-life" sourcing strategy: instead of using new cells, it procures surplus and rejected battery modules from EV manufacturing lines and end-of-life vehicles, tests and re-packages them into standardised racks, cabinets and containers, and layers on its own battery-management and monitoring software. This lets it offer storage at a lower cost and lower embodied carbon than new-cell systems while extending the usable life of batteries that would otherwise be scrapped or sent for early recycling. The company has scaled through venture and strategic investment (EUR 14 million raised from A&G Energy Transition Tech Fund, Teknia, HCapital New Ideas II and Swiss energy utility EBL) and is investing that capital into the new Biberist facility to industrialise its upcycling and increase annual production capacity toward 500 MWh by 2026, positioning it to serve growing commercial/utility demand for grid storage and renewable-energy buffering.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Libattion is a credible and well-documented example of industrial-scale battery reuse, with independent trade-press verification (electrive.com, Charged EVs, ess-news.com) of its funding round, Biberist facility opening, and second-life sourcing model. It directly extends the useful life of EV battery cells that would otherwise be scrapped or prematurely recycled, which is a clear and significant circular-economy activity at industrial scale. The main gap is a lack of independently audited, quantified environmental impact metrics (e.g., tonnes of batteries diverted, CO2e avoided); current figures are limited to self-reported project counts and facility capacity targets.

Priority: ★★★★ High (verified, funded, scaling industrial battery-reuse operation with real environmental relevance; impact quantification still limited)
