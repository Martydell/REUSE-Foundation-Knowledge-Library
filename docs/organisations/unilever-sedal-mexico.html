Unilever (Sedal brand) — Mexico Refill Stations

Sedal shampoo refill stations piloted in Walmart Mexico stores using reusable aluminum bottles.

Primary Category Refill Systems

Organisation Overview

Unilever's Sedal brand set up shampoo refill stations in ten Walmart stores across Mexico in late 2019, letting shoppers fill reusable aluminum bottles instead of buying plastic bottles — the first reuse-refill trial for a Unilever Beauty & Personal Care brand in Mexico.

Business Model

In-store refill kiosks placed inside Walmart Mexico locations: customers purchase a durable aluminum bottle once, then return to refill with Sedal shampoo at a lower per-use cost than buying new plastic bottles, reducing packaging waste per purchase cycle.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems

Products & Services

Sedal shampoo dispensed via in-store refill stations into reusable aluminum bottles.

Customers

Walmart Mexico shoppers purchasing Sedal haircare products.

Partnerships

Walmart Mexico (in-store refill station hosting).

Measurable Impact

Two tonnes of Sedal shampoo were sold via the new aluminum refill bottles within the first ten days of the pilot; the program was popular enough that Unilever planned to expand it nationally.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A concrete, metrics-backed refill pilot demonstrating retail-partner refill infrastructure at meaningful (multi-store) scale, relevant as a replicable retail-refill model for personal care categories. Priority: ★★★★ High
