Organisation Overview

Club Saludable (formally "Dietética Club Saludable") is a small independent health-food and bulk-goods shop in the Barrio Norte area of Buenos Aires, Argentina, selling dried fruits, cereals, legumes, organic products, and vegetarian/vegan items, including options sold by weight ("a granel") — a bulk-refill format that reduces packaging compared to pre-packaged goods. During this research pass, its online store (mitiendanube.com) displayed a notice that the store had been discontinued ("la tienda fue dada de baja"), while third-party directory listings (Foursquare, Yelp, Unilocal) still reference the physical Av. Callao location as an operating business — the discrepancy is unresolved.

Business Model

Club Saludable operates as a neighbourhood dietética (health-food store), selling bulk dried goods (fruits, cereals, legumes, seeds) by weight alongside organic and vegetarian/vegan products, light desserts, and alternative milks. The bulk/by-weight format is the core reuse-relevant mechanism: customers can bring their own containers or use minimal packaging compared to buying pre-packaged equivalents. It appears to operate primarily as a physical retail location with a Buenos Aires customer base and social-media/WhatsApp-based customer contact, plus a (seemingly since-discontinued) online storefront.
