A Bulgarian zero-waste awareness initiative best known for its crowdsourced map of public drinking-water fountains and refill points across Bulgaria.

Organisation Overview

Zero Waste Sofia is a Bulgarian sustainable-living initiative founded by corporate-communications professional Simona Stiliyanova(-Pavlova), aimed at reducing single-use plastic and bottled-water consumption. Its flagship project, the "Fountains of Bulgaria" map, catalogues around 1,000 public water fountains and refill-friendly venues nationwide.

Business Model

Zero Waste Sofia is a volunteer-driven, non-commercial initiative rather than a retail business. It maintains a public, web-based (non-app) map of water fountains and venues — including around 39 restaurants — willing to refill customers' bottles with tap or filtered water for free. Hundreds of volunteers contribute new locations via a website submission form, and venues can request to be added.

Products & Services

Free, crowdsourced online map of ~1,000 public water fountains and refill-friendly venues (including free tap/filtered-water refill restaurants) across Bulgaria.

Customers

Individuals in Bulgaria seeking free water-refill points; restaurants/venues participating as refill hosts.

Not publicly available.

Measurable Impact

Approximately 1,000 water fountains and 39 refill-friendly restaurants mapped, per the organisation's own "Fountains of Bulgaria" project description.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A useful, low-cost grassroots refill-infrastructure mapping project that directly supports reuse of personal water bottles in Bulgaria. Its informal/individual-led structure and lack of confirmed non-profit registration or independent verification limit its institutional weight.
