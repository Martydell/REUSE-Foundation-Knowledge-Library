Organisation Overview

EcoRoots is a US e-commerce retailer of plastic-free personal care and household products, founded by Alin Turcea and Antonia Pitica, originally based in Aspen, Colorado, and now operating out of Phoenix, Arizona. The company sells vegan, small-batch, cruelty-free items such as soap and shampoo bars, dental floss refills, laundry detergent sheets, and reusable/refillable alternatives to conventional plastic personal-care products, shipping all orders using plastic-free packaging materials (cardboard, paper, compostable fill). It positions itself as a "one-stop shop" for consumers seeking to transition to a zero-waste lifestyle.

Business Model

EcoRoots operates a direct-to-consumer e-commerce model, selling zero-waste and plastic-free household, personal-care, and beauty products through its own website (ecoroots.us). Its product range spans shampoo/conditioner bars, bar soaps, vegan refillable floss, safety razors, deodorant, and laundry detergent sheets, with an emphasis on products packaged in glass, paper, metal or compostable materials rather than plastic. Orders are shipped plastic-free, and the company markets itself around small-business values, community product-feedback, and cruelty-free/vegan formulation. Revenue is generated purely through direct product sales rather than a subscription or refill-station model; the "reuse" element of the business is concentrated in encouraging customers to replace single-use plastic items (e.g., plastic floss containers, disposable razors, plastic bottles) with durable or refillable alternatives, rather than through a formal take-back or in-store refill infrastructure.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

EcoRoots is a small, single-brand direct-to-consumer zero-waste retailer whose environmental contribution is primarily plastic-avoidance (via plastic-free shipping and durable/refillable product formats) rather than a structural reuse system such as refill stations, deposit-return, or packaging-as-a-service. Its relevance to the Foundation's reuse-systems focus is limited — it is a consumer-education and waste-reduction retailer rather than an operator of reuse infrastructure — though it may be a useful example of DTC plastic-free retailing.
