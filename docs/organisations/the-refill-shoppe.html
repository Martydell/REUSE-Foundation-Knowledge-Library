The Refill Shoppe

A pioneering Certified B Corp refill retailer in Ventura, California, selling bath, body, home and cleaning products in bulk since 2010.

Organisation Overview

The Refill Shoppe was founded by Michelle Stevens in Ventura, California, as one of the earliest dedicated refill retailers in the US zero-waste movement. The business sells bath, body, home-cleaning and personal-care liquids in bulk from a physical storefront (relocated within Ventura since opening) and via e-commerce, allowing customers to refill their own containers or purchase one of the store's reusable bottles. It has held Certified B Corporation status since 2015 and describes itself as a social enterprise focused on reducing single-use plastic.

Business Model

The Refill Shoppe operates a direct-to-consumer refill model: customers bring in empty containers (or buy a reusable bottle from the shop) and have them filled by weight/volume with bath, body, home and cleaning liquids, avoiding the purchase of new single-use packaging on repeat purchases. The company sells through its Ventura, California retail storefront and its own e-commerce site, therefillshoppe.com, which ships refill and personal-care products more broadly across the US. It is a small, independently owned business — LinkedIn and other business-data aggregators list it at roughly 2–10 employees, and third-party aggregator RocketReach cites approximately $5 million in annual revenue, though this figure is not independently confirmed by the company itself and should be treated as an estimate rather than verified fact. The company has held Certified B Corporation status since August 2015, holds a B Impact Score of 114.9 (versus a median of 50.9 for ordinary businesses assessed), is a Certified Green Business under the City of Ventura's program, and has been a member of 1% for the Planet since opening, donating a portion of revenue to a rotating slate of environmental non-profits. Founder Michelle Stevens was named a Tory Burch Fellow in 2023 and received a NAWBO Ventura County "Innovator of the Year" award in 2012.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (bulk refill of bath, body, home and cleaning liquids from customer-supplied or shop-sold reusable containers)
  • Waste Reduction (core mission is reducing single-use plastic packaging)
  • Consumer Education (in-store and online education on refill/zero-waste practices; B Corp/1% for the Planet public reporting)

Products & Services

Refillable bath and body products (soap, shampoo, conditioner, lotion), home cleaning products (dish soap, laundry detergent, all-purpose cleaner), and reusable bottles/containers sold in-store and online.

Customers

Individual retail consumers, primarily in the Ventura, California area (in-store) and across the United States via e-commerce.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

The Refill Shoppe is one of the longest-running dedicated refill retailers in the United States and a credible, well-documented example of a small-business refill model with independent third-party verification (B Corp certification since 2015, City of Ventura Green Business status). Its relevance is as a proof-of-concept and legacy case study for consumer-facing refill retail rather than as a scalable infrastructure or technology provider — it remains a single-location, small-team operation (an estimated 2–10 employees) without published environmental-outcome metrics, a digital reuse-tracking system, or named commercial partnerships beyond certification bodies. It is a useful illustrative example for the knowledge library, particularly for the history and mechanics of independent refill retail, but is of limited priority for organisations seeking scalable or quantifiably impactful reuse models.

Priority: ★★★ Medium
