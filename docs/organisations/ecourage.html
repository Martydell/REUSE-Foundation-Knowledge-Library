Organisation Overview

Ecourage is a refill-technology company providing a B2B2C "refill-as-a-service" platform: smart, NFC-enabled reusable packaging and dispensing hardware that retailers and consumer brands can deploy to offer in-store or in-machine refills while capturing data on individual customer refill behaviour. Its named retail and brand partners/pilots include Colruyt, Albert Heijn, Lidl, Delhaize, ecostore and Cargill, and it has piloted deployments in Europe, Asia (including AEON stores in Tokyo from October 2025, per industry press) and, via the NextCycle Michigan accelerator, North America. The company's exact headquarters location and founding date could not be confirmed with confidence in this research pass — see Verification Notes.

Business Model

Ecourage sells or licenses a refill-dispensing system to retailers and brands rather than selling refill products directly to consumers: its OmniCap™ is an NFC-tagged smart cap fitted to reusable packaging that identifies the individual container/customer at each refill, while its OmniTap™ dispensing hardware handles the actual liquid fill (for both food and non-food liquid products) at in-store refill points. Because each refill is tied to an NFC identity, the system enables personalised interactions — refill history, loyalty/promotional tie-ins, and customer analytics — that a generic unbranded refill station cannot offer, giving retail partners a data and loyalty layer alongside the sustainability benefit. Ecourage positions this as revenue-additive for retailers (citing claims of increased revenue per square metre and repeat visits) rather than purely a cost-reduction/compliance play, and offers relatively fast pilot deployment (as little as three months, per company materials). Confirmed retail/brand relationships include Colruyt, Albert Heijn, Lidl, Delhaize, ecostore and Cargill.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (NFC-enabled smart refill dispensing for reusable packaging)
  • Packaging-as-a-Service (B2B2C refill infrastructure supplied to retailers/brands)
  • Digital Tracking (NFC-based per-container/per-customer refill tracking and analytics)

Products & Services

OmniCap™ (NFC-enabled smart cap for reusable packaging, tracking refill history and enabling personalisation); OmniTap™ (refill dispensing system for food and non-food liquids); retailer/brand analytics dashboard for measuring refill-programme impact.

Customers

Retailers and consumer brands deploying in-store refill infrastructure, including Colruyt, Albert Heijn, Lidl and Delhaize (retail), and ecostore and Cargill (brand/supplier side); end consumers refill personal reusable containers at partner retail locations.

Partnerships

Retail partners: Colruyt, Albert Heijn, Lidl, Delhaize. Brand/supplier partners: ecostore, Cargill. Accelerator/recognition: NextCycle Michigan Circular Accelerator (Best in Show, SHAPE Sustainable Packaging Showcase, October 2025; planned deployment of 10 refill stations across Michigan). Reported pilot in AEON stores, Tokyo, Japan (October 2025, per industry trade coverage).

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available as dated, verified figures. Company materials reference general claims (increased revenue per square metre, enhanced loyalty/return visits, supply-chain improvements) without specific dated metrics; one secondary source states the system can reduce plastic use by "over 95%" compared to single-use packaging, but this figure is not independently corroborated by a primary company disclosure in this pass.

Technology

NFC (near-field communication) tagging embedded in reusable packaging caps (OmniCap™) for per-container identification at each refill; automated dispensing hardware (OmniTap™) compatible with multiple liquid product types; back-end analytics dashboard for retailers/brands.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ecourage represents a genuinely novel technology layer for reuse systems — using NFC identification to add personalisation, loyalty and analytics to what is otherwise a generic refill station — and has secured pilots with several major European grocery retailers (Colruyt, Albert Heijn, Lidl, Delhaize) plus international expansion signals (Japan, North America), suggesting real commercial traction. However, basic organisational facts (headquarters country, founding year, ownership) could not be confirmed in this pass and conflict across sources (Portugal per seed data vs. Norway per one secondary source), which should be resolved with a direct company enquiry or company-registry lookup before this profile is considered fully verified.
