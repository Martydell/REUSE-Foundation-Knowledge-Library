Organisation Overview

The Korea Zero Waste Movement Network (KZWMN) is a South Korean non-profit NGO network founded in 1997, comprising approximately 180 grassroots environmental, citizens' and women's-movement organisations, organised into roughly 5 regional networks with 53 constituent organisations nationwide. It advocates for alternative waste policy — including deposit-return systems for glass bottles, mandatory refunds on plastic-bag charges, packaging-reduction regulation, and prohibitions on single-use cups, bags and containers — with the stated goal of building an "ecological society" through recycling and waste-reduction systems rather than incineration. KZWMN is a member of the Global Alliance for Incinerator Alternatives (GAIA), which also profiles the network on its no-burn.org platform.

Business Model

As a non-profit NGO network rather than a commercial entity, KZWMN's "business model" is advocacy- and coalition-based: it convenes approximately 180 member grassroots organisations to develop and push policy proposals to national and local government (e.g., South Korea's Ministry of Environment) and to negotiate voluntary commitments from industry. Notably, the network's proposal that retailers charge for plastic bags and provide deposit refunds when bags are returned was accepted by the Ministry of Environment, becoming government policy. KZWMN also monitors implementation of deposit systems for empty glass bottles and associated handling-fee schemes, runs Zero Waste-shop policy frameworks, proposes food-culture and packaging-reduction policy, and engages directly with approximately 12 major corporations (including P&G Korea, Coca-Cola and Lotte Chilsung Beverage) and delivery companies on plastic-reduction initiatives. Funding is typical of an NGO coalition — member organisation contributions, grants and campaign-based fundraising — rather than product sales or service fees.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Deposit Return — advocacy for and monitoring of deposit-refund systems on glass bottles and plastic bags
  • Consumer Education — public campaigns on waste reduction, Zero Waste shop models, and disposable-product-free youth/school programmes (reported ~4,300 youth participants across ~50 schools)
  • Waste Reduction — policy proposals for packaging reduction, prohibition of single-use cups/bags/containers, and food-culture reform
  • Other (Policy Advocacy) — direct engagement with government ministries and major corporations to enact deposit-return and packaging regulation

Products & Services

Not a goods/services provider — KZWMN produces policy proposals, monitoring/reporting on deposit and recycling systems, public education campaigns, and pilot Zero Waste shop/market/university models implemented in partnership with local government and member organisations.

Customers

Not applicable in a commercial sense — its constituencies are the ~180 member grassroots organisations nationwide, South Korean government bodies (notably the Ministry of Environment), corporate partners engaged on plastic reduction, and the general public reached through education campaigns.

Partnerships

  • Global Alliance for Incinerator Alternatives (GAIA) — network membership, profiled via no-burn.org
  • South Korea Ministry of Environment — policy partner (adopted KZWMN's plastic-bag deposit-refund proposal)
  • P&G Korea, Coca-Cola, Lotte Chilsung Beverage, and delivery companies — corporate engagement on plastic-reduction initiatives (12 major corporations reported)
  • ~180 member grassroots environmental, citizens' and women's-movement organisations nationwide

Measurable Impact

Self-reported network figures (source: GAIA/no-burn.org member profile): approximately $3 billion reduction in national budget attributed to disposable-product-reduction campaigns; approximately 4,300 youth participants engaged across approximately 50 schools; 5 regional networks comprising 53 member organisations established nationwide; multiple Zero Waste models created (tourist attractions, university programmes, markets). These figures are as reported by the organisation/GAIA and have not been independently audited in sources reviewed.

Technology

Not publicly available — KZWMN is a policy-advocacy and coalition-coordination network rather than a technology operator; no digital tracking or app-based systems were identified for the network itself (though it advocates for deposit/refund infrastructure operated by government and retailers).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

KZWMN is a long-running (since 1997), well-documented civil-society network with a genuine, verifiable policy track record — most notably its role in South Korea's national plastic-bag deposit-refund system, a significant deposit-return policy win directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's interest in return/refill infrastructure. As an advocacy coalition rather than an operating business, it does not itself run reuse infrastructure, but its policy influence over government and major corporations on container-deposit and packaging-reduction systems gives it outsized relevance for understanding how reuse-supportive regulation is achieved. Reported impact figures are self-sourced via GAIA rather than independently audited.
