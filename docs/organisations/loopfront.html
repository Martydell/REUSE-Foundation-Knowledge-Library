A Norwegian digital platform that maps, tracks and manages the reuse of building materials and interior fixtures, helping the construction industry cut waste and cost.

Organisation Overview

Loopfront is a Norwegian company founded in 2018 by architect Michael Anthony Curtis and four co-founders, born from frustration with the lack of effective digital tools for reusing building materials. It has built what it describes as Norway's largest digital platform for the reuse of building materials and interiors, using AI-assisted surveying tools to give buildings a digital material inventory within days, then managing the full process — mapping, documentation, internal/external marketplace listing, logistics coordination, and reporting on cost, CO2 and waste savings. The company now has around 80 paying customers across Norway, Sweden and Germany, spanning public sector bodies, property owners and large private companies.

Business Model

Loopfront operates a B2B Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) model targeted at the construction, property and public sectors. Field specialists or client teams use Loopfront's AI-assisted surveying tool to inventory a building's reusable materials and fixtures within a few days, creating a "digital identity" for each item that can be tracked, listed for internal reuse (within a project or organisation) or externally on a marketplace, and coordinated for logistics/collection. The platform automatically generates reports quantifying reduced costs, CO2 emissions and waste diverted from disposal. Pricing is structured as a monthly subscription pegged to a small fraction (reported as 0.01% or less) of total project cost, making it scalable across projects of varying size. Its roughly 80 paying customers span Norway, Sweden and Germany, with growing interest particularly among public-sector clients, property owners and larger companies — reflecting increasing regulatory and ESG-driven demand for material reuse documentation in construction and demolition projects.

Products & Services

Digital reuse platform for building materials and interiors; AI-assisted surveying tool for rapid material inventory; internal and external marketplace listing functionality; logistics/collection coordination; automated reporting on cost, CO2 and waste-reduction outcomes.

Customers

Approximately 80 paying customers across Norway, Sweden and Germany, including public-sector bodies, property owners, and large private construction/property companies.

Measurable Impact

  • A large municipality using Loopfront for 18 months saved 90 tonnes of waste, 230 tonnes of CO2, and EUR 1.6 million (company-reported case study, via Business Norway)
  • Approximately 80 paying customers across Norway, Sweden and Germany (company-reported)
  • 12 million NOK raised for its "Greenstock" circular-economy platform for the building and construction industry (reported by Loopfront's own blog/press)

Technology

AI-assisted digital surveying tool for rapid building-material inventory; digital "identity" and tracking system for individual reusable items; internal/external reuse marketplace; automated cost/CO2/waste reporting engine.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Loopfront is a strong, well-verified example of digital infrastructure enabling industrial-scale reuse in the construction sector, corroborated by independent sources including Business Norway, UKGBC, and Dealroom/M&A tracking sites, alongside a specific, quantified municipal case study (90 tonnes waste, 230 tonnes CO2, EUR 1.6m saved over 18 months). Its subscription-based SaaS model, AI-assisted surveying, and growing base of ~80 paying customers across three countries demonstrate genuine commercial traction in a sector (construction/demolition) where material reuse has historically been difficult to track and scale. This is one of the more credible and impactful organisations in this research batch.

Priority: ★★★★★ Essential (verified, scaling digital infrastructure directly enabling industrial building-material reuse, with quantified case-study impact and multi-country commercial traction)
