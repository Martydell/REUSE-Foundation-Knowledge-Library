Organisation Overview

RingKarp is an Estonian family business that built a nationwide reusable food-container and drink-cup deposit system, letting customers order food in a returnable container, pay a small deposit, and return it at network locations or via the reCIRCLE European reuse network. Founded in 2021, its containers are designed for at least 200 uses.

Business Model

RingKarp sells reusable, food-hygiene-approved containers and cups to food vendors (roughly €10 per container, €5 per cup), who resell the deposit-inclusive packaging to customers at the same price. Customers scan a QR code on the container to register a return, and containers can be returned to any participating restaurant in the network — restaurants in RingKarp's model wash returned containers themselves rather than relying on centralised industrial cleaning. RingKarp participates in reCIRCLE, letting deposits be reclaimed across other European countries too.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging (food containers, drink cups)
  • Deposit Return (deposit-based container/cup system)
  • Digital Tracking (QR-code scan-based return registration)
  • Reverse Logistics (multi-restaurant network return system)

Products & Services

Reusable, food-safe containers and drink cups sold to restaurants and food vendors as a deposit-return packaging system, with QR-code tracking and cross-network returns via reCIRCLE.

Customers

Restaurants and food vendors across nearly every county in Estonia; end consumers ordering food/drinks who pay and reclaim the container deposit.

Partnerships

Member of the pan-European reCIRCLE reusable-packaging network, allowing deposit reclaim across participating European countries; containers are recycled via a partner facility in Switzerland at end of life.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available (no independently verified figures on containers in circulation or waste diverted were found).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

RingKarp is a verifiable, nationwide deposit-return reusable-packaging system with genuine cross-border interoperability via reCIRCLE, making it a strong example of the deposit-return model despite limited published impact metrics. ★★★★ High
