TRAMUCO (via COOPI initiative)

Women-led community waste-management and recycling cooperative in Caracas, Venezuela.

Organisation Overview

TRAMUCO is a women-led cooperative established in 2023 that runs a participatory solid-waste management system across several Caracas parishes (Antímano, La Vega, Sucre, Altagracia, San Agustín, Coche, Valle), developed through a COOPI–International Cooperation project with local partner Tinta Violeta and EU funding.

Business Model

Non-profit/community cooperative: members (reported as 45-72 women, many survivors of gender-based violence or economic hardship) collect, sort and compact glass, paper and plastic waste, selling recovered materials to industry or converting them into products traded within their communities, after training in waste management and business administration.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Material Recovery
  • Waste Reduction
  • Consumer Education

Products & Services

Community-based waste collection, sorting and compacting of glass/paper/plastic; sale of recovered materials and community-traded upcycled products.

Customers

Local Caracas communities and industrial buyers of recovered materials.

Partnerships

COOPI (Cooperazione Internazionale), Tinta Violeta, European Union funding; part of the World March of Women network.

Measurable Impact

Reported cooperative membership of 45-72 women across seven Caracas parishes (sources vary on exact count).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A genuine, EU-backed community recycling and social-empowerment initiative with a clear gender-equity dimension; valuable as a Latin American material-recovery/social-impact case study, though scale is local. ★★★ Medium
