Organisation Overview

Cleancult is a US household-cleaning brand founded by Ryan Lupberger and Zachary Bedrosian, following roughly four years of R&D into formula and packaging before its 2019 public launch. Its distinguishing feature is refill packaging: customers buy one reusable glass bottle/vessel and then purchase plant-derived soap and cleaning-product refills shipped in plastic-free, paper-based milk cartons — cited as the first company to successfully apply this packaging format to soap and detergent. The company raised $25 million in funding (2021, per Forbes) to scale the model.

Business Model

Cleancult's core mechanism: a customer buys a durable glass bottle once, then reorders concentrate/product refills shipped in recyclable, plastic-free paper cartons (the same format used for milk), which are poured into the reusable glass vessel — intended to be "the last bottle you'll ever need to buy." Products are plant-derived cleaning and soap formulas (dish soap, hand soap, laundry, multi-surface cleaners, etc.), sold direct-to-consumer via subscription and one-off purchase online. The $25M funding round (2021) supported broader retail and product-line expansion beyond the initial DTC subscription model.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Cleancult is a well-documented, venture-backed example of refill-based reuse in household cleaning, notable for its specific packaging innovation (milk-carton-style plastic-free refills) and real commercial scale (a $25M funding round, national press coverage). It is a strong case study for how refill mechanics can be paired with a genuinely novel packaging format rather than a generic pouch/bottle refill.
