Great Wrap

An Australian compostable cling-, catering-, and pallet-wrap manufacturer made from food/potato waste, which raised over AU$28 million before collapsing into liquidation in 2025.

Organisation Overview

Great Wrap was an Australian manufacturer of compostable cling wrap, catering wrap, and pallet (stretch) wrap made using food waste and potato-starch-derived bioplastics, founded in 2019 by married couple Jordy and Julia Kay (an architect and a former winemaker) after meeting in Melbourne. The company aimed to replace fossil-fuel-based plastic film with a compostable alternative and raised substantial venture and non-dilutive funding — including a reported AU$24 million Series A round and AU$13 million in asset financing from Rabobank's DLL Group — to scale manufacturing at a Tullamarine, Victoria factory. Despite early recognition and rapid fundraising, the company collapsed into administration in September 2025 and was liquidated with debts exceeding AU$39 million.

Business Model

Great Wrap manufactured and sold compostable plastic-film alternatives — cling wrap, catering wrap, and industrial pallet/stretch wrap — targeting both consumer and B2B (logistics/warehousing) markets, alongside a reusable dispenser product ("Great Mate"). Feedstock was sourced from food waste (reported use of potato waste/starch) and other plant-based polymers, positioning the product as compostable rather than merely recyclable. The company pursued an aggressive scale-up strategy funded by a mix of equity and non-dilutive asset finance: a reported AU$24 million Series A round, AU$13 million in equipment financing from DLL Group (Rabobank's leasing arm), and a further AU$5 million raised with packaging manufacturer Opal ANZ to scale compostable pallet wrap production — reported total investment approaching AU$28 million from government and private sources. Ultimately the model proved financially unsustainable: reported revenue fell roughly 32% from FY2023 to FY2025 while costs in FY2025 exceeded income by more than 800%, driven by high input costs, working-capital shortfalls, and a market shift toward recyclable/recycled-content packaging rather than compostables. The company entered administration on 17 September 2025, made all five remaining employees redundant, and ceased operations at its Tullamarine factory before proceeding to liquidation with debts over AU$39 million.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Waste Reduction (compostable packaging films manufactured from food/potato waste as a fossil-plastic substitute)
  • Material Recovery (use of food-processing waste streams — potato starch — as bioplastic feedstock)
  • Reusable Packaging (the "Great Mate" reusable dispenser product line)

Products & Services

Compostable cling wrap, compostable catering wrap, compostable pallet/stretch wrap for industrial logistics use, and the "Great Mate" reusable wrap dispenser — all manufactured from food-waste-derived bioplastic (including potato starch).

Customers

Consumer retail buyers (cling/catering wrap) and B2B logistics/warehousing customers requiring compostable pallet wrap; specific named commercial customers were not identified in verified sources.

Partnerships

  • DLL Group (Rabobank's asset-financing arm) — provided AU$13 million in non-dilutive equipment financing
  • Opal ANZ (Australian packaging manufacturer) — AU$5 million partnership to scale compostable pallet wrap production
  • Reported Australian government grant/support programmes contributing to the company's approximately AU$28 million total raised (specific agencies not confirmed in sources reviewed)

Measurable Impact

  • Raised a reported AU$24 million Series A funding round (2022) plus AU$13 million in non-dilutive financing from DLL Group, and a further AU$5 million from Opal ANZ — total reported investment approaching AU$28 million.
  • Company revenue reportedly declined approximately 32% from FY2023 to FY2025; FY2025 costs exceeded revenue by over 800% (Packaging Gateway/PKN Packaging News reporting on administrators' findings).
  • Entered administration 17 September 2025 with debts reported over AU$39 million; all five remaining staff made redundant and Tullamarine factory operations ceased.

Technology

Proprietary bioplastic formulation using food-waste and potato-starch-derived polymers engineered to be both stretchable (matching conventional plastic film's clinging/stretching performance) and industrially compostable.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Great Wrap is an important cautionary case study rather than a straightforward success story: it demonstrated genuine technical innovation (compostable stretch/cling film matching conventional plastic performance) and attracted significant investment and press attention as an Australian "sustainability darling," but ultimately failed commercially, entering administration and liquidation in September 2025 with debts exceeding AU$39 million. Reported causes — rising input costs, a market shift toward recyclable/recycled-content packaging over compostables, and unsustainable unit economics — are directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's assessment of which circular-economy business models are durable versus which attract capital without proving viable at scale. It should be retained in the knowledge library as a documented failure/lessons-learned case rather than a template for replication in its current (ceased) form.

Verification Notes

Seed data listed Great Wrap's year established as 2020 — research finds the company name was registered in 2019 (founders met and registered the "Great Wrap" name within three months in 2019) with the commercial business launching a year later in 2020, so both years appear in different sources depending on whether "founding" refers to registration or commercial launch; this profile lists 2019 as founding with 2020 launch noted for clarity. Critically, seed data did not reflect that the company ceased trading: multiple 2025 sources (Packaging Gateway, PKN Packaging News, Geoff Isaac) confirm Great Wrap entered administration on 17 September 2025 and was liquidated with debts exceeding AU$39 million — this is a major status correction and the single most important verification finding for this profile.
