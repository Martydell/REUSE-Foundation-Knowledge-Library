Organisation Overview

Seventh Generation, Inc. is a Burlington, Vermont-based manufacturer of plant-derived cleaning, laundry, paper and personal-care products, founded in 1988 by Alan Newman and Jeffrey Hollender. The company built its identity around the "seventh generation" principle drawn from Haudenosaunee (Iroquois) philosophy — weighing decisions against their impact on descendants seven generations forward. It became a Certified B Corporation in 2007. In September 2016, Unilever acquired the company in a deal reported at roughly $700 million; Seventh Generation continues to operate as a distinct brand within Unilever's home-care portfolio.

Business Model

Seventh Generation is a B2C consumer packaged goods (CPG) business selling plant-based, biodegradable cleaning, laundry, dish, paper and personal-care products through natural food retailers, mainstream supermarkets, mass merchants (e.g., Target, Walmart) and online channels (including Amazon and its own DTC site) across the United States. Its differentiation rests on ingredient transparency (full disclosure on every label), USDA-certified biobased content (95%+ in laundry and dish lines), and packaging reduction rather than reuse: initiatives such as the EasyDose concentrated laundry format cut plastic and water per unit, and the company reports that the large majority of its packaging by volume is recyclable or otherwise reduced in material intensity. Unlike several peers researched in this batch (e.g., Splosh, Siklus), Seventh Generation's public materials do not describe a refill, deposit-return, or reusable-packaging system as a core part of its model — its circular-economy contribution is concentrated in material substitution (biobased/recycled content) and recyclability rather than reuse loops.

Laundry detergents and packs, dish soap, all-purpose and bathroom cleaners, disinfecting products, paper towels/tissue/toilet paper, baby diapers and wipes, and personal-care items, sold under the Seventh Generation brand.

Challenges

  • The company's own "Our Company" page (seventhgeneration.com) states Unilever's acquisition occurred in "2020," which conflicts with extensive contemporaneous press coverage (VTDigger, Vermont Business Magazine, Vermont Public, CNN, CS Monitor, Fortune) dating the deal to September 2016. This research uses 2016, the date supported by the weight of independent reporting, and flags the discrepancy for the organisation's own record-keeping.
  • Employee counts vary significantly by source and date (109–170 at time of 2016 acquisition per press; 51–200 as of mid-2024 per LinkedIn/Tracxn aggregation), suggesting no single authoritative current figure is publicly disclosed.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Seventh Generation is a well-known "green" CPG brand, but on current public evidence its model is built on biobased/recyclable material substitution and packaging lightweighting, not on reusable packaging, refill systems, deposit-return, or take-back — the core activities of interest to the REUSE Foundation. Seed data describing it simply as "environmentally friendly household products" is accurate but the seed's implied reuse relevance should be downgraded: no refill or reuse program was found in company materials. It may still be worth tracking as a large-scale sustainability comparator or for potential future refill initiatives under Unilever (which does run refill pilots elsewhere in its portfolio), but as of this research it does not meet the bar for a reuse-focused organisation.
