Organisation Overview

Foreword Coffee is a Singapore-based social enterprise café chain founded in 2017 by Lim Wei Jie and Nadi Chan. Its stated mission is to "mainstream disability and normalise inclusion" — Foreword was Singapore's first café specifically designed to train and employ persons with disabilities (including autism, intellectual disability, physical disability, deafness and mental-health conditions) as baristas. Alongside its social mission, the company sources green coffee directly from smallholder farms and cooperatives in Asia (reported sourcing countries include China, Laos, Thailand and Indonesia), aiming to pay farmers above commodity-market prices.

Business Model

Foreword Coffee operates multiple revenue streams built around its café network: physical outlets (locations have included Esplanade Mall, Temasek Shophouse, Mediacorp Campus, NUS's Yong Siew Toh Conservatory, and Momentus Hotel), online sales of roasted beans and brew bags, and B2B corporate services such as coffee-cart rentals, in-house café setup and barista services for events and offices. The company has also announced a Roastery & Training Academy planned at Enabling Village Vista, expanding its capacity to train baristas with disabilities and roast coffee in-house. Revenue from cafés and corporate services subsidises the higher cost of direct, ethically-sourced green coffee and above-market payments to smallholder producers and cooperatives, while public recognition (e.g. a 2023 Brands For Good award) and media coverage support brand visibility and B2B contracts. The seed data's specific claim of sourcing from "6 farms" across "4 Asian countries" could not be independently verified in exact numbers — direct-to-farm/cooperative sourcing across at least 4 Asian countries (China, Laos, Thailand, Indonesia) is corroborated, but the precise farm count is Not publicly available from verified sources.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Foreword Coffee's primary verified impact is social (disability employment and inclusion) and ethical-trade (direct sourcing from Asian smallholder farms), rather than circular economy/reuse. Its one clear reuse-economy activity — the CupCycle reusable-cup discount programme — is real but appears to be a minor, unquantified part of its operations rather than a core business model. The organisation is a legitimate and well-documented social enterprise, but its relevance to a reuse-focused knowledge library is limited to this single consumer-education/reusable-packaging angle.

Priority: ★★ Low (strong social enterprise with a minor, unquantified reuse-adjacent programme; core mission is ethical sourcing and disability inclusion, not reuse)
