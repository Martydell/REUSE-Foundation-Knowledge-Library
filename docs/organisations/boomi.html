Boomi

Organisation Overview

Boomi (from the Indonesian word "bumi," meaning earth) was a Shanghai-founded sustainability marketplace co-founded in 2018 by Emmanuel Dean and Miguel Boy, operating primarily through WeChat and serving Chinese consumers in cities including Shanghai and Beijing. It began by selling bamboo toothbrushes and grew into a curated platform listing more than 50 local sustainability-focused brands and businesses. As of this research (July 2026), Boomi's former website domain redirects to the unrelated Dell Boomi integration-software company, and a third-party Shanghai venue directory lists the business as closed, suggesting the organisation is no longer operating.

Business Model

Boomi operated as a curated multi-vendor marketplace connecting Chinese consumers with sustainable, eco-friendly brands, rather than manufacturing its own product range beyond an initial flagship line (bamboo toothbrushes, shampoo bars). Discovery and sales ran chiefly through WeChat, reflecting the platform's China-first, mobile-social-commerce approach. The company positioned itself as helping "local purpose-driven businesses thrive" by providing them a shared storefront and audience, while also acting as a content and information hub for sustainable living. It layered a charitable/impact mechanic onto commerce: Boomi pledged to donate 8.8% of revenue to a tree-planting initiative in Inner Mongolia, in partnership with Shanghai Roots & Shoots' Forest Initiative, and created social-engagement mechanics (e.g., "Wow" reactions on posts converting into tree-planting pledges) to blend community engagement with fundraising. No evidence was found of manufacturing collaboration, industrial reuse, or B2B services under the Boomi name — the seed data's "manufacturing collaboration" tag could not be verified.

Circular Economy Activities

No reusable-packaging, refill, deposit-return, repair, rental, reverse-logistics, or take-back program was identified for Boomi; its activity centred on retailing sustainable alternative products and environmental content/fundraising rather than reuse-system operations.

Products & Services

Bamboo toothbrushes (flagship product), shampoo bars, and other eco-friendly personal-care and household items; a marketplace listing 50+ local sustainability brands covering "sustainability needs, information, and services."

Customers

Chinese consumers, particularly in Shanghai and Beijing, reached primarily through WeChat.

Partnerships

  • Shanghai Roots & Shoots' Forest Initiative (tree-planting partner in Inner Mongolia)

Measurable Impact

  • "Over 6,000 trees planted" toward a stated goal of 9,000 by 2022, per a 2021 beijing-kids.com article citing the company. This figure is dated (2021) and not independently verified by an audited source, and no more recent impact data could be found.

Technology

WeChat-based mini-program/social-commerce storefront; no distinct proprietary technology platform identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Boomi represented a small-scale, China-focused curated marketplace for sustainable products, blending e-commerce with a charitable tree-planting mechanic. It does not appear to have operated significant reuse infrastructure (no refill, deposit, or take-back systems identified), and its relevance rests mainly on being an early example of a sustainability-branded marketplace aggregator in China. Critically, the organisation appears to be defunct: its official domain now redirects to the unrelated Dell Boomi software company, and a Shanghai business directory lists it as closed, with the most recent substantive coverage dating to 2021. Given the apparent closure and its limited direct reuse activity even when active, this profile has low priority for the knowledge library.
