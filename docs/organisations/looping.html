Organisation Overview

Looping AS is a Norwegian company founded in 2017 by Jens Brustad after he observed construction and housing modules being wrapped in large volumes of disposable plastic at building sites. The company designs and supplies durable, reusable protective covers — branded Modulcover™ — for contractor modules, housing modules, and steel containers used in the construction and module-rental industries. Looping frames its offering as "reuse as a service," leasing covers to clients rather than selling single-use wrap, and has expanded from Norway into Sweden, Finland, Denmark and the Baltics.

Business Model

Looping's core business is manufacturing and leasing reusable protective packaging (Modulcover™) to companies that transport and store prefabricated construction and housing modules, replacing the disposable plastic film traditionally shrink-wrapped around such units for transport and weather protection. Revenue is generated through a reuse-as-a-service/leasing model: clients pay to use covers repeatedly across many transport and storage cycles rather than purchasing single-use wrap for every shipment, which the company positions as both cost-efficient and faster to apply/remove (reducing mounting and disassembly time by up to 75%, per company claims). Customers are primarily large module-rental and construction-logistics firms such as Ramirent and Cramo Group. The company has raised external growth capital (a NOK 23 million round backed by Norselab) to fund expansion into new Nordic and European markets, and participates in Norwegian innovation/industry programmes (e.g., Norwegian Wood Cluster, Circular Packaging Cluster, Skattefunn R&D tax incentive).

Products & Services

Modulcover™ — durable, reusable protective covers sized for contractor modules, housing/prefab modules, and steel containers, offered on a reuse-as-a-service (leasing) basis; associated logistics support for cover deployment, retrieval and reuse cycling.

Customers

Construction module manufacturers and rental companies, including named clients Ramirent, Cramo Group, BAS, Adapteo and Algeco, operating across Norway, Sweden, Finland, Denmark and the Baltic states.

Partnerships

Named collaborators/programme affiliations include Klimapartnere, Circular Packaging Cluster, Norwegian Wood Cluster, UN Global Compact, Miljøfyrtårn (Eco-Lighthouse certification scheme), Norner, Forte Digital, and Skattefunn (Norwegian R&D tax incentive scheme). Norselab is a named investor.

Measurable Impact

Company-reported (not independently audited in a public third-party report): over 100 tonnes of single-use plastic eliminated from the construction and rental industry to date; over 8,000 Modulcovers™ in circulation; up to 75% reduction in mounting/disassembly time versus disposable wrap. Figures as stated on company website, undated.

Technology

Not publicly available beyond durable/reusable material design; no specific digital tracking (RFID/QR) system for individual covers was identified in verified sources.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Looping is a clear, well-documented example of industrial reuse displacing single-use plastic in a B2B logistics context (construction/housing modules), with a genuine reuse-as-a-service commercial model, named blue-chip customers, and credible (if company-self-reported) impact figures. It has attracted external growth investment and is expanding across the Nordics, indicating a scalable, commercially validated model rather than a pilot. This makes it a strong, directly relevant case for the REUSE Foundation's library as an industrial/B2B reusable packaging operator.
