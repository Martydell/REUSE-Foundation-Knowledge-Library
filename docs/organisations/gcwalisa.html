Organisation Overview

Gcwalisa (isiZulu for "fill up" or "refill") is a South African bulk-food and household-essentials retail model founded by entrepreneur Miles Kubheka, who also founded the Wakanda Food Accelerator in 2020. Conceived during COVID-19 lockdowns to address rising food insecurity in low-income communities, Gcwalisa operates a "weigh-and-pay" model from shipping-container-based stores, letting customers buy staples like maize meal, sugar, cooking oil and detergent in the exact quantities they can afford, cutting packaging costs out of the price. Its first outlet opened in Alexandra township, Johannesburg, in April 2022; by 2025 the network had grown to include additional outlets. The model has since incorporated a reusable-container deposit system, developed with GIZ's Circular City Labs programme, reducing packaging waste alongside cost.

Business Model

Gcwalisa's core retail model lets customers pay only for the exact weight/volume of product they need — as little as R4–R12 for small quantities of mealie meal or cooking oil — using the store's own bulk stock, removing the fixed cost of manufacturer packaging that is otherwise embedded in small-format retail pricing. Stores are set up in low-cost, repurposed shipping containers, keeping capital costs down and allowing rapid, low-risk deployment into township communities underserved by traditional retail. Through the "Circular City Labs" initiative — a partnership with German development agency GIZ (funded by Germany's Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development, BMZ) and South African industry body Plastics SA — Gcwalisa introduced a reusable-container option: customers bring or purchase a durable container, pay a small refundable deposit, and receive it back when the container is returned empty to the store. The company has stated that packaging accounts for 8–15% of food costs in South Africa, underpinning its core value proposition. Gcwalisa has partnered with community-based organisations to plan expansion beyond its pilot store toward a target of roughly 25 outlets.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (weigh-and-pay bulk dispensing of food and household staples)
  • Deposit Return (refundable-deposit reusable containers, piloted via GIZ Circular City Labs)
  • Reusable Packaging (reusable container option for customers)
  • Waste Reduction (elimination of manufacturer packaging cost/waste for bulk-purchased staples)
  • Consumer Education (affordability and packaging-cost awareness messaging)

Products & Services

Bulk "weigh-and-pay" retail of maize meal, sugar, cooking oil, dish soap, laundry detergent and other household/food staples; reusable, deposit-based containers for repeat purchases; shipping-container-based retail outlets in township communities.

Customers

Low-income households in South African townships, initially in Alexandra (Alex), Johannesburg, with expansion planned via community-based organisation partnerships.

Partnerships

  • Wakanda Food Accelerator (Miles Kubheka's own venture platform, incubated Gcwalisa)
  • GIZ (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit) — "Circular City Labs" project, funded by Germany's BMZ
  • Plastics SA — local implementing partner on the reusable-container/packaging-reduction initiative
  • SAB Foundation — featured Miles Kubheka/Gcwalisa in its entrepreneur profiles
  • Local community-based organisations partnering on planned store expansion

Measurable Impact

  • First shipping-container outlet opened April 2022 in Alexandra, Johannesburg.
  • By 2025, the Gcwalisa Food Hub model had expanded to include 5 additional outlets beyond the original pilot (per Wakanda Food Accelerator materials).
  • Reported pricing impact: staples available from as little as R4–R12 for small quantities (e.g., 250g mealie meal, 250ml cooking oil) — self-reported.
  • No independently audited tonnage, waste-diversion or deposit-return-rate figures were found in public sources.

Technology

Not publicly available (no distinct proprietary digital platform identified; operates as a physical weigh-and-pay retail format).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Gcwalisa is a well-documented, multiply-covered South African social enterprise (Africa Outlook, EWN, GIZ, Sunday Independent, IOL) combining food-affordability and circular-packaging goals through a genuinely innovative weigh-and-pay retail format. Its reusable-container deposit system, developed with GIZ and Plastics SA, is a concrete and directly relevant reuse/deposit-return initiative, though scale remains modest (a handful of outlets as of 2025) and no independent impact metrics on the deposit system's uptake or waste-diversion volumes were found.

Priority: ★★★★ High (verified deposit-return/refill model directly addressing both circular packaging and food affordability in underserved communities)

Verification Notes

Seed data correctly described the pay-and-weigh bulk food model and reusable containers with deposit; both are independently confirmed, with the deposit-based reusable-container system specifically documented via the GIZ Circular City Labs partnership (with Plastics SA as local implementer). Founding narrative is somewhat split across sources: Wakanda Food Accelerator was founded in 2020, and Gcwalisa is described as a COVID-era pivot from Kubheka's earlier ventures, but the first physical outlet did not open until April 2022 — both dates are presented above for clarity. Outlet-count figures (originally "opening 25 others" as a plan, later "5 additional outlets" as of 2025) come from different points in time and should not be conflated as the same claim.
