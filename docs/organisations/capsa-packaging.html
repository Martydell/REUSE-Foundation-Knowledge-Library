Organisation Overview

Embalajes Capsa, S.L., trading as Capsa Packaging, is a privately held Spanish company founded in 1998 in Canet de Mar, Barcelona, by Manel López (CEO) and Olga Vidal (commercial director). The company designs, manufactures, and licenses corrugated-cardboard packaging solutions, with its flagship product being the patented Capsa 2in1® reusable box, which combines a shipping box and a storage/carrying container in one dual-closure design. The company holds a substantial patent portfolio (75 patents reported in trade press) and operates both direct sales and a licensing model with other cardboard manufacturers. Named commercial clients include MediaMarkt, Tous, Leroy Merlin, Acer Ibérica, and Würth.

Business Model

Capsa Packaging is a B2B manufacturer and licensor of reusable corrugated-cardboard packaging, primarily the Capsa 2in1® box line (variants include Duo, Plus, Carry, e-Flex, and eZ-Postal, plus reusable pallet boxes) sold to e-commerce, retail, and logistics companies as an alternative to single-use shipping boxes. The core product uses a patented dual-closure system enabling the same box to function as shipping packaging and then be reused as a storage or carrying container, intended to be reused multiple times before disposal/recycling. Beyond direct sales, the company licenses its patented technology to other cardboard manufacturers, extending its reach without direct production. Its sustainability positioning is backed by a third-party Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) commissioned via Texsospack (a Packaging Cluster / AEI Tèxtils / Catalan Waste Agency / Leitat Technologies collaborative project), published in 2024, comparing six single-use boxes against one Capsa 2in1® box reused six times.

Products & Services

Capsa 2in1® reusable corrugated cardboard boxes (Duo, Plus, Carry, e-Flex, eZ-Postal variants) and reusable pallet boxes; patent licensing program for other cardboard manufacturers; packaging consulting/customization services.

Customers

B2B — e-commerce, retail, and logistics companies. Named clients per company materials: MediaMarkt, Tous, Leroy Merlin, Acer Ibérica, Würth, and Ignion.

Partnerships

Texsospack LCA study collaborators: Packaging Cluster, AEI Tèxtils, Agència de Residus de Catalunya (Catalan Waste Agency), and Leitat Technologies (research/technology center) — verified via company and trade press sources. Client relationships with MediaMarkt, Tous, Leroy Merlin, Acer Ibérica, Würth, and Ignion are described as commercial customers rather than formal partnerships.

Measurable Impact

Per a 2024 Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) conducted via the Texsospack project (Packaging Cluster, AEI Tèxtils, Catalan Waste Agency, Leitat Technologies), comparing six conventional single-use boxes against one Capsa 2in1® box reused six times at a 50 km transport distance: 80% reduction in carbon footprint, 84% reduction in water consumption, and 82% reduction in fossil fuel resource consumption, versus single-use cardboard boxes. The company states results remain favourable at greater distances (100, 500, 1000 km) though exact figures at those distances were not detailed in sources reviewed. Source: Packaging Europe (Sustainability Awards 2024 finalist interview) and Capsa's own sustainability page.

Technology

Patented dual-closure box design (75 patents reported) enabling the same corrugated box to switch between sealed shipping mode and open storage/carrying mode; no digital tracking technology identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Capsa Packaging is a well-established (founded 1998), Spain-based manufacturer with a genuinely verified reusable-packaging product and a credible, third-party-conducted LCA substantiating its headline "80% less CO2" claim — a stronger evidence base than many packaging sustainability claims. Its B2B model, patent portfolio, and named enterprise clients (MediaMarkt, Leroy Merlin, Würth) indicate real commercial traction. The main gap for reuse-tracking purposes is the absence of a formal company-run collection/reverse-logistics loop — reuse currently depends on the end customer choosing to reuse the box rather than a closed-loop return system. Overall a strong, verifiable candidate for the knowledge library.
