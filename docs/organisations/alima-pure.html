Organisation Overview

Alima Pure was a natural/mineral cosmetics company founded in Portland, Oregon in 2004 by Kate O'Brien. It manufactured mineral-based makeup — foundation, concealer, eyeshadow and related products — sold in refillable compact cases with swappable magnetic product pans, alongside standalone product refills, positioning refill as a core part of its packaging strategy. The company held Certified B Corporation and Leaping Bunny (cruelty-free) certifications. As of this research pass, the company's website displays a permanent closure notice; Alima Pure is no longer operating.

Business Model

Alima Pure sold direct-to-consumer via its own e-commerce site (alimapure.com) and through third-party natural-beauty retailers (e.g., Beauty Heroes, The Nature of Beauty, Laurel & Reed, Safe & Chic), targeting consumers seeking natural, cruelty-free, and lower-waste cosmetics. Its core reuse mechanism was the refillable compact: customers purchased a durable compact case once and then bought replacement product "pans" or powder refills to insert into the same case, reducing the amount of new packaging generated per repurchase compared to buying a new compact each time. The company was woman-founded (by Kate O'Brien, who started production in her home in 2004) and woman-led throughout its operating life, and held Certified B Corporation status (as Alima Cosmetics, Inc.) and Leaping Bunny cruelty-free certification. No revenue or company-size figures were found in verified public sources; the company's homepage now displays only a closure announcement thanking customers for "21 amazing years of support," with no detailed statement on the reasons for closure, exact closure date, or disposition of the business.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Alima Pure is a relevant, well-documented historical example of a refillable-packaging model in the cosmetics category — a durable compact reused across multiple product refills — and held credible third-party sustainability certifications (B Corp, Leaping Bunny) over a 20+ year operating history. However, the company has now ceased trading, which substantially limits its ongoing relevance as an active reuse-infrastructure case study; it is better catalogued as a closed/historical example than as a current practitioner. The seed tracker's country designation ("Canada") is not supported by any verified source, which consistently places the company in Portland, Oregon, USA — this should be corrected in the master list.
