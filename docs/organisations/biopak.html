Organisation Overview

BioPak is an Australian-founded manufacturer and supplier of certified compostable food-service packaging (cups, containers, cutlery, bags), established in Sydney in 2006 by Richard Fine (a former plastics-industry executive from South Africa) and Gary Smith. The company has grown to serve a reported 10,000+ venues across Australia, New Zealand, Singapore, the UK and the US, with named enterprise customers including McDonald's, Woolworths, Aldi, Qantas and Commonwealth Bank cited in press coverage. BioPak is B Corp certified and operates Compost Connect, a commercial composting collection service that diverts used compostable packaging and food waste from landfill in serviced areas.

Business Model

BioPak's core business is B2B manufacturing and wholesale/direct supply of certified-compostable single-use food-service packaging (hot/cold cups, lids, straws, food containers, bowls, clamshells, cutlery, napkins, bags) to cafes, quick-service restaurants, event caterers, coffee roasters and distributors, including custom-branded packaging with design services. Beyond packaging sales, BioPak operates Compost Connect, a commercial organics-collection service in select markets that collects used compostable packaging and food waste from customer sites for industrial composting, closing the loop on the material's end-of-life claim. The company has also grown via acquisition (e.g., Bygreen/Strong Straws/One Tree Cutlery, per press reporting) and donates a stated 5% of profits to a "Give Back Fund."

Products & Services

Certified compostable food-service packaging (cups, lids, straws, containers, bowls, clamshells, cutlery, gloves, paper/bioplastic bags), custom branding and packaging design services, and Compost Connect commercial composting collection.

Customers

B2B: quick-service restaurants, cafes, coffee roasters, event caterers and distributors. Press coverage names large enterprise customers including McDonald's, Woolworths, Aldi, Qantas and Commonwealth Bank; company materials cite over 10,000 served venues.

Partnerships

Acquisitions reported in Australian business press: Bygreen (Sunshine Coast), including its Strong Straws and One Tree Cutlery brands. BioPak is a certified B Corp and holds AS 4736/AS 5810 compostability certification and FSC chain-of-custody certification (FSC C110879). No specific reuse-sector partnerships were identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

As the seed data itself flags, BioPak's core activity — single-use compostable packaging — is adjacent to, but distinct from, reuse: compostables are a linear "make once, use once, decompose" model, not a "use many times" reuse model. Its Compost Connect collection service is a genuine circular/material-recovery infrastructure investment worth noting, but the company's primary revenue and product identity remain single-use packaging replacement, not reusable packaging, refill systems, or deposit-return. For a REUSE Foundation library specifically scoped to reuse, BioPak's relevance is limited to contextual/comparative value (illustrating the compostables-vs-reuse distinction) rather than as a core reuse case study.
