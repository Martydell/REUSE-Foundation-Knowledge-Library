Organisation Overview

reCIRCLE Ireland is Ireland's first reusable takeaway food-container deposit scheme, brought to University College Cork (UCC) by the environmental non-profit VOICE Ireland, in partnership with catering company KSG and academics from UCC's Cleaner Production Promotion Unit. It licenses the reCIRCLE reusable-container system (originated by reCIRCLE AG, Switzerland) for a campus setting.

Business Model

UCC food outlets offer takeaway food in reCIRCLE reusable containers; customers pay a deposit, refunded when the container is returned to a participating outlet for washing and reuse. The pilot was funded through the UCC Living Lab Fund as a research-backed demonstration project rather than a standalone commercial operation.

Products & Services

Reusable takeaway food containers used at UCC campus food outlets under a deposit-and-return model, based on the reCIRCLE container system.

Customers

University College Cork students, staff and visitors using participating on-campus food outlets.

Partnerships

VOICE Ireland (lead non-profit), University College Cork (Cleaner Production Promotion Unit, Living Lab Fund), catering company KSG; licenses the reCIRCLE brand/system originated by reCIRCLE AG (Switzerland).

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available (framed by VOICE/UCC as a demonstration project; Ireland's high per-capita plastic consumption, ~61kg/year versus EU average, cited as pilot motivation rather than a measured outcome of the scheme itself).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A useful campus-scale pilot demonstrating reusable deposit-container feasibility in Ireland, licensed from a proven European system, though still limited to a single-university demonstration stage with no public scale-up or impact data yet. Priority: ★★★ Medium
