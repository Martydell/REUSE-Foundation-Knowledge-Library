Organisation Overview

Hispack is a biennial trade fair held at Fira de Barcelona's Gran Via venue, dedicated to packaging technology, machinery, labelling/bottling, logistics/automation and brand packaging. It is organized by Fira de Barcelona, the same institution behind Mobile World Congress, and bills itself as one of the leading packaging fairs in Southern Europe and a commercial gateway between Iberian, Mediterranean and Latin American markets. It is an event, not a manufacturer or service provider, and does not itself produce or sell reuse products.

Business Model

Hispack is a B2B trade-show event, not a commercial enterprise producing goods or reuse services. Fira de Barcelona generates revenue through exhibitor stand fees, sponsorships, visitor/delegate program fees, and associated services (catalogues, matchmaking, themed exhibition routes such as "Hispack Unboxing" and "Best in Class"). Its core value proposition is connecting packaging machinery/material suppliers, brand owners, and logistics providers to showcase innovations — including circular-economy and sustainable-packaging solutions — and facilitate B2B deals. The event runs biennially, with the 2024 edition hosting 900+ exhibitors from many countries and the 2027 edition (its 20th) expected to be its largest yet. Because it is an event rather than a company, it has no products/customers of its own in the traditional sense — its "customers" are exhibiting companies and trade visitors.

Products & Services

Trade exhibition space, exhibitor programming, industry conferences/talks on packaging innovation and circular economy, matchmaking/networking services, and thematic showcases (e.g., sustainable and circular packaging routes) for exhibitors spanning food/beverage, cosmetics, pharma, chemical and industrial sectors.

Customers

Packaging machinery manufacturers, material/substrate suppliers, brand owners (food, beverage, cosmetics, pharma), logistics/automation companies, and trade visitors/buyers, primarily from Spain, the wider Mediterranean, and Latin America.

Partnerships

Not publicly available as formal named partnerships beyond its parent organization, Fira de Barcelona; exhibitor lists change per edition (2024 edition drew exhibitors from 28 countries) but no specific strategic partners were verifiable from the sources reviewed.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available. 2024 edition figures cited by the organizer: 900+ exhibitors (some secondary sources reference 50+/100+ brand figures for specific sub-sections), but no independently audited circularity or waste-reduction impact metrics were found.

Technology

Not applicable — Hispack is an event platform, not a technology provider.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Hispack is correctly classified as a trade fair/event rather than a company; it should not be profiled as an operating reuse business. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation is as an industry convening point where circular-economy packaging technology is showcased and discussed, useful for tracking sector trends, exhibitor lists, and emerging reuse-packaging suppliers rather than as a direct subject of impact assessment.
