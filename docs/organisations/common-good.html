Organisation Overview

Common Good is a plant-based soap, detergent, and cleaner brand founded in 2010 by Sacha Dunn (an Australian-born founder based in Brooklyn, New York), built around reducing single-use plastic through in-store refill infrastructure. The company operates more than 100 physical refill stations at retail partners, where customers can bring back and refill bottles rather than purchasing new packaged product each time, alongside conventional retail and wholesale product sales.

Business Model

Common Good's core reuse mechanism is its retail refill-station network: rather than operating its own standalone stores, it partners with over 100 retail locations to host refill stations where customers refill their own bottles with Common Good's plant-based cleaning and soap formulas, reducing packaging waste per repeat purchase. Alongside this refill network, the company sells pre-packaged product through conventional retail and wholesale channels (including via platforms like Faire for wholesale buyers), giving it a hybrid refill-plus-retail revenue model rather than being refill-exclusive.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Common Good is a well-established (15+ year) refill-focused cleaning brand with real physical retail-network scale (100+ refill stations), giving it solid relevance as an example of a refill business that has grown a genuine partner-retailer footprint rather than remaining a single-location or online-only operation. Its founder's origin story (personal motivation to combat single-use plastic waste) and sustained multi-year operation support reasonable confidence in its ongoing relevance.
