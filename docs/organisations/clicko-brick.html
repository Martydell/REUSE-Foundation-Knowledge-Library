Organisation Overview

Clicko-Brick is an Israeli startup, founded in 2015 by Yaron Toren and Gilead Meroz, that redesigns used plastic liquid containers into interlocking modular building blocks. The containers connect via a firm mechanism in all directions (including top and bottom) and are available in square, hexagonal, and triangular shapes ranging from 250ml to 4.5 litres, fillable with air, liquids, or granular solids to give the resulting structure different properties — targeting applications in construction, agriculture, and floating structures.

Business Model

Clicko-Brick's model is industrial/material reuse: rather than recycling used plastic containers into raw material for new products, the containers themselves become the reusable building unit via an interlocking connector design, potentially serving construction, agricultural, and marine/floating-structure use cases. The company appears to be at an early or startup stage; no specific manufacturing scale, customer base, revenue model, or named commercial partners were confirmed in sources accessed during this research pass.
