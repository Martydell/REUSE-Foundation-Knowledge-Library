Organisation Overview

Lamazuna was founded in 2010 in France by Laëtitia Van de Walle, then 25, after she replaced disposable make-up remover pads with washable microfibre alternatives. The company grew from that single reusable product into a full zero-waste beauty brand, launching the bamboo Oriculi reusable ear stick in 2013 and solid shampoo, toothpaste and deodorant from 2014. Since mid-2018 Lamazuna has been headquartered on a 6-hectare eco-site in Marches, in the Drôme — France's leading organic-farming region — combining offices with a micro-farm and on-site childcare.

Business Model

Lamazuna designs, manufactures and sells reusable and solid personal-care products intended to permanently replace disposable single-use equivalents — washable make-up-remover pads instead of disposable cotton rounds, a reusable bamboo ear-cleaning stick instead of single-use cotton swabs, and solid shampoo/toothpaste/deodorant bars instead of liquid products in plastic bottles. Its revenue model is direct-to-consumer and retail product sales (own website, French pharmacies/organic shops, and international distributors such as Green-Shop and Hakuna Taka), rather than a service or subscription model. Lamazuna differentiates on formulation (100% zero-waste product design from inception, PETA Cruelty-Free certification — the first French brand to hold it — and Slow Cosmétique labelling) and on vertical integration, having built its own eco-campus for production, R&D and staff facilities. The founder has since 2022 also launched a related circular-cosmetics venture (Andromée), reflecting continued expansion of the founder's zero-waste cosmetics ecosystem.

Products & Services

Washable microfibre make-up remover pads, the Oriculi reusable bamboo ear stick, solid shampoo bars, solid toothpaste, solid deodorants, and other zero-waste personal-care and beauty accessories.

Customers

Environmentally conscious consumers in France and internationally (retail and online), reached through Lamazuna's own e-commerce site and specialist organic/zero-waste retailers across Europe.

Partnerships

Distribution partnerships with organic and zero-waste retail networks (e.g. Green-Shop, Hakuna Taka, Boutique Zéro Déchet); certified by PETA (Cruelty-Free) and Slow Cosmétique.

Measurable Impact

As of October 2021 (company-reported, per Lamazuna blog/press): over 400,000 single-use plastic toothbrushes avoided; over 700,000 plastic deodorant sticks saved; over 58 million disposable cotton pads avoided through use of Lamazuna's reusable/solid products.

Technology

E-commerce platform (lamazuna.com) with content/blog for consumer education; no proprietary reuse-tracking or logistics technology identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Lamazuna is a well-established, verifiably impactful French reuse brand with over a decade of operation, credible third-party certifications, and company-reported (though not independently audited) waste-avoidance metrics. Its core offering — reusable and solid alternatives to disposable personal-care items — is directly aligned with the REUSE Foundation's mission, and its scale (national retail distribution, international export) gives it above-average relevance versus smaller single-location refill shops.
