Organisation Overview

Lock & Lock (trading as LocknLock) is a large, publicly listed South Korean household-products manufacturer founded in 1978, known globally for its patented four-side locking system for airtight, leak-proof plastic food containers. Headquartered in Seoul, it exports to 119 countries through 11 overseas sales units and 87 directly operated overseas stores, with reported 2021 revenue of approximately US$418 million. Since 2020 it has run "Love for Planet," an ongoing take-back and upcycling campaign that collects customers' old airtight containers and repurposes the plastic into household items, industrial materials, and public-facility products, partnering with firms such as Hanwha TotalEnergies and CJ Logistics.

Business Model

LocknLock's core business is manufacturing and selling household plastic and stainless-steel storage containers, cookware, tumblers and water bottles, sold globally through its own retail stores, e-commerce, and wholesale/export channels as a listed, for-profit consumer-goods company. Layered onto this core business is its "Love for Planet" ESG/CSR campaign, launched in 2020, under which consumers return old airtight containers to LocknLock collection points; the company then works with industrial partners to convert the recovered plastic into new materials — for example, partnering with Hanwha TotalEnergies to process material into eco-friendly automobile parts, and with upcycling company Ujoolove to make household items installed in a public village. Separately, in partnership with logistics firm CJ Logistics, LocknLock has manufactured modular recycling pallets from leftover plastics for use in logistics operations, converting roughly 10 tonnes of recovered plastics into an estimated 300 reusable pallets. These initiatives function as brand-building, take-back and material-recovery programmes layered on top of a conventional consumer-goods manufacturing and retail business, rather than as the company's primary revenue model.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Take-back ("Love for Planet" collection of used airtight containers)
  • Material Recovery (upcycling collected plastic into new materials/products)
  • Industrial Reuse (modular recycling pallets made from recovered plastics, with CJ Logistics)
  • Consumer Education (campaign messaging encouraging container return/reuse awareness)

Products & Services

Airtight food storage containers, cookware, tumblers and water bottles (core commercial product range); "Love for Planet" container take-back and upcycling campaign; upcycled outputs including automobile parts (with Hanwha TotalEnergies), household items (with Ujoolove), and modular logistics recycling pallets (with CJ Logistics).

Customers

Global consumer market for household kitchenware and storage products across 119 export markets; campaign participants are existing LocknLock product owners in South Korea.

Partnerships

  • Hanwha TotalEnergies (recycling collected plastic into automobile parts)
  • Ujoolove (upcycling company; converted waste plastic into household items installed in Dongcheon-dong village)
  • CJ Logistics (co-development of modular recycling pallets from recovered plastics)

Measurable Impact

  • Approximately 20,000 airtight containers collected in the most recent "Love for Planet" campaign cycle reported, with around 3,830 participants (The Asia Business Daily, 2026)
  • 10 tonnes of recovered plastics upcycled into approximately 300 recycling pallets with CJ Logistics (company-reported, via LocknLock official news page)
  • 2021 company revenue approximately US$418 million (general company scale, not campaign-specific)
  • No independently audited, campaign-wide cumulative totals (e.g., total containers collected since 2020) were found.

Technology

None beyond standard plastics-recycling/upcycling processes carried out with industrial partners; no proprietary digital tracking system identified for the take-back programme.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

LocknLock is a large, publicly listed, well-established manufacturer whose "Love for Planet" take-back and upcycling campaign is independently corroborated by trade press (The Asia Business Daily) and the company's own official news releases naming specific industrial partners (Hanwha TotalEnergies, Ujoolove, CJ Logistics). This gives it more third-party verification than several other organisations in this batch. However, the take-back/upcycling programme is a CSR-style adjunct to a large conventional consumer-goods manufacturing business rather than a circular-economy-first operating model, and reported impact figures (20,000 containers, ~300 pallets) are modest relative to the company's overall scale and remain company-reported rather than independently audited.

Priority: ★★★ Medium (large, verified, publicly listed manufacturer with a genuine but modest-scale take-back/upcycling programme; core business is not circular by design)
