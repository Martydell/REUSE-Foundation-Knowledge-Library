Cia Muller

Brazilian spirits producer (Cachaça 51) operating a large-scale returnable-glass-bottle reverse-logistics system, with roughly 57% of packaging returnable.

Organisation Overview

Companhia Müller de Bebidas is a large Brazilian beverage producer best known for the Cachaça 51 brand, producing over 100 million bottles annually and exporting to 60+ countries. Distinct from marketing-only sustainability claims, the company operates a substantial physical reverse-logistics system: approximately 57% of its packaging is returnable, with roughly 20 trucks arriving daily nationwide to deliver used bottles back to the factory for sanitization, inspection, and refilling.

Business Model

Cia Müller's returnable-bottle system gives around 1,500 distributor/retail clients an "asset account" in containers, replenished as bottles are returned — creating a commercial incentive for distributors and retailers to organise their own collection networks, which in turn engage independent bottle collectors and waste-picker cooperatives. The factory's unloading capacity for returned containers (936,000 litres/day) is reported as nearly double its filling capacity, indicating the return stream is a core logistics function rather than a marginal side activity. In 2025 the company partnered with reverse-logistics startup Circulagem to further improve bottle-collection logistics for the Cachaça 51 line, and works with roughly ten glass-recycling cooperatives in the greater São Paulo area as part of the collection network.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reverse Logistics
  • Deposit Return (via distributor "asset account" replenishment mechanism)
  • Reusable Packaging

Products & Services

Cachaça (multiple lines, including Cachaça 51), conhaques, ready-to-drink cachaça-based beverages, vodka — primarily sold in returnable glass bottles for the core Cachaça 51 line.

Customers

Approximately 1,500 distributor and retail clients in Brazil; export markets in 60+ countries (export packaging is not necessarily returnable).

Partnerships

  • Circulagem (reverse-logistics technology/startup partner, 2025)
  • Approximately ten glass-recycling cooperatives in greater São Paulo

Measurable Impact

~57% of packaging returnable (company-reported); 95% of Cachaça 51 specifically produced with returnable packaging (2020 reporting) — later reporting cites 90%; ~20 trucks/day nationwide delivering returned bottles; unloading capacity of 936,000 litres/day (company-reported, undated to a specific year in sources accessed). No independently audited figures were found.

Technology

Not publicly available in detail — reverse logistics is coordinated through distributor account-based incentives rather than a described consumer-facing app or tracking system.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Cia Muller is a strong example of industrial-scale returnable-packaging logistics embedded in a mainstream consumer beverage business, with a genuinely large physical collection network (daily truck deliveries, cooperative partnerships, majority-returnable packaging on its flagship product) rather than a niche pilot. Its relevance is somewhat tempered by the absence of independently audited figures and limited transparency on founding history, but the operational scale is well-documented via independent trade press.

Verification Notes
