Organisation Overview

Verdemar is a regional supermarket chain operating multiple stores in Belo Horizonte, Minas Gerais, positioned as a higher-end grocery retailer with an emphasis on variety, fresh/organic produce, and prepared foods. It is consistently noted by customers as not supplying single-use plastic shopping bags, instead packing purchases with staff assistance into customer-brought or store-sold reusable bags.

Business Model

Verdemar operates as a full-service supermarket chain generating revenue through conventional grocery retail (produce, wine, prepared foods, organic goods), differentiated from discount competitors by service quality and product variety. Its reuse-relevant practice is a store-wide policy of not distributing disposable plastic bags at checkout, instead encouraging customers to bring or purchase reusable bags, with staff assistance packing groceries — a policy embedded in standard operations rather than a separate reuse business line.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Verdemar's reusable-bag policy is corroborated by numerous independent customer reviews, but this is one practice within a conventional supermarket business rather than a purpose-built reuse enterprise. The seed's framing as a company centred on "returnable shopping bags" overstates the reuse focus — bag reuse is a checkout policy, not the core business model. Modest relevance as a mainstream-retail reuse example.
