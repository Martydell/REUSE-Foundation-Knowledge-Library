Organisation Overview

Excess Materials Exchange (EME) is an Amsterdam-based digital platform founded in 2017 by Maayke-Aimée Damen and Christian van Maaren. It matches companies' surplus materials and waste streams with other businesses seeking inputs, aiming to find the highest-value reuse option for each material rather than defaulting to recycling or disposal. After several years of piloting, EME's digital platform officially launched in June 2020. A central feature is the "Resource Passport" — a digital identity record for materials (composition, origin, condition) that supports AI-assisted matchmaking between supply and demand.

Business Model

EME operates a B2B marketplace/matching model: businesses list surplus materials, by-products or decommissioned assets, and EME's platform — combining an AI-powered matching engine with human material-flow expertise — identifies counterparties who can reuse those materials at the highest value point in the hierarchy (reuse over recycling, recycling over disposal). Revenue is generated through platform/matching services and the Resource Passport digital-identity product, which has also been adopted as a reference concept within Dutch and EU circular-economy policy discussions. The company has raised outside investment (reported around $5M in a funding round) to scale its platform and geographic reach across Europe.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Excess Materials Exchange is a credible, well-documented B2B industrial-reuse platform with a genuinely novel digital tool (Resource Passport) that has gained policy-level recognition in the Netherlands and EU. It is a strong example of digital-technology-enabled industrial reuse/material recovery, though quantified impact data (tonnes diverted, materials matched) was not found in the sources reviewed here and should be sought directly from the company for a fuller assessment.
