Organisation Overview

Meadow is a Swedish packaging-technology startup, founded in 2020 and based in Stockholm (with a London office), that has reengineered the standard aluminium beverage can into a refillable format branded MEADOW KAPSUL™. Aluminium cans (available from 250ml to 568ml) are filled with products such as shampoo, soap or other liquid consumer goods and inserted as swappable cartridges into a reusable dispenser, combining the widely recyclable material properties of aluminium with a refill-based reuse system. Meadow licenses its technology to can manufacturers, dispenser producers and co-packers rather than manufacturing directly, and has partnered with major industry players including Ball Corporation to bring the system to hospitality and personal-care markets.

Business Model

Meadow operates a licensing/technology-partner model rather than manufacturing and selling finished products directly: it embeds its MEADOW KAPSUL™ technology into the existing production workflows of established can body and can end manufacturers, dispenser producers, and co-packers, who then produce and distribute refillable aluminium-can-based packaging for brand-owner customers in categories such as home care, personal care and hospitality. The core innovation is a modified aluminium beverage can (standard format, 250-568ml) that functions as a refill cartridge, paired with a reusable dispenser (offered as either a fully custom design, with a 6-8 month lead time, or a modular/standard platform with a 10-12 week lead time). Because the packaging is built on the standard aluminium can format, it is designed to remain compatible with existing recycling infrastructure at end of life, differentiating it from bespoke refill-pouch or proprietary-container systems. Meadow's most significant commercial partnership is with Ball Corporation, a major global can manufacturer, targeting the hospitality sector with an aluminium refill dispenser system, alongside a factory-scaling partnership with Fillsy to expand refillable aluminium can production.

Products & Services

MEADOW KAPSUL™ refillable aluminium can cartridges (250-568ml, sleek and standard formats); custom and modular reusable dispenser hardware; technology-licensing services for can manufacturers, dispenser producers and co-packers.

Customers

Brand owners in home care, personal care/cosmetics and hospitality sectors seeking refillable packaging formats; can manufacturers, dispenser producers and co-packers who license Meadow's technology into their own production lines.

Partnerships

Ball Corporation (alliance to produce fully recyclable aluminium cans for home and personal care, targeting hospitality with a refill dispenser system); Fillsy (new factory partnership to scale refillable aluminium can production); Stephen Gould Corporation (supplier/packaging-industry collaboration, per company communications).

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available as a company-specific, dated metric. Contextual industry figures cited by the company include an average 60-day turnaround for aluminium cans to return to shelves through standard recycling, and a global market context of more than 400 billion beverage cans used annually; these are industry-context statistics rather than Meadow-specific reuse impact data.

Technology

Reengineered aluminium can-end/cartridge mechanism (MEADOW KAPSUL™) enabling refill across multiple product categories while remaining compatible with existing aluminium recycling infrastructure; custom and modular reusable dispenser hardware platforms.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Meadow presents a genuinely novel refill-system innovation — using the ubiquitous, highly recyclable aluminium can as a refillable cartridge rather than inventing a new packaging format from scratch — and has secured a credible, large-scale industry partnership with Ball Corporation, indicating real commercial traction beyond a pilot stage. Its licensing-based business model means impact will depend on adoption by brand owners and manufacturers rather than Meadow's own direct-to-consumer volumes, and no dated, company-specific impact metrics are yet publicly available. It is a strong, technically distinctive case for the REUSE Foundation's refill-systems category.
