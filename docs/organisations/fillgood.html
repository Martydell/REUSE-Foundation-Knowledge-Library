FillGood

A Wellington, New Zealand reusable-serviceware system providing cups, plates, and trays for events, replacing single-use items with a delivery-collection-wash-return loop.

Organisation Overview

FillGood is a Wellington-based reusable-serviceware system for events, venues, and hospitality businesses, developed by a cross-sector working group and operated as an initiative of Sustainability Trust, a Wellington charitable trust. Officially launched in 2024 after several years of feasibility work, FillGood supplies reusable cups, plates, trays, and bowls to festivals, stadiums, and other events, and manages their delivery, collection, commercial washing, tracking, and reporting. Its stated mission is to make reusable serviceware "the new standard" and eliminate single-use disposables at large public events across New Zealand's capital.

Business Model

FillGood provides a turnkey reusable-serviceware service to event organisers, venues, and hospitality operators: it supplies branded or generic reusable cups, plates, trays, and bowls, then handles logistics — delivery to the event, collection afterward, industrial washing (via a commercial-grade wash facility and a mobile wash trailer for on-site cleaning), tracking of item circulation, and post-event reporting to clients. Revenue comes from service fees charged to event organisers and venues, tiered by package/service level rather than per-unit retail sales. FillGood operates as a non-profit-affiliated programme rather than a conventional for-profit company, reinvesting through its parent trust and a "Wash Trailer Club" knowledge-sharing network that helps other regions (e.g., Sustainable Hawke's Bay) replicate the model. It has served large-scale events including stadium events and festivals with thousands of attendees, reporting a 92% average return rate of items across 300+ events.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging (cups, plates, trays, bowls for events)
  • Sharing (shared serviceware pool across multiple events/venues)
  • Reverse Logistics (delivery, collection, and return coordination)
  • Digital Tracking (tracking and reporting of item circulation/return rates)
  • Waste Reduction (displacement of single-use event disposables)
  • Consumer Education (promoting reuse as the default for event serviceware)

Products & Services

  • Reusable cups, plates, trays, and bowls for events and venues
  • Delivery and collection logistics for event serviceware
  • Commercial-grade washing (fixed wash facility plus mobile wash trailer)
  • Circulation tracking and post-event impact reporting
  • "Wash Trailer Club" knowledge-sharing network supporting replication in other regions

Customers

Event organisers, festivals, stadiums/venues, and hospitality businesses in the Wellington region (reported service to 300+ events); replication support extended to other New Zealand regions via the Wash Trailer Club network.

Partnerships

  • Sustainability Trust Wellington (parent charitable trust, CC35964)
  • Wellington City Council (public reporting/promotion of FillGood at Wellington events)
  • Sustainable Hawke's Bay (related reusable-serviceware initiative via the Wash Trailer Club network)
  • A Wellington cross-sector working group of hospitality, events, zero-waste, and venue-sector representatives that helped design the system

Measurable Impact

  • 92% average return rate of reusable items across 300+ events (source: FillGood/Sustainability Trust website, figures as of website access July 2026, not independently audited)
  • Over 100,000 reusable items reported in active circulation (source: FillGood/Sustainability Trust website)
  • Recognised as a finalist/winner in the "2025 Emerging Services" category at the Gold Awards (Wellington business awards)

Technology

Item-tracking and reporting system for monitoring circulation and return rates of serviceware across events (specific technology platform/vendor not disclosed in public sources).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

FillGood is a well-designed, non-profit-backed reuse infrastructure model for event serviceware, distinguished by cross-sector collaborative design (hospitality, venues, and zero-waste groups), a working wash-and-return logistics system, and credible reported return rates (92% across 300+ events). Its non-profit/charitable-trust structure and replication mechanism (the Wash Trailer Club) suggest genuine intent toward scaling reuse across New Zealand regions rather than remaining a single-city pilot. As a young programme (launched 2024) it lacks a long track record and independently audited impact data, and its reach remains regional (Wellington) with only nascent replication elsewhere. It is a strong example of collaborative, charity-backed reusable serviceware infrastructure for the events sector.
