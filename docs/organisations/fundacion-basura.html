Chilean non-profit foundation combating the climate crisis through zero-waste education, policy advocacy, and food-recovery programmes at open-air markets.

Organisation Overview

Fundación Basura is a Chilean non-profit founded in 2015 by Macarena Guajardo after she observed the contrast between German and Chilean waste management practices while studying sustainable architecture, and after witnessing severe waste problems in Haiti. Its mission is to protect planetary health through responsible waste management, working toward a "Zero Waste Circular Economy" where materials are re-inserted into new cycles via ecodesign, reuse, recycling, composting and biodigestion. The foundation operates through three pillars: environmental education, political advocacy, and paid consulting services (Zero Waste Action Plans, circular-economy assessments, greenwashing evaluations) for businesses and municipalities.

Business Model

As a non-profit foundation, Fundación Basura funds its operations through a mix of consulting fees (Zero Waste Action Plans and circular-economy diagnostics sold to companies and municipalities), grants, and partnerships with government and international bodies. It holds seats on Chilean policy committees relevant to the country's Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) Law and the national Circular Economy Roadmap 2040, giving it a direct advocacy channel into legislation. Its flagship applied programme, "Ferias Libres Cero Desperdicio" (Zero Waste Free Markets), intervenes at Santiago and Valparaíso open-air produce markets (ferias libres) to recover unsold fruit and vegetables that would otherwise go to landfill, redistributing edible surplus to community organisations and processing the remainder through composting or animal feed. A companion programme, "Sabores Sin Desperdicio" (Flavors Without Waste), runs workshops teaching women heads of household, community leaders and entrepreneurs to cook with normally discarded parts of produce (peels, stems, leaves), combining food-waste reduction with food-security and livelihood goals.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Waste Reduction (food-waste recovery from open-air markets; composting/animal-feed diversion)
  • Consumer Education (Zero Waste workshops, talks, courses, community cleanups; "Flavors Without Waste" cooking workshops)
  • Material Recovery (organic waste diverted to composting and municipal composting systems)
  • Other (policy advocacy on EPR law and Circular Economy Roadmap 2040; paid Zero Waste consulting/greenwashing-assessment services for businesses)

Products & Services

Zero Waste Action Plans and circular-economy consulting for companies/municipalities; greenwashing evaluations; environmental education workshops and courses (in-person and online); the Ferias Libres Cero Desperdicio market-intervention programme; Flavors Without Waste cooking workshops; an entrepreneur/business network focused on zero-waste practices.

Customers

Businesses and municipalities purchasing consulting services; market vendors and municipal market administrators (feria libre interventions); community organisations receiving recovered food; women, community leaders and entrepreneurs attending workshops; policymakers engaged through advocacy work.

Partnerships

  • GAIA (Global Alliance for Incinerator Alternatives) / No-Burn — published a September 2024 case study on Fundación Basura's food-waste market model
  • BASF Chile — co-listed as a partner on the "Ferias Libres Cero Desperdicio: Conectar para Transformar 2024" initiative (via Pacto Global Chile / UN Global Compact network)
  • Chilean Ministry of Environment (Ministerio del Medio Ambiente) — referenced in relation to national composting and circular-economy policy work
  • Participation in Chilean government committees on the Extended Producer Responsibility Law and Circular Economy Roadmap 2040

Measurable Impact

  • Zero Food Waste Farmers Markets model implemented since 2021, with 35 interventions carried out in Santiago and Valparaíso free markets (as of the GAIA/No-Burn case study, September 2024).
  • In two pilot sessions, over 3,300 kg of food waste was recovered; 217 kg went to a community pot serving 180 people weekly, with the remainder processed via municipal composting (GAIA/No-Burn case study, 2024).
  • Cumulative programme figures cited by the foundation: more than 30 tons of organic waste managed, avoiding an estimated 18 tons of CO2 and 3 tons of CH4 emissions (self-reported, undated aggregate figure on official website).

Technology

Not publicly available (no distinct proprietary digital platform identified; programme delivery is via in-person market interventions, workshops and consulting).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Fundación Basura is a well-established, decade-old Chilean NGO with verifiable, third-party-documented impact (GAIA/No-Burn case study) in food-waste recovery and composting at open-air markets, combined with genuine policy-advocacy influence on Chile's national circular-economy framework. Its activities sit more in waste reduction, composting and consumer education than in "reuse" narrowly defined (repeated use of the same product/packaging), but its market-based food-recovery model and consulting services are directly relevant to the broader circular-economy mission of the REUSE Foundation's knowledge library.

Priority: ★★★ Medium (credible, verified NGO impact in food-waste/composting and circular-economy advocacy; limited direct connection to packaging/product reuse specifically)
