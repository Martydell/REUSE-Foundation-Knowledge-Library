Organisation Overview

GRIN is a Norwegian circular-economy technology company founded in 2018 in Oslo. It designs and manufactures smart collection machines — branded Revore™ — that use image recognition and machine learning to identify and validate returned reusable items such as food boxes and takeaway cups, similar to a reverse vending machine but generalized to many packaging types. GRIN positions itself as a hardware-and-software infrastructure provider for reuse operators, brands, and foodservice chains that want to run their own reusable packaging or deposit-return schemes without building collection infrastructure themselves.

Business Model

GRIN sells (or provides via a hybrid purchase/subscription model) its Revore™ smart collection machines to reuse operators, retailers, and foodservice businesses, paired with a software layer for fleet management, item recognition, and data reporting. Machines use onboard cameras and AI models (GRIN reports very high recognition accuracy in its own materials) to identify a wide range of non-hazardous packaging items dropped into the unit, validate the return, and trigger any associated deposit refund or loyalty reward. Revenue appears to combine hardware sales/leasing with recurring software/service fees. Clients include large quick-service restaurant chains — the company's largest known deployment is with one of the world's largest QSR chains, which has installed GRIN machines across its Netherlands locations to collect reusable deposit cups. GRIN is also a partner in the EU Horizon Europe "DiCE" (Digital Health in the Circular Economy) project alongside Johnson & Johnson and Philips, extending its collection technology into medtech device take-back, with pilots planned in Belgium, Slovenia and Spain.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

GRIN provides genuine reuse-enabling infrastructure — its core product exists specifically to make reusable packaging collection frictionless for operators and consumers, which is a foundational capability for scaling reuse systems generally. Its expansion beyond food packaging into medtech take-back (with credible large partners like J&J and Philips) suggests a broadly applicable technology platform rather than a single-vertical product. Verified deployment numbers (100+ machines, 1M+ items) are modest but demonstrate real-world traction rather than concept-stage claims. Priority rating: ★★★★ High.
