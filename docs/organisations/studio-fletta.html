Studio Fletta (Flétta)

Icelandic circular design studio transforming discarded materials into high-value products.

Organisation Overview

Founded in 2018 by product designers Birta Rós Brynjólfsdóttir and Hrefna Sigurðardóttir after research beginning in 2014 into where local industrial waste ends up, Flétta transforms discarded materials — including retired Icelandair uniforms and airbags — into unique, high-value design products.

Business Model

Design-studio model: sources waste streams from local industries (airlines, manufacturing) as raw material, then designs and crafts limited-run consumer products (cushions, accessories) sold at a premium reflecting the craftsmanship and circular-design narrative, rather than mass-market retail.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Material Recovery
  • Industrial Reuse
  • Consumer Education

Products & Services

Design objects and accessories crafted from repurposed industrial waste (e.g. airline uniforms, airbags).

Customers

Design-conscious consumers and collaborators (e.g. Polestar, FÓLK) in Iceland and internationally.

Partnerships

Icelandair (uniform material source), FÓLK x Polestar (Airbag cushion collaboration).

Measurable Impact

Icelandic Design Award winner (Pizza Time, Airbag cushion); featured as Maison&Objet Rising Talent.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

An award-winning, well-documented circular design studio converting industrial waste into desirable products — a strong material-recovery/industrial-reuse showcase, if niche in scale. ★★★★ High
