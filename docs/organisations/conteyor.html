Organisation Overview

conTeyor was founded in 1995 by Jos van Bree in Belgium, developing a textile-based hanging/folding packaging system originally designed to protect delicate automotive parts through repeated reuse cycles. In October 2024, conTeyor merged with Germany's KTP Kunststoff Palettentechnik GmbH (founded 1988 by Horst Wintrich, originally a recycling operation for a Ford plant before evolving into reusable foldable-container systems) to form a combined group with approximately 1,000 employees and roughly €200 million in sales, combining KTP's foldable-container expertise with conTeyor's textile inner-packaging expertise.

Business Model

The combined conTeyor Group–KTP designs and manufactures B2B returnable/reusable packaging and storage systems — primarily foldable containers and textile-based protective inner packaging — sold to industrial customers (notably automotive supply chains, per both companies' origin stories) as an alternative to single-use industrial packaging. Revenue comes from sales/leasing of reusable packaging systems to manufacturers. The 2024 merger combined two previously separate mid-sized European reusable-packaging manufacturers under shared private-equity-adjacent ownership (Gilde and VR Equitypartner as respective backers) into a larger group with greater scale and combined product range (foldable containers + textile inner packaging).
