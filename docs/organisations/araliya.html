Organisation Overview

Araliya Packaging Lanka is a Sri Lankan manufacturer of non-woven polypropylene (PP) bags, established in 2021 as a packaging subsidiary of the diversified Araliya Group (founded 1994). It was originally created to supply durable packaging for the group's flagship Araliya Rice product line and has since expanded to serve external retail and industrial customers across Sri Lanka and export markets. The company positions its non-woven bags as eco-friendly, long-lasting alternatives to single-use plastic packaging for shopping, retail, and bulk food distribution.

Business Model

Araliya Packaging Lanka is a B2B manufacturer producing custom non-woven PP bags — including D-cut bags with bottom gussets, W-cut shopping bags, 5kg/10kg rice bags, box bags, and laminated bags — using in-house spun-bond fabric production and Flexo, Screen, and Gravure printing methods. The company supplies both its parent group (notably Araliya Rice packaging) and third-party retail clients including major Sri Lankan supermarket and retail chains. It emphasises custom-built machinery for consistent fabric quality and markets its non-woven bags as durable, reusable, and economical compared with single-use plastic bags. The facility holds ISO 45001 (occupational health and safety) and ISO 14001 (environmental management) certification, and the company states it has invested in an upgraded quality-control laboratory. No revenue, production volume, or employee-count figures are publicly disclosed.

Products & Services

Non-woven polypropylene shopping bags (D-cut, W-cut), 5kg and 10kg rice bags, laminated box bags, and custom-printed non-woven packaging in various sizes, designs and colours for retail and food distribution.

Customers

B2B — retail and supermarket chains (including Cargills, SPAR, Damro, and Arpico per company materials) and the parent Araliya Group's own food brands (notably Araliya Rice); also serves export markets, per the company's Sri Lanka Export Development Board listing.

Partnerships

Not publicly available beyond its confirmed relationship as a subsidiary of the Araliya Group and stated retail clients (Cargills, SPAR, Damro, Arpico); no formal third-party sustainability or NGO partnerships were found.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available — no quantified figures for bags produced, plastic displaced, or emissions/waste reduction were found on the company website or in press coverage.

Technology

State-of-the-art, custom-built non-woven fabric spinning and printing machinery (Flexo, Screen, Gravure); an upgraded in-house quality-control laboratory was announced via press coverage. No consumer-facing digital tracking or app technology identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Araliya Packaging Lanka is a credible, ISO-certified B2B manufacturer of durable non-woven reusable bags serving major Sri Lankan retailers, giving it real supply-chain relevance to reuse-packaging infrastructure in South Asia. However, it is young (est. 2021), part of a larger conglomerate rather than a dedicated reuse enterprise, and publishes no measurable environmental impact data. It is a reasonable regional case study for reusable packaging manufacturing but not a leading or well-documented reuse innovator.
