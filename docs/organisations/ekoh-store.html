Organisation Overview

EKOH-Store (Ekohstore NZ Limited) is a small, privately owned New Zealand company, incorporated in Auckland in November 2020, selling reusable and refillable household, personal-care and pet-care products online. Its range includes refillable glass spray/pump bottles, Swedish dishcloths, reusable produce bags, beeswax-style wraps, and natural/vegan personal-care items under its "HealthyClean" and EKOH lines. The company markets itself as "100% NZ owned and operated" and a family business, ships internationally (site references NZ, Australia, Canada, US and UK storefronts), and positions its concentrated, refillable products as both plastic-reducing and lower-cost per use.

Business Model

EKOH-Store sells directly to consumers through its own e-commerce site and marketplace-style storefronts, generating revenue from repeat sales of reusable hardware (glass bottles, spray/pump heads, produce bags) paired with concentrated refill formulations for cleaning and personal-care products. The model relies on customers reusing durable glass/bottle components indefinitely while purchasing consumable refills or concentrates, reducing single-use plastic packaging per use compared to conventional bottled products. As a small direct-to-consumer brand, it does not operate physical retail locations or an in-store refill/dispensing system; its "refill" proposition is centred on shipped concentrate/refill products rather than local dispensing infrastructure. The company states support for environmental causes (tree planting, wildlife rescue) as part of its brand positioning, and describes itself as a family-run start-up expanding via direct sales and wholesale enquiries.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

EKOH-Store is a small, young (est. 2020) direct-to-consumer brand rather than an infrastructure-scale reuse system — its "refill" model is product-level (durable bottle + shipped concentrate) rather than a local dispensing or return-loop network. It is a legitimate example of reusable packaging design for household/personal care but offers limited scalability lessons or measurable system impact for the REUSE Foundation's knowledge base at this stage. Worth monitoring for growth but not currently a priority case study.
