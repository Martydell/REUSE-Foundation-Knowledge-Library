Organisation Overview

GoBEBA is a Nairobi-based Kenyan startup founded in 2019 by Peter Ndiang'ui (a former OLX general manager) and Lesley Mbogo (a former Amazon head of product). It is an e-commerce platform for "unpiped" bulky household utilities — principally drinking water and cooking gas (LPG) — that are conventionally distributed via bulky, refillable containers requiring two-way delivery trips. GoBEBA built a distributed network of AI-enabled micro-fulfillment hubs across Nairobi to shorten this loop, enabling customers to order refills online and receive delivery to their doorstep in under an hour, while efficiently managing the reverse logistics of collecting and refilling empty containers.

Business Model

GoBEBA operates a "fulfilment as a service" e-commerce model: rather than owning the entire supply chain end-to-end, it built a technology stack and network of small micro-fulfillment stores (hubs) distributed across Nairobi neighbourhoods, using an AI-enabled inventory-management system to predict demand and optimise stock levels at each hub. Customers order refillable essentials (water, cooking gas) via GoBEBA's platform; because these products are inherently a two-way exchange (empty containers must be picked up when full ones are delivered), the company's core innovation is minimising the logistics cost and delivery time of this reverse-logistics loop, targeting under-one-hour delivery. Revenue comes from product margins and, increasingly, commissions from offering its fulfilment/hub infrastructure "as a service" to other bulky-goods providers. The company is largely self-funded, supplemented by early-stage angel investment, an accelerator, and — as of April 2024 — a $200,000 investment from Madica, alongside support from Praxis and DevelopPPP grants, to fund expansion beyond Nairobi into Kenya's other fast-growing cities and eventually East and Central Africa (with Kinshasa and Lusaka cited as future targets).

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (refillable cooking gas and drinking water containers)
  • Reverse Logistics (collection of empty containers integrated into delivery routes via micro-fulfillment hubs)
  • Digital Tracking (AI-enabled inventory management across distributed hub network)

Products & Services

On-demand delivery of refillable drinking water and cooking gas (LPG); office pantry-essential delivery; a distributed micro-fulfillment hub network; "fulfilment as a service" infrastructure offered to other bulky-goods distributors.

Customers

Urban households and offices in Nairobi ordering refillable water and cooking gas; potentially other essential-goods distributors purchasing GoBEBA's fulfilment infrastructure as a service.

Partnerships

  • Madica — $200,000 equity investment (April 2024), part of first funding round
  • Praxis (Praxis Portfolio Ventures) — investor
  • DevelopPPP (German development-finance programme) — grant funder
  • Impact Foundation — portfolio/investor relationship

Measurable Impact

  • Revenue grew approximately five-fold in the year to May 2023, with minimal additional capital investment (company-reported via Disrupt Africa).
  • Nearly reached break-even as of May 2023 (company-reported).
  • Monthly active customers doubled over the same period, with roughly 70% being repeat customers (company-reported).
  • Secured $200,000 in equity funding from Madica in April 2024, part of a plan to expand into 4 additional Kenyan cities.
  • Longer-term ambition to expand into 30 of Africa's fastest-growing cities, including Kinshasa and Lusaka (stated plan, not yet achieved as of available reporting).

Technology

AI-enabled inventory-management system optimising stock distribution across a network of micro-fulfillment hubs; e-commerce ordering platform with sub-one-hour delivery logistics for refillable bulky essentials.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

GoBEBA is a credible, venture-backed Kenyan startup (Madica, Praxis, DevelopPPP) with independent press coverage (Disrupt Africa) confirming its refillable-essentials delivery model and reverse-logistics innovation. Its focus on drinking water and cooking gas — both classically refillable, reusable-container categories — places it squarely within the reuse economy's refill-systems and reverse-logistics categories, with a scalable, technology-enabled model that has already demonstrated revenue growth and expansion plans. Impact metrics, while credible, remain self-reported and relatively early-stage (single-city operations as of the most recent verified reporting).

Priority: ★★★★ High (venture-backed, technology-enabled refill/reverse-logistics model with demonstrated growth and credible institutional investors)
