Organisation Overview

Ecotutu is a Nigerian cleantech startup founded in 2020 by Babajide Oluwase (CEO) and Michael Akinsete, providing off-grid, solar-powered cold storage units for perishable agricultural produce. Rather than selling cooling equipment outright, Ecotutu operates a "pay-as-you-chill" service model, deploying its units into open-air markets and farm clusters across Nigeria to reduce post-harvest food loss for smallholder farmers and traders who otherwise lack access to capital-intensive refrigeration. The company has been recognised in several cleantech/social-enterprise competitions, including as a 2021 finalist for the UN World Food Programme's Zero Hunger Challenge and a 2022 winner of Zenith Bank's FutureForward Tech Fair.

Business Model

Ecotutu addresses Nigeria's high rate of post-harvest food loss by installing solar-powered cold storage units — using phase-change/evaporative cooling technology capable of maintaining temperature for up to three days with minimal or no sunlight — at markets and farm clusters, then charging farmers, traders and small agribusinesses a low, flexible usage fee (reported as low as $0.50/day) only when produce is actually being stored or transported, rather than requiring upfront capital investment in a fridge or generator. This "cooling-as-a-service" or rental/shared-infrastructure model removes the barrier of high upfront refrigeration costs for smallholders, extending the shelf life of fruit and vegetables and reducing spoilage losses across the supply chain. The seed description of the units as "refillable" was not corroborated — the verified business model centres on shared/rented access to fixed cold-storage infrastructure (a service/rental model) rather than a refill or consumable-replenishment product.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Sharing (shared solar cold-storage infrastructure accessed on a pay-per-use basis)
  • Rental (equipment/infrastructure rental rather than ownership)
  • Waste Reduction (reduction of post-harvest food loss/spoilage)

Products & Services

Solar-powered, off-grid cold storage units deployed in open markets and farm clusters; "pay-as-you-chill" subscription/usage-based access for farmers, traders and agribusinesses; cold-chain logistics support for perishable produce transport.

Customers

Smallholder farmers, produce traders and small agribusinesses in Nigerian agricultural markets (e.g., Mile 12 Market, Lagos) and farm clusters across multiple Nigerian states.

Partnerships

Recognition/award relationships: UN World Food Programme Zero Hunger Challenge (2021 finalist), Global Entrepreneurship Summit (2022 top-three finalist), Zenith Bank FutureForward Tech Fair (2022 winner, N20 million prize). No named commercial distribution or investment partners were confirmed in this pass beyond these award bodies.

Measurable Impact

Secondary press coverage (not a dated primary Ecotutu impact report) states the company has impacted over 6,000 farmers and helped preserve approximately 70,000 tonnes of produce cumulatively, and separately that in 2025 it moved/stored approximately 18,000 tonnes of perishable produce, preventing roughly 13,000 tonnes of food loss. These figures vary in specificity and dating across sources and should be treated as approximate pending confirmation from Ecotutu's own reporting.

Technology

Solar-powered phase-change/evaporative cooling technology enabling cold storage to persist for up to three days with minimal or no continuous power input; off-grid deployment suited to markets without reliable electricity.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ecotutu is a genuine shared-infrastructure/rental-access model — farmers pay to use cold storage rather than owning it — which fits a "sharing economy" reuse framing (maximising utilisation of a durable asset) even though the seed's "refillable" descriptor does not match the verified model. Its social impact on food-loss reduction in an emerging-market agricultural context is credible and independently reported, though quantified figures are not from primary dated sources. Relevance to the Foundation is moderate: it is asset-sharing/rental infrastructure rather than packaging reuse or refill.

Seed data described Ecotutu's units as "refillable solar-powered cold storage units for agricultural use."
