Organisation Overview

ZERO PC is a Yokohama-based social enterprise project run by People Port Inc. that refurbishes used laptops into "ethical PCs" while training and employing refugees living in Japan as upcycling technicians. It combines environmental goals (reducing e-waste and CO2) with a social mission of refugee livelihood support.

Business Model

People Port collects used laptops and computer parts, refurbishes and upcycles them into resold "ZERO PC" branded computers, powered by 100% renewable energy at its facility. Refugees are hired and trained in refurbishment/upcycling skills that do not require Japanese fluency (English and math suffice), building transferable expertise. Revenue comes from sales of refurbished computers, alongside stated ambitions to scale refugee employment and expand internationally.

Measurable Impact

Stated goal to employ up to 100 refugees; roughly 3 million computers are discarded annually in Japan (context figure, not a Zero PC-specific impact metric).

A distinctive dual-mission model combining electronics reuse/repair with refugee employment — high relevance as an example of social-impact-driven reuse, though it remains a small-scale operation with limited public impact metrics. Priority: ★★★★ High
