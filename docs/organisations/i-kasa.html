Organisation Overview

iKasa is an umbrella-sharing service launched in 2018 in Tokyo by Nature Innovation Group. Users rent durable, glass-fibre umbrellas via the iKasa smartphone app, scanning a QR code at umbrella stands located in stations, office buildings, and other public spaces, paying 140 yen per use or 280 yen/month for a subscription. The service has grown to over 1,600 locations across the Tokyo metropolitan area plus Kansai, Nagoya, and Fukuoka, with approximately 600,000 members.

Business Model

iKasa operates a pay-per-use or subscription umbrella-rental model: users scan a QR code via the app to unlock a durable umbrella from a stand, use it, and return it to any participating stand (not necessarily the same one) — a "one-way" sharing model similar to bike-share systems. Revenue comes from per-use fees (140 yen) and monthly subscriptions (280 yen). The company partners with property owners, transit operators, and commercial buildings to host umbrella stands, and cites a notably high return rate for its umbrellas.

Not specifically named beyond general references to hosting locations (stations, office buildings, commercial facilities); featured in a City of Yokohama Circular Economy case study and Tokyo Metropolitan Government's "2030 Zero Disposable Umbrellas Project."

Measurable Impact

1,600+ locations; ~600,000 members (company-reported, per press coverage). Reported as having a notably high (cited as near-100%) return rate for shared umbrellas (Elite Readers coverage). No independently audited waste/CO2 figures were found.

Technology

QR-code scanning via smartphone app for umbrella unlock/return and payment processing.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

iKasa is a well-documented, multi-city sharing-economy reuse service with substantial verified scale (1,600+ locations, ~600,000 members) and recognition from Tokyo Metropolitan Government's own disposable-umbrella-reduction initiative — a strong example of "sharing" as a distinct reuse mechanism applied to an everyday low-cost item category not typically associated with circular economy solutions.
