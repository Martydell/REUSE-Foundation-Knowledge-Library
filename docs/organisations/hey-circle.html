Organisation Overview

hey circle is a Munich-based startup founded in 2021 by Doris Diebold, Morris Kurz, Christian Krawczyk, and Rebecca Steinke, offering reusable shipping packaging — boxes and bags in eight sizes (XS–XXL) — as a drop-in replacement for single-use cardboard cartons in e-commerce and logistics. Its packaging is designed for at least 50 reuse cycles and is intended to be used "like cardboard" but returned, cleaned, and recirculated. The company raised a €1.5 million seed round (announced January 2024) and works with logistics carriers and online retailers across Germany and Austria.

Business Model

hey circle sells/provides B2B reusable packaging (foldable boxes and bags made from recyclable materials) to online retailers and logistics companies as a substitute for disposable cardboard. Packaging is shipped to end consumers as normal parcels; once received, consumers fold and return the empty packaging via partnering postal/parcel carriers (DHL, DPD, GLS, UPS, Österreichische Post) for cleaning, inspection, and recirculation back into the pool — a pooled reusable-packaging-as-a-service model rather than per-item ownership. An optional IT platform provides "consignment-accurate" digital tracking of packaging inventory, return rates, and CO₂/waste savings data for retailer ESG reporting. Named retail customers include DRYKORN, WMF, Interzero, SÜWAG, VRESH, pricon, and ADP Merkur, alongside logistics partnerships with DPD (which announced favouring hey circle's reusable packaging in its network) and other major carriers. Revenue is generated from retailers/brands paying for packaging-as-a-service rather than one-off carton purchases, with the value proposition centred on waste and emissions reduction plus (per company figures) lower long-run packaging costs.

Customers

Online retailers and logistics/parcel companies in Germany and Austria, including named clients DRYKORN, WMF, Interzero, SÜWAG, VRESH, pricon, ADP Merkur, and Trigema; company states 40+ customers as of its 2022 market launch period.

Partnerships

Verified logistics/carrier partnerships: DHL, DPD, GLS, UPS, Österreichische Post (Austrian Post). DPD Germany has publicly stated it favours hey circle's reusable packaging in its network. Retail partnerships as listed above (DRYKORN, WMF, Interzero, SÜWAG, VRESH, pricon, ADP Merkur, Trigema).

Measurable Impact

Per company/Ökopol life-cycle-assessment figures (undated in available sources): 94% waste reduction and 76% CO₂ emission reduction (Box L model) versus single-use cardboard, and packaging designed for a minimum of 50 reuse cycles; one source also cites a 53% packaging cost reduction. These figures derive from company-referenced LCA work rather than an independently dated public report, so should be treated as company-sourced rather than fully independently audited.

Technology

Optional IT platform providing shipment-level inventory tracking, return-rate/logistics optimisation, and CO₂/waste savings data for ESG reporting. No public evidence found of RFID or QR-code-level tracking specifics.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

hey circle is a clear, well-documented example of B2B reusable packaging-as-a-service for e-commerce, with real named retail and logistics partners (including a major carrier, DPD, publicly endorsing the model) and a credible reuse-cycle design (50+ trips). Funding, customer names, and carrier integration are independently corroborated across multiple sources, giving reasonable confidence in the business model description. Impact figures come from company/LCA sources rather than independently audited reports. This is a strong, directly on-thesis case study for reusable shipping packaging.
