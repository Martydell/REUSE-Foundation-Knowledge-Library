Organisation Overview

REUSO provides a checkout/return system for reusable polypropylene food containers used by restaurants, museums, and venues in the Chicago area. Customers scan a QR code, receive a text-message PIN, and check out a container; a $1 service fee covers washing and technology costs, with containers reusable over 1,000 times.

Business Model

REUSO operates a B2B software-and-hardware-as-a-service model: restaurants, museums, and venues (e.g., Lincoln Park Zoo, Wild Onion Brewery & Banquets) subscribe to REUSO's container-tracking system, paying for containers and washing infrastructure. Consumers pay a small per-use service fee and have a return window (about 14 days) before additional charges apply. REUSO launched its first dedicated software product in 2023, following incubation support that reportedly began around 2020 (including from Shedd Aquarium).

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging (polypropylene containers reused 1,000+ times)
  • Digital Tracking (QR code/SMS-based checkout and return system)
  • Reverse Logistics (washing and redistribution to partner venues)
  • Consumer Education (waste-reduction messaging to consumers and partners)

Products & Services

QR-code-based reusable food container checkout/return system, plus washing and redistribution logistics, sold as a subscription service to restaurants, museums, and venues.

Customers

Restaurants, museums, zoos, and event venues in the Chicago area, and their end-consumer diners/visitors.

Partnerships

Lincoln Park Zoo and Wild Onion Brewery & Banquets are cited as onboarded venues; Shedd Aquarium supported early concept development.
