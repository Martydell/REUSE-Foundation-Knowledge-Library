Ecocitex

Chilean social enterprise converting post-consumer textile waste into recycled yarn without using water or dyes, while employing formerly incarcerated women.

Organisation Overview

Ecocitex is a Chilean social enterprise founded by Rosario Hevia and Daniela Ehijo, who discovered old spinning machinery in 2019 and by January 2020 had acquired a mill in the Macul district of Santiago to industrially recover textiles too damaged for resale. The company converts post-consumer clothing waste into 100%-recycled yarn, produced without water or dyes — a globally distinctive process — while running a social-reintegration employment programme for women, including formerly incarcerated women, who help sort and process the material. It also operates a retail storefront, e-commerce, and B2B/wholesale channels.

Business Model

Ecocitex generates revenue across several channels: direct-to-consumer e-commerce and a physical retail location (MUT, Las Condes) selling recycled yarn (in "Rústica," "Vanguardia," and "Florence" product lines), textile cones/cords, accessories (bags, cases, hats), home décor, and recycled fabric filling; B2B services for corporate clients seeking textile-recycling solutions; wholesale distribution to resellers; and a membership programme ("Club Ecocitex") offering promotions and early access in exchange for recurring engagement. Input material is collected as donated or purchased used clothing, then classified by material, colour, and condition — wearable items are resold while damaged items are mechanically processed into fibre and respun into yarn, closing the loop without water-intensive dyeing. The social mission is integrated directly into operations via an employment/reintegration programme for women.

Measurable Impact

Per company-published and USACH-study figures (cumulative, undated as to exact measurement period, reported as of source publication ~2022–2023): 234 metric tons of textile diverted from landfill; 1,357 metric tons of carbon footprint mitigated (per USACH study); 269,000 garments reused; 8,145 garments donated; 18 people participated in the social reintegration employment programme.

Technology

Reclaimed/repurposed industrial spinning machinery for mechanical fibre processing and yarn spinning; process notably omits water and synthetic dyes, distinguishing it from conventional textile recycling.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ecocitex is a strong, well-documented example of textile circularity combined with social impact: it has third-party academic validation (USACH carbon study), independent press coverage (País Circular, Forbes Chile, AmoSantiago), an NGO/impact-investor relationship (NESsT), and a Wikipedia entry, giving it more verifiable public credibility than most organisations in this batch. Its water-free, dye-free recycled yarn process is a genuinely differentiated technical approach, and the integration of a social employment programme for vulnerable women adds a dual-impact dimension relevant to holistic circular-economy assessment. Recommended as a strong case-study candidate.
