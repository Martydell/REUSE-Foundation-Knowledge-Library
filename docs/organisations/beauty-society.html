Organisation Overview

Beauty Society is a US skincare and cosmetics company founded in 2004 by Jeannie Lorin, headquartered in Las Vegas, Nevada. It operates as a direct-sales (multi-level marketing) business: independent "Advisors" sell products and build their own customer networks and downline teams in exchange for commissions, rather than the company selling through a conventional retail marketplace. Seed data's framing of the company as a "sustainable beauty marketplace" is not accurate — Beauty Society is a direct-selling/MLM company, and "marketplace" appears to reflect marketing language rather than the company's actual commercial structure.

Business Model

Beauty Society's core revenue model is multi-level direct selling: independent Advisors purchase products at wholesale and sell to consumers, earning commissions on personal sales and on sales made by Advisors they recruit into their downline, per the standard MLM/direct-selling structure described in industry trade sources (e.g., Modern Direct Seller, Business For Home). Layered onto this direct-selling structure is a refill programme: customers can purchase product refills for select items rather than new full packaging each time, positioned by the company as a waste-reduction feature, with an associated tree-planting incentive ("for every refill purchased, we plant a tree") run through the verified environmental-restoration platform Veritree, covering reforestation, agroforestry and marine-restoration projects across nine countries.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems — dedicated refill product category allowing repurchase of product without new full packaging
  • Consumer Education — company communications framing refills and reduced packaging as a sustainability commitment
  • Other — tree-planting incentive (via Veritree) tied to refill purchases, which is an environmental offset/restoration mechanism rather than a reuse activity per se, noted here for completeness

Formal deposit-return, take-back, or reverse-logistics infrastructure was not identified in sources reviewed — the refill programme functions as a repurchase-format option rather than a return-and-refill loop with used packaging coming back to the company.

Products & Services

Skincare and cosmetics products (including a "12-month money-back guarantee"), sold via independent Advisors, with a dedicated refill product line and biodegradable shipping packaging; an "Advisor" business-opportunity programme forms a parallel service line (recruitment and compensation-plan participation) alongside product sales.

Customers

Individual consumers purchasing skincare/cosmetics products through independent Advisors across the United States; prospective and current Advisors participating in the company's direct-selling compensation plan.

Partnerships

Veritree (verified tree-planting/environmental-restoration platform tied to the refill-purchase incentive). No other named commercial or NGO partnerships were found in sources reviewed.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available. The company states a tree is planted "for every refill purchased" via Veritree, and cites recognition as "15th most eco-friendly beauty company in the world" (per seed/company-adjacent sources), but no independently verified quantitative figures (number of trees planted, refills sold, packaging avoided) with a specific year were found in sources reviewed.

Technology

Standard e-commerce platform supporting Advisor storefronts and refill product ordering; no dedicated reuse-tracking technology was identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Beauty Society's relevance to the REUSE Foundation's focus is limited and should be scoped carefully: its primary business model is multi-level direct selling, and its refill programme — while a genuine product feature — is a secondary element of a company whose core structure (recruitment-based commission selling) is not itself a circular-economy activity. The seed data's "marketplace" framing was not supported by research and has been corrected. Given the absence of independently verified impact data and the company's primary identity as an MLM business rather than a reuse-focused enterprise, its priority for the library is low, though the refill product line itself could merit a narrower, product-level note if the library later develops product-level (rather than organisation-level) entries.
