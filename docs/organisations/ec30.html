Organisation Overview

EC30 is a cleaning-products brand developed within Procter & Gamble's P&G Ventures innovation incubator, first launched around 2019 and later repositioned under the "Clean 3.0" banner (2023) as the successor to Ivory Soap (1879) and Tide (1946) in P&G's cleaning-product lineage. Instead of liquid products shipped in plastic bottles, EC30 sells dry, compact single-use pads/strips — for body wash, hand wash, shampoo, conditioner, laundry detergent and toilet-bowl cleaner — that activate into a usable cleaning product on contact with water, shipped direct-to-consumer in recyclable cardboard packaging.

Business Model

EC30 sells via direct-to-consumer e-commerce (ec30clean.com) rather than traditional retail shelf space, shipping recyclable cardboard boxes containing individual dry pads/swatches instead of heavy, plastic-bottled liquids. The brand's stated model is to eliminate plastic bottles, cut shipping weight/volume (and associated transport emissions), and remove the need for warehouse/retail space devoted to bulky liquid products, while its manufacturing process spins active cleaning chemistry into soluble fibre pads. Product lines include laundry detergent (rated "Best Overall Laundry Detergent Sheet" by Good Housekeeping in past testing), toilet-bowl cleaner, shampoo, conditioner, body wash and hand wash, sold individually or in a "Try It All" starter kit. As a P&G-incubated brand, EC30 functions as a corporate innovation/sustainability play testing an ultra-low-waste, packaging-light format rather than as an independent reuse or refill company — there is no container return, deposit or refill-at-retail mechanism; waste reduction comes purely from format substitution (dry pad vs. liquid-in-plastic-bottle) plus recyclable secondary packaging.

Products & Services

Water-activated cleaning pads/strips: laundry detergent, toilet-bowl cleaner, shampoo, conditioner, body wash, hand wash; sold as individual product lines or as a multi-product "Try It All Kit" (58 total uses across products).

Customers

US direct-to-consumer households purchasing via ec30clean.com.

Partnerships

None publicly documented beyond internal P&G development (P&G Ventures incubator). One unrelated 2020s product-line note references PureCycle recycled polypropylene use in an EC30 shower dispenser accessory, but this is a materials-sourcing detail, not a reuse partnership.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available — P&G/EC30's own marketing claims "up to 50% reduction in CO2 emissions from manufacturing, chemistry and shipping compared with traditional liquid cleaning products" (per company/press materials), but no independently verified, dated quantitative figures (e.g., total plastic avoided, water saved) were found on the official site.

Technology

Patented P&G fibre-spinning technology that embeds active cleaning chemistry into soluble woven pads/strips; no digital tracking or logistics platform identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

EC30 is a packaging-substitution / waste-reduction innovation (single-use dry pads replacing single-use liquid-in-plastic-bottle) rather than a reuse, refill or deposit-return system, so it sits at the margin of the REUSE Foundation's core reuse mandate. It is a useful example of large-FMCG-corporate innovation aimed at reducing packaging mass and shipping emissions, but does not involve container reuse, refill infrastructure, or take-back.
