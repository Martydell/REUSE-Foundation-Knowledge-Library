TOMRA (Reusable Takeaway Packaging / REUSEABLE Aarhus)

Norwegian reverse-vending and circular-technology group operating the world's first city-wide reusable takeaway packaging system, piloted in Aarhus, Denmark.

Organisation Overview

TOMRA, the Norwegian reverse-vending machine pioneer founded in 1972, launched "REUSEABLE" — the world's first open, city-wide system for reusable takeaway packaging — in Aarhus, Denmark in January 2024, using its Rotake collection infrastructure, deposit incentives and a central sanitization facility.

Business Model

B2B2C infrastructure provider: TOMRA supplies automated collection machines, deposit-return logistics, and industrial sanitization/reverse-logistics services to city and retailer partners; consumers pay a deposit on takeaway packaging at purchase, refunded in full when returned to any participating collection point.

Products & Services

Automated packaging collection machines (Rotake), deposit-return system infrastructure, industrial cup/container sanitization, and an open multi-retailer webshop for packaging resupply.

Customers

Municipalities, cafes, restaurants and takeaway retailers in Aarhus, Denmark (with further city expansions).

Partnerships

City of Aarhus; multiple local retailers and packaging providers within the open REUSEABLE system.

Measurable Impact

Over 1.8 million reusable cups collected and an 88% average return rate after two years of the Aarhus pilot (as of TOMRA's own reporting).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A world-leading, publicly listed reuse-infrastructure operator with strong, independently reportable impact data from a live city-scale deposit-return pilot — a flagship reference case for reusable packaging systems. ★★★★★ Essential
