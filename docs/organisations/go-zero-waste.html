Organisation Overview

Go Zero Waste is a mobile app founded in Barcelona, Spain, by Martí Morató and Magda Cebrián, and published in May 2020 in partnership with Sparsity Technologies, a spin-off of the Data Management Group at the Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC). The app provides an interactive, crowdsourced map that lets users locate nearby shops, markets and services offering plastic-free or zero-waste alternatives — including bulk/refill stores, package-free markets, thrift and secondhand shops, and repair, reuse and recycling services. Although the seed data associated Go Zero Waste with Argentina, verified sources show it is a Spanish-founded app that, as of its 2020 launch, already operated across nine countries including Spain, Chile, Argentina, the UK, Netherlands, France, Belgium, Andorra, Germany and Italy — Argentina is one of its operating markets, not its country of origin.

Business Model

Go Zero Waste operates as a free-to-use, crowdsourced discovery app: any user can add a new zero-waste business to the map, and the resulting collaboratively maintained database is searchable by product category, location or shop name. The app's stated monetisation direction (per its developer, CIT UPC) includes a "Plus" tier and functionality allowing shopkeepers to manage their own listing information and offer discounts to app users, suggesting a freemium/business-listing revenue model layered on top of the free consumer-facing map. Its core value proposition is directory/discovery infrastructure for the zero-waste retail sector rather than direct provision of reusable packaging, refill or take-back services itself — it is an enabling technology that helps consumers find businesses which do offer those services.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Digital Tracking (crowdsourced, geolocated mapping of zero-waste/plastic-free businesses)
  • Consumer Education (user challenges and awareness content promoting sustainable, low-waste shopping habits)
  • Other (facilitation/directory role connecting consumers to bulk stores, repair services, and reuse/recycling points — does not itself operate reuse infrastructure)

Products & Services

Mobile app (iOS and Android) with an interactive map of zero-waste and plastic-free businesses; category filters for food/bulk stores, beverages, hygiene/cleaning products, household items, clothing/thrift, and repair/reuse/recycling services; a "Plus" tier; business-facing tools for shopkeepers to manage listings.

Customers

Individual consumers seeking zero-waste and plastic-free shopping options across Spain, Argentina, Chile, and six other countries (UK, Netherlands, France, Belgium, Andorra, Germany, Italy); zero-waste retail businesses as listed/featured entities; NGO partners (e.g., Fundación Lenga in Chile) using the app as a promotional tool for their own local initiatives.

Partnerships

  • CIT UPC (Centre d'Innovació i Tecnologia, Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya) — incubation/technology support
  • Sparsity Technologies — technology development partner, DAMA-UPC spin-off
  • Fundación Lenga (Chile) — uses Go Zero Waste's map to list zero-waste tourism ventures in Patagonia as part of its own "Tourism towards Zero Waste Chile" programme
  • Generalitat de Catalunya (Gencat) environment department — featured the app in sustainability content

Measurable Impact

  • Over 15,000 downloads as of its initial multi-country launch period (~2020, per CIT UPC).
  • Operating in 9 countries (Spain, Chile, Argentina, UK, Netherlands, France, Belgium, Andorra, Germany, Italy) as of 2020.
  • No independently verified, more recent (2024-2026) download, active-user or listed-business counts were found in public sources.

Technology

Crowdsourced mobile mapping application (iOS/Android) built with Sparsity Technologies (a graph-database/data-management spin-off of UPC's DAMA research group); interactive geolocation search by product category, location or business name.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Go Zero Waste is a legitimate and well-documented consumer-facing discovery tool that plays a genuine supporting role in the reuse/zero-waste ecosystem by helping consumers find bulk, refill, repair and reuse businesses — including as an explicit partner tool for other organisations in this research batch (Fundación Lenga). However, it is important to correct the seed data: Go Zero Waste is a Spanish app operating across multiple countries, not an Argentine organisation, and its own operations are limited to digital mapping/directory services rather than operating reuse infrastructure directly. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation is as an enabling technology and cross-reference point for other listed organisations, rather than as a reuse operator in its own right.

Priority: ★★★ Medium (credible, multi-country digital-mapping tool supporting the reuse/zero-waste ecosystem; not itself a reuse operator, and country attribution in seed data required correction)

Verification Notes

This is a material correction to the seed data, which listed Go Zero Waste's country as Argentina and its website as https://gozerowaste.app/. The website is confirmed correct, but the organisation's actual origin is Barcelona, Spain (founders Martí Morató and Magda Cebrián, developed with UPC-linked Sparsity Technologies), with Argentina being one of nine countries where the app operates rather than its home country. This distinction matters for a research-grade knowledge library attributing organisations to countries of origin. Download and country-coverage figures are from the app's ~2020 launch period; no more recent public metrics were found, so current scale should be treated as approximate.
