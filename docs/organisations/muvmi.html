Organisation Overview

MuvMi was founded in 2016 by Krisada Kritayakirana, Pipat Tangsiripaisan and Supapong Kitiwattanasak, launching its ride-hailing app for electric tuk-tuks in 2018. It operates a fleet of 600+ electric tuk-tuks across roughly 12 Bangkok neighbourhoods, connecting riders to mass-transit hubs.

Business Model

MuvMi runs an app-based, on-demand ride-sharing service using electric tuk-tuks, with route planning, fare-splitting and passenger-driver matching. It generates revenue from per-ride fares and also offers monthly rental tuk-tuks for businesses. The model is built around shared, electric last-mile transport rather than packaging or product reuse.

Products & Services

On-demand and monthly-rental electric tuk-tuk transport via a mobile app, primarily for last-mile connections to Bangkok's mass transit network.

Customers

Individual commuters and tourists in Bangkok; businesses renting tuk-tuks on a monthly basis.

Partnerships

Supported by Thailand's National Innovation Agency; fleet vehicles reported as locally manufactured electric tuk-tuks. Not publicly available in further detail.

Measurable Impact

600+ electric tuk-tuks in operation as of 2024, tripling fleet size amid rising demand (per Nation Thailand reporting).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

MuvMi is an electric vehicle ride-sharing/mobility-sharing service, not a reuse, refill, or packaging operation. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's circular-economy/reuse focus is limited to the general "sharing economy" category (vehicle sharing) rather than material or packaging reuse. Included for completeness but should be flagged as borderline scope.
