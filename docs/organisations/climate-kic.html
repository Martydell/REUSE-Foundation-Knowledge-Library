Organisation Overview

EIT Climate-KIC was established in 2010 as one of the European Institute of Innovation and Technology's first Knowledge and Innovation Communities, funded predominantly by the EU's EIT body. It brings together 400+ partners from business, academia, and the public/non-profit sectors to develop and scale climate-relevant innovation, including — but not limited to — circular-economy ventures. Its remit spans business acceleration, municipal climate-entrepreneurship support, leadership upskilling, and innovation-project funding across the whole climate-action space, of which circular economy/reuse is one strand rather than the central focus.

Business Model

Climate-KIC operates as a non-profit innovation intermediary rather than a company: it runs tailored acceleration programmes for climate ventures (including circular-economy-specific tracks such as the "Circular Cities ClimAccelerator" and "Built Environment Circular ClimAccelerator," the latter supporting 25 European start-up teams working on reuse, leasing, upcycling, and recycling of construction materials), collaborates with municipalities on local climate goals, and delivers education/upskilling programmes. Funding comes from the EIT (an EU body) rather than commercial revenue, and its "customers" are effectively the start-ups, municipalities, and institutions it supports rather than end consumers.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Industrial Reuse (via its Built Environment Circular ClimAccelerator's focus on reusing/upcycling construction materials)
  • Other (broad climate-innovation funding and acceleration, not specific to reuse)

Products & Services

Startup acceleration programmes (ClimAccelerator tracks, including circular-economy- and built-environment-specific cohorts); municipal climate-entrepreneurship support; climate/innovation leadership education programmes; innovation-project funding.

Customers

Climate and circular-economy start-ups (accelerator participants), municipalities, and public/private sector leadership seeking climate-innovation capacity building — not individual consumers.

Partnerships

400+ partner organisations across business, academia, public, and non-profit sectors (not individually itemised in sources accessed).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Climate-KIC's seed classification as "Refill and Reuse supply chain" focused is too narrow — it is a large, EU-funded, broad-mandate climate-innovation network for which circular economy (including some reuse-adjacent construction-material programmes) is one of many focus areas, not its primary identity. It is relevant to the REUSE Foundation library as a potential funding/partnership node for reuse start-ups rather than as a reuse operator itself.

Priority Rating: ★★ Low (broad climate-innovation mandate; reuse is a minor sub-programme, not the core focus)
