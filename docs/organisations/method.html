Method

US-born eco-cleaning brand sold in the UK, offering refill pouches and closed-loop packaging design under SC Johnson ownership.

Organisation Overview

Method was founded in 2001 in San Francisco by Eric Ryan and Adam Lowry to make design-led, non-toxic home and personal care products, selling its first item in February 2001. The brand expanded into the UK market and was acquired by SC Johnson in 2017 alongside Belgian eco-brand Ecover. In the UK, Method sells cleaning, hand soap, and laundry products, with a growing range offered in refill pouch formats designed to cut plastic and water/energy use versus rigid bottles, alongside bottles made from recycled recovered coastal plastic.

Business Model

Method sells direct-to-consumer online (methodproducts.co.uk) and through UK grocery and homeware retailers, generating revenue from bottled products and increasingly from refill pouches/concentrates that reduce plastic and shipping weight per use. As an SC Johnson subsidiary, Method benefits from parent-company manufacturing, supply chain, and sustainability R&D, including SC Johnson's global "recycled recovered coastal plastic" program (in partnership with Plastic Bank, active since 2018 across Indonesia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Brazil) which supplies input material for bottles. Method's UK refill push follows SC Johnson's broader UK concentrated-refill rollout announced in 2019 (first applied to the Mr Muscle brand). The brand's differentiation rests on design-forward packaging, plant-based formulations, and sustainability marketing rather than a distinct reuse-logistics network of its own.

Products & Services

Household cleaning products (surface cleaners, dish soap), hand soaps, and laundry detergents sold in standard bottles, concentrated refill pouches, and bottles incorporating recycled recovered coastal plastic.

Customers

UK consumers purchasing via methodproducts.co.uk and UK grocery/homeware retail partners; broader SC Johnson retail distribution network.

Partnerships

SC Johnson (parent company since 2017); Plastic Bank (coastal plastic collection since 2018, feeding recycled material into Method/SC Johnson bottles).

Measurable Impact

Method's refill pouches deliver an estimated 80% reduction in water, energy, and plastic use compared to equivalent bottle purchases (SC Johnson company figures); SC Johnson's Plastic Bank collaboration has established over 550 plastic collection points across four countries since 2018. No Method-specific (UK-only) volume or waste-diversion metrics are publicly available.

Technology

Concentrate/refill pouch packaging engineering; recycled recovered coastal plastic bottle formulations developed with Plastic Bank sourcing.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Method is a well-known, design-led eco-cleaning brand whose UK refill pouches and recycled-content bottles represent incremental packaging-reduction wins within a large conventional retail model, rather than a systemic reuse infrastructure play. Its relevance to REUSE Foundation's research stems from its scale and brand visibility (useful for showing mainstream consumer uptake of refill formats) and its parent SC Johnson's broader sustainable-packaging investments, though independent verified impact data specific to Method UK is limited. Priority: ★★★ Medium.
