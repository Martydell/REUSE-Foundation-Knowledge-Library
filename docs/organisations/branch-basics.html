Organisation Overview

Branch Basics is a US direct-to-consumer cleaning-products company built around a single "Concentrate" that customers dilute with water into refillable glass or plastic bottles, replacing multiple single-purpose, single-use cleaning products. It was founded in 2012 by Marilee Nelson, Allison Evans, and Kelly Love, whose personal health experiences motivated a focus on human-safe, plant-based formulations. The company paused sales in December 2015 over formula-transparency concerns with a licensed supplier, relaunched under its own proprietary formula in 2016–2017, and has since grown substantially, reporting $40 million in revenue in 2023 and expanding into retail distribution, including a Target partnership beginning in 2025.

Business Model

Branch Basics' core product model is a single concentrated cleaning formula that customers dilute at defined ratios into refillable bottles (glass or plastic) to create all-purpose, glass, bathroom, and other cleaners, replacing the need to purchase multiple pre-mixed, single-use-packaged products. Refills are shipped as concentrate (rather than diluted, water-heavy liquid), reducing packaging and shipping weight/emissions relative to conventional ready-to-use cleaners. The company sells direct-to-consumer online with a "Subscribe & Save" subscription programme, and has expanded distribution into physical retail, including a Target partnership launched in 2025. It also offers an educational "Toss the Toxins" course as part of its broader mission around reducing household chemical exposure, positioning consumer education alongside its refill-based product model.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (single concentrate diluted at home into reusable bottles, replacing multiple single-use pre-mixed products)
  • Reusable Packaging (refillable glass/plastic bottles central to the product system)
  • Waste Reduction (concentrate shipping model reduces packaging/water weight versus ready-to-use liquids)
  • Consumer Education ("Toss the Toxins" course on reducing household chemical/plastic use)

No deposit-return, take-back, rental, or reverse-logistics activity was identified; the reuse model relies on customers retaining and refilling their own bottles rather than a company-run collection system.

Products & Services

The Concentrate (core all-purpose cleaning formula), refillable bottles (glass and plastic, including spray and other formats), laundry detergent, dish soap, hand soap, oxygen booster, starter kits, and the "Toss the Toxins" educational course.

Customers

US health-conscious households, particularly consumers with chemical sensitivities or chronic health concerns, purchasing via branchbasics.com subscription and, since 2025, via Target retail stores.

Partnerships

  • Target — US retail distribution partnership launched 2025

Measurable Impact

  • Company states its refill model has prevented approximately 14 million single-use plastic bottles from reaching landfills, and that not shipping water has avoided over 8 million pounds of CO2 (company website; cumulative figures, exact reporting date/methodology not specified).
  • Reported $40 million revenue in 2023, with 42% year-over-year growth (Entrepreneur, 2024) — a business-performance metric rather than an environmental-impact metric, included here for context.
  • These figures are self-reported by the company; no independently audited environmental-impact verification was found in public sources.

Technology

No distinguishing reuse-tracking or digital technology identified; the core "technology" is the concentrated, human-safe formulation designed for at-home dilution.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Branch Basics is a well-documented, commercially significant US refill/concentrate cleaning brand with substantial media coverage (CNBC, Forbes, Entrepreneur) corroborating its founding story, 2015 shutdown-and-relaunch pivot, and recent growth, including a 2025 Target retail expansion. Its refill model is a genuine, verifiable example of packaging-waste reduction through concentrate-plus-reusable-bottle design, though its self-reported environmental-impact figures (plastic bottles avoided, CO2 avoided) lack independent audit. The company's unusual origin story — shutting down in 2015 over ingredient-transparency concerns before relaunching with its own formula — is a notable governance/trust narrative relevant to the REUSE Foundation's documentation of brand credibility in the refill space.
