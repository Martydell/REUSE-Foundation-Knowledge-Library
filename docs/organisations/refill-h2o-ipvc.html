Organisation Overview

Refill H2O is a research-led pilot project at the Instituto Politécnico de Viana do Castelo (IPVC) that built a "Smartbottle Ecosystem": reusable bottles with digital tags communicating with IoT-enabled refill stations, to eliminate single-use plastic bottles across the institute's campuses. Coordinated by researcher António Curado.

Business Model

Refill H2O is not a commercial venture but a publicly funded institutional sustainability project, financed with over €140,000 from Portugal's "Environment, Climate Change and Low-Carbon Economy" Program (EEA Grants). It distributed 10,000 free reusable bottles to students and staff and installed nine IoT refill stations across IPVC's six schools, with bottle-to-station identification integrated into the institute's existing student/staff ID card system.

Products & Services

Free reusable "smart" bottles with digital ID tags; nine campus-based IoT refill stations integrated with student/staff ID cards.

Customers

IPVC students, faculty, and staff across six affiliated schools in Viana do Castelo, Portugal.

Partnerships

Funded by Portugal's Ministry of Environment and Climate Action via EEA Grants; selected as best of 24 applications to the program.

Measurable Impact

Project aims to remove over 1 tonne of single-use plastic annually from IPVC campuses; 10,000 reusable bottles distributed and 9 refill stations installed (per institutional/press sources).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A well-documented, publicly funded academic case study of IoT-enabled refill infrastructure at institutional scale; useful as a technology/pilot reference but limited in scope to one university rather than a scalable commercial model.
