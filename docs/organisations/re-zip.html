Organisation Overview

RE-ZIP is a Danish startup, founded in 2018 by CEO Bo Bach Boddum and Thomas Fugl, that builds reusable packaging for e-commerce to replace single-use cardboard shipping boxes. It operates a SaaS platform (RE-ZIP Manager and consumer app) to track packaging circulation, returns and environmental savings.

Business Model

RE-ZIP supplies collapsible, reusable shipping packaging to e-commerce retailers; consumers fold and drop off used packaging at network drop points (scanned via the app for a reward), after which packaging is collected, cleaned at a hub, and recirculated — each unit can be reused more than 20 times. Revenue comes from retailer subscriptions/packaging-as-a-service contracts.

Products & Services

Reusable, collapsible e-commerce shipping packaging with the RE-ZIP Manager SaaS platform and consumer mobile app for tracking usage, returns and CO2/water/waste savings.

Customers

E-commerce retailers in Denmark and, per Collateral Good reporting, expanding to Belgium, the Netherlands, and pilot markets including Romania, Poland, France and Italy.

Partnerships

Piloted with Amazon (2023 reusable packaging trial); joint programme with DS Smith and Raja aiming to put 250,000 reusable packages into circulation; backed by Planetary Impact Ventures, Collateral Good, Tangent Line Ventures and Denmark's Export and Investment Fund.

Measurable Impact

Packaging can be circulated more than 20 times, beginning to save CO2 within the first or second use (per company claims); specific aggregate CO2/waste savings not independently verified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A well-funded, multi-country reusable e-commerce packaging system with major retail partnerships (Amazon, DS Smith/Raja), representing a strong scalable model for replacing single-use shipping packaging. Priority: ★★★★★ Essential
