Organisation Overview

KOOSHOO is a Canadian direct-to-consumer brand that designs and sells plastic-free hair accessories — hair ties, scrunchies and headbands — marketed as the world's first 100% plastic-free hair elastics, launched around 2012. Products are handmade using Fairtrade-certified organic cotton (GOTS certified) and natural tree rubber sourced from a Fair Rubber Association-certified, FSC (Forest Stewardship Council)-certified sustainable rubber plantation in southern India, dyed with OEKO-TEX-certified low-impact, non-toxic dyes. The company ships orders in 100% plastic-free packaging using recycled FSC-certified paper materials and paper tape, and is a member of SEDEX (an ethical/social-audit membership organisation). Kooshoo sells via its own website, a wholesale channel, and third-party zero-waste retailers.

Business Model

Kooshoo operates a direct-to-consumer (D2C) e-commerce model, selling plastic-free hair accessories through its own website and a dedicated wholesale portal supplying independent zero-waste and eco-retail shops. Rather than a reuse/refill or rental system, Kooshoo's circularity contribution is materials substitution: replacing conventional synthetic-rubber/plastic hair ties (which shed microplastics and are not biodegradable) with biodegradable, natural-rubber and organic-cotton alternatives sourced through certified fair-trade and sustainable-forestry supply chains. The company differentiates on ethical sourcing credentials — Fairtrade organic cotton, Fair Rubber Association-certified natural rubber, GOTS and OEKO-TEX certification, and SEDEX membership — positioning its premium pricing around verified supply-chain transparency rather than reuse infrastructure. Distribution spans direct online retail, a wholesale programme for third-party retailers, and listings on zero-waste marketplace sites (e.g., ZeroWasteStore.com, Fill Up Buttercup).

Circular Economy Activities

  • Waste Reduction — biodegradable, plastic-free hair accessories replacing conventional synthetic/plastic hair ties that shed microplastics
  • Consumer Education — blog content on supply-chain transparency, fair-trade sourcing and plastic-free manufacturing practices
  • Material Recovery — use of certified sustainably-harvested natural rubber and organic cotton as biodegradable material substitutes for plastic

Products & Services

Plastic-free hair ties (round/"mondo" styles), scrunchies, and organic headbands made from organic cotton and FSC/Fair Rubber Association-certified natural tree rubber; sold direct-to-consumer and via wholesale to independent retailers.

Customers

Individual consumers purchasing via the Kooshoo website and third-party zero-waste retail sites; independent eco/zero-waste shops purchasing through Kooshoo's wholesale programme.

Partnerships

  • Fair Rubber Association — certification body for the natural rubber supply chain
  • Fair-trade rubber plantation supplier, southern India (unnamed in public sources)
  • SEDEX — ethical trade/social-audit membership
  • Wholesale retail partners including ZeroWasteStore.com and Fill Up Buttercup (product distribution)

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available — no independently verified figures for units sold, plastic avoided, or microplastic reduction were found in public sources; the company cites an industry-wide (not company-specific) statistic that up to 200,000 tons of textile dye enters the environment annually as context for its low-impact dye choice.

Technology

Not publicly available — no digital tracking, RFID or reuse-logistics technology identified; core innovation is materials science/sourcing (biodegradable natural rubber and organic cotton) rather than digital or logistics technology.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Kooshoo is a credible, long-running (since approximately 2012) example of plastic/materials substitution with strong, verifiable ethical-sourcing certifications (Fair Rubber Association, GOTS, OEKO-TEX, SEDEX). However, its activity is materials substitution and waste reduction rather than reuse, refill, repair, rental or sharing in the strict sense the REUSE Foundation prioritises — it manufactures single-purchase consumer goods designed to biodegrade rather than be reused/refilled/returned. It is a relevant but secondary case study for the knowledge library, illustrating adjacent plastic-reduction strategies rather than core reuse-system infrastructure.

Priority Rating: ★★ Low
