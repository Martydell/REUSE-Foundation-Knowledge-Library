Organisation Overview

Zoya was founded in 2008 as Sofia's first organic and natural products shop and has grown into Bulgaria's largest natural/organic retailer, with seven physical stores, an e-commerce arm, and wholesale distribution to 250+ shops across Bulgaria.

Business Model

Zoya operates as a multi-channel retailer: seven physical stores in Sofia (including a dedicated cosmetics shop), an online store with same-day Sofia delivery and international shipping to Greece, Romania, and other EU countries, and a wholesale division supplying 250+ organic shops, health stores, and pharmacies nationally. Bulk bins allow customers to refill pulses and grains into their own paper bags, reducing packaging waste at the point of sale.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems

  • Consumer Education

Products & Services

Organic foods, cosmetics, household products (~7,500 SKUs); in-store bulk refill of pulses/grains; wholesale distribution; own-brand "Zoya goes pretty" cosmetics line.

Customers

Retail consumers in Sofia and across Bulgaria; 250+ wholesale partners (organic shops, health stores, pharmacies); international online customers in Greece, Romania, and other EU countries.

Partnerships

Not publicly available (product certifications referenced include Soil Association, EcoCert, and USDA organic standards, but no named commercial partners confirmed).

Measurable Impact

Reported ~180 staff, 105,000 orders fulfilled, and 60,000 new customers in 2025 (company-reported).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

An established, scaled natural-products retailer with genuine bulk-refill offerings and national wholesale reach — a solid regional (Bulgaria/SE Europe) reuse-adjacent retail example, though refill is a secondary rather than core feature of the business.
