Organisation Overview

Refill Revolution is a nationwide New Zealand campaign and toolkit, launched in February 2023, encouraging producers, retailers and consumers to adopt refill practices instead of single-use packaging. It sits under Mission Zero, a climate-action programme for businesses, and was initiated by Abbie Tebbutt (Chia Sisters) together with Caitlin Attenburrow and Kate Robertson (Pic's Peanut Butter). Rather than operating its own refill infrastructure, it functions as an advocacy and coordination initiative that supports and publicises existing and new refill offerings across the country.

Business Model

Refill Revolution operates as a non-profit advocacy and behaviour-change campaign rather than a commercial enterprise. It provides a "toolkit" that helps food and beverage producers and retailers set up or promote their own refill and reuse solutions, and it runs periodic nationwide campaign activations (the first ran throughout February 2023) during which participating businesses showcase their refill/reuse offerings. Example participating models include Chia Sisters supplying juice in refillable kegs at events and market pop-ups, and Pic's Peanut Butter accepting returned 1kg "Perfit" jars at its "Peanut Butter World" site for washing and refilling, with a discount incentive for customers. The initiative is funded and supported by a mix of local government (councils), community foundations, and business-sector partners, and its core "product" is behaviour change and market normalisation of refill rather than a fee-for-service or product-sales model.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (nationwide toolkit and campaign for producer/retailer refill stations)
  • Consumer Education (campaign activations, awareness-building on reuse)
  • Reusable Packaging (e.g. Pic's returnable "Perfit" jars washed and refilled)

Products & Services

A refill-adoption toolkit for producers/retailers; coordinated nationwide campaign activations; promotion of existing refill and reuse solutions (kegged refill at events, in-store jar return-and-refill schemes).

Customers

Consumers across New Zealand; participating food and beverage producers and retailers (e.g. Chia Sisters, Pic's Peanut Butter) who adopt refill models.

Partnerships

  • Nelson City Council and Tasman District Council
  • Rata Foundation
  • Network Tasman
  • Tonkin + Taylor

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available (campaign describes aims to "drive significant consumer demand for more refill stations" but no dated, quantified outcome metrics were found).

Technology

No proprietary technology platform identified; campaign relies on a producer/retailer toolkit and physical refill infrastructure at participating business sites.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Mission Zero's Refill Revolution is a useful regional example of grassroots, coalition-driven refill advocacy, mobilising food and beverage producers around practical refill and reuse models (kegged refill, jar return-and-refill). Its non-profit, campaign-based structure means it functions more as a movement-builder and toolkit provider than an operator of reuse infrastructure, and no quantified impact data is publicly available, limiting current assessment of its measurable effect.
