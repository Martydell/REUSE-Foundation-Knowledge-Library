Organisation Overview

Since 2018, Unilever has run more than 50 refill and reuse pilots worldwide as part of its packaging sustainability strategy. In the Philippines, this included the "All Things Hair Refillery," launched in March 2019 across three Manila shopping malls, allowing consumers to refill haircare products and drop off used sachets/bottles.

Business Model

Multinational FMCG company piloting refill/reuse formats (in-mall refillery kiosks, refill stations, reusable packaging trials) across brands and markets to reduce virgin plastic use, supported centrally through initiatives like the Unilever TRANSFORM fund which backs enterprises testing new packaging models.

Products & Services

In-mall refill stations for haircare products (All Things Hair Refillery, Philippines); broader global refill/reuse pilots across Unilever's personal care and home care brands.

Customers

Consumers in Manila shopping malls (Philippines pilot); consumers across 50+ global pilot markets more broadly.

Partnerships

TRANSFORM initiative (Unilever-backed fund supporting reuse/refill enterprises); shopping mall operators in Manila (specific mall partners not confirmed in this pass).

Measurable Impact

Company reports having reduced virgin plastic use by 18% against a 2019 baseline and targets 100% reusable, recyclable, or compostable rigid plastic packaging by 2030 (2035 for flexibles), alongside 50+ refill/reuse pilots run since 2018.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

As one of the world's largest FMCG companies, Unilever's refill pilots (including the Philippines) carry outsized potential to shift consumer packaging norms, though critics (e.g., Greenpeace) note the pace lags the scale of the company's plastic footprint, especially sachet use in Southeast Asia. High relevance for benchmarking large-scale refill rollouts. Priority: ★★★★ High
