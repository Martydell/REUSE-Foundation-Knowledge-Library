Green Bubble

A Dublin-based reuse enterprise that collects discarded bubble wrap, foam roll, hexacomb board, and acetate sheet from local businesses and resells it to consumers and companies at reduced cost.

Organisation Overview

Green Bubble is a Dublin-based enterprise founded by Peter Gregory that gives previously-used packaging materials — bubble wrap, foam roll, hexacomb cardboard board, and acetate sheet — a second life rather than allowing them to be incinerated or landfilled. The company collects discarded bubble wrap from furniture warehouses and relocation companies in Dublin, cleans and repackages it (removing tape, rolling it into resalable lengths), and sells it at a discount to households and businesses via its own e-commerce site. Gregory, originally from Northampton, England, moved to Ireland in 2018 and has been recognised by Repak (Ireland's packaging compliance scheme) as its "Green Business Person of the Month."

Business Model

Green Bubble operates a reverse-logistics and resale model: it sources used bubble wrap, foam roll (including silver-foil-lined insulation rolls), hexacomb board, and acetate sheet as waste streams from furniture warehouses and relocation/moving companies in the Dublin area, which would otherwise send this material for incineration or export. The company inspects, cleans, and processes the material (removing tape and residue, re-rolling to standard lengths) before selling it through its own website to individual consumers (for home moves, shipping fragile items, and artwork transport) and businesses, at prices below virgin packaging. Green Bubble partners with Sunflower Recycling — a social-economy enterprise employing long-term unemployed people from disadvantaged backgrounds — for aspects of its material processing, adding a social as well as environmental dimension to the model. It has also worked with the Rediscovery Centre, The Earthy Marketplace, and Fingal County Council on reuse education and business engagement, and became a Repak member, receiving that organisation's "Green Business Person of the Month" recognition for founder Peter Gregory.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reverse Logistics (collection of used packaging materials from Dublin warehouses/relocation firms)
  • Material Recovery (cleaning, sorting, and reprocessing bubble wrap, foam roll, hexacomb board, acetate sheet for resale)
  • Waste Reduction (diversion of packaging materials from incineration/export; avoided consumption of virgin bubble wrap)
  • Reusable Packaging (resale of used packaging materials for continued reuse rather than single-use)

Products & Services

Reused bubble wrap (various sizes/specifications), foam rolls (including foil-lined insulation rolls), hexacomb cardboard panels, acetate sheets, eco metallic bubble envelopes, artwork shipping boxes, and home-moving kits — sold online with delivery across Ireland and Northern Ireland.

Customers

Individual consumers (home movers, online sellers shipping fragile goods, artists/galleries shipping artwork) and businesses across Ireland and Northern Ireland seeking lower-cost, lower-impact packaging materials.

Partnerships

  • Sunflower Recycling — social-economy recycling/processing partner employing long-term unemployed individuals
  • Repak — Irish packaging compliance/producer-responsibility scheme; Green Bubble is a member and founder Peter Gregory received its "Green Business Person of the Month" recognition
  • Rediscovery Centre — collaboration on reuse education
  • The Earthy Marketplace — retail/marketplace partner
  • Fingal County Council — collaboration connecting and educating local businesses on material reuse

Measurable Impact

  • Diverted over 4,500 square metres (reported as over three tonnes) of bubble wrap away from incineration and export, per the company's own reporting.
  • Company states this also represents 4,500+ square metres of virgin bubble wrap production avoided.
  • No independently audited (third-party, non-company) verification of these figures was found; treat as company-reported.

Technology

No advanced digital tracking, app, or IoT technology identified; the operation relies on manual collection, cleaning, and reprocessing logistics plus a standard e-commerce website.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Green Bubble is a small but clearly documented reverse-logistics and packaging-reuse operation with a distinctive social dimension (partnership with a disadvantaged-employment recycling social enterprise) and formal recognition from Ireland's Repak packaging scheme. Its core activity — diverting genuinely used bubble wrap and related materials from incineration back into circulation — is a clean, easily-understood example of "reuse over recycling" that aligns well with the REUSE Foundation's mission. The main gaps are an unconfirmed founding date and impact figures that originate solely from company self-reporting rather than independent audit.
