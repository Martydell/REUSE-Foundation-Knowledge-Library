Organisation Overview

MIWA Technologies, a.s. is a Prague-based company that builds a "smart reuse" ecosystem for selling dry food and household products without single-use packaging. Founded by CEO Petr Báča, the company supplies durable, chip-equipped B2B capsules and electronic in-store dispensers to retailers, alongside a consumer-facing app and reusable smart cups. It has run paid pilots and commercial installations with European grocery chains including REWE, Aldi (UK), Albert Heijn, and dm-drogerie markt, and has received significant blended EU funding (grant plus equity) through the European Innovation Council's EIC Accelerator programme.

Business Model

MIWA operates a business-to-business "packaging-as-a-service" model rather than selling packaging outright. Manufacturers and retailers use MIWA's durable 12-litre capsules — designed for 300+ reuse cycles — which are filled at source, shipped to stores, and slotted into MIWA's electronic dispensers; the capsule's embedded chip communicates product identity, batch, expiry and allergen data to the dispenser and to MIWA's cloud-based back-end (Business Management System). Shoppers bring their own container or a MIWA-branded reusable "smart cup," register it via the MIWA mobile app, and dispense the exact quantity they want, paying by weight/volume. Empty capsules are collected through MIWA's reverse-logistics network and returned to MIWA's Prague service centre for washing and refilling by manufacturers, closing the loop. Revenue is generated from equipment supply, service/logistics fees, and software, positioning MIWA as an infrastructure and technology provider to brands and retailers rather than a direct-to-consumer product company. The company has used EU Structural Fund "Innovation Voucher" grants and EIC Accelerator blended finance (grant plus equity investment) to fund hardware development (capsule, dispensing valve, consumer container prototype) and is pursuing further commercial expansion, including a private investment round, according to its own disclosures and EU programme records.

Products & Services

  • MIWA capsules — 12-litre reusable B2B containers rated for 300+ reuse cycles, chip-equipped for product tracking
  • Electronic in-store dispensers ("MIWA valve" modules), including a second-generation unit with integrated scale and display
  • MIWA Smart Cups — reusable consumer containers made of recyclable mono-material, registered via app for tracking and take-back
  • MIWA mobile app — enables container registration, in-store payment, product information lookup, and return/take-back
  • Cloud-based Business Management System (BMS) software linking capsules, dispensers and the consumer app
  • Service centre operations (Prague) for capsule washing, refilling and logistics

Customers

Primarily B2B: grocery/retail chains and consumer-goods manufacturers deploying MIWA's dispensing systems in-store (e.g., REWE, Aldi UK, Albert Heijn, dm-drogerie markt, Albert Czechia), plus office/workplace and hospitality "packaging-free kiosk" deployments. End consumers interact with the system as in-store shoppers via the MIWA app and reusable containers.

Partnerships

Verified from press coverage and EU programme records: - REWE (Germany) — pilot across 11 stores, reported November 2023 (Packaging Europe) - Nestlé / Purina (Switzerland) — piloted in 3 shops from 2020, expanded to 16 locations (Ellen MacArthur Foundation) - Aldi (UK) and Albert Heijn (Netherlands) — cited as active/piloting retailers (Silicon Canals, Packaging Insights, 2022–2023) - dm-drogerie markt (Germany) — piloted MIWA's refill system for food (Packaging World) - European Innovation Council (EISMEA) / EIC Fund (Luxembourg) — grant and equity funder

Measurable Impact

  • Independent Life Cycle Assessment study (commissioned via an EU Innovation Voucher grant, registration CZ.01.1.02/0.0/0.0/17_205/0013184): reports MIWA's system has approximately 70% lower overall environmental impact than single-use packaging, uses roughly 90% less packaging material, and cuts carbon footprint by approximately 62% (miwa.eu; corroborated by Crunchbase's company summary). Impakter's coverage cites a slightly different figure of 71% for overall impact reduction — minor variance across secondary sources, same underlying study.
  • REWE pilot (2023): each returnable container is projected to save approximately 2,000 pieces of disposable packaging over its lifecycle (Packaging Europe, November 2023).
  • No independently audited, company-wide totals for cumulative plastic avoided, dispenser installation counts, or number of active stores were found in public sources.

Technology

MIWA's system is a hybrid of two chip technologies, not a single "NFC system" as sometimes described in secondary summaries: - RFID: MIWA's B2B capsules and the electronic dispensing shelves/valves are equipped with RFID chips and readers, used to identify capsule contents (product type, batch, expiry, allergen and nutrition data) and to control automated dispensing (Crunchbase; miwa.eu FAQ). - NFC: MIWA's consumer-facing reusable "smart cups"/containers carry NFC chips. Shoppers tap their phone to the chip to register the container in the MIWA app, enabling traceability and enabling the container to be returned to the manufacturer for reuse (miwa.eu FAQ). - IoT/cloud back-end: dispensers and capsules connect to MIWA's cloud-based Business Management System for monitoring, inventory and reordering.

The seed data's characterisation as an "NFC refill dispenser system" is a simplification: the core B2B capsule-and-shelf infrastructure runs on RFID, while NFC is used specifically at the consumer-container/app layer.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

MIWA is a directly relevant example of business-to-business reuse and refill infrastructure: it replaces single-use packaging with a closed-loop, chip-tracked capsule system, operates real (if still pilot-scale) deployments with major European grocery retailers, and has secured substantial, independently vetted EU innovation funding (EIC Accelerator blended finance) — a signal of external technical and commercial due diligence beyond self-reported claims. Its core activity — refill dispensing plus reverse logistics for reusable containers — sits squarely within the REUSE Foundation's scope. Caveats: deployment scale remains at the pilot/multi-store level rather than mass retail rollout, headline environmental-impact figures derive from a single EU-grant-funded LCA study rather than independent third-party audit, and the company's own founding-year claim conflicts with its commercial registry record.

Priority Rating: ★★★★★ Essential (core B2B refill/reuse infrastructure provider, directly in-scope for the REUSE Foundation's mission, with verifiable retail deployments and EU-vetted funding)
