Organisation Overview

FloWater is a US company operating purified-water refill stations built around mostly-stainless-steel construction, deployed in offices, gyms, schools, and other business/institutional settings (including at least one confirmed US school district, Oakland Unified School District). Its stations use a "7x Advanced Purification" filtration system claimed to remove up to 99.9% of contaminants including microplastics, lead, PFAS, chlorine, pharmaceuticals, and heavy metals, holding seven gallons of purified water on tap at all times with automatic refill and activated-oxygen tank sanitisation.

Business Model

FloWater deploys and presumably services/leases refill stations to business and institutional customers (offices, gyms, schools, warehouses), replacing bottled or lower-quality tap water with an on-site purification and refill system — customers bring reusable bottles/cups to fill rather than purchasing single-use bottled water. The stations' stainless-steel fill areas incorporate injection-molded materials described as reusing scrap material, and front panels use high-strength polycarbonate. The specific commercial model (sale vs. lease, service contract terms) was not confirmed in sources accessed during this research pass.

Products & Services

Stainless-steel purified-water refill stations (business/warehouse and consumer-facing models); water filtration/purification technology; a faucet water-filter product line sold via retail (e.g. Amazon).

Customers

Businesses, gyms, schools (confirmed: Oakland Unified School District), warehouses, and other institutions in the United States, plus an international presence noted (FloWater UAE).

Partnerships

Oakland Unified School District (confirmed institutional deployment); no other named partners confirmed in sources accessed.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available as an independently audited figure — "removes up to 99.9% of contaminants" is a manufacturer product-performance claim, not an environmental-impact metric.

Technology

"7x Advanced Purification" filtration system; activated-oxygen tank sanitisation; automatic tank refill mechanism.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

FloWater is a credible, multi-location refill-station operator addressing bottled-water reduction in institutional settings, with at least one confirmed public-sector deployment (a US school district) and apparent international expansion (UAE). Its relevance is somewhat limited by the absence of independently verified deployment scale or environmental-impact figures, and founding history is not clearly documented in accessible sources.
