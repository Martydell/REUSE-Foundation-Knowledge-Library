Organisation Overview

Reath is an Edinburgh-based startup co-founded in 2019 by Claire Rampen and Emily Rogers, building digital track-and-trace software for reusable packaging systems. It authored the "reuse.id" Open Data Standard, an agreed vocabulary enabling different organisations in a reuse supply chain (manufacturer, retailer, cleaner, customer) to share packaging usage data.

Business Model

Reath sells a SaaS platform combining machine-readable trackers (e.g. QR codes) with its reuse.id Data Standard, giving each reusable packaging item a "Digital Passport" that accumulates usage data as it moves through a reuse loop. Clients (brands, retailers, logistics/washing providers) use the analytics to monitor cycle times, loss rates and environmental savings, paying via software subscription.

Products & Services

Track-and-trace software and mobile app for reusable packaging, the reuse.id Open Data Standard, and a data/analytics platform for packaging performance (cycle times, loss rates, CO2/water savings).

Customers

Brands, retailers and logistics/cleaning providers operating reusable packaging systems (food service, e-commerce, industrial packaging).

Partnerships

Received funding from UK Research and Innovation's Smart Sustainable Plastic Packaging fund; grant-funded to develop the reuse.id Open Data Standard; academic ties to University of Edinburgh's Bayes Centre AI Accelerator.
