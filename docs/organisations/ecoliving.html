Organisation Overview

ecoLiving was founded in 2014 by Jo Mack, registered under the legal entity Dani & Jo Ltd and based in Worthing, West Sussex. It has grown into one of the largest vegan, zero-waste/plastic-free non-food product brands in the UK and EU, offering plastic-free alternatives across kitchen, bathroom, and household categories, with all packaging designed to be reused, recycled, or composted. The company operates direct-to-consumer e-commerce alongside B2B wholesale/trade distribution to retailers, including via the Faire wholesale marketplace.

Business Model

ecoLiving combines direct-to-consumer e-commerce (with same-day dispatch for orders before 11am and UK delivery from £2.95) with a B2B wholesale/trade channel supplying independent retailers, distributing both its own-brand "ecoLiving" product line and other plastic-free brands. Its product philosophy is built around eliminating microplastics and single-use plastic from household and personal care routines — offering reusable and durable substitutes (e.g., shampoo bars, toothpaste tablets, reusable/compostable sponges, wooden utensils, FSC-certified brushes) rather than a refill/dispensing system. The company embeds environmental commitments structurally: it is a 1% For The Planet member (donating a share of profits to environmental causes, with a stated ocean-protection focus), partners with Eden Reforestation Projects for tree planting, and is affiliated with the World Land Trust, alongside using FSC-certified materials for wood-based products.

Not publicly available — standard e-commerce platform with same-day dispatch logistics; no proprietary refill-dispensing or digital tracking technology identified (business model is reusable/compostable product retail rather than in-store refill dispensing).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

ecoLiving is a credible, over a decade-old UK plastic-free retail brand with verifiable environmental commitments (1% For The Planet membership, Eden Reforestation Projects and World Land Trust affiliations, FSC certification) and a dual retail/wholesale model giving it broader market reach than many single-channel competitors. Its focus, however, is on selling reusable/compostable consumer goods rather than operating reuse infrastructure such as refill stations, deposit-return, or take-back loops — positioning it as a "sustainable product retailer" rather than a circular-systems operator. Lack of disclosed quantitative impact metrics limits deeper assessment, though its longevity and stated market-leading UK/EU position merit continued monitoring.
