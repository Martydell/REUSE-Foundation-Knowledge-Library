Organisation Overview

HACARI is an independent greengrocer ("yaoya") in the Nakameguro district of Tokyo, described in Japanese consumer and industry media as Japan's only produce shop selling fresh vegetables by weight ("量り売り"). Founded by Yoshitaka Katayama to connect consumers directly with small farmers producing outside conventional mass-market supply chains, HACARI sources from a network reported at over 200 producers nationwide. The shop also sells prepared side dishes and partner products such as "Traveling Cheesecake" and a vegetable subscription service ("Saikuru"), alongside an online store.

Business Model

HACARI operates a physical neighborhood retail shop plus an online store, selling weighed, package-minimized fresh produce sourced directly from small producers rather than through conventional wholesale distribution. Its core circularity mechanism is a "my container" ("マイ容器") practice: regular customers bring their own reusable containers or bags to purchase vegetables and prepared side dishes by weight, avoiding single-use packaging; Japanese consumer media describe this as an environmentally motivated, community-supported practice comparable to bringing a reusable shopping bag. The business model otherwise resembles a specialty direct-from-farm retailer, monetizing through in-store and online produce sales plus complementary curated products.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

HACARI is a small, single-location independent retailer whose relevance to reuse rests entirely on its "bring your own container" practice and weight-based, minimal-packaging sales model — a genuine, if very small-scale, example of consumer-facing packaging-free retail. Its listing in Refill Japan's directory situates it within Japan's broader refill/BYOC movement. Given its limited scale (single shop) and near-total absence of public reporting, its measurable contribution to circular-economy goals cannot be verified, though the model itself is directly on-mission. Priority rating: ★★ Low.
