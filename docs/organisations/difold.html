Organisation Overview

DiFOLD is a Bulgarian startup that designed the "Origami Bottle," a foldable, reusable 750ml bottle made from bio-based thermoplastic polymer that collapses to roughly 20% of its expanded size for portability. Co-founded by ad-industry veteran Radina Popova and architect/designer Petar Zaharinov after they met through the Climate-KIC clean-tech accelerator in 2018, the company combines origami-inspired folding mechanics with recyclable, multi-use plastic to create an alternative to single-use plastic bottles. The product line has expanded to include an Origami Cup, straws and accessories. DiFOLD raised over €100,000 via Kickstarter crowdfunding and sells through its own website and retail partners in Bulgaria and internationally.

Business Model

DiFOLD is a direct-to-consumer and retail product company: it designs, patents and (via manufacturing partners) produces its foldable bottle and cup products, then sells them online through difold.com and via physical retail stockists in Bulgaria, supplemented by international shipping and past crowdfunding campaigns (Kickstarter) for new product launches. Revenue comes from one-time product sales rather than a subscription or refill-service model — customers buy a durable, reusable bottle/cup designed for years of repeated use (the company states the material supports intensive use for around five years) rather than paying per refill or leasing. The company markets itself around outdoor/travel use-cases (portability, folding to fit in a pocket or bag) and sustainability messaging (reducing single-use plastic bottle consumption). As a hardware/product startup rather than a service operator, its business model depends on unit sales volume, retail distribution deals, and, historically, crowdfunding to finance production runs and new SKUs.

Not publicly available — no independently audited figures for units sold, single-use bottles displaced, or plastic-waste-avoided were found in verified public sources.

Technology

Bio-based thermoplastic copolyester material engineered for repeated fold/unfold cycles; no digital tracking, app or IoT component identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

DiFOLD is a genuine reusable-packaging product innovator solving a real adoption barrier (bulkiness of rigid reusable bottles) through material and mechanical design, with demonstrated market validation via a successful Kickstarter campaign and retail distribution. However, it is a single-product consumer-goods company rather than a reuse infrastructure or service provider, and no independent impact data is available. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation is as a product-design case study for reuse adoption rather than a systemic reuse-infrastructure operator.
