OPA

Israeli startup that ran a mobile-app platform for reusable takeout food containers in Tel Aviv before halting operations in 2024.

Organisation Overview

OPA was founded by former Wolt executives Ran Doron and Dar Hasson to tackle single-use takeout waste in Tel Aviv, where an estimated seven million disposable containers were discarded monthly. It offered reusable, recyclable bowls returnable via dedicated collection bins.

Business Model

OPA ran a two-sided app: customers ordered via an OPA-generated code entered into partner delivery apps (e.g. Wolt), received food in reusable bowls, and returned them (rinsed) within 10 days to manufactured collection bins around Tel Aviv. The service was free to consumers and partnered with roughly 20 Wolt-registered restaurants before expanding toward nearby suburbs.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

OPA demonstrated a workable reusable-packaging delivery model but ceased Israel operations in April 2024, limiting its ongoing relevance. Valuable as a case study of both the promise and fragility of app-based reuse-in-delivery models. ★★ Low
