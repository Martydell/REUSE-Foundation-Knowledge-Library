Organisation Overview

Rezero is a Catalan non-profit foundation founded in 2005 to advance waste prevention and a "zero waste society" in Catalonia. It conducts research and pilot projects through RezeroLab, provides waste-prevention consulting to companies and public bodies, and builds community engagement around responsible consumption, including projects touching on reusable-container systems.

Business Model

Rezero is funded as a foundation supported by civil society, public administrations, and business-sector participants, rather than operating a direct-to-consumer commercial model. It generates activity through RezeroLab research and pilots, paid consulting services ("Re-solutions") for companies and municipalities on waste prevention, and community programs (the "gentRezero" network). It participates in EU-level projects such as Circulandia, which addresses reusable containers within circular food systems.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Consumer Education (community engagement, gentRezero network)
  • Waste Reduction (core mission and consulting focus)
  • Deposit Return / Reusable Packaging (project-based, e.g. Circulandia; not a Rezero-operated commercial system)
  • Other (research and policy work via RezeroLab)

Products & Services

Waste-prevention consulting for businesses and public entities; applied research and pilot projects (RezeroLab); community sustainability programs and publications.

Customers

Companies, municipalities, and public administrations in Catalonia seeking waste-prevention consulting; the general public via community engagement programs.

Partnerships

Member of Zero Waste Europe and Break Free From Plastic; involved in the EU Circulandia project on circular food systems and reusable containers.

Measurable Impact

One cited project example: a German reusable wine-bottle system recovers approximately 40% of bottles sold after two years of operation (per Rezero project reporting; not a Rezero-operated system).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Rezero is a credible, long-running non-profit shaping Catalan and EU circular-economy policy and pilots, but it functions primarily as a research/advocacy/consulting body rather than a direct operator of a restaurant or café reusable-container deposit system, as the seed description implied — this discrepancy is flagged. ★★★ Medium
