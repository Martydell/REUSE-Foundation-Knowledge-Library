Organisation Overview

Divers Clean Action (DCA) is an Indonesian non-profit founded in 2015 by diver and marine conservationist Swietenia Puspa Lestari, alongside co-founders Nesha Ichida and Adi Septiono, following a beach/underwater clean-up mapping activity that drew over 100 volunteers. Legally registered as Yayasan Penyelam Lestari Indonesia, DCA works across research, community campaigns/workshops, community development and corporate CSR/EPR collaboration to reduce marine plastic pollution. Its "Toko Cura" ("Reuse and Refill" shop) initiative, launched in Jakarta's Kepulauan Seribu (Thousand Islands) since 2022, helps local shops offer daily necessities via refill and container-return models rather than single-use plastic packaging.

Business Model

Divers Clean Action is a non-profit foundation, not a commercial company; it funds and operates its programmes through grants, corporate CSR/EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) partnerships, and donor support. Its Toko Cura initiative is a community-retail intervention model: DCA works with existing small shops in the Thousand Islands (Kepulauan Seribu) to convert or supplement their offering with a "Reuse and Refill" system, structured around three approaches — "Refill on the go" (customers refill containers in-store), "Return from home" (customers bring containers from home to refill) and "Return on the go" (customers return packaging while out) — reducing single-use sachet and bottle consumption in a geographically isolated island community where waste management/collection is logistically difficult. DCA's broader programme areas include ocean/underwater debris research and mapping, youth and community education campaigns (e.g. the Indonesian Youth Marine Debris Summit), and partnerships with companies on producer-responsibility schemes, positioning Toko Cura as one applied intervention within a wider marine-conservation mission.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reverse Logistics — "Return from home" / "Return on the go" container-return mechanisms
  • Waste Reduction — reducing single-use plastic sachet/bottle consumption in island retail
  • Consumer Education — community workshops, campaigns and youth summits on marine debris and plastic reduction
  • Material Recovery — broader clean-up and waste-research programmes (beach/underwater debris mapping)

Products & Services

Toko Cura reuse/refill retail-conversion programme for local shops; ocean and underwater clean-up operations; marine-debris research and mapping; community workshops and corporate CSR/EPR collaboration programmes.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Divers Clean Action is a credible, decade-old Indonesian marine-conservation non-profit whose Toko Cura initiative is a genuine, if geographically localised, reuse/refill retail intervention addressing a real waste-management gap in an isolated island community. Its founder-led credibility and multi-programme structure (research, education, CSR partnerships) suggest organisational capacity beyond a single pilot, but public information on Toko Cura's scale, shop count, and measurable plastic-reduction impact remains limited. It is a relevant example of grassroots reuse-system deployment in a hard-to-serve context.
