Organisation Overview

Cosmogen is a French cosmetics-applicator and packaging specialist based in Paris, founded roughly 35 years ago (circa 1990-1991), specialising in refillable and reusable applicator systems for colour cosmetics — including its patented "Powder Pack" (an airtight, refillable compact with paper refills) and "Tense Cushion" dual-sided applicator for concealer, blush, and highlighter. The company reported record 2025 turnover (+16% growth), with the US accounting for 20% of sales, driven by product launches with major beauty brands including L'Oréal, Dior, and Estée Lauder.

Business Model

Cosmogen operates as a B2B applicator and packaging manufacturer/supplier to major cosmetics brands rather than selling directly to consumers: its patented refillable systems (Powder Pack, Tense Cushion, and others) are licensed or supplied to beauty companies who incorporate them into their own branded products. The refill mechanism — reusable outer compact/applicator body with replaceable inner refills (e.g. paper-based Powder Pack refills) — reduces packaging waste per repurchase for the end consumer, while Cosmogen's own revenue comes from B2B supply contracts with brands such as L'Oréal, Dior, and Estée Lauder.

Products & Services

Refillable cosmetic applicator systems: Powder Pack (refillable pressed-powder compact with paper refills), Tense Cushion (dual-sided liquid/cream applicator with washable sponge), refillable sticks, portable brush sets, and related packaging/applicator hardware for colour cosmetics.

Customers

Major global cosmetics brands (B2B), including named clients L'Oréal, Dior, and Estée Lauder.

Partnerships

  • L'Oréal, Dior, Estée Lauder (named brand clients/product-launch partners, 2025-2026 reporting)

Measurable Impact

Record 2025 turnover reported with +16% year-on-year growth; US market accounting for 20% of sales (company/trade-press reporting, 2025-2026). No independently audited packaging-waste-reduction figures were found.

Technology

Patented refillable applicator mechanisms (Powder Pack push-button dispensing system, Tense Cushion cannula-and-sponge applicator) — proprietary hardware design rather than digital tracking technology.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Cosmogen is a well-established, commercially successful B2B refill-technology supplier to major global cosmetics brands, giving it real-world validation at meaningful scale (named partnerships with L'Oréal, Dior, Estée Lauder; documented revenue growth). It is a relevant example of refill infrastructure operating "upstream" in a B2B supply-chain role rather than as a consumer-facing brand itself.
