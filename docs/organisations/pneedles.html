Organisation Overview

Pneedles is a Nigerian company founded by entrepreneur Joshua Attat that manufactures buttons and beads from repurposed plastic waste, positioned as one of the first plastic-button producers in Nigeria. The business evolved from a local Ankara footwear operation in Uyo into a dedicated sustainable-materials manufacturer, gaining attention including at Lagos Fashion Week.

Business Model

Pneedles collects and processes plastic waste into raw material used to manufacture fashion notions (buttons and beads), which are sold to garment makers, designers, and fashion brands. The company aims to align sustainability with profitability and has stated ambitions to expand into the US, UK, and French markets.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Pneedles is a novel, small-scale example of industrial plastic-waste upcycling into a fashion-industry input in West Africa. Relevant as an emerging-market material-recovery case study, though currently early-stage with no published production or diversion metrics. ★★★ Medium
