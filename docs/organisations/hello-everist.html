Organisation Overview

Everist (marketed as "Hello Everist") is a Toronto-based beauty company that makes patent-pending, 3x-concentrated shampoo, conditioner and body wash pastes, packaged in 100ml aluminium tubes rather than plastic bottles filled mostly with water. Customers add water in the shower to activate the product. Founded by Jayme Jenkins and Jessica Stevenson and launched publicly in January 2021, the company has since won multiple sustainability and innovation awards, including a TIME Best Inventions recognition, and positions itself as part of the "waterless beauty" movement.

Business Model

Everist is a direct-to-consumer (with some retail distribution) personal-care brand operating on a product-substitution model: it replaces conventional water-heavy liquid shampoo/conditioner/body wash in plastic bottles with concentrated pastes in aluminium tubes. Revenue comes from one-time and subscription purchases of tubes and refill inserts sold via its own e-commerce site and retail partners. The core sustainability claim is reduced shipping weight/emissions (products are ~3x concentrated) and use of recyclable aluminium instead of virgin plastic, rather than a classic refill-at-store or deposit-return system. The company also runs a "CapBack" program encouraging return/recycling of plastic caps. It is a for-profit, VC/angel-backed style beauty startup (specific funding rounds not verified) rather than a circular-logistics or B2B reuse-infrastructure company; its circularity contribution is concentrated in material/format substitution and lower embodied transport emissions rather than closed-loop reuse of packaging itself.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Waste Reduction (concentrated formulas eliminate most water content, cutting packaging and shipping volume)
  • Reusable Packaging (aluminium tubes marketed as more readily recyclable than multi-material plastic bottles)
  • Take-back (CapBack program for plastic cap collection)
  • Consumer Education (impact/education content on carbon and waste footprint per product)

Products & Services

Shampoo concentrate, conditioner concentrate, deep conditioning concentrate, and body wash concentrate, sold in aluminium tubes with accompanying reusable bottles/dispensers for some SKUs; sample/travel formats also offered.

Customers

Individual consumers (direct-to-consumer, primarily North American market) purchasing via the Everist website and select retail/online partners.

Partnerships

  • Leaping Bunny (cruelty-free certification body)
  • Bluebird Climate (third-party carbon footprint measurement/verification per product)
  • SeaTrees (coastal ecosystem restoration; carbon offset partner) Source: Everist "Impact" page (https://helloeverist.com/pages/impact).

Measurable Impact

  • "77% smaller carbon footprint" versus a traditional water-filled equivalent product, as measured by Bluebird Climate (per Everist Impact page, undated on page, retrieved 2026-07-18)
  • "62% smaller waste impact" versus traditional plastic-bottled equivalents (per Everist Impact page, retrieved 2026-07-18) No independently audited or third-party-published impact report was located beyond these on-site claims; figures are company-reported and methodology is not fully disclosed publicly.

Technology

No proprietary reuse/tracking technology identified; core technology is the concentrated (patent-pending) product formulation itself rather than a packaging-reuse or logistics platform.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Everist is a well-known "waterless beauty" brand whose environmental value proposition rests on concentration (less water, less shipping weight) and a switch from plastic bottles to aluminium tubes, rather than genuine closed-loop reuse or refill infrastructure. It is relevant to the circular economy conversation as a packaging/material substitution case study and for its CapBack take-back program, but it does not operate reusable packaging loops, deposit-return, or rental/sharing systems. Suitable for inclusion as a waste-reduction/packaging-innovation example rather than a core reuse-system operator.
