Organisation Overview

Kjaer Weis is a luxury beauty brand founded in 2010 by Danish makeup artist Kirsten Kjaer Weis in New York City. It is widely credited as the first luxury beauty brand to introduce fully refillable, certified-organic makeup packaging, launching its "Iconic Edition" line (cream blush, lip tint, eye shadow) in reusable metal compacts. The brand's products are certified organic, gluten-free, and cruelty-free, with recyclable and compostable packaging elements alongside the refillable cases. In 2021, private equity/brand-incubation firm Waldencast Ventures acquired a majority stake following strong sales growth; Kirsten Kjaer Weis parted ways with the company she founded in January 2025.

Business Model

Kjaer Weis operates a direct-to-consumer (D2C) luxury cosmetics model, selling makeup and skincare primarily through its own website, supplemented by distribution via high-end retail partners and beauty editorial coverage. The core of its model is a "buy the case once, buy refills after" system: customers purchase a reusable metal compact case (available in silver "Iconic Edition" or gold "Red Edition") and then repurchase only the interior product refill when it runs out, reducing packaging waste compared to buying a new compact each time. This durable-case-plus-consumable-refill model is intended to justify premium pricing through reduced long-term packaging waste and a reusable, giftable object. Waldencast Ventures took a majority ownership stake in 2021 after the brand's sales grew substantially, and continues to control/operate the brand following founder Kirsten Kjaer Weis's departure in January 2025. Products are sold online globally with free shipping thresholds and a loyalty programme ("Care-Wise Rewards").

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging — refillable metal compact cases for blush, lipstick, eyeshadow and other products, designed for repeated refill rather than repurchase
  • Consumer Education — marketing and editorial content promoting refill-over-replace behaviour in luxury cosmetics
  • Waste Reduction — recyclable/compostable secondary packaging alongside refillable primary cases

Products & Services

Refillable cream blush, lip tint, eyeshadow, lipstick, cream foundation, cleansers, toners and other skincare/makeup items, sold with reusable cases and separately purchasable refill inserts.

Customers

Luxury/premium beauty consumers globally, purchasing primarily via the brand's direct-to-consumer website and select high-end beauty retailers.

Partnerships

  • Waldencast Ventures — majority investor/owner since 2021 (brand acquisition and operational backing)
  • Retail/editorial features with Byrdie, Allure, Elle, Glamour, Essence, Vogue (media coverage, not confirmed as formal commercial partnerships)

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Kjaer Weis is a genuine and long-standing (since 2010) example of reusable packaging in the luxury cosmetics sector, having pioneered the refillable-compact model years before most competitors. Its acquisition by Waldencast Ventures in 2021 and the founder's 2025 departure indicate the brand has moved from founder-led mission-driven operation toward conventional private-equity-backed brand management, which may affect its future sustainability focus. No independently verified impact metrics (e.g., refill adoption rate, packaging waste avoided) are publicly available, limiting assessment of real-world effectiveness. It remains a relevant, credible case study of premium reusable packaging design.
