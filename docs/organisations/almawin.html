Organisation Overview

AlmaWin is a German manufacturer of biodegradable laundry detergents, cleaning agents, and dishwashing liquids, founded in 1993 by Rudolf Bund in Winterbach, near Stuttgart. The company is family-owned and now in its second generation of leadership. Its products are formulated as concentrates from plant- and mineral-based ingredients, several certified organic, and are sold through conventional retail as well as through Germany's "unverpackt" (zero-waste/package-free) shop network, where empty bottles can be refilled rather than discarded. The seed data's listing of the United Kingdom as the company's country was incorrect; AlmaWin is a German company, confirmed by its .de domain, Winterbach headquarters, and company history page.

Business Model

AlmaWin manufactures concentrated, biodegradable household products (laundry detergent, general cleaners, dishwashing liquid, baby-sensitive formulations under its own brand and the fragrance-free "Klar" sub-brand introduced in 2002) and sells them via two main channels: mainstream retail/distribution (the company states it supplies products to roughly 40 countries) and Germany's dedicated zero-waste ("unverpackt") shop network, where its "Zero Waste" product line is designed to be refilled from bulk by consumers using their own empty AlmaWin bottles, reducing single-use packaging. The company actively invites new zero-waste retailers to become distribution partners. It has also introduced bottles made with 95%+ recycled plastic content (from 2015, per company history) for its non-refill retail lines.

Not identified from public sources: Reusable Packaging (as a company-run deposit/return system), Deposit Return, Packaging-as-a-Service, Repair, Rental, Sharing, Reverse Logistics, Take-back, Industrial Reuse, Digital Tracking, Material Recovery (beyond recycled-content packaging).

Products & Services

Laundry detergents, household cleaning agents, dishwashing liquids, baby-sensitive product lines, and a dedicated "Zero Waste" bulk/refill product range, sold under the AlmaWin and Klar brand names.

Customers

Zero-waste/"unverpackt" shops across Germany; conventional retail distributors and grocery chains; reported export to approximately 40 countries.

Partnerships

  • Unverpackt Verband (German zero-waste/package-free retail association) — referenced on official site
  • Zero-waste ("unverpackt") retail shops generally, as refill-channel partners (specific store names not identified in this research pass)

Measurable Impact

REUSE Foundation Assessment

AlmaWin is a long-established (1993), family-owned German manufacturer with a credible, verifiable refill-shop distribution model via the country's dedicated "unverpackt" zero-waste retail network, plus recycled-content packaging for its conventional retail lines. It is a genuine refill-systems participant, though the refill infrastructure (in-store bulk dispensing) is operated by third-party zero-waste retailers rather than AlmaWin itself, and no quantified impact data is available to size its contribution. The seed data's country field (UK) was incorrect and has been corrected to Germany, consistent with the founding year (1993) which the seed data got right.

Priority Rating: ★★★ Medium (credible, long-running refill-channel manufacturer; impact unquantified; refill infrastructure operated by third-party retail partners, not the company itself)
