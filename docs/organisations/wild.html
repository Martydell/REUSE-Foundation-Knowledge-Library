Wild

Organisation Overview

Wild is a UK personal-care brand built around refillable, plastic-reduced products, starting with a natural deodorant sold as a reusable metal case plus compostable/bamboo-pulp refill sticks. It was co-founded by childhood friends Charlie Bowes-Lyon and Freddy Ward, with the operating company (Wild Cosmetics Limited) incorporated in December 2018 and the brand launching commercially in the UK in 2019/2020. Wild grew rapidly through direct-to-consumer subscription and expansion into major UK retailers, reporting revenue of £46.9 million for the year to December 2023. On 1 April 2025, Unilever announced it had acquired Wild, adding it to its global personal-care portfolio; terms were not disclosed.

Business Model

Wild's core model is refill-based personal care: customers buy a durable, reusable case (deodorant, and later body wash, hand wash and lip balm formats) and then purchase compostable or recyclable refill inserts rather than a new plastic container each time. Sales channels include a direct-to-consumer subscription/e-commerce model via wearewild.com and broad UK retail distribution — the brand is stocked in Tesco, Sainsbury's, Waitrose, Boots, Superdrug and Selfridges, reportedly reaching over 10,000 stores. Financially, Wild scaled quickly: revenue grew 77% to £46.9 million in the year to December 2023, with the company recording its first annual profit (£509,000) that year; industry estimates cited net sales of roughly £65 million for 2024, though this figure comes from trade press rather than filed accounts and should be treated as an estimate. On 1 April 2025, Unilever acquired Wild outright; pre-acquisition reporting indicated founders Bowes-Lyon and Ward held roughly 40% of the company between them, implying a deal value in the region of £250 million (unconfirmed by either party, as terms were not officially disclosed). Wild continues to operate under Unilever's Personal Care division.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (reusable cases refilled with compostable/bamboo-pulp inserts across deodorant, body wash, hand wash and lip balm lines)
  • Waste Reduction (stated per-refill plastic savings; plastic-free/compostable packaging materials)
  • Consumer Education (public sustainability/impact reporting; certifications communicated to consumers)

Products & Services

Refillable natural deodorant (roll-on and stick formats), refillable body wash, refillable hand wash, and refillable lip balm, sold as a reusable case plus recurring refill inserts, available via direct-to-consumer subscription and UK/international retail.

Customers

Individual consumers in the UK (primary market) and increasingly other geographies, purchasing via direct-to-consumer subscription (wearewild.com) and through major UK supermarket and health/beauty retail chains.

Partnerships

Retail distribution: Tesco, Sainsbury's, Waitrose, Boots, Superdrug, Selfridges. Sustainability/certification partners: OnAMission (tree-planting partner — over 368,736 trees planted as of the most recent company disclosure found), 1% for the Planet, B Corp (B Lab), Leaping Bunny (cruelty-free), The Vegan Society, and M-Tick (menopause-friendly product certification). Corporate parent (since April 2025): Unilever plc.

Measurable Impact

Company disclosures state Wild has more than 4.5 million customers who have "switched" to its products, and over 72,000 five-star reviews on Trustpilot (wearewild.com, self-reported, undated). The brand states each deodorant refill saves approximately 30 grams of plastic versus a single-use container (self-reported). Its tree-planting partner OnAMission is credited with planting over 368,736 trees on Wild's behalf (self-reported, undated), and Wild states it is a "carbon negative" business, offsetting 1.5 times its emissions. Wild was named a Which? Best Buy for natural deodorant by the UK consumer group Which? — an independently conducted, third-party comparative test, making this one of the more credibly independent claims in the profile. Revenue and profit figures (£46.9m revenue, £509k profit, year to December 2023) are sourced from UK company filings as reported by trade press (The Grocer) and are relatively high-confidence, though more recent (2024–2025) revenue figures circulating in trade press are estimates rather than confirmed filed accounts.

Technology used

Not publicly available (no proprietary app, RFID or digital reuse-tracking system identified; the refill model is physical/manual — customers insert new refill sticks into a reusable case).

Challenges

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Wild is one of the most commercially successful refillable-packaging consumer brands to emerge from the UK reuse/refill sector, with credible, filed-accounts-based revenue figures, extensive mainstream retail distribution, a genuinely independent third-party endorsement (Which? Best Buy), and multiple recognised sustainability certifications (B Corp, Leaping Bunny, 1% for the Planet). Its acquisition by Unilever in April 2025 is the single most important fact for the knowledge library to capture: Wild is no longer an independent reuse-sector challenger brand but a subsidiary within a major multinational consumer-goods portfolio, which changes its strategic profile from "independent disruptor" to "acquired sustainability brand within Big FMCG" — a pattern with implications for how the REUSE Foundation frames scale-and-exit pathways for refill brands. It remains a high-priority, well-documented case study.
