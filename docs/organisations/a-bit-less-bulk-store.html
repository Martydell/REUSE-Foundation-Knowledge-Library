Organisation Overview

A Bit Less Bulk Store is a small, independently run zero-waste retail shop in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, operating since 2018. Its mission is to eliminate single-use plastic packaging by letting customers refill organic food staples and household/personal-care products using their own containers. It is a physical, single-location bricks-and-mortar store rather than a chain, franchise, or online-first business, and its public presence is primarily via Facebook rather than a dedicated company website.

Business Model

The store operates a bring-your-own-container bulk-refill model: customers bring reusable containers or tubs and fill them with organic dry goods (nuts, grains, snacks, sauces, cereals, tea, cocoa, legumes, seeds, spices), alongside fresh organic vegetables, free-range eggs, and homemade goods such as sourdough bread. It also sells reusable items directly (silicone/stainless-steel lunch boxes and collapsible cups) and runs occasional workshops. Revenue comes from direct in-store retail sales to individual consumers, with delivery offered as an option. As a small independent shop, it has no disclosed formal company registration details, revenue figures, or employee count in public sources.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

A Bit Less Bulk Store is a small but genuine example of grassroots, community-scale reuse infrastructure — the bring-your-own-container bulk model is one of the most direct and low-tech forms of packaging reuse, and its multi-year operation (since 2018) in a market (Malaysia) with limited zero-waste retail options adds some regional relevance. Its value as a case study is limited by the near-total absence of published data (no impact metrics, no company website, single social-media presence), making it hard to use for benchmarking or quantified analysis.
