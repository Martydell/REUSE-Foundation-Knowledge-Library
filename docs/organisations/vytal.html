Organisation Overview

Vytal (VYTAL Global GmbH) is a Cologne-based company that operates a digital, deposit-free reusable packaging system for takeaway and delivery food and drink. Founded in 2019 by Sven Witthöft and Tim Breker (joined shortly after by Fabian Barthel in 2020), the company provides durable, microwave-safe containers to restaurants, canteens, delivery platforms, caterers and event operators; consumers borrow and return containers for free via a smartphone app and QR-code scanning, with partner businesses paying per use rather than consumers paying a deposit. Vytal reports operating across more than 20 countries as of 2025.

Business Model

Vytal runs a B2B software-and-hardware platform: it supplies reusable containers to food-service partners (independent restaurants, canteens, corporate catering, delivery platforms, stadiums and event operators), who pay Vytal a per-use fee for each container issued. Consumers do not pay a cash deposit; instead they register in the Vytal app, scan a QR code on the container at the point of collection, and return it — empty and at any participating partner location or dedicated collection point — within a set window, with the return tracked digitally. This "deposit-free" model is Vytal's key differentiator from Germany's traditional bottle-deposit (Pfand) systems. The company reports a 99.2% container return rate. Vytal has raised institutional venture funding, including a reported €14.2 million round announced in March 2025 (EU-Startups) to scale its technology and international expansion, and has grown from an initial German market focus to operations reported across more than 20 countries, including France, Austria, the Netherlands, the UK and the United States, plus newer franchise-model expansion into markets such as Albania, Greece, the UAE and South Africa.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging (durable, multi-use food/drink containers replacing single-use takeaway packaging)
  • Deposit Return (deposit-free variant — no cash deposit, but a tracked borrow/return system)
  • Digital Tracking (QR-code and app-based tracking of container issue/return; RFID used in some stadium/event deployments)
  • Reverse Logistics (collection, washing/sanitising and redistribution of containers via partner network and dedicated collection points)
  • Waste Reduction (stated aim of reducing single-use packaging waste in food service)

Products & Services

Reusable, microwave-safe food and drink containers; a consumer-facing mobile app for borrowing/returning containers via QR code; a B2B operations platform ("Vytal.ONE") for partner businesses to manage container issue, tracking and billing; point-of-sale (POS) integrations; RFID-based tracking for large event/stadium catering deployments.

Customers

B2B: independent restaurants, corporate and institutional canteens, catering companies (e.g., Aramark, Compass Group), delivery platforms, retail/convenience chains, event and stadium operators, and municipalities. B2C: individual consumers who borrow containers when ordering takeaway or delivery food from partner venues.

Partnerships

Named partners reported in company and press materials include Uber Eats, Lieferando, KFC, PepsiCo, Aramark, Compass Group, Valora, Center Parcs, and event/media partners including UEFA Euro 2024 Fanzones, Hyrox, and Live Nation's Insomniac Festival Group. The company states it has more than 7,000 partner locations across 20+ countries (self-reported, current figure not independently audited).

Measurable Impact

Vytal states a 99.2% container return rate, achieved through its app-based tracking system (company website, 2025). The company's website displays an animated counter citing very large cumulative figures for "waste diverted" and consumers reached; these figures are marketing-page counters rather than dated, sourced disclosures, are not independently audited, and should be treated with caution rather than cited as verified statistics. No independently audited tonnage of plastic or CO2 avoided was found in this research pass.

Technology used

Proprietary smartphone app for consumers (QR-code scan-out/scan-in of containers); B2B "Vytal.ONE" operations and billing platform for partner venues; POS-system integrations; RFID tracking used in some large-scale event/stadium deployments.

Challenges

Not publicly available (no independently documented operational or financial challenges were found in this research pass).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Vytal is a substantive, well-documented reusable-packaging infrastructure operator with a clearly differentiated deposit-free model, a credible reported return rate (99.2%), verifiable named enterprise partners (Uber Eats, KFC, PepsiCo, Aramark, Compass Group), and recent institutional funding (reported €14.2M, March 2025) supporting international scale-up. It is a strong example of digitally enabled B2B reuse infrastructure for the food-service sector and merits high priority for the knowledge library. The main caveat is that its own published impact figures (e.g., the large "waste diverted" counter on its homepage) are self-reported marketing statistics without clear methodology or independent audit, so headline numbers should be sourced cautiously and distinguished from the better-substantiated return-rate figure.

Priority: ★★★★ High

Verification Notes

Seed data described Vytal's model as "deposit-return"; verified sources consistently describe it as deposit-free (no cash deposit — tracked borrow/return via app instead), which is Vytal's own stated point of differentiation from conventional deposit systems. This is a meaningful correction to the seed characterisation. Founding date verified as summer 2019 in Cologne by three co-founders (Sven Witthöft, Tim Breker, and Fabian Barthel, who joined March 2020), matching the seed "2019" figure. Employee count varies by source (LinkedIn: 51-200, matching seed; PitchBook: ~89) — treated as a range. The large "waste diverted" figure shown as an animated counter on the company homepage could not be corroborated with a dated, methodology-backed disclosure and is flagged as low-confidence marketing content rather than a verified impact metric.
