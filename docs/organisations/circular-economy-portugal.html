Organisation Overview

Circular Economy Portugal (CEP) is a Lisbon-based non-profit association working to advance a circular economy and a "society without waste" in Portugal. It focuses on waste prevention, reuse, and repair through knowledge-sharing, research, capacity-building, and project implementation grounded in social innovation and collaborative action. CEP supports public and private-sector entities — including businesses, government bodies, and civil society groups — with consultancy, training, and applied research on circular economy transitions, including reusable packaging systems. It is a member of the Zero Waste Europe network.

Business Model

CEP operates as a membership-based non-profit association funded through a mix of individual and organisational membership fees (individual membership listed at €5/year, with tiered organisational rates), donations, and paid project/consultancy work. Its activities span three broad tracks: (1) knowledge production, including studies such as "Making the Business Case for Packaging Reuse Systems," which evaluates the scaling potential of reusable packaging for companies and policymakers; (2) direct-delivery programmes such as Repair Café events tied to the "Right to Repair" movement, and a "Circular Pioneers" practitioner network; and (3) paid consultancy services helping public and private organisations design and implement circular economy strategies, from policy support to packaging design and waste collection systems. Rather than selling a product, CEP monetises its expertise and convening power, positioning itself as a hub connecting businesses, policymakers, and civil society around circular practices.

Products & Services

Consultancy services for public and private entities on circular economy strategy, packaging design, and waste collection; applied research/studies (e.g., packaging reuse systems scaling study); Repair Café events; the "Circular Pioneers" practitioner network; training and capacity-building workshops and presentations.

Customers

Portuguese businesses, municipal and national government bodies, and civil society organisations seeking support to adopt circular economy practices; individual and organisational members of the association.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Circular Economy Portugal is a credible, established national NGO with a specific and relevant focus on reuse, repair, and waste prevention, including original research on reusable packaging system scalability that is directly useful for the REUSE Foundation's knowledge base. Its role is advisory/advocacy rather than operational, so it does not itself run reuse infrastructure, but its studies, policy engagement, and network position (Zero Waste Europe membership) make it a useful source of Portuguese and EU-level circular economy intelligence.
