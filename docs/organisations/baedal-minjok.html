Baedal Minjok (Baemin)

South Korea's largest food delivery app, operated by Woowa Brothers (a Delivery Hero subsidiary), which since August 2022 has run a reusable-container delivery pilot — starting in Gangnam, Seoul, and later expanded to other districts — in partnership with zero-waste operator Itgreen.

Organisation Overview

Baedal Minjok ("Baemin") is South Korea's largest food delivery app, launched in 2010 by founder Kim Bong-jin and operated by Woowa Brothers Corp., which became a Delivery Hero subsidiary following Delivery Hero's acquisition (with Delivery Hero required to divest its other Korean platform, Yogiyo, to address antitrust concerns). Within this large conventional delivery business, Woowa Brothers began piloting a reusable-container ("다회용기") delivery order option on 29 August 2022, first available in Seoul's Gangnam district and later extended to other districts (e.g. Gwanak, Gwangjin) and, by 2025, further regional expansion with added user incentives.

Business Model

Baemin's core business is a commission-based food delivery marketplace connecting restaurants, delivery riders and consumers across South Korea — a large-scale, conventional on-demand delivery platform. Layered onto this is the reusable-container pilot: consumers ordering from participating restaurants can select a "reusable container" delivery option via a banner on the Baemin app's home screen for eligible addresses; supply, collection and washing of the containers is managed not by Woowa Brothers directly but by Itgreen (잇그린), a zero-waste startup that operates the "Returnit" (리턴잇) reusable-container service. To encourage adoption, participating users who register with Korea's national Carbon Neutral Practice Point system earn 1,000 points per reusable-container order, redeemable as cash or card-issuer points, up to an annual cap per person (reported as up to KRW 70,000/year). The programme sits within a citywide push — Seoul city government and multiple delivery apps have collaborated on reusable-container "zero-waste restaurant" expansion — rather than being a Baemin-exclusive initiative.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging (reusable food-delivery containers offered as an order option)
  • Reverse Logistics (container collection and washing, managed by partner Itgreen/Returnit)
  • Consumer Education (in-app promotion and carbon-neutral-points incentive to encourage uptake)

Not identified from public sources: refill systems, deposit-return schemes operated directly by Baemin, or company-wide (non-pilot) packaging reuse commitments.

Products & Services

Food delivery marketplace app connecting restaurants and consumers across South Korea; within this, an optional reusable-container delivery service for eligible restaurants/addresses, with containers supplied, collected and washed by third-party partner Itgreen (Returnit).

Customers

Millions of South Korean consumers using the Baemin app for food delivery generally; within the reusable-container pilot specifically, a smaller subset of users in participating districts (initially Gangnam, later expanded) who opt into container reuse and register for carbon-neutral incentive points.

Partnerships

Itgreen (잇그린) — Seoul-based zero-waste startup operating the "Returnit" (리턴잇) reusable-container supply/collection/washing service, which manages logistics for Baemin's reusable-container pilot; Seoul Metropolitan Government — city-led "zero-waste restaurant" and reusable-container expansion initiatives involving multiple delivery apps, including Baemin.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available as an independently audited figure specific to Baemin's programme. Korean press coverage (2022-2025) documents the pilot's geographic rollout (Gangnam initial launch, later expansion to additional Seoul districts and beyond) and the incentive structure (1,000 carbon-neutral points per order, up to ~KRW 70,000/year per person), but no verified total figures for containers in circulation, orders fulfilled, or plastic waste avoided were found.

Technology

In-app ordering feature (banner/selection for reusable-container-eligible restaurants within the Baemin app); reusable-container supply chain and washing/collection logistics are operated by partner Itgreen under its Returnit service.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Baemin's reusable-container pilot is a verified, well-documented example of a large mainstream delivery platform integrating reuse infrastructure via a specialist third-party operator (Itgreen/Returnit), backed by a public carbon-incentive scheme — a notable model for how big platforms can bootstrap reuse systems without owning the logistics themselves. However, it remains a geographically limited pilot/opt-in feature within an overwhelmingly larger single-use-dominated delivery business, and no independent scale or impact metrics are publicly available, so its current footprint should not be overstated. It is a valuable "platform-enabled reuse" case study for the library, distinct from companies where reuse is the core business.

Verification Notes

The seed data's characterisation is largely confirmed: Baemin is indeed Korea's largest food delivery app, a Woowa Brothers/Delivery Hero subsidiary, and does run a reusable-container pilot that began in Gangnam. One correction/clarification: the seed data's 2010 founding year is corroborated by most sources (app/service launched 2010 by Kim Bong-jin), though at least one secondary source states the Woowa Brothers company was formally founded in 2011, roughly five months after the June 2010 app launch — both years appear in circulation and are noted here rather than silently resolved. The reusable-container pilot itself launched specifically on 29 August 2022 (not 2010) and is operated in partnership with third-party startup Itgreen/Returnit, a detail not in the seed data but confirmed across multiple independent Korean news sources.
