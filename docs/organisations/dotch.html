Organisation Overview

dotch is a Berlin-based startup founded in 2022 that builds a reusable packaging system for cooking oils, vinegars, preserves and sauces. It supplies producers with a standardised, unbreakable bottle designed for up to 50 reuse cycles, and operates the full reverse-logistics loop — pool management, empties collection, industrial cleaning and redistribution — as a service to food producers and retailers, so that "reusable" becomes as operationally simple as single-use packaging. Its flagship deployment is with Bio Planète olive/cooking oils, distributed in dotch reusable bottles through more than 140 Alnatura organic-supermarket branches across Germany; the partnership won a WorldStar Award from the World Packaging Organisation.

Business Model

dotch operates a Packaging-as-a-Service (PaaS) B2B model: rather than selling bottles outright, it provides food producers (e.g. oil brands like Bio Planète) with reusable glass/durable bottles and manages the entire reuse cycle — bottle pooling/ownership, logistics for collecting empties from retail/consumer touchpoints, industrial washing and sanitising, quality control, and redistribution back into the supply chain for refilling. Producers pay dotch for this managed reusable-packaging service instead of purchasing single-use bottles, aiming to make reusable packaging cost- and operations-competitive with disposables at retail scale. Retail distribution is achieved through partnerships with grocery chains (Alnatura, over 140 branches) that stock the reusable-bottled products; consumers return empty bottles at retail collection points rather than dealing directly with dotch. The company reports its system saves roughly 50% of CO2 emissions compared to single-use glass packaging, positioning reusable oil bottles as a direct swap-in replacement for conventional pre-packaged food goods.

Measurable Impact

dotch's reusable bottle system is reported to save approximately 50% of CO2 emissions compared to single-use glass packaging (company/press materials, gica.community); Bio Planète oils in dotch bottles are available in 140+ Alnatura branches across Germany (fachpack.de, 2024). No independently audited third-party lifecycle assessment or total-bottles-reused figures were found in verified public sources.

Technology

Standardised reusable bottle design engineered for durability across up to 50 reuse cycles; managed reverse-logistics and industrial cleaning infrastructure. No specific digital tracking (RFID/QR/app) system was confirmed in public sources.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

dotch is a strong, clearly-scoped example of the Packaging-as-a-Service model applied to food packaging reuse, with verified retail-scale deployment (140+ Alnatura branches) and industry recognition (WorldStar Award). Its full-service approach — designed explicitly to make reusable packaging as convenient as single-use — directly addresses a core barrier to reuse adoption in FMCG supply chains, making it a highly relevant model for the REUSE Foundation, albeit still an early-stage startup (founded 2022) with impact data currently limited to company-reported figures rather than independent audits.
