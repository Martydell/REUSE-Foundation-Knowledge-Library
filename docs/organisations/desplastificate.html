Organisation Overview

Desplastifícate ("De-plasticize yourself") is a Mexico City-based social enterprise that sells biodegradable, non-toxic cleaning and home-care products in returnable glass containers. Its circular model charges a small deposit (reported as 10 Mexican pesos per container) that is refunded or credited when customers return empties with their next order. The company positions itself against single-use plastic and chemical-heavy cleaning products, offering formulas described as biodegradable within 14 days. It is recognised as a social enterprise by the IKEA Social Entrepreneurship initiative. The seed name "Deplastificate" is a misspelling; the verified legal/brand name is "Desplastifícate," matching its domain desplastificate.com.mx.

Business Model

Desplastifícate runs a direct-to-consumer e-commerce and subscription model centred on Mexico City (CDMX). Customers order biodegradable cleaning products online; products are delivered in reusable glass bottles, and when customers place a repeat order, empty bottles are collected for cleaning and refilling, closing the packaging loop. A small refundable deposit (~10 MXN per bottle) incentivises returns. This bottle-exchange "circular economy" model is currently limited to CDMX due to the logistics of empties collection; customers outside the return zone can still buy the biodegradable products but without the physical bottle-return loop. Revenue comes from product sales (cleaning liquids, detergents, personal-care items) with the deposit functioning as a returnable-packaging mechanism rather than a separate revenue stream. The company markets itself as a social/environmental enterprise rather than a conventional FMCG brand, and its inclusion in the IKEA Social Entrepreneurship network suggests external validation and possibly incubation support.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging — biodegradable cleaning products sold in reusable glass bottles
  • Deposit Return — refundable per-bottle deposit (~10 MXN) for returned empties
  • Reverse Logistics — home delivery paired with empty-container pickup on repeat orders (CDMX only)
  • Consumer Education — communication around plastic reduction and biodegradability on-site

Products & Services

Biodegradable, non-toxic home-cleaning products (multi-surface cleaners, detergents and related household items) sold in returnable/refillable glass bottles, plus (per site collections) some biodegradable disposable/eco product lines.

Customers

Environmentally conscious households and consumers in Mexico City ordering via the company's online store; broader Mexico-wide customers for non-returnable biodegradable product lines.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Desplastifícate is a small but genuine reuse/refill operator: it runs an actual returnable-glass-bottle system with a deposit mechanism and reverse logistics, which is closer to core reuse-system practice than many marketing-only "eco" brands. Its scale is limited (CDMX-only for the return loop) and no independent impact data is publicly available, but the underlying model — deposit, reuse, reverse logistics — is directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's mission. Verification and expansion of its impact reporting would strengthen its case for a higher priority rating.
