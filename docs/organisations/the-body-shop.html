The Body Shop

Pioneering ethical-beauty retailer with a long-running in-store refill scheme, recently rebuilt after 2024 administration under new ownership (Auréa Group).

Year Founded 1976
Status Active (post-administration; restructured)
Ownership Private — owned by Auréa Group consortium (acquired September 2024, following a February 2024 UK administration under prior owner Aurelius)

Organisation Overview

The Body Shop was founded in 1976 by Anita Roddick, who opened the first shop in Brighton, England, built around ethically sourced, cruelty-free beauty products and environmental/social campaigning. Ownership passed from L'Oréal (2006–2017, £652.3m) to Natura &Co (2017–2023, £880m) to Aurelius Group (autumn 2023, £207m). Aurelius placed the UK business into administration in February 2024 owing an estimated £276m to creditors, leading to roughly 85 UK store closures and around 500 retail plus 270 head-office redundancies. In September 2024, a consortium led by Auréa Group (Mike Jatania and Charles Denton) acquired The Body Shop's British business, trademarks, and its remaining ~113 UK stores plus assets in Australia and the US. Mike Jatania became CEO in 2025 after Charles Denton's departure.

Business Model

The Body Shop is a B2C beauty and personal-care retailer operating through owned stores, franchise/master-franchise partners, and e-commerce across roughly 1,000+ locations globally (pre- and post-restructuring figures vary by market). Its differentiator within the REUSE Foundation's scope is an in-store refill scheme, launched globally in 2021 with an initial five-year rollout target, allowing customers to refill shower gel, shampoo, conditioner and hand-wash formats into returned/reused bottles at dedicated refill stations, earning loyalty points and receiving bonus product volume as an incentive. Separately, the company runs a Community Fair Trade Recycled Plastic (CFTRP) program with the social enterprise Plastics For Change, sourcing recycled plastic collected by waste-picker communities in India under fair-trade terms for use in packaging — a material-recovery/recycling initiative distinct from the refill scheme. Following the 2024 administration, company statements indicate the refill program was disrupted alongside the wider business, with a broader relaunch positioned as part of a 2026 sustainability "reset" tied to the brand's 50th anniversary.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (in-store refill stations for shower gel, shampoo, conditioner, hand wash)
  • Reusable Packaging (customer-returned bottles at refill stations; refillable makeup cases)
  • Material Recovery (Community Fair Trade Recycled Plastic supply chain with Plastics For Change)
  • Consumer Education (in-store and campaign-based sustainability messaging)
  • Take-back (historic in-store recycling points; scope/current status not independently verified)

Products & Services

Skincare, body care, haircare, fragrance and cosmetics, sold through owned/franchised retail stores and e-commerce; in-store refill format available for a subset of shower gel, shampoo, conditioner and hand-wash SKUs, and for a limited makeup range (e.g. refillable Peptalk Lipstick, launched with 100% recycled-aluminium refillable casing).

Customers

Mass-market beauty consumers globally, through owned and franchised retail plus online channels; refill program specifically targets repeat in-store shoppers via loyalty incentives (double Club member points on refill purchases).

Partnerships

  • Plastics For Change — Community Fair Trade Recycled Plastic supply chain partner since 2019 (source: Beauty Packaging, 2019; Plastics For Change blog, 2024)
  • Auréa Group (Mike Jatania, Charles Denton) — current majority owner/investor consortium since September 2024 (source: Auréa Group press release; Wikipedia)

Measurable Impact

  • Plastics For Change reports that its Community Fair Trade Recycled Plastic partnership with The Body Shop had diverted over 2,424.13 metric tons of plastic from polluting oceans, as of a February 2024 blog post (source: plasticsforchange.org, 23 Feb 2024). This figure relates to the fair-trade recycled-plastic sourcing program, not the in-store refill scheme specifically; not independently audited.
  • Company's own "Refill Scheme" page (thebodyshop.com) states a 2023 target of 850 refill stations (up from 720) and a stated goal of saving "over 1 million" units of plastic, alternatively described elsewhere on the same page as "over 30 tonnes," by end of 2023 — these are company-stated targets/projections, not confirmed audited outcomes, and the page itself does not reconcile the two figures.
  • Press reporting (2022) states the refill program "prevented more than half a million plastic bottles from entering the environment" in 2022, with refill stations in roughly 800 stores globally as of late 2025 reporting. Figures are company-sourced via press materials, not independently audited.

Technology used

Not publicly available in verified detail (refill stations are described as in-store dispensing units; no proprietary tracking/app technology identified in sources reviewed).

Challenges

  • February 2024 UK administration (Aurelius ownership) resulted in store closures, redundancies, and — per subsequent reporting — disruption to sustainability initiatives including the refill program, which the company describes intending to "restore" as part of a 2026 strategic reset (source: premiumbeautynews.com).
  • Multiple ownership changes since 2006 (L'Oréal, Natura &Co, Aurelius, Auréa Group) have coincided with inconsistent public reporting of store counts, headcount and program scope; current, precise employee numbers post-restructuring are "Not publicly available."

REUSE Foundation Assessment

The Body Shop is a historically important, high-visibility example of in-store refill retail at global mass-market scale, and its refill scheme (launched 2021) is a genuine, purpose-built reuse mechanism rather than a peripheral initiative. However, recent corporate turmoil — administration in February 2024, a change of ownership in September 2024, and a further CEO change in 2025 — means current program scope, store counts and impact figures are in flux and should be re-verified before being treated as current. The seed data's plastic-saved figure (2,424.13 metric tons) is verifiable to a named source but describes the recycled-plastic sourcing program, not the refill scheme, and this distinction should be preserved in the tracker. Given the brand's scale and its planned 2026 sustainability relaunch, it remains a priority organisation to track and re-verify.
