Organisation Overview

Woodly Oy is a Finnish materials-technology company, headquartered in Helsinki, that has developed Woodly®, a patented bioplastic made from FSC-certified wood cellulose. Development began in 2011 in cooperation with VTT, Finland's state research institute, and the company has since built out UK-facing commercial leadership to support European growth (announced July 2026).

Business Model

Woodly is a B2B materials supplier, licensing/selling its Woodly® wood-cellulose bioplastic as films, flexibles, and rigid formats to packaging producers and food companies (e.g., Sodexo among branded partners) as a substitute for conventional fossil-based plastic in fresh-food packaging. The material is marketed as mechanically recyclable and free of PFAS, BPA, phthalates, and CMR chemicals, extending shelf life and supporting customers' packaging-sustainability goals; Woodly earned RECOUP certification (June 2026) confirming it is collectable, detectable, and mechanically recyclable.

Products & Services

Woodly® wood-cellulose-based bioplastic, supplied as films, flexible packaging, and rigid packaging formats for fresh-food applications.

Customers

B2B packaging producers and food/retail companies, including Sodexo and other branded partners; used in fresh-produce packaging (e.g., with HKScan in Finland).

Partnerships

VTT (Finland's state research institute, material co-development); Sodexo; HKScan; RECOUP (UK recycling certification body).

Measurable Impact

RECOUP-certified (June 2026) as collectable, detectable, and mechanically recyclable; company states the material extends fresh-produce shelf life and reduces food waste/CO2e, though no independently audited tonnage figures were found.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Woodly is a well-established (2011-founded), technically credentialed Finnish materials innovator with a genuine alternative-materials product (not reuse/refill per se, but material substitution reducing fossil-plastic packaging and supporting recyclability). It fits the "Other"/material-innovation category adjacent to core reuse activities; relevant primarily for its recyclability certification and food-waste-reduction packaging function rather than direct reuse infrastructure.
