Organisation Overview

Eco Product is a Karachi, Pakistan-based e-commerce business supplying sustainable and biodegradable packaging plus eco-friendly lifestyle products to consumers and businesses across Pakistan. Its catalogue spans kraft standup pouches (45+ variants) for food, cosmetics and beverage packaging, bakery and burger-box packaging, coffee cups, biodegradable food containers, e-commerce shipping boxes, and bamboo/lifestyle items such as toothbrushes, cutlery, cosmetic brushes and soap dishes. No founding date, founders, or ownership structure could be verified from the website or search results.

Business Model

Eco Product operates as a B2B/B2C e-commerce supplier of eco-friendly packaging materials and lifestyle products, selling primarily to Pakistani businesses (food service, bakery, cosmetics, e-commerce sellers) needing sustainable packaging alternatives, as well as directly to consumers seeking bamboo/reusable lifestyle items. Its core packaging lines — kraft standup pouches, bakery/burger boxes, biodegradable food containers and coffee cups — are predominantly single-use, compostable or recyclable materials-substitution products (paper/kraft/bamboo replacing plastic) rather than reusable or refillable packaging systems; the pouches described in the seed data function as compostable/recyclable single-use packaging supplied to other businesses, not a consumer-facing reuse or refill scheme. Separately, the company sells genuinely reusable consumer items (bamboo/stainless-steel cutlery, drinking straws, water bottles) as lifestyle retail products. The business appears to operate as a materials-substitution packaging supplier and eco-lifestyle retailer rather than as a reuse-system operator.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Eco Product is primarily a supplier of single-use, compostable/recyclable packaging materials (kraft pouches, food containers, boxes) to Pakistani businesses, alongside a secondary line of genuinely reusable bamboo/lifestyle consumer goods. The seed's characterisation as "reusable food/cosmetics/beverage packaging" is only partly accurate — most of its pouch/box catalogue is single-use materials substitution rather than reuse or refill infrastructure — limiting its direct relevance to the REUSE Foundation's reuse-focused mandate.
