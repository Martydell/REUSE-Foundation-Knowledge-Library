Organisation Overview

LENA the Fashion Library was conceived in 2013 and launched its pilot store in Amsterdam in December 2014, founded by three sisters — Angela, Diana and Elisa Jansen — together with Suzanne Smulders. It was the first combined online and offline clothing library in the Netherlands, distinguishing itself from occasion-wear rental competitors by focusing on everyday clothing. Over more than a decade of operation, LENA has outlasted many clothing-rental peers, refining its model into a "pay-as-you-borrow" system after experimenting with flat subscriptions and single-item rentals.

Business Model

LENA operates a clothing-as-a-service model in which members rent everyday garments — rather than special-occasion wear — for a period of time instead of purchasing them outright, returning items to the library for cleaning, repair and re-circulation to other members. The company iterated its pricing model over its ten-plus years of operation, moving away from flat-fee subscription and single-rental transaction models toward a "pay-as-you-borrow" structure that charges based on actual usage/borrowing patterns, which the founders identified as a better fit for customer behaviour than fixed subscriptions. The business operates from a physical location in Amsterdam combined with online browsing/booking, and has been cited in circular-economy case-study platforms (Holland Circular Hotspot) as a reference example of clothing-sharing/rental as an alternative to fast fashion.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

LENA the Fashion Library is a well-documented, long-running pioneer of clothing rental-as-reuse in the Netherlands, notable for its decade-plus survival in a sector where many competitors have failed, and for its focus on everyday (rather than occasion) wear, which broadens the addressable reuse impact versus typical formalwear rental services. Its evolution toward pay-as-you-borrow pricing is a documented, transferable business-model lesson for the sector. Independently verified impact metrics are not available, which is the main gap in an otherwise strong profile.
