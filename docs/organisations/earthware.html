Organisation Overview

Earthware Eco Lifestyle is a South African e-commerce retailer specialising in disposable tableware made from fallen areca palm leaves. The company markets its plates, bowls and platters as fully biodegradable and organic, made without harming trees or using chemicals or artificial additives, converting an agricultural waste by-product into single-use dinnerware. The business is based in North Riding, Randburg, Johannesburg, and ships nationally within South Africa. No founding date, founders, or ownership structure was found on the public website or in independent coverage.

Business Model

Earthware operates as a direct-to-consumer and business-to-business online retailer, selling imported/sourced areca palm-leaf disposable tableware (plates, platters, bowls, including a "heart" bowl range) alongside complementary compostable products such as eco napitkins, plastic-free cellophane bags and wooden cutlery. Customers order via the website with stated delivery times of 3-7 working days. The core value proposition is substituting single-use plastic or paper tableware — commonly used for events, catering and takeaway food service — with a compostable, plant-based alternative made from a fallen agricultural by-product rather than virgin material. This is a materials-substitution model (biodegradable disposables replacing plastic disposables) rather than a reuse or refill model: the products are still single-use and are disposed of (composted) after one use, distinguishing Earthware from genuine reuse/refill operators in the REUSE Foundation's scope.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Earthware sells compostable single-use tableware rather than reusable or refillable products, so its fit with the REUSE Foundation's reuse-focused mandate is weak — it is a "better disposable" substitution business, not a circular reuse system. It may be of tangential interest as a material-recovery/waste-reduction example (upcycling a fallen agricultural by-product) but offers no repair, refill, deposit-return or take-back activity.
