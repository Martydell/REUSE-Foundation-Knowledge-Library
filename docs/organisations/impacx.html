Organisation Overview

ImpacX (now operating and trading as Water.io) is an Israeli IoT technology company founded in 2015 and based in Rehovot. It builds a connected-packaging platform centered on smart caps — Bluetooth-enabled lids for reusable water bottles and vitamin/supplement containers — that measure liquid level or dosage and sync usage data to a companion mobile app. Its two product lines are Water.io (smart hydration) and Vitamins.io (smart supplement tracking). The company is publicly traded on the Tel Aviv Stock Exchange. Its most notable commercial deployment is powering Gatorade's "Smart Gx" connected bottle line.

Business Model

ImpacX/Water.io sells a hardware-plus-software platform: reusable bottle caps (and equivalent lids for pill/vitamin bottles) embedded with sensors that track fill level, consumption timing, and usage patterns, paired with a mobile app that delivers hydration or medication-adherence reminders and personalized coaching. Revenue is generated through B2B licensing/co-branding deals with consumer brands (its highest-profile partnership being with Gatorade, whose Smart Gx bottles are powered by ImpacX's underlying technology) and through direct-to-consumer sales of its own Water.io and Vitamins.io branded products. The core value proposition is data — turning a reusable container into a connected device that generates behavioral and consumption data for both the end user (habit tracking, reminders) and brand partners (consumer usage insight). The bottles/caps themselves are designed for repeated, long-term reuse rather than being disposable, but the company's core differentiator is the embedded electronics and software layer, not a packaging-reuse logistics system.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

ImpacX/Water.io's relevance to reuse is real but narrow: its smart caps are designed to sit on reusable bottles and encourage sustained reuse through habit-tracking and reminders, and its Gatorade partnership demonstrates commercial traction. However, its core business is IoT/data technology, not a reuse system, refill infrastructure, or packaging pooling operation — the reusability of the underlying bottle is almost incidental to the product's actual value proposition (hydration/adherence tracking). It is a useful adjacent technology enabler for reuse behavior rather than a reuse operator in its own right.
