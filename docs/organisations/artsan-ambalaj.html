Organisation Overview

Artsan Ambalaj is a Turkish flexible-packaging manufacturer established in May 2020 and operational since 2022, part of the diversified Sanat Group (industrial operations since 1973; flexible-packaging investment since 2016). Operating from an integrated production campus in Tekirdağ with a stated annual capacity of 60,000 tons, Artsan produces films, pouches, wicket bags and laminated structures — including mono-material, recyclable, and post-consumer-recycled (PCR) content options — for food and beverage, personal care, home care, and industrial B2B customers. The company frames sustainability, including recyclability and reusability of its packaging materials, as a core part of its product strategy.

Business Model

Artsan is a B2B contract packaging-film manufacturer, not a direct-to-consumer or reuse-service provider. It sells engineered flexible packaging materials — MDO PE films, high-barrier multi-layer recyclable films, PIR (post-industrial recycled) products, and PCR (post-consumer recycled) materials made with 100% PCR scrap — to brand owners in food, personal care, home care and industrial sectors, who use them for pouches, wicket bags, and packaging/industrial films. Its role in the value chain is upstream material supply rather than operating reuse infrastructure directly. The company sits alongside sister companies Sanat Packaging and Artpack Packaging, and a dedicated recycling operation, Ecoriver Recycle, within Sanat Group — giving the group internal recycled-feedstock capability. Net sales reportedly grew 134.17% in 2023, with a reported headcount of 197 employees as of 2026 (unverified secondary source; see Verification Notes).

Products & Services

Flexible packaging films and pouches: MDO PE films, high-barrier multi-layer recyclable films, PIR (post-industrial recycled) films, PCR (100% post-consumer recycled scrap) materials, wicket bags, and industrial films, marketed under a "sustainable solutions" range emphasising recyclability and reduced material use.

Customers

B2B — brand owners and manufacturers in food & beverage, personal care, home care, and industrial sectors (specific named customer companies not disclosed).

Partnerships

Sister companies within Sanat Group: Sanat Packaging, Artpack Packaging, and Ecoriver Recycle (an in-group recycling operation providing recycled feedstock). No external third-party partnerships were independently verified.

Technology

Flexo printing (up to 10 colours); multi-layer film co-extrusion and lamination production lines with a stated 60,000-tons/year integrated capacity; no evidence of consumer-facing or reuse-tracking digital technology, consistent with its B2B materials-manufacturer role.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Artsan Ambalaj is a legitimate, scaled B2B flexible-packaging manufacturer with a credible sustainability-oriented product line (recyclable mono-material films, PCR content) and group-level recycling capability via Ecoriver Recycle. However, its packaging is predominantly recyclable/reduced-material rather than reuse-system packaging in the REUSE Foundation's sense (i.e., it does not appear to operate or supply a discrete refill, deposit-return, or reusable-container system) — its relevance is closer to sustainable-materials supply than to reuse-economy infrastructure. Useful as an example of upstream packaging-material sustainability, but of secondary priority for a reuse-focused library.
