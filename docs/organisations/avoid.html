Organisation Overview

AVOID is a Brazilian greentech startup, headquartered in São Paulo and founded in 2020, that develops refill infrastructure for consumer goods companies. Its core proposition is removing water from liquid product formulas (e.g. cleaning or personal-care products) and combining the concentrate with water directly at in-store refill machines, converting retail locations from conventional points of sale into refill points. The company frames this as a way to cut transportation costs, fuel use and carbon emissions associated with shipping mostly-water liquid products, while reducing single-use plastic bottle consumption.

Business Model

AVOID operates a B2B model, working with consumer goods manufacturers and retailers to deploy concentrate-based refill infrastructure rather than selling directly to end consumers. The company supplies or partners on refill machines placed in retail locations, which are stocked with concentrated formula and mix it with water on demand, so retailers can offer refills of cleaning, personal-care or similar liquid products without shipping, storing and disposing of full-volume single-use bottles at every stage of the supply chain. This targets the "last mile" inefficiency of shipping products that are mostly water, aiming to reduce transportation emissions, storage footprint, and single-use plastic packaging simultaneously. AVOID markets itself as offering "a concept beyond a product" — a shift in how liquid consumer goods are formulated, distributed and sold — positioning refill as core infrastructure for retailer and brand partners rather than a niche add-on.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

AVOID is a genuine, verifiable B2B refill-infrastructure startup addressing a real inefficiency (shipping water-heavy liquid products) with a clear circular-economy mechanism — concentrate-plus-refill-machine — that directly reduces single-use plastic bottle consumption. It is a small, early-stage company (2020 founding, small team) with limited public documentation of named partners, deployment scale, or impact metrics, which constrains how much can currently be verified. As a B2B refill-infrastructure model, it is a relevant example of "packaging-as-a-service"-style innovation for the REUSE Foundation library, meriting inclusion but with a note that independent verification of scale/impact is currently limited.
