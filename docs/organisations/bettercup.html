Organisation Overview

bettercup is a Melbourne-founded, female-led enterprise that supplies reusable cups (and related dishware) plus a full-service reuse operating system — collection, washing, drying, storage and logistics — to events, venues, stadiums, universities and bars across Australia and New Zealand. Founded in 2018 by Sam Stone and Christie Kamphuis, the company (legally registered as Encoro Pty Ltd) designs and locally manufactures its own reusable cups. It has received Victorian government circular-economy grant funding to expand manufacturing and washing capacity, and reports serving major clients including the Australian Open, Sydney Opera House and Melbourne Fashion Week.

Business Model

bettercup operates a B2B "reuse-as-a-service" model for the events and hospitality sector. Clients (event organisers, venues, stadiums, festivals, universities and bars) purchase or rent custom-branded reusable cups and dishware, and bettercup provides the surrounding circular infrastructure needed to make reuse operationally viable: collection points at events, industrial washing (via a dedicated Victoria washing hub with tunnel-washer capacity of 3,000 cups/hour), drying, storage, redistribution and consulting support. The company locally manufactures its own cups (reported manufacturing capacity of 3,000 cups/day following a 2023-era capacity expansion) rather than reselling third-party products. Revenue comes from cup sales/rental fees and service contracts with venues and events, positioning bettercup as an outsourced reuse-system operator rather than a pure product seller. It also partners with regional reuse schemes such as FillGood in New Zealand.

Products & Services

Custom-branded reusable cups (purchase or rental) and reusable food dishware; circular support services including on-site collection, industrial washing/drying, storage, logistics, and implementation consulting for events and venues.

Customers

B2B: events, festivals, stadiums, sports venues, universities, bars and brand activations across Australia and New Zealand. Named clients per company sources include the Australian Open, Sydney Opera House, Darwin Festival, Queen Victoria Market and Melbourne Fashion Week.

Partnerships

FillGood (a New Zealand regionwide reuse system) is referenced as a partner/case study on bettercup's own materials. The company also received grant funding from Victoria's Circular Economy Business Innovation Centre (CEBIC) / Circular Economy Business Support Fund. No other independently verified named partners were found.

Measurable Impact

Per bettercup/CEBIC project reporting (CEBIC funded-project page, accessed 2026): over 1,200 tonnes of single-use plastic waste eliminated and 391 events/venues transitioned to reusable systems through the CEBIC-funded capacity-expansion project; company has supplied over 500,000 reusable cups to date, each averaging approximately 300 uses; grant funding of $446,851 (with $275,050.96 co-contribution) doubled manufacturing capacity to 3,000 cups/day and washing capacity to 3,000 cups/hour, creating three full-time positions. Source: CEBIC (Victorian Government) funded-project page.
