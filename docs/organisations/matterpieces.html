Organisation Overview

Matterpieces is a Portuguese startup, founded in 2021 and headquartered in Porto, that converts construction and demolition (C&D) waste into decorative cladding and surface materials for architecture and interior design. Rather than a reusable-components or scaffolding provider, the company operates as a materials-upcycling business: debris sourced from demolition sites and waste-management partners is processed into two composite material collections ("Matter" and "Nebula"), sold as standard catalogue products or custom-developed from a client's own demolition waste. The company positions its work as "circular design," targeting architects, designers and the construction industry, and has been recognised in Portuguese circular-economy and startup-accelerator programmes.

Business Model

Matterpieces operates a two-tier commercial model. Its standard offering is a catalogue of debris-based composite cladding materials (the Matter Collection and Nebula Collection, each with several colour/texture variants) manufactured from construction and demolition waste sourced through waste-management partnerships, sold to architects, designers and construction/renovation clients. Its custom-solutions tier processes a specific client's own demolition debris (minimum project size around 300 m²) into bespoke cladding material for that project, turning what would otherwise be landfilled or down-cycled rubble into a high-value finished building material. This model creates revenue both from stock/catalogue material sales and from bespoke material-development contracts tied to renovation and demolition projects, positioning the company within the construction-industry circular economy as a materials-recovery and upcycling specialist rather than a component-reuse or rental operator.

Products & Services

Matter Collection (decorative cladding materials in several variants, made from construction/demolition debris); Nebula Collection (additional cladding/surface finishes); custom material-development service converting a client's own demolition waste into bespoke cladding (minimum ~300 m² project size).

Customers

Architects, interior designers, and construction/renovation companies seeking sustainable, debris-derived cladding and surface materials (specific named clients not published).

Partnerships

Named collaborators/waste-sourcing partners include RMC and Zircom; Casa do Impacto (Lisbon social-innovation hub) is a named partner/host organisation. The company has been recognised in the Digital Built Accelerator programme (with Typeble and OZU) hosted at Oeiras Campus/Instituto Superior Técnico.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Matterpieces is best classified as a material-recovery/upcycling company for construction and demolition waste rather than a reusable-packaging, rental, or component-reuse operator — this corrects the original seed description, which characterised it as a "reusable construction components (scaffolding/façade systems)" provider. Its work is nonetheless relevant to circular construction, converting waste debris directly into finished, sellable cladding material, and it has attracted recognition in Portuguese circular-economy accelerator programmes. Given the current lack of published impact metrics and a business model focused on material transformation rather than reuse-in-place, it is a moderate-priority entry for the REUSE Foundation's library.
