Alterpacks

Singapore start-up that upcycles spent grains and other food-manufacturing waste into home-compostable, single-use food packaging — a material-substitution business, not a reuse-loop operator.

Organisation Overview

Alterpacks is a Singapore-based start-up, founded in 2019 by Karen Cheah (Founder and CEO) alongside co-founders Steven Tan and Herbin Chia, that converts spent grains and other agricultural/food-manufacturing by-products (e.g. wheat husks) into home-compostable food packaging. Incubated at Singapore Management University's Institute of Innovation & Entrepreneurship (Business Innovation Generator) and an NUS LLP alumnus, the company raised US$1 million in pre-seed funding in late 2022/January 2023. It operates a B2B model supplying food-container alternatives to distributors serving restaurants and hotels, with pilots reported in Indonesia and Vietnam.

Business Model

Alterpacks generates revenue by manufacturing plant-based food containers from upcycled spent grains — a by-product of brewing and other food production — using a process akin to paper-pulp manufacturing, in which automated machinery cleans, mixes and presses the material into container shapes. Its go-to-market approach is B2B, partnering with distribution companies that supply food-and-beverage businesses (restaurants, hotels, event caterers) rather than selling directly to end consumers, though the company also operates a small online shop. Its containers are marketed as freezer- and microwave-safe, PFAS-free and certified home compostable, positioned as a direct substitute for single-use plastic and paper-composite takeaway packaging. The company has stated plans to expand into bio-pellets made from spent grains and other agricultural waste (e.g. coconut shells) as a replacement for petroleum-based resins used in manufacturing equipment, and has targeted commercial expansion into Asia, Australia and Europe following its 2022 pre-seed round. Reported pilots include supplying containers for the 2022 MotoGP event in Mandalika, Indonesia, and for Vietnamese restaurant chain Pizza 4P's.

Products & Services

Home-compostable food containers made from spent grains and other agricultural by-products (freezer- and microwave-friendly, PFAS-free); in-development bio-pellets from spent grains/coconut shells intended to replace petroleum-based resins in manufacturing.

Customers

B2B: food-and-beverage distributors, restaurants and hotels (reported pilots with Pizza 4P's, a Vietnamese restaurant chain, and packaging supplied for the 2022 MotoGP event in Mandalika, Indonesia); a limited direct-to-consumer online shop.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Alterpacks is a materials-innovation manufacturer, not a reuse-loop operator: its core product is a home-compostable, single-use packaging alternative rather than a returnable, refillable, or repeatedly-used system. This distinguishes it clearly from deposit-return, refill, or reusable-packaging models central to the REUSE Foundation's mission. Its relevance lies primarily as a materials-substitution and waste-diversion case study (converting food-manufacturing waste into packaging) rather than as a circularity/reuse exemplar. Verified information is thin beyond its 2022–2023 funding announcement — no independently audited impact metrics, and no confirmation the company remains commercially active beyond that period. Given the seed data's own flag ("not a reuse-loop operator"), this organisation should be classified as low-priority for a reuse-focused knowledge library, though it may merit a brief cross-reference entry under packaging-materials innovation.
