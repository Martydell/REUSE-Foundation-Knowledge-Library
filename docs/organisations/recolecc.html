Organisation Overview

Recolecc is a Peruvian collective management system for electrical and electronic equipment waste (RAEE), representing manufacturers in meeting Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) recycling obligations through nationwide collection campaigns and drop-off points.

Business Model

Recolecc is funded by member electronics manufacturers/brands who need to comply with Peru's Extended Producer Responsibility regulations for WEEE/RAEE. It operates collection points and periodic campaigns across Peruvian regions (Lima, Arequipa, Trujillo, Cusco, Chiclayo, Juliaca), then manages transport, decontamination, and valorization of collected e-waste through downstream partners, reporting recycling volumes back to member brands and regulators.

Products & Services

Nationwide e-waste (RAEE) collection campaigns, fixed and pop-up drop-off points, and EPR compliance/reporting management for electronics manufacturers.

Customers

Electronics and appliance manufacturers/brands (e.g., Epson) needing EPR compliance, and the general public who deposit e-waste at collection points.

Partnerships

Manufacturer members including Epson (joint collection campaigns); no independently verified connection to Petroperu was found.

Measurable Impact

Reported collection of over 433 tonnes of electrical/electronic waste in 2024, avoiding an estimated 835 tonnes of CO2 emissions (per company/press sources, not independently audited).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Recolecc runs a well-documented, regulation-driven reverse logistics and take-back system for e-waste in Peru, relevant to material recovery and EPR case studies, though its focus is recycling/decontamination rather than component reuse.
