Organisation Overview

Delterra is a global environmental non-profit that spun out of McKinsey & Company's philanthropic arm, McKinsey.org, in 2021, inheriting the "Rethinking Recycling" initiative launched in 2018. Led by CEO Shannon Bouton, a former McKinsey.org executive, Delterra designs and operates municipal recycling and waste-management systems in emerging-market cities. In Argentina, its two flagship programmes are "A Todo Reciclaje" in Barrio Mugica (an informal Buenos Aires community, since 2019) and GIRO in Olavarría, plus an innovation cohort spanning five additional cities (Bahía Blanca, Bariloche, Mendoza, Posadas, Santa Fe).

Business Model

Delterra operates as a technical-assistance and programme-delivery non-profit rather than a commercial company. It partners with municipal governments, corporate funders (e.g. Dow Chemical) and local worker cooperatives to design, finance and operationally run city-wide waste-collection, sorting, recycling and composting systems, particularly in informal or under-served urban communities. Its Argentina work has trained roughly 350 waste workers (80% women) in Barrio Mugica and is scaling a replicable municipal model to Olavarría (population 120,000+) and a further cohort of five cities. Funding comes from a mix of philanthropic/corporate grants (Dow is a named partner) and its McKinsey.org seed funding. Delterra also publishes research and knowledge-hub content (e.g. with The Circulate Initiative) on recycling behaviour to support the wider sector. Verified public sources describe Delterra's Argentina activity as centred on recycling and composting infrastructure and workforce development — not on reuse or refill packaging systems, despite corporate materials elsewhere referencing reuse/refill models (e.g. Algramo, Siklus) as examples of complementary approaches other companies pursue.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Material Recovery — municipal recycling and composting systems (Barrio Mugica, Olavarría)
  • Waste Reduction — city-wide waste collection and sorting infrastructure
  • Consumer Education — community engagement and behaviour-change work tied to recycling programmes
  • Other — workforce training/formalisation of informal waste-picker cooperatives

Products & Services

Not a product company; Delterra delivers programme design, funding facilitation, operational set-up and technical assistance for municipal recycling/composting systems, plus sector research and knowledge-hub publications.

Customers

Municipal governments (City of Olavarría; Buenos Aires informal-settlement authorities), local waste-worker cooperatives, and corporate/philanthropic funders; ultimate beneficiaries are residents of partner cities/communities.

Partnerships

  • Dow Chemical / Dow Partners (corporate circular-economy funding partner, Argentina) — corporate.dow.com
  • McKinsey.org (founding parent organisation and funder)
  • The Circulate Initiative (joint research publication on recycling behaviours)
  • Local labor/waste-picker cooperatives in Barrio Mugica

Measurable Impact

Approximately 350 waste workers trained in Barrio Mugica, Buenos Aires, of whom 80% are women (Delterra programme materials, delterra.org). GIRO programme targets expansion to serve 120,000+ residents of Olavarría. No independently audited tonnage-diverted or recycling-rate figures with specific dates were found in verified public sources for the Argentina programmes.

Technology

Not publicly available in detail; Delterra's public materials describe operational/logistics system design for collection and sorting rather than a named proprietary digital platform.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Delterra is a credible, well-funded non-profit with genuine circular-economy impact in Argentina, but verified sources show its core activity there — "A Todo Reciclaje" and GIRO — is recycling and composting infrastructure and waste-worker formalisation, not reuse or refill packaging systems as the seed data implied. Delterra's own thought-leadership references reuse/refill companies (Algramo, Siklus) as models worth emulating, but these are not Delterra's own operating programmes. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's specific reuse/refill mission is therefore indirect; its value lies more in circular-economy capacity-building and waste-system infrastructure than in reuse-specific innovation.
