Organisation Overview

Cero Market is described in Argentine press as the country's first "container-free" (package-free) supermarket, opened in 2020 in the Belgrano neighborhood of Buenos Aires at Avenida Monroe 2468. Co-founded by Ana Pierre and her partner Juan José, who researched similar bulk-retail models in Europe (citing a long-running example in Germany) before launching, the store lets customers bring their own containers — bags, jars, bottles, tuppers — to buy food, cleaning, and personal-care products by weight, with no minimum purchase. Reporting indicates the store stocked 400+ products at launch and later expanded to additional locations, including Recoleta.

Business Model

Cero Market operates as a bricks-and-mortar package-free grocery retailer. Customers bring reusable containers, which are weighed empty ("tare"), filled from bulk dispensers, and weighed again at checkout — the tare weight is subtracted so customers pay only for the product itself. The store carries a broad assortment spanning food staples (pasta, rice, flour, oils, vinegars, cookies, candy, dried fruit and nuts), personal care and cosmetics (including toothpaste), household cleaning products, and pet food, with press coverage noting an emphasis on agroecological products from local producers. Pricing is reported as comparable to conventional supermarkets rather than a premium model. The business has since added an online store and delivery/pickup service for the City of Buenos Aires and expanded from its original Belgrano site to additional branches (Recoleta identified via a branch-specific subdomain). No B2B, wholesale, or franchise model was confirmed in public sources.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available. No independently verified waste-diversion, sales-volume, or customer-count figures were found; a LinkedIn listing cites a headcount range (51–200 employees), but this is an unverified aggregator estimate rather than a company-disclosed figure.

Technology

Standard retail point-of-sale (tare-weight scale system for bulk sales) plus an e-commerce storefront for delivery/pickup orders. No evidence of a deposit-tracking app or digital reuse-tracking system was found.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Cero Market is a credible, well-covered example of Latin America's package-free/bulk retail movement and is frequently cited in Argentine media as the country's first supermarket of this kind. Its refill-based model directly supports plastic and packaging waste reduction at the consumer level. However, independent/audited impact data is absent, and the organisation's primary website was unreachable during verification (raising a question about current operating status that could not be resolved from other public sources), so ongoing scale and operational status should be treated with some caution pending re-verification.
