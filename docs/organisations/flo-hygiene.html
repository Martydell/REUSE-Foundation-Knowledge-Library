Organisation Overview

Flo Hygiene is a Nepalese household-cleaning products brand offering dishwashing liquid, glass cleaner, and floor cleaner, sold under the tagline "Best Dishwash Glass & Floor Cleaner Product Nepal." Search-engine indexing of the company's own site describes Flo Hygiene as "a quality product of TNT Hygiene," a Nepalese manufacturer reported to have been established in Nepali calendar year 2077 B.S. (approximately 2020–2021 CE) with a stated mission to promote Nepal-made products and support the local economy. The site itself could not be directly accessed by automated research tools (returning HTTP 403), so this profile relies on third-party listings (Daraz.com.np) and cached search snippets rather than direct verification of the full site content.

Business Model

Flo Hygiene sells household cleaning liquids — dishwash liquid, glass cleaner and floor cleaner — direct to consumers in Nepal, both via its own e-commerce website (flohygiene.com) and through third-party online marketplaces such as Daraz.com.np. Products are offered in multiple container sizes, including large-format 1-litre and 5-litre dishwash liquid bottles alongside smaller 250ml and 500ml sizes, and the company runs bundled "cleaning package" promotions (e.g., a combined dishwash/glass/floor cleaner bundle priced around NPR 325, and a "save up to NPR 180" bundle). The large-format bulk containers are consistent with a refill-oriented model in which households buy cleaning liquid in bulk and decant it into smaller reusable bottles for daily use, reducing the number of single-use packaging units purchased over time, though the company's own marketing language explicitly framing this as a "refill" or reuse initiative could not be directly verified due to inaccessibility of the primary website. Revenue is generated through direct-to-consumer retail sales in the Nepalese domestic market.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Flo Hygiene is a small Nepalese household-cleaning brand whose large-format container offerings (1-litre and 5-litre dishwash liquid) are consistent with a bulk-buy/refill-at-home consumption pattern common among value-focused cleaning brands in South Asia, which can reduce per-use packaging relative to buying multiple small single-use bottles. However, this research was unable to directly access the company's own website (persistent HTTP 403 errors), and no independent source confirms the company frames its business explicitly around reuse/refill as a core sustainability strategy, nor provides any measurable environmental impact data, named partnerships, or verified founding details beyond an approximate Nepali-calendar establishment date for its parent company. Given the low verifiability of claims and the absence of confirmed reuse-specific messaging or infrastructure (e.g., in-store refill stations, deposit-return systems), this organisation currently offers limited substantiated relevance to the REUSE Foundation's knowledge library, though it may warrant re-assessment if direct site access or company contact becomes possible.
