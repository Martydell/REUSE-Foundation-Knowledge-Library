Organisation Overview

Khaalisisi is a Nepali digital platform founded by Aayushi KC, who left a role at USAID Nepal to start the company, registering it in February 2017 and launching the live service in June 2017. It connects households and businesses in Kathmandu ("waste sellers") with independent local waste entrepreneurs ("Khaalisisi friends") who collect, sort, and recycle trash, with users able to schedule pickups and choose to sell or donate their waste. The company's stated mission is to help build Nepal into a top-20 global recycling nation by 2030.

Business Model

Khaalisisi operates a two-sided digital marketplace: households/businesses schedule waste pickup through the platform, and independent waste entrepreneurs (informal-sector waste pickers formalised as "Khaalisisi friends") collect, sort, and channel materials into recycling streams, earning income from the resale value of recyclables. Users can opt to sell their waste for cash or donate it. This model formalises and digitises what was previously an informal waste-picking economy in Kathmandu, creating structured income opportunities for waste entrepreneurs while improving recycling rates.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Khaalisisi is primarily a recycling/waste-management platform rather than a reuse system in the strict sense — it formalises and digitises waste collection and resale rather than closing reuse loops for products or packaging. It is a relevant example of digital-platform-enabled formalisation of informal waste economies in an emerging market, with credible founder story and multiple independent press profiles, but its core activity sits closer to recycling than reuse.
