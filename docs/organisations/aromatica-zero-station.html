Organisation Overview

AROMATICA Zero Station is a flagship retail concept store operated by Korean beauty brand AROMATICA, opened in June 2021 in the Sinsa-dong area of Gangnam-gu, Seoul, on the site of the brand's earlier "House of AROMATICA" refill store (2020). Described by AROMATICA and trade press as one of the first spaces of its kind in Korea, it combines a bulk product refill station, a recycling/upcycling education area, an aromatherapy experience zone, and a tea house serving homemade beverages, positioning the site as both a retail outlet and a sustainability showcase. It is not a separate company but a branded initiative of AROMATICA.

Business Model

Zero Station is a physical retail space, not an e-commerce or standalone business; it generates revenue through in-store sales of AROMATICA's product range (rosemary scalp shampoo, aloe vera gel, conditioner, cleanser, toner, body oil, and others — 18 products offered on a refill basis at launch) and through experiential/cultural elements (tea house, aromatherapy zone) that support brand engagement. Customers can buy a post-consumer-recycled plastic or glass container in-store or bring their own to refill with bulk product, reducing single-use packaging per purchase. The store's fit-out reused materials (glass, wood, brick, furniture) salvaged from the prior "House of AROMATICA" store, and some furnishings were upcycled from waste materials including disposable masks. All refill products are manufactured at AROMATICA's Osan Smart Factory.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Zero Station is a well-publicized, verifiable flagship example of in-store refill retail in the Korean beauty sector, with independent trade-press coverage (Packaging Insights, Inside Retail Asia, PR Newswire) corroborating its launch and format. As a single flagship store rather than a scaled network, and without store-level impact metrics, its direct measurable contribution is unclear, but it is a valuable qualitative case study of refill-retail design. Because it is not a separate legal entity from AROMATICA, its assessment should be read alongside the AROMATICA parent profile.
