Organisation Overview

Gracz is the consumer-facing brand of BPE (Biodegradable Packaging for Environment Co., Ltd.), a Thai manufacturer founded in 2005 that produces compostable tableware — plates, bowls, cups, clamshells, and cutlery — made from bagasse (sugarcane pulp fibre) and bamboo fibre blends. Originally sold under the "Biochanaoy" name, the brand was relaunched as "Gracz" in 2012. Manufacturing takes place in Chai Nat Province, Thailand, with products distributed regionally, including through the gracz.my e-commerce site serving Malaysia. The seed data's suggestion that the .my domain indicates a Malaysian headquarters is incorrect; Thailand is the verified home base.

Business Model

BPE/Gracz operates as a B2B and private-label manufacturer of compostable disposable foodware, supplying both branded "Gracz" retail lines and white-label products. Historically, roughly 70% of annual revenue has come from exports to developed markets, with BPE serving as a private-label supplier to major retailers such as Walmart and Lidl. The company has also pursued direct-to-consumer and regional distribution channels, including e-commerce storefronts such as gracz.my, which sells Gracz-branded compostable lunch boxes, bowls, and plates to Malaysian consumers and small businesses (cafes, caterers, retailers). Products are marketed as single-use replacements for plastic and polystyrene foodware, positioned as compostable/biodegradable (claimed to break down within 45 days) rather than reusable. As of research, the gracz.my site displayed an "under maintenance" placeholder with only a contact email listed, suggesting the Malaysian storefront may be inactive or being rebuilt.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Waste Reduction (single-use plastic/polystyrene substitution with compostable materials)
  • Material Recovery (products designed to biodegrade/compost, returning material to the soil rather than landfill as plastic)
  • Consumer Education (marketing materials on compostability and disposal)

Products & Services

Bagasse and bamboo-fibre compostable tableware: plates, bowls, cups, clamshell containers, cutlery, lunch boxes (marketed under "Gracz Classic" and "Gracz Simple" lines), plus non-food industrial packaging.

Customers

B2B retail and food-service private-label clients (reported past supply to Walmart and Lidl); regional consumers and small food businesses via e-commerce platforms (Lazada, Amazon Malaysia/US listings, gracz.my, EcoQuote.com.my).

Partnerships

Not publicly available (historical retail-supply relationships with Walmart and Lidl reported in secondary sources but not independently verified via primary company statements).

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available (no dated, verifiable sustainability metrics found; claims of "45-day" biodegradability and "100% decomposable" are marketing claims not sourced to a specific test standard or report).

Technology

Manufacturing uses bagasse (sugarcane pulp) and bamboo fibre moulding processes; the Chai Nat factory is reported to run on LPG and steam with production waste reportedly recycled on-site. No proprietary digital tracking or reuse-specific technology identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Gracz is a compostable single-use foodware manufacturer, not a reuse business — its core model displaces one disposable material (plastic) with another (bagasse), rather than eliminating single-use consumption through reuse, refill, or return systems. It has some relevance to the REUSE Foundation's broader circular-economy remit as a waste-reduction/material-substitution case study, but it does not represent a reuse, refill, or repair model. Given the mismatch with core "reuse" criteria, and the limited, largely unverifiable public information (inactive Malaysian storefront, no recent impact reporting), priority for in-depth profiling is low.
