Alner (formerly Koinpack)

Indonesian "packaging-as-a-service" venture, incubated by Enviu's Zero Waste Living Lab, providing FMCG brands with returnable/reusable packaging and app-based cashback for empty-container returns; rebranded from Koinpack to Alner in August 2023.

Organisation Overview

Alner (known as Koinpack from its March 2020 founding until its rebrand on 1 August 2023) is an Indonesian returnable-packaging venture founded by CEO Bintang Ekananda and co-founder Trian Effransa, incubated through Enviu's Zero Waste Living Lab programme. It supplies FMCG brands (household, personal care, and food products) with reusable containers, sells the filled containers through points of sale in Greater Jakarta, and operates app-based reverse logistics — including cleaning, refilling, and cashback incentives — to bring empty containers back into circulation. The seed data's listing of "Alner" alongside a Koinpack URL reflects a genuine same-entity rebrand, not a naming error, and should be updated to reflect Alner as the current operating name.

Business Model

Alner operates a packaging-as-a-service model for FMCG companies transitioning to reusable packaging: it designs and supplies durable containers (100ml–5L) which brand partners fill with their own products (e.g., detergent, shampoo, food items); Alner then distributes the filled containers to points of sale — including waste banks and neighbourhood "warung" stores — across four satellite cities in Greater Jakarta. Consumers pay a deposit-inclusive price and receive app-tracked cashback (reported at US$0.08–0.65 per container, depending on size) when they return empty packaging, which Alner collects, industrially cleans, and recirculates for reuse an estimated 10–20 times before recycling. Reported partner FMCG brands include Wipro Unza, Barco, and Yagi Natural; the company has also been reported working with products from P&G in some coverage. As of the 2023 rebrand announcement, the venture reported operating in 60+ retail locations plus online channels, with a 2024 target of 1,500+ partner locations, 2 million products sold, and 200 million single-use flexible sachets avoided.

Products & Services

Reusable/returnable packaging supply for FMCG brands (household cleaning, personal care, food products); reverse logistics (collection, industrial cleaning, refilling); consumer-facing app for tracking returns and cashback.

Customers

FMCG brand partners (reported: Wipro Unza, Barco, Yagi Natural); consumers in Greater Jakarta purchasing everyday household/personal-care products via waste banks, "warung" stores, and other points of sale.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Alner (formerly Koinpack) is a well-documented, purpose-built reusable-packaging venture directly matching the REUSE Foundation's core scope — packaging-as-a-service, deposit-based returns, reverse logistics, and app-based tracking, aimed squarely at Indonesia's single-use sachet problem. It has verifiable founder identity, an NGO/incubator backer (Enviu) with public reporting, and named FMCG brand partners. Impact figures are self-reported and partly aspirational (2024 targets), which should be clearly distinguished from achieved results in any external-facing summary. The venture's direct relevance to reuse infrastructure — rather than materials substitution or recycling — makes it a strong candidate for inclusion.

Priority Rating: ★★★★★ Essential (purpose-built reusable-packaging-as-a-service model, well-documented rebrand history, named partners; impact figures self-reported/partly target-based rather than audited)
