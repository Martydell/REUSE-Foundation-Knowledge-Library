Organisation Overview

Bean Around is an Irish skincare company founded in 2020 by sisters Sadhbh and Aisling Wood while they were college students in Dún Laoghaire. The company collects used coffee grounds from local cafés and corporate canteens and repurposes them into handcrafted exfoliating soap bars, sold in plastic-free, recyclable packaging. The business has grown from a campus project into a nationally stocked brand, including selection for Lidl Ireland's "Kickstart" small-business programme, placing its products in 222 Lidl stores across Ireland (Republic and Northern Ireland) as of 2024.

Business Model

Bean Around operates a dual consumer/B2B model. On the consumer side, it sells handcrafted exfoliating coffee soap bars — used to remove fake tan and soothe dry skin — direct-to-consumer online and through retail partners including Lidl Ireland and independent stockists. On the B2B/corporate side, the company runs a "coffee ground upcycling" gifting service: it collects spent coffee grounds directly from a business's own canteen or office coffee machines and turns them into soap bars branded with that company's logo, marketed to employers as both a sustainability initiative and a branded gifting product for staff or clients. Packaging is plastic-free and recyclable, with any plastic components made partly from post-consumer recycled material. According to press coverage, the company grew revenue by 300% in one year on the strength of this corporate program alongside retail expansion.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Material Recovery — spent coffee grounds collected from cafés and corporate canteens are converted into new skincare products rather than discarded as waste
  • Waste Reduction — corporate coffee-waste collection programme diverts grounds from landfill/compost streams into product manufacturing
  • Consumer Education — marketing around coffee-waste upcycling used to demonstrate corporate sustainability commitments

Not identified from public sources: refill, deposit-return, or take-back systems for the finished product's own packaging.

Products & Services

Handcrafted exfoliating coffee soap bars (including scented/variant options such as peppermint) sold at retail and online; a B2B corporate programme collecting a business's own spent coffee grounds and converting them into custom-branded soap bars for staff or client gifting.

Customers

Individual retail consumers (via Lidl Ireland, independent stockists, and online); corporate clients purchasing branded coffee-waste-to-soap gifting programmes.

Partnerships

Lidl Ireland (Food Academy / Kickstart small-business programme, placing products in 222 stores as of 2024); SuperValu (Food Academy programme); local cafés supplying spent coffee grounds (specific café names not publicly available).

Measurable Impact

Reported by Business Post (year: reporting describes growth "in a year," article undated in search results but describes recent performance): 300% revenue growth attributed largely to the corporate coffee-waste-to-gifting programme. No independently verified figures for total coffee-ground volume diverted or units sold were found in public sources.

Technology

Standard e-commerce platform for online retail sales; no dedicated reuse-tracking or logistics technology identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Bean Around is a small but well-documented Irish company with a clear, verifiable circular-economy activity: converting a genuine waste stream (spent coffee grounds) into new consumer and corporate-gifting products. Its inclusion in Lidl Ireland's Kickstart programme and reported strong revenue growth indicate real commercial traction for a young (2020-founded) company. Its scale remains modest and it operates in a single country, but the model — a waste-stream upcycling business with both B2C and B2B/corporate revenue lines — is a clean, easily explainable example of material recovery/waste reduction relevant to the REUSE Foundation's library.
