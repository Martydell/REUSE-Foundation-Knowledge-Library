Organisation Overview

ecostore was founded in 1993 by Malcolm and Melanie Rands on a permaculture eco-village on New Zealand's Northland coast, initially as a mail-order natural cleaning and body-care business. It moved to Auckland in 1997 and opened its first retail refill point the same year. The brand expanded into Australia in 2004 and across Asian markets (China, Japan, Singapore, South Korea, Malaysia, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Philippines) from around 2010–2016. Ownership transferred from the Rands family to the Kraus family between 2013 and 2016; ecostore is now led by Managing Director Pablo Kraus and became a Certified B Corporation in 2021.

Business Model

ecostore is a manufacturer and retailer of plant- and mineral-based cleaning, laundry, personal-care and baby-care products, sold through its own retail stores, supermarkets and other third-party retail partners across New Zealand, Australia, and several Asian markets. Its central sustainability differentiator is a long-running refill programme: starting with a single Auckland store in 1997, the company has built out a network reported at over 200 refill stations in New Zealand and additional stations in Australian supermarkets (from 2019–2020) and Asian markets, allowing customers to refill reusable bottles with concentrate rather than buying new plastic packaging each time. Internationally, ecostore has grown via licensing/franchise-style regional operating companies rather than direct ownership everywhere — for example, ecostore JAPAN Co., Ltd. operates as a separately incorporated licensee in Japan (see related profile). Revenue comes from product sales across owned and third-party retail channels, supplemented by e-commerce.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (in-store refill stations for cleaning/body-care concentrates, 200+ locations in NZ plus additional Australian and Asian sites)
  • Consumer Education
  • Waste Reduction

Products & Services

Plant- and mineral-based laundry, dish, surface-cleaning, body-care, hair-care and baby-care products; reusable bottles and packaging designed for repeat refilling; in-store and supermarket refill stations.

Customers

Retail consumers in New Zealand, Australia and multiple Asian markets, purchasing through ecostore's own stores, supermarket partners, and e-commerce.

Partnerships

Retail/refill placements in New Zealand supermarkets and green/independent stores; refill-station rollouts in Australian supermarkets (from 2019–2020); regional licensee operating companies in Asian markets including ecostore JAPAN Co., Ltd. (est. 2017). B Corp certification (B Lab), achieved 2021.

Measurable Impact

Company/press reporting (Supermarket News, NZ) describes ecostore reaching a "milestone" for refill-station numbers in New Zealand, with the network described elsewhere as exceeding 200 stations, but a precise, dated, primary count of total litres refilled or packaging avoided was not identified in this research pass and should be sourced from ecostore's own sustainability reporting for precise figures.

Technology

In-store refill-dispensing infrastructure (bulk concentrate dispensers) rather than digital/app-based tracking; no proprietary digital reuse-tracking platform was identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

ecostore is one of the longest-running and most geographically extensive refill-station operators in the consumer packaged-goods sector, having run refill infrastructure since 1997 — well before "refill" became a mainstream retail trend — and having scaled it across New Zealand, Australia and parts of Asia via both owned stores and regional licensees. Its B Corp certification and multi-decade operating history make it a strong, well-documented case study for the Foundation, though quantified, dated impact metrics (litres/packaging avoided) were not found in this pass and should be requested directly from the company for a fuller profile.
