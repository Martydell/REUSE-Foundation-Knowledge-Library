French social-economy initiative operating a deposit-return network that collects, washes and redistributes glass bottles and jars for reuse by regional food and drink producers.

Organisation Overview

Ma Bouteille s'appelle Reviens is a French deposit-return (consigne) network for glass bottles and jars, operated by the company Locaverre, created in November 2017 with its bottle-washing facility inaugurated in Chabeuil (Drôme) in July 2019. Consumers return used glass containers to participating shops and producers; Locaverre collects, washes and redistributes the standardized bottles for reuse by regional wine, beer, fruit juice and other food producers. The initiative operates mainly around Valence, in the Drôme and Ardèche departments, partnering with around 80 producers using varied bottle shapes, and is structured as a Social and Solidarity Economy (ESS) enterprise that also provides professional reintegration employment.

Business Model

The organisation runs a regional deposit-return system for glass packaging: consumers pay a small deposit on bottles/jars of wine, beer, fruit juice, compote, yogurt, honey and jam from participating regional producers, and return them via independent retailers and larger grocery distribution points after use. Locaverre, the operating company, collects the returned containers, washes them at its Chabeuil facility, and supplies clean, reusable bottles back to producers for refilling — displacing single-use glass recycling with direct reuse. Revenue comes from service fees charged to participating producers/retailers for the collection-washing-redistribution service, positioned as both an environmental initiative (glass consignment) and a Social and Solidarity Economy (ESS) enterprise, since Locaverre's washing operations provide professional reintegration employment opportunities. The venture cites ADEME (French environment agency) research indicating deposit/reuse systems cut energy use, water use and greenhouse gas emissions substantially versus single-use recycling, underpinning its value proposition to producers.

Products & Services

Collection, washing and redistribution of standardized reusable glass bottles and jars for regional beverage and food producers; a consumer-facing deposit-return (consigne) network via participating shops and points of sale; an online map ("cartographie") of drop-off/return points.

Customers

Regional food and beverage producers (wine, beer, fruit juice, compote, yogurt, honey, jam) in the Drôme-Ardèche region of France (around 80 producers), independent retailers, and larger grocery distribution networks that host return points; end consumers who purchase products in reusable, deposit-bearing containers.

Partnerships

Approximately 80 regional producers; independent retailers and grocery distribution networks hosting collection points; SYPP (Syndicat des Producteurs) noted as a partner organisation. Locaverre operates the washing facility as the ESS partner delivering professional reintegration employment.

Measurable Impact

Per an ADEME (French environment and energy agency) 2018 study cited by the organisation, the glass deposit/reuse system reduces energy consumption, water consumption, and greenhouse gas emissions substantially compared with conventional single-use glass recycling (cited by the organisation as roughly 75-79% less energy, around half less water, and around three-quarters less GHG emissions versus recycling, per differing sources reporting the same ADEME study). Specific bottle volumes or tonnage handled by Ma Bouteille s'appelle Reviens itself are not publicly available.

Technology

Not publicly available; no specific digital tracking (RFID/QR) system identified. An online map ("cartographie") lists physical collection/return points for consumers.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ma Bouteille s'appelle Reviens is a well-established, operating example of a regional glass deposit-return network in France, combining environmental reuse objectives with Social and Solidarity Economy (ESS) employment goals, and citing credible third-party (ADEME) research to support its environmental case. Its scale (around 80 producers in one French region) is modest relative to national deposit-return schemes, and it does not publish its own quantified bottle-reuse volumes, but it is a valuable, well-documented regional model of reuse-network infrastructure directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's mission.
