Organisation Overview

La Sfuseria (di Gusto Vivo) is a bulk-goods shop in Faenza, Italy, offering over 300 organic and package-free food items — cereals, pasta, legumes, nuts, seeds, flours and baked goods — alongside fresh/fermented foods, eco-friendly household cleaners, solid cosmetics and zero-waste accessories. Note: "La Sfuseria" is a common generic name used by several unrelated bulk shops across Italy (Milan, Modena, Arco di Trento); this profile covers only the business operating at lasfuseria.it in Faenza.

Business Model

La Sfuseria operates as a neighbourhood bulk retail store where nearly all dry goods, cleaning products and cosmetics are sold loose, priced by weight rather than pre-packaged units. Customers bring their own reusable containers or use the store's paper bags, eliminating single-use packaging at the point of sale. Revenue comes from in-store retail sales across food, home-care and personal-care categories, with a positioning around organic ("biologico"), raw, plastic-free and km-0 (locally sourced) products. The model relies on customer behaviour change (bringing containers) rather than a formal deposit or container-return infrastructure, distinguishing it from packaging-as-a-service operators. As an independent single-store operation, its business model is simple direct retail rather than franchise or multi-site expansion, though it is part of a broader Italian "negozio alla spina" (tap/bulk-dispenser shop) movement that has grown significantly since the early 2020s.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

La Sfuseria is a small, locally relevant example of the "negozio alla spina" bulk/refill retail model common across Italy, directly reducing packaging waste through container reuse and loose-goods sale. It has no detectable regional or national scale, no published impact metrics, and no visible partnerships or franchise ambitions, limiting its significance beyond the local community. It is nonetheless a legitimate, operating case study of grassroots refill retail.
