Organisation Overview

Minimakers is described as Singapore's largest manufacturer of beeswax wraps — a reusable, compostable alternative to plastic cling film and food storage bags. Beyond its product line, the company runs a distinctive social mission: since 2021, it has partnered with the Autism Resource Center to redesign its manufacturing process specifically to employ individuals on the autism spectrum, positioning itself as building "a collective of makers, designers that supports differently abled artisans through shared employment."

Business Model

Minimakers manufactures and sells beeswax food wraps (in multiple sizes, including a "Foldie" variant), sponge cloths, fire starters, and DIY beeswax-wrap kits, sold to sustainable retailers, government agencies, and regional/global corporations, as well as via online marketplaces (Shopee) and in international markets including Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. Its distinguishing feature is an inclusive-employment manufacturing model built around its Autism Resource Center partnership, integrating social-enterprise practice directly into production rather than as a separate CSR initiative.
