Organisation Overview

Ecoworks is a Singaporean company operating a growing network of automated household-product refill kiosks ("Ecofill Refill Stations") placed in everyday community locations such as HDB (public housing) void decks, community centres and university campuses. Founder Sean Lam, a repeat entrepreneur, started the company after learning that Singapore is one of the world's largest per-capita producers of single-use plastic waste, seeking a venture with deeper long-term environmental impact. The company rolled out its first refill station in late January 2024 and had expanded to 16 stations at time of research, offering laundry detergent and dishwashing liquid refills, including a partnership with household-cleaning brand Bio-Home.

Business Model

Ecoworks installs automated, self-service refill kiosks in high-footfall but everyday community locations across Singapore — void decks, community centres, university campuses — where customers bring their own reusable containers and refill laundry detergent or dishwashing liquid at a lower price than buying new bottled product (with entry pricing noted around S$3). This is a fixed-infrastructure network model rather than a mobile or pop-up operation: the seed description of "mobile/pop-up" stations does not match verified sourcing, which consistently describes permanently sited automated kiosks. The company partners with household-product brand Bio-Home to supply refill product at select stations, and is developing a mobile app (described as "launching real soon") to help users locate nearby stations. Ecoworks has also received institutional recognition, including as a winner of the UNDP/ASEAN Blue Innovation Challenge, and engages with Singapore town councils, NUS Enterprise and SMU as community/innovation partners.

Products & Services

Automated Ecofill refill kiosks dispensing laundry detergent and dishwashing liquid (including Bio-Home branded product); planned mobile app for station-finding; reusable-container refill model for household cleaning products.

Customers

Residential communities across Singapore (HDB estates), community-centre visitors, and university-campus populations, accessing refill stations directly in person.

Partnerships

Bio-Home (branded product supply at select refill stations); UNDP and ASEAN (Blue Innovation Challenge winner); Singapore town councils and community organisations; NUS Enterprise and SMU (innovation/enterprise support).

Measurable Impact

Company materials (as reported mid-2026) state Ecoworks is on track to eliminate 100,000 single-use bottles by the end of 2025, and had avoided 558 kilograms of single-use plastic to date at time of reporting. These are company-stated figures; the precise reporting date and methodology were not independently confirmed against a formal, audited sustainability disclosure.

Technology

Automated self-service refill dispensing kiosks ("Ecofill machines"); mobile app for station location in development at time of research; no confirmed NFC/digital per-container tracking technology (unlike, e.g., Ecourage).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ecoworks is a fast-growing (16 stations within roughly two years of launch), well-covered Singaporean refill-station network with credible institutional recognition (UNDP/ASEAN Blue Innovation Challenge) and a clear public-health/community placement strategy (void decks, community centres, campuses) that lowers access barriers compared to standalone retail refill shops. The seed data's "mobile/pop-up" framing is not supported by verified sourcing — the network consists of fixed, permanently sited automated kiosks — which is an important correction for accurate categorisation. Overall relevance to the Foundation is solid given the network's growth trajectory and community-embedded distribution model.
