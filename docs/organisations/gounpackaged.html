Organisation Overview

GoUnpackaged is a UK-based reuse-and-refill consultancy tracing its roots to Unpackaged, a zero-waste shop founded by Catherine Conway in 2006 — widely credited as the world's first modern zero-waste retail concept, originally a market stall before becoming a bricks-and-mortar shop in London stocking around 700 products, roughly 80% of them refillable. Following a 2015 supermarket trial with Planet Organic, the business transitioned from retail into consultancy, rebranding over time (via "Just Unpackaged") to GoUnpackaged. It now advises brands, retailers and local authorities on transitioning to reuse and refill, and convenes the multi-retailer "Refill Coalition" initiative.

Business Model

GoUnpackaged operates as a B2B reuse consultancy rather than a retailer, offering: product, service and business-model innovation for reuse; supply-chain analysis and systems modelling; commercial business-case development for clients considering refill/reuse; multi-party collaborative project delivery; consumer-insights and behaviour-change strategy; and policy development/thought leadership (the site lists a dedicated "Policy and Research" practice area). Its most visible current initiative is the Refill Coalition, convened in 2020, which brought together Aldi UK, Ocado Retail and supply-chain/logistics partner CHEP to develop a standardised, reusable bulk "vessel" for dry goods (e.g. cereals, pasta) and household products (e.g. cleaning and personal-care items) that can move through the supply chain and be refilled by any supplier — an attempt to create an industry-wide reusable-packaging standard rather than a single-retailer solution. The Coalition's work was funded in part by Innovate UK; a real-world in-store trial with Aldi launched in October 2023, and an online consumer version with Ocado launched on 8 August 2024. GoUnpackaged is led by a small named team: Catherine Conway (Director, Policy Lead), Helen Clements (Director, Innovation & Behaviour Change Lead), Rob Spencer (Director, Analytics & Supply Chain Lead), Helen Robshaw (Lead Consultant), and Megan Adams (Solutions Lead).

Products & Services

Reuse/refill business-model and supply-chain consultancy for brands, retailers and local authorities; systems modelling for reusable packaging logistics; and, via the Refill Coalition, a standardised reusable bulk "vessel" design for dry goods and household liquids intended to work across suppliers and retailers.

Measurable Impact

  • GoUnpackaged states its in-store Refill Coalition vessel replaces the equivalent of 24 single-use packs (based on a 500g rice pack comparison), and its online/home-delivery consumer vessel replaces the equivalent of 5 single-use packs — self-reported, relative (not cumulative) figures, undated to a specific year (gounpackaged.com/refill-coalition)
  • Awards received: Environmental Packaging Awards 2024 (Gold), National Recycling Awards 2024, and a Silver at the 2025 Sustainable Foods Awards (gounpackaged.com) — award recognition, not independently audited impact data

Seed data's claim of a "30% reuse target" could not be verified from any public source found and is not publicly available.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

GoUnpackaged is one of the UK's most established reuse/refill consultancies, with a credible lineage back to the country's first modern zero-waste retail concept and current, verifiable delivery partnerships with two major grocers (Aldi UK, Ocado) and a logistics provider (CHEP) through the publicly documented Refill Coalition. Its focus on supply-chain-level, cross-retailer standardisation (a shared reusable "vessel" rather than a single-brand solution) is directly relevant to REUSE Foundation's interest in scalable reuse infrastructure. However, several seed-data claims (major-retailer partner list beyond Aldi/Ocado, a "30% reuse target," and a "leading govt advisory voice" characterisation) could not be corroborated in this pass and have been excluded or flagged; the organisation's own site does list a policy/research practice area, lending some support to an advisory role, but not the specific superlative claimed.

Priority Rating: ★★★★ High (established, verifiably active reuse-systems consultancy with confirmed major-retailer delivery partnerships; downgraded from Essential pending stronger verification of scale/impact claims)

Verification Notes

The exact registered legal entity behind the "GoUnpackaged" trading name was not conclusively confirmed in this research pass. A Companies House entity, "Unpackaged Systems Ltd" (company no. 13492629, incorporated 5 July 2021, registered in Lancashire), surfaced in search results as a possible match, but its registered address is inconsistent with GoUnpackaged's London-based public profile, and no direct corroborating link (e.g. a filing, officer match, or site reference) was found connecting it to Catherine Conway or gounpackaged.com. It is presented here only as an unconfirmed lead, not as the verified entity — a direct Companies House officer search for "Catherine Conway" is recommended for a future research pass. Given this, Name confidence is rated Medium rather than High. The seed data's founding year (2006) is well corroborated for the origin of the "Unpackaged" retail concept, but the year GoUnpackaged itself (the consultancy/Refill-Coalition entity) was established is not separately documented in sources found — Founding Year confidence rated Medium accordingly.
