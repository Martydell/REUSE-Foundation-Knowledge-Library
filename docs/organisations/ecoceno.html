Organisation Overview

Ecoceno is a Portuguese reusable-packaging startup, developed in 2022 and launched commercially in early 2023, created out of Embal, an existing aluminium packaging manufacturer. Based in Vila Nova de Gaia (legal entity Ecoceno Unipessoal Lda, NIF 517334542), it provides a deposit-return system of reusable food and drink containers for restaurants, takeaway venues, stadiums, and events, allowing consumers to return containers to collection points instead of using single-use packaging. The company has received financial support from Portugal's Sociedade Ponto Verde (SPV) and the state Fundo Ambiental (Environmental Fund).

Business Model

Ecoceno operates two parallel deposit models tailored to different venue types. For high-turnover, anonymous-customer settings such as stadiums and bars, users pay a per-item deposit (€8 for a standard reusable container, €12 for pizza boxes, €2 for bags) that is refunded on return. For regular-customer settings such as restaurants and supermarkets, a simpler registration model charges a deposit via linked mobile number/bank card only on first use, streamlining repeat transactions. Containers — made from plastic, metal, or glass and rated for 100+ reuse cycles — must be returned within 14 days or the deposit converts into a purchase charge. Partner establishments handle hygiene sanitisation under food-safety regulation, and end-of-life containers are returned to manufacturers for recycling, closing the material loop. Mobile apps (iOS/Android) support code generation and locating return points.

Customers

Restaurants, takeaway/food-service venues, stadiums, bars, supermarkets, and event organisers in Portugal seeking to reduce single-use packaging and comply with national waste/packaging-fee regulations.

Partnerships

Sociedade Ponto Verde (SPV, Portugal's packaging recovery/compliance body) and Fundo Ambiental (state Environmental Fund) as financial supporters; VISA and SIBS (payments infrastructure); AHRESP (Portuguese hospitality/restaurant association); Bloomidea (technology/app development partner). Spun out of aluminium packaging manufacturer Embal.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ecoceno is a well-documented, credibly backed reusable-packaging system directly addressing Portugal's single-use food packaging and associated levy, with institutional support from the national packaging-compliance body (SPV) and government environmental fund — a strong signal of legitimacy and regulatory alignment. Its dual deposit model (high-turnover vs. regular-customer) is a thoughtful design choice addressing a common reuse-system adoption barrier. The main gap is a lack of published quantitative impact data (container volumes, diversion rates), which limits assessment of scale to date. This is a strong candidate for deeper case-study inclusion given clear reuse infrastructure and traceable partnerships.
