Organisation Overview

Fundación Lenga is a Chilean non-profit that has worked since 2019 to promote "Zero Waste" practices in the tourism sector across southern Chile, beginning in the Magallanes region (Patagonia) and later expanding north into the Araucanía region. Its name is inspired by the native lenga tree (Nothofagus pumilio) of Chile's austral forests, whose saplings grow in clusters, supporting one another — a metaphor for the foundation's collaborative approach with tourism businesses, residents and visitors. The foundation runs its flagship "Tourism towards Zero Waste Chile" project in partnership with GAIA Latin America and the Caribbean (GAIA LAC) and the Break Free From Plastic movement.

Business Model

As a non-profit, Fundación Lenga is funded through grants and programme partnerships rather than commercial revenue — its "Tourism towards Zero Waste Chile" project has been financed and supported for at least two consecutive years by GAIA LAC and Break Free From Plastic. Its core activity model is to recruit and publicly recognise tourism-sector businesses (hotels, tour operators, restaurants) in destination towns — Pucón, Puerto Natales, Punta Arenas and Puerto Williams — that commit to reducing waste, and to make these businesses discoverable to travellers by listing them on the crowdsourced Go Zero Waste app map. It complements this with on-the-ground public engagement: "Toxic Tours" (guided walks highlighting plastic pollution, run with Break Free From Plastic), informational talks, and volunteer clean-ups of natural spaces, plus printed flyers/posters distributed in partner towns to raise visitor awareness.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Consumer Education (informational talks, "Toxic Tours," public awareness campaigns aimed at tourists and residents)
  • Waste Reduction (recruiting and certifying tourism businesses committed to reducing waste)
  • Digital Tracking (adding vetted zero-waste tourism ventures to the collaborative Go Zero Waste app map)
  • Other (community clean-up volunteering; zero-waste tourism policy/kit development; member of the UN's Global Tourism Plastics Initiative)

Products & Services

Zero-waste tourism certification/recognition for participating businesses; "Toxic Tours" guided awareness walks; educational talks and workshops; a "Zero Waste Tourism Kit" (Kit de Turismo hacia Basura Cero) referenced by GAIA; promotional flyers/maps for zero-waste ventures in partner towns.

Customers

Tourism-sector businesses (hotels, tour operators, restaurants, guesthouses) in Pucón, Puerto Natales, Punta Arenas and Puerto Williams; tourists and local residents as end audiences for education/advocacy.

Partnerships

  • GAIA (Global Alliance for Incinerator Alternatives) Latin America and the Caribbean (GAIA LAC) — co-funder of "Tourism towards Zero Waste Chile"
  • Break Free From Plastic — co-funder and partner on "Toxic Tours" and awareness campaigns
  • Go Zero Waste app (gozerowaste.app/gozerowaste.es) — collaborative mapping partner listing Fundación Lenga's vetted zero-waste tourism ventures
  • UN Global Tourism Plastics Initiative — Fundación Lenga is a listed member
