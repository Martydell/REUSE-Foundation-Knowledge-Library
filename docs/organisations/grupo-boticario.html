Organisation Overview

Grupo Boticário, founded in 1977 in Curitiba, Brazil, is one of Latin America's largest beauty and cosmetics conglomerates, operating a multi-brand portfolio (O Boticário, Eudora, Quem Disse Berenice?, O.U.i, The Beauty Box, Vult) through a large franchise retail network across Brazil and internationally. Its flagship brand, O Boticário, has for decades sold "Refil" (refill) versions of its cologne/deodorant ("desodorante colônia") lines — customers keep a decorative bottle and purchase cheaper refill units to replenish it — and has been progressively shifting refill packaging to recycled and lighter aluminium.

Business Model

Grupo Boticário operates a franchise-heavy retail model across thousands of physical stores plus e-commerce, selling personal care, fragrance and cosmetics products under several house brands. Within this, the refill ("Refil") line is a long-standing commercial mechanism: customers buy an initial decorative bottle once, then purchase lower-cost, lighter refill units repeatedly, which the company frames as both a cost and sustainability benefit. Recent packaging R&D has focused on replacing plastic components in refill/aerosol packaging with recycled aluminium — including a 2023-launched aerosol packaging combining post-consumer recycled aluminium with BPA-free varnish, developed with global metal-packaging firm Trivium Packaging and produced at Trivium's Argentina facility. Separately, group brand O.U.i sells eau de parfum refills in recyclable aluminium bottles, cutting packaging weight substantially versus the original bottle. These initiatives sit within the group's broader "Beleza Transparente" and ESG sustainability programs, which also cover reverse logistics for packaging collection.

Customers

Mass-market and premium beauty consumers in Brazil and other Latin American/international markets, purchasing through Grupo Boticário's franchise store network and e-commerce.

Partnerships

  • Trivium Packaging (multinational metal packaging manufacturer) — co-developed recycled-aluminium aerosol packaging, produced at Trivium's Argentina plant

Measurable Impact

  • The 2023-launched recycled-aluminium aerosol packaging line had, as of the source's publication, avoided more than 240 metric tons of CO2 emissions and prevented over 100 metric tons of aluminium waste generation since launch, with a 6% reduction in product carbon footprint attributed to the lighter design (source: Grupo Boticário press release)
  • Specific unit volumes or plastic-reduction percentages for the "Refil" cologne/deodorant line specifically were not found in publicly available sources

Technology

Recycled post-consumer aluminium (PCR) combined with BPA-free varnish for aerosol/refill packaging — described by the company as a first-of-its-kind combination in Brazil.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Grupo Boticário's refill line is a genuine, long-running, mass-market refill system operating at very large retail scale in Brazil, which gives it real potential reuse impact despite being only one part of a large conventional cosmetics conglomerate. The shift toward recycled aluminium in refill/aerosol packaging is a verified, dated initiative with a named industrial partner (Trivium) and quantified emissions/waste figures, though these figures describe a packaging-material improvement rather than the refill mechanism itself. Because the parent group is overwhelmingly a conventional multi-brand retailer, only the refill-specific activity is directly relevant to reuse assessment. Priority rating: ★★★ Medium.
