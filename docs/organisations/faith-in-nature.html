Organisation Overview

Faith In Nature is a long-established UK natural personal-care brand, tracing its origin to 1974, making shampoo, conditioner, body wash and household products from naturally-derived, cruelty-free ingredients. Its registered company (Faith In Nature Limited, Companies House number SC068493) was incorporated in 1979. The brand is best known in the reuse/circular-economy space for two things: its "refill reuse repeat" aluminium bottle refill system, and a landmark 2022 governance move in which it amended its constitution to give "Nature" a formal voice and vote on its board of directors — a widely-reported first for a UK company.

Business Model

Faith In Nature sells natural personal-care and household products through UK and international retail (supermarkets, health stores, pharmacies) and online. Its refill model centres on infinitely-recyclable aluminium bottles that customers can refill (via in-store refill stations or return/refill bottle schemes) rather than repeatedly buying new plastic containers, positioned under the "refill reuse repeat" branding. Since 2022, following legal advice from the Lawyers for Nature initiative, the company rewrote its constitution to add "Nature" as a director with a vote on the board (represented in practice by appointed guardians), explicitly linking business decisions — including packaging and sourcing choices — to environmental outcomes. This governance change is distinct from, but closely tied to, its product-level refill/reuse initiatives.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Faith In Nature is a strong candidate for the knowledge library on two fronts: a genuine, long-running refill/reuse packaging system (aluminium bottles under "refill reuse repeat"), and its notable 2022 "rights of nature" governance innovation, which — while not itself a reuse activity — reinforces the credibility of its environmental commitments. The brand's long history (since 1974) and UK retail scale make it a well-documented, medium-to-high relevance example, though quantified refill-volume impact data is currently missing from public sources.
