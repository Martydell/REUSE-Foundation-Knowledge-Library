Organisation Overview

Faerly is an Irish online retailer of sustainable-living products, founded by married couple James Byrne and Eoin Houlihan and launched in October 2020 from their home in Kilcullen, Co. Kildare. The legal entity trading as Faerly, Simply Good Fair Limited, was incorporated on 27 May 2020. The business curates several hundred products — bamboo and metal personal-care items, natural cleaning and body-care concentrates, and refillable containers — sourced from small Irish makers and eco-conscious international brands, positioning itself as a one-stop shop to "take the work out of shopping sustainably" for Irish consumers.

Business Model

Faerly operates a business-to-consumer (B2C) e-commerce model, selling curated third-party sustainable-living products via faerly.ie and shipping to customers across Ireland (and reportedly further afield) in plastic-free, recyclable/compostable packaging. Rather than manufacturing its own goods, it acts as a specialist retailer/marketplace, selecting ranges from small Irish makers (e.g. West Cork skincare producer Organicules) and established eco-brands (e.g. Wild deodorant, Aarke carbonators, "neat" cleaning concentrates). A distinct component of the model is its CO2 Gas Cylinder Exchange Programme, under which customers return empty CO2 cylinders (compatible with Aarke, SodaStream, Philips and CO2 You carbonators) in exchange for a discount on a refilled cylinder — a take-back/refill loop layered onto the retail business. The company states it is a member of 1% for the Planet, donating 1% of sales to environmental causes, and plants a tree for each order. It was run initially as a side venture by both founders before James Byrne moved to it full-time in June 2021; Eoin Houlihan continues to work as a teacher/guidance counsellor alongside the business.

  • Consumer Education (sustainability-focused content and curated "eco swap" guidance)
  • Waste Reduction (plastic-free/compostable order packaging)

Not identified from public sources: Reusable Packaging (closed-loop logistics beyond CO2 cylinders), Packaging-as-a-Service, Repair, Rental, Sharing, Reverse Logistics (beyond CO2 cylinders), Industrial Reuse, Digital Tracking, Material Recovery.

Products & Services

Curated online marketplace spanning: home and kitchen (reusable food storage, cleaning concentrates and refill bottles), skincare and bathroom (natural soaps, solid shampoo/conditioner bars, dental and body care), on-the-go items (reusable water bottles, coffee cups, food containers), gifting (curated gift boxes), and lifestyle/wellness goods. Notable ranges include Aarke water carbonators with CO2 cylinder exchange, Wild refillable deodorant, and "neat" concentrated cleaning refills.

Customers

Individual Irish consumers (B2C) purchasing online; the founders describe their target customer as "eco-conscious Irish consumers" seeking to reduce plastic/chemical use and to shift spend toward Irish-based retailers. No B2B, wholesale or institutional customer base is publicly documented.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Faerly is primarily a curated sustainable-living e-commerce retailer rather than a dedicated reuse/refill infrastructure operator: most of its catalogue is third-party eco-alternative products sold conventionally online. Its genuine circular-economy contribution is narrower than the "Refillable Products" seed label suggests — it centres on stocking refillable product lines (concentrated cleaning refills, refillable deodorant, refillable hand-wash) made by other brands, plus its own CO2 gas-cylinder take-back/exchange programme, which is the clearest deposit-return-style loop it operates directly. It is a small, founder-run Irish business (incorporated May 2020) with credible third-party recognition (Retail Excellence Ireland's Small Online Retailer of the Year 2022; Irish Ecommerce Awards recognition) but no published quantitative circularity metrics. It is a useful example of a consumer-facing refill/eco-retail aggregator in the Irish market rather than a systems-level reuse innovator.

Priority Rating: ★★★ Medium (genuine refill/take-back elements present via curated product lines and CO2 cylinder exchange, but core model is third-party retail curation rather than proprietary reuse infrastructure, and no verifiable impact data exists)
