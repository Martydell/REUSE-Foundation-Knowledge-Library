Organisation Overview

Wura is a community item-sharing app founded by Nigerian entrepreneur Damilola Kadiri, letting users post household items, food, clothing, tools and electronics they no longer need for others nearby to request for free. The app computes an estimated environmental impact/carbon-footprint saving for each exchange and is available on Android and iOS in multiple languages.

Business Model

Wura operates as a free, community-driven sharing platform rather than a marketplace: users list unwanted items for free giveaway within invitation-based trusted circles or wider local visibility, and other users request them at no cost, with logistics for pickup/exchange arranged between users. The app's revenue model is not publicly disclosed; the platform likely monetises through future premium features, partnerships or grant/impact funding rather than transaction fees, consistent with its "generosity first" positioning.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Wura is a straightforward, tech-enabled reuse/sharing platform addressing household item waste in an underserved African market, with a genuine focus on free exchange over resale. Its relevance is good as an example of grassroots digital reuse infrastructure, though independently verified usage or impact metrics are not yet publicly available.
