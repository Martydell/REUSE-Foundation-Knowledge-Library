Organisation Overview

Lemon & Lavender was founded in 2021 in downtown Madison, Alabama, by local residents Shannon Fisher and Brandy Booth, who set out to build a retail shop centred on sustainability. Billed as "North Alabama's first refillery," the store lets customers bring in their own containers to refill everyday products such as shampoo, body wash, laundry detergent and dish soap, alongside a curated selection of eco-conscious home, gift and pet products sold at its Main Street storefront.

Business Model

Lemon & Lavender operates a single-location bricks-and-mortar refillery combined with a curated eco-retail shop. Its refill station allows customers to bring reusable containers (or purchase them in-store) and refill household and personal-care liquids by volume, reducing single-use plastic bottle consumption on repeat purchases. Alongside the refill offering, the store sells pre-packaged sustainable products — organic and natural pet products, home goods and gift items — chosen for reduced environmental impact across ingredients, shipping materials and packaging. Revenue comes from direct in-store retail sales; the business also maintains an e-commerce storefront (lemonandlavendermadison.com) that lists both refill-station items and packaged products, extending reach beyond walk-in customers in Madison, Alabama.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Lemon & Lavender is a genuine, community-scale U.S. refillery, notable as the first of its kind in the North Alabama region, providing a real (if small) reduction in single-use plastic packaging for household and personal-care goods. It lacks published impact metrics, multi-location scale, or documented partnerships, positioning it as a solid local case study rather than a benchmark operator.
