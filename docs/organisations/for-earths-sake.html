Organisation Overview

For Earth's Sake CIC is a not-for-profit, community-interest refill shop based in Shamley Green, near Guildford, Surrey, England. Incorporated in November 2018 and opened to the public in April 2019 by founder Vanessa Ford-Robbins, the business relocated in April 2024 to a barn premises powered entirely by an on-site solar array. It offers plastic-free, refillable groceries and household goods alongside a personal shopping/delivery service, positioning itself as a community-focused alternative to conventional packaged retail with an explicit mission to reduce unnecessary waste entering the environment through consumption.

Business Model

For Earth's Sake operates a bricks-and-mortar refill shop where customers bring reusable containers to fill with pantry staples (pasta, rice, grains, flour, sugar), nuts and seeds, tea and coffee, oils and vinegars, herbs and spices, and refillable liquid products including shampoo, conditioner, liquid handwash, laundry liquid, fabric conditioner and washing-up liquid. The shop also sells organic fruit and vegetables (delivered Wednesdays), dairy, eggs, bread, tinned goods, jams and spreads, and a specialty item (spanakopita). A distinctive service is its shopping-assistance model: customers can drop off containers and a shopping list, and staff will shop and prepare the order for collection or delivery the next day. As a Community Interest Company, profits are directed toward the organisation's stated community/environmental purpose rather than private shareholder returns; revenue comes from direct retail sales at its Shamley Green premises (open Wednesday–Saturday). Its physical operations are powered by an on-site solar array, reducing the business's own operational carbon footprint alongside its packaging-reduction mission.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

For Earth's Sake CIC is a small but well-documented UK community refill shop with a formally verifiable legal structure (Community Interest Company, Companies House no. 11698425, incorporated 2018) and a clear, mission-driven reuse model spanning both dry-goods and liquid refill systems. Its solar-powered premises and stated 100% waste recycling/reuse practice go beyond the refill offering alone, indicating a broader operational sustainability commitment. Its self-reported cumulative customer and waste figures, while unaudited, are more specific and structurally credible than many small retailers due to the CIC's public benefit reporting obligations. As with other single-location community refill shops, its scale is limited and there is no evidence of replication, franchising, or corporate partnership activity, but it is a solid example of grassroots reuse retail infrastructure in the UK.
