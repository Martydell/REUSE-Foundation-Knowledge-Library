Organisation Overview

ebebek is Turkey's largest mother-and-baby products retail chain, founded in 2001 as an e-commerce site and expanding into physical stores from 2003. Majority-owned by the Topbaş family since 2011 and publicly listed via IPO in 2023, ebebek operates 300+ stores across 71 Turkish cities plus recent international expansion into the UK (2024) and Iraq (2025). Under its "baby me" private label, ebebek has installed in-store detergent refill stations across a subset of its Turkish stores, allowing customers to refill previously purchased bottles of laundry detergent, fabric softener, stain remover and bottle/pacifier cleaner at a discount versus new bottles.

Business Model

ebebek's core business is omnichannel retail of baby and mother-care products (clothing, diapers, toys, feeding equipment, maternal health products) through 300+ physical stores and e-commerce in Turkey, with newer storefronts in the UK and Iraq. Layered onto this core retail model is a refill initiative branded "baby me Deterjan Dolum İstasyonu" (baby me Detergent Refill Station), rolled out to 11 stores across Turkey (including Istanbul Bostancı and Beylikdüzü, Bursa, Eskişehir, Aksaray, Antalya, Samsun, Diyarbakır, Adana and Izmir), where customers bring back empty "baby me" branded bottles of detergent, fabric softener, stain remover and bottle-cleaner to refill in-store at a c.30% discount versus buying new bottles. This is a manufacturer/retailer-run, closed-loop refill model tied to ebebek's own private-label products rather than an open multi-brand refill system, incentivised through pricing rather than deposit-return. The refill programme sits within a broader "Gelecek Bebeklerin" (Future of Babies) sustainability and social-responsibility platform the company publishes reporting on.

Products & Services

Baby and maternal retail: clothing, diapers, wet wipes, toys, feeding/breastfeeding equipment, maternal health products; own-brand "baby me" laundry detergent, fabric softener, stain remover, and bottle/pacifier cleaner available via refill stations in 11 Turkish stores.

Customers

Parents and expectant parents in Turkey (primary market), and growing customer bases in the UK and Iraq via new storefronts.

Partnerships

No third-party reuse/refill partnerships identified; the refill programme uses ebebek's own "baby me" private label. Company publishes its own sustainability/social-responsibility reports via its corporate site.

Measurable Impact

Per ebebek's own reporting (via Perakende.org/company social media, exact reporting date not stated but referencing cumulative programme totals): the baby me Detergent Refill Stations have saved approximately 5,873 plastic bottles from disposal, comprising ~2,877 detergent bottles, ~1,355 fabric softener bottles, ~934 bottle-cleaner bottles and ~707 stain-remover bottles. This figure is self-reported by the company/its retail-press coverage, not independently audited.

Technology

In-store dispensing/refill stations for liquid detergent products; no evidence of app-based tracking or deposit-return technology.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

ebebek demonstrates a working, multi-location in-store refill model for household liquids with a disclosed (if self-reported and modest) plastic-bottle-avoidance metric, making it a legitimate and useful example of retailer-led refill infrastructure at some scale (11 stores, several product lines). However, refill is a small side-programme within a much larger conventional retail business, and the seed claim of "bulk dispensers for laundry/fabric care" is verified — but scale and independent verification remain limited.
