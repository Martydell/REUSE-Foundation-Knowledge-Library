Organisation Overview

Ecandi (Eco Create and Innovate) is a zero-waste eco-shop based at Rosslyn Gates Centre on Limuru Road in Nairobi, Kenya, founded by Amos Njiraini. It positions itself as Nairobi's premier zero-waste retailer, selling ethically sourced, sustainable everyday products, and operates what is described in industry coverage as the first dedicated bulk refill station of its kind in Kenya, part of its "Eco Bulk Bar" offering. The business has been recognised through participation in the Nairobi Circular Economy ClimAccelerator programme and features in reporting on Kenya's food-and-beverage refill/reuse sector.

Business Model

Ecandi operates a physical retail eco-shop model combined with a bulk refill station: customers bring empty containers to refill everyday household and personal-care products sourced from ethically vetted partner suppliers, avoiding single-use packaging. Beyond refill, Ecandi also runs a recycling take-back service ("Recycle With Us") and sells upcycled/eco-manufactured items, including furniture such as tables crafted from recycled glass waste. The business has engaged with Kenya's formal circular-economy support ecosystem, participating in the Nairobi Circular Economy ClimAccelerator, which suggests external validation/support (mentorship, possibly funding) for scaling its refill and recycling model. Its combination of refill retail, recycling collection and upcycled product manufacture positions it as a multi-service circular-economy hub rather than a single-format refill shop, though detailed financials, ownership structure, and scale (store count, revenue, throughput) are not publicly disclosed.
