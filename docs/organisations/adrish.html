Organisation Overview

Adrish is described in Indian press coverage as the country's first organised zero-waste organic lifestyle store chain, co-founded by chartered accountant Akshay Agarwal and Gajendra Choudhary, who opened their first store in Pune (Koregaon Park) in September 2018 after growing concerned about plastic pollution. Building on the founders' earlier organic-food venture, Satvyk, Adrish sells organic groceries, home decor, cleaning products, personal care, and zero-waste essentials, sourcing directly from a large network of organic farmers and requiring customers to bring their own bags or containers.

Business Model

Adrish operates a multi-city retail model (physical stores plus e-commerce at adrish.co) selling organic food and grocery items, zero-waste essentials (reusable bags, containers, travel items, steel straws, bamboo toothbrushes), cleaning and personal-care products, and home decor, sourced directly from a reported network of organic farmers across multiple Indian states, cutting out middlemen. In-store, customers are expected to bring their own bags or containers for bulk items such as grains and oils; the company has reported providing rental containers for customers who do not bring their own. All product-facing plastic packaging is reported to be substituted with wooden crates, steel containers, and cane-woven fixtures. Online orders ship with "plastic-free shipping," free above ₹799. The company has expanded from its original two Pune locations to a reported presence (via store listings/partners) across more than a dozen Indian cities including Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi, and Hyderabad.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (bring-your-own-container bulk buying for grains, oils; in-store container rental option reported)
  • Waste Reduction (elimination of single-use plastic packaging in-store; plastic-free shipping)
  • Consumer Education (zero-waste lifestyle promotion, bring-your-own-container norm-setting)
  • Reusable Packaging (retail of reusable bags, containers, and zero-waste kits)

Not identified from public sources: Deposit Return, Packaging-as-a-Service, Repair, Rental (beyond reported in-store container lending), Sharing, Reverse Logistics, Take-back, Industrial Reuse, Digital Tracking, Material Recovery.

Products & Services

Organic groceries (oils, ghee, spices, grains, sweeteners, snacks, pickles); zero-waste essentials (reusable bags, containers, travel and eating/drinking items, zero-waste kits); cleaning products; personal care (bamboo toothbrushes, wooden combs, copper bottles, steel straws); home decor and gifting items; plastic-free shipping.

Customers

Individual consumers across multiple Indian cities (in-store) and nationally via e-commerce; the company also states support for a large organic-farmer supplier network (reported as approximately 8,000 farmers as of 2019 press coverage — a supplier relationship, not a customer base).

Partnerships

  • Network of an estimated 8,000 organic farmers across roughly 14 Indian states (as of 2019 reporting) — direct-sourcing supplier relationships, not named individual partners
  • No specific named corporate or NGO partners were identified from public sources

Measurable Impact

  • The Better India (2019) and related press reports cite the company sourcing from "around 8,000 farmers" across "14 Indian states" (as of June 2019); this figure is now several years old and not independently re-verified for the present day.
  • A press report (Startup360/similar coverage, undated in the source reviewed) states the company "saves a minimum of 5,000 plastic bags and other items from being exposed to landfills every day" — this figure is self/press-reported, not independently audited, and its currency and methodology could not be confirmed.
  • No verified, dated, audited impact metrics were found on the official website.

Technology

Not publicly available — no evidence of digital tracking, RFID, or app-based systems; the model relies on physical bring-your-own-container practices and direct farmer sourcing.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Adrish is a genuine, multi-city zero-waste organic retail chain with a real bring-your-own-container refill model and direct organic-farmer sourcing, placing it within the REUSE Foundation's scope on waste reduction and refill practices, though it functions primarily as a plastic-free grocery retailer rather than a dedicated refill-infrastructure or return-loop operator. Its expansion across more than a dozen Indian cities suggests meaningful reach for an independent retailer, but publicly available impact figures are dated (2019) and unaudited, and the specific seed-data URL for the organisation is no longer live (redirects/404), suggesting the website has been restructured since data collection. Verification of current operational status per city would benefit from a follow-up check.

Priority Rating: ★★★ Medium (verified refill/reduce model within scope; dated and unaudited impact data; website structure has changed since seed data was collected)
