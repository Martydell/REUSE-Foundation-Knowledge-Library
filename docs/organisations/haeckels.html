Organisation Overview

Haeckels was founded in Margate, Kent in 2012 by Dom Bridges, a volunteer beach warden, who began experimenting with seaweed (bladderwrack, kelp and dulse) hand-harvested from the local shoreline; the brand's first small lab opened on Cliff Terrace in 2013. Haeckels grew into a marine-derived skincare, fragrance and homeware brand sold through its own stores, "Community Hubs" in Margate and London, and wholesale/retail partners. In November 2024 the company announced it would remove the "Haeckels" name from packaging and communications over a 12-month transition period, re-emerging under the name "Dulcie," while retaining its Margate-based operations and sustainability model.

Business Model

Haeckels/Dulcie sells marine-ingredient-based skincare, fragrance and home products through direct retail (its own Margate and London locations, styled as "Community Hubs"), e-commerce, and third-party stockists. Its core reuse mechanism is an in-store refill system: customers can bring any clean, dry container — not necessarily Haeckels' own packaging — to refill select products, explicitly prioritizing avoiding new packaging production over brand-consistent vessels. Certain home-line products are sold in sandblasted glass bottles designed to be refilled repeatedly in-store. The company is B Corp certified (achieved 2022) and frames its business around "people care, planet care, fair share" principles, including stated ambitions toward zero-waste infrastructure and zero-plastic packaging, though without fully quantified public targets or dates found in sources reviewed.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Haeckels/Dulcie operates a genuine, consumer-friendly in-store refill model notable for accepting any clean container rather than requiring branded packaging — a practice well aligned with reuse-system principles and consumer convenience. However, its relevance is tempered by very limited physical footprint (two "Community Hub" locations), an ongoing brand-identity transition that creates near-term uncertainty, and an absence of quantified public impact data. Priority rating: ★★★ Medium.
