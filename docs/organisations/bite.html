Organisation Overview

Bite is a Los Angeles-based oral-care company founded in 2018 by Lindsay McCormick, who developed chewable toothpaste "bits" as an alternative to plastic toothpaste tubes after learning of the scale of tube waste going to landfill and ocean. The company sells toothpaste bits, mouthwash bits, whitening gel, deodorant and related personal-care products direct-to-consumer, packaged in a reusable glass jar refilled via compostable pouches. Bite is B Corp certified and gained mainstream visibility through a "Shark Tank" appearance (declining offers made) and features in outlets including Forbes, Oprah Magazine and Vogue.

Business Model

Bite operates a direct-to-consumer e-commerce and subscription model: a customer's first purchase includes a reusable 4oz glass jar (and, for some product lines, other durable holders), and subsequent orders — available as one-off purchases or auto-ship subscriptions (company materials reference a roughly 4-month refill cadence) — are compostable refill pouches rather than new plastic containers. This design eliminates repeat purchase of single-use plastic toothpaste tubes, replacing them with a reusable jar plus a lower-material, compostable pouch format. The company states its refill pouches are designed to break down in home compost within a stated timeframe. Bite has expanded from toothpaste bits into a broader personal-care range (mouthwash, whitening, deodorant, floss, tongue scrapers) sold through its own site and, per company materials, wholesale/retail partnerships.

Products & Services

Toothpaste Bits (fluoride and fluoride-free), Mouthwash Bits, Whitening Gel, Deodorant and Body Balm, plus complementary items (toothbrushes, floss, tongue scrapers) and bundled kits; core packaging model is a reusable glass jar refilled via compostable pouches.

Customers

B2C consumers, primarily in the United States, purchasing via the company's own e-commerce site (one-time or subscription); company materials also reference wholesale/retail distribution, though specific retail partners were not independently confirmed in this research.

Partnerships

Bite states a partnership with the Orangutan Alliance (per company website) and is B Corp certified (third-party certification, not a commercial partnership). The company appeared on "Shark Tank" but reportedly declined the investment offers made, so no ongoing investor partnership resulted from that appearance. No other named commercial partners were independently verified.

Measurable Impact

Not independently verified. Company materials cite 22,000+ five-star customer reviews and state that refill pouches are compostable/designed to disintegrate in home compost within a stated multi-week window; no independently audited figures for total plastic tubes avoided or tonnage diverted were found.

Technology

Not publicly available in technical detail. No proprietary dispensing or tracking technology identified; the innovation is primarily in product formulation (compressed toothpaste tablets) and compostable pouch packaging design rather than hardware/software.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Bite is a well-documented, venture-visible (Shark Tank, national media coverage) direct-to-consumer brand with a clear and verifiable reuse mechanic: a durable jar refilled repeatedly via low-material compostable pouches, directly displacing single-use plastic toothpaste tubes at meaningful consumer scale (22,000+ reviews suggests substantial customer base). Its B Corp status adds a layer of external verification. The main gap is the absence of independently audited environmental impact figures (only company-stated claims). This is a solid, moderately well-sourced consumer reuse case study for the personal-care category.
