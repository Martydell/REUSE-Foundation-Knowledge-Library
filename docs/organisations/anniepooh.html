Organisation Overview

AnniePooh is a small, independently owned zero-waste shop and refillery based in Greystones, County Wicklow, Ireland, with an accompanying online store. Founded in 2012 by Ann Teehan after she struggled to find chemical- and plastic-free products for her children, the business began with pop-up markets before opening a permanent shop. It sells reusable and refillable personal care, household, and lifestyle products, both own-curated and from third-party eco brands, aimed at helping customers reduce single-use plastic consumption.

Business Model

AnniePooh operates a hybrid bricks-and-mortar-plus-e-commerce model. In its physical Greystones shop, customers can refill containers with household liquids (cleaning products, laundry liquid) and buy loose/bulk dry goods (pasta, rice, spices, nuts), avoiding single-use packaging. Online, AnniePooh functions as a curated marketplace, stocking reusable and refillable products from a range of third-party eco brands (e.g. EcoEgg, Ocean Saver, Bambaw, WUKA) across categories including reusable menstrual products, deodorants, skincare, hair care, cleaning and laundry refills, tooth care, kitchen items, reusable bags/bottles/lunchboxes, and children's and pet products. Revenue is generated through direct retail sale of physical goods rather than a subscription or service model. There is no publicly disclosed information on revenue, employee count, or investment/funding.

Products & Services

In-store refill of household cleaning and laundry liquids; bulk/loose dry food goods; reusable menstrual products (pads, cups, period pants); refillable deodorants, skincare and hair care; reusable nappies and beeswax wraps; reusable bags, bottles, cups and lunchboxes; eco-friendly stationery and gifts; children's and pet items.

Customers

Individual consumers (B2C), described by the founder as primarily women aged 30–55, though marketed to all genders and ages; both local Greystones/Wicklow shoppers and online customers across Ireland (free shipping offered on orders over €50 to the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

AnniePooh is a small, founder-led independent retailer rather than a scalable reuse-infrastructure provider. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation library lies in its refill-shop model and role in Irish zero-waste retail, but it lacks published impact data, technology infrastructure, or documented partnerships that would elevate it beyond a local case study. It is a useful example of grassroots refill retail but not a strategically central organisation for the knowledge library.
