Organisation Overview

Yes Future opened in 2017 in Barcelona's Sant Antoni neighbourhood as Spain's first plastic-free, zero-waste supermarket, later adding a second store in Poblenou (2019). Co-founded by Olga Rodríguez, the store sells food, drinks, cleaning and hygiene products in bulk via dispensers and refillable containers, with customers bringing their own containers to be weighed, filled and charged by quantity.

Business Model

Yes Future operates physical bulk-format retail stores where roughly 300 food items (spices, legumes, nuts, honey, etc.) plus household cleaning and personal hygiene products are sold from dispensers or bulk containers; customers bring reusable containers, which are tare-weighed before filling so they pay only for product weight. Revenue comes from in-store retail sales, positioned as a premium/ethical grocery alternative rather than discount retail, with an online store (shop.yesfuture.store) supplementing the two physical Barcelona locations.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Yes Future is a pioneering and long-running physical refill/bulk retail model in Spain, credited as the country's first plastic-free supermarket, and remains a useful reference case for bring-your-own-container grocery retail. Its relevance is solid, though it operates at boutique/local scale (two stores) rather than a scaled or franchised model.
