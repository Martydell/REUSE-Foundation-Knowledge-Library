Organisation Overview

Gram Malmö, founded in 2016 by Rowan Drury, was Sweden's first package-free grocery store. Located at Sankt Knuts Torg in Malmö, it sells over 300 items — food staples, produce, soaps, detergents, and household goods — entirely without pre-set packaging. Customers bring their own jars, boxes, or bags (or use provided paper bags) and buy loose goods by weight, paying only for what they need. The shop emphasises local and organic sourcing from Swedish producers alongside its zero-packaging retail model, and has become a reference case internationally for the "unpackaged" retail movement.

Business Model

Gram operates a bring-your-own-container (BYOC) retail model: dry goods, oils, cleaning products, and other consumables are dispensed from bulk containers, weighed at point of sale, and priced per unit weight, eliminating single-use packaging at checkout. The store prioritises sourcing from local and organic Swedish producers (flour, oats, legumes, honey, oils, dairy, seasonal vegetables, chocolate, coffee) alongside imported staples where local sourcing isn't feasible. Revenue comes from in-store retail sales plus online ordering with in-store pickup, Malmö-area delivery, or postal shipping across Sweden. The business also plays an educational/advocacy role — publishing blog content on package-free living and hosting or participating in sustainability-sector events (e.g. YEE network interviews, Top Packaging Summit) — positioning itself as a model and advisor for other package-free retail start-ups in Scandinavia.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (bulk dispensing of dry goods, liquids, and household products by weight)
  • Reusable Packaging (customer-supplied reusable containers, jars, bags)
  • Waste Reduction (elimination of single-use retail packaging)
  • Consumer Education (blog content and public engagement on zero-waste shopping)

Products & Services

Package-free dry goods (grains, legumes, flour, nuts, spices), produce, honey, oils, dairy and eggs, coffee and tea, soaps, laundry detergent, shampoo bars, toothpaste tablets, coffee filters, and reusable bottles; in-store shopping, local delivery, and nationwide postal shipping.

Customers

Local Malmö residents and environmentally conscious consumers seeking package-free/zero-waste grocery options; online customers across Sweden via mail order.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Gram Malmö is a strong, well-documented example of the bring-your-own-container refill retail model — a core reuse archetype directly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's remit. As one of the earliest package-free grocery stores in Sweden (2016), it has a track record and media profile that could offer useful case-study material, though detailed quantitative impact data (waste diverted, packaging avoided) is not publicly available. Its scale is that of a single independent shop rather than a scaled or replicated network, which somewhat limits systemic relevance, but its pioneering status and educational output make it a valuable qualitative case.
