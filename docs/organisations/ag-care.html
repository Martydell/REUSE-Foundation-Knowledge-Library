Organisation Overview

AG Care (formerly branded AG Hair) is a Canadian professional haircare manufacturer founded in 1989 by Lotte Davis and John Davis in North Vancouver, initially selling directly to hair salons from 1990. The company describes itself as the largest independent manufacturer of professional hair-care products in Canada, formulating and bottling products at its own facility in Vancouver, British Columbia. In 2022 the brand relaunched in the US market under the "AG Care" name and introduced sustainability-focused packaging changes, including refillable aluminium bottles and post-consumer-recycled (PCR) plastic, supplied via 1-litre refill pouches.

Business Model

AG Care manufactures and sells professional-grade hair and body care products (shampoo, conditioner, styling and treatment products) through two main channels: B2B sales to hair salons/stylists (its original and core channel since 1990) and direct-to-consumer/retail sales, including a partnership with Ulta Beauty under its Conscious Beauty programme. Manufacturing, formulation, quality assurance and bottling occur in-house at the company's own Vancouver facility, which the company states reduces its supply-chain footprint versus outsourced production. Since 2022, selected product lines (e.g., shampoo and conditioner) have moved to aluminium and PCR-plastic bottles that customers refill using separately sold 1-litre refill pouches, rather than repeatedly buying new full-size plastic bottles. The company states this refill format uses less plastic and energy than producing new litre bottles each purchase cycle.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems — 1-litre refill pouches for aluminium/PCR shampoo and conditioner bottles (introduced 2022)
  • Waste Reduction — reported shift away from large-format single-use plastic bottles; increasing post-consumer recycled plastic content
  • Consumer Education — company/press communications on the environmental rationale for refill packaging

Products & Services

Professional shampoo, conditioner, styling products (gels, sprays, curl activators, detanglers), hand care, sold in aluminium, PCR-plastic or refill-pouch formats depending on product line.

Customers

Hair salons and professional stylists (original core channel); retail/direct-to-consumer customers via e-commerce and retail partners such as Ulta Beauty.

Partnerships

  • Ulta Beauty — US retail partner, products meet Ulta's "Conscious Beauty" packaging/ingredient standards
  • HairCo Australia — distribution partner
  • Beauté Star — Quebec (French-language market) sales partner
  • One Girl Can — charity partner supporting gender-parity education initiatives in Kenya; company states it has contributed roughly $1.2 million since 2008

Measurable Impact

Company states its refill pouches "use 73% less plastic and take 62% less energy to produce" compared with its previous full-size litre bottles, and that plastic bottles contain roughly 30% post-consumer regrind content (with plans to increase this from 2023). These are company-reported figures relayed via a third-party sustainability outlet (Earth911); no independently audited lifecycle assessment was found in public sources.

Technology used

Not publicly available — no digital tracking, app, or automated refill-dispensing technology was identified; the refill mechanism is a manually poured pouch-to-bottle system.

Challenges

Not publicly available.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

AG Care is a long-established (1989), privately/founder-owned Canadian manufacturer that has made a concrete, verifiable move toward reuse: replacing full-size single-use bottle purchases with refillable aluminium/PCR bottles supplied via pouches, for at least its shampoo and conditioner lines. Its claimed 73% plastic and 62% energy reduction figures are self-reported and unaudited, and it is not yet B Corp certified (described as "working toward" certification). As a domestic manufacturer with its own production facility and an established B2B salon distribution network, it has credible potential to extend refill formats across more of its range. Its relevance is currently moderate — refill is one part of a broader conventional personal-care product line, not the company's sole model.
