Organisation Overview

IpohRefill is Malaysia's first zero-waste store in Ipoh, Perak, founded by Peisee and opened in February 2024 after she was selected for the Perak Environmental Accelerator Program 2023 (run by Biji-Biji Initiative), which provided funding to establish refill stations. The flagship store, located at Ipoh Garden South, sells package-free household and personal-care products and operates a refill system where customers bring their own containers to refill cleaning products, shampoos, detergents and dishwashing liquids. Beyond retail, it functions as a community hub offering a recyclables collection point, a thrift shop, zero-waste and food-waste workshops, and collaboration space for other social enterprises.

Business Model

IpohRefill operates as a social enterprise combining direct refill retail with community environmental education and infrastructure. Its core retail model lets customers bring reusable containers to refill cleaning and personal-care liquids (floor/toilet cleaners, shampoo, detergents, dishwashing liquid), sourced partly through a partnership with local bio-eco manufacturer Cleanme Chemicals. Beyond the flagship store, IpohRefill built out a network of five refill stations across Perak (Ipoh, Kampar, Taiping) as a funded grant KPI under its accelerator program, extending refill access beyond the single physical location. Additional revenue/impact streams include a recyclables collection hub, a thrift shop for secondhand goods, and paid or sponsored workshops on zero-waste living and food-waste reduction (including bokashi composting), with 2025 programming extending into school partnerships and public talks, plus community "Food Waste Station" rollouts with local councils.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

IpohRefill is a well-documented, verifiable grassroots refill and zero-waste initiative with a credible founding story, an accelerator-funded refill-station network, and a concrete, dated impact figure (7,000+ plastic containers avoided in 2024). Its combination of refill retail, recyclables collection, and community education workshops makes it a strong small-scale/regional example of an integrated zero-waste hub model, though its scale remains local to Ipoh and Perak state rather than national or international.
