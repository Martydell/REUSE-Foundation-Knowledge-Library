Organisation Overview

etee is a Canadian sustainable-products brand founded in Ontario in 2015 by Steve Reble, after an encounter with plastic pollution while kayaking on a local river prompted him to develop plastic-free alternatives to everyday household items. The brand's name and philosophy — "Everything Touches Everything Else" — reflect a "from the earth and back to the earth" design ethos. Its best-known product is a reusable, biodegradable beeswax-alternative food wrap used in place of plastic cling film, alongside a broader range of plastic-free household and personal-hygiene products, all shipped in plastic-free, biodegradable packaging.

Business Model

etee sells direct-to-consumer (and via retail partners such as Healthy Planet Canada and Simply Natural Canada) a range of reusable and biodegradable household products, most notably its signature reusable food wraps, positioned as durable replacements for single-use plastic cling film and sandwich bags. Revenue comes from repeat online and retail sales of these reusable items, which are designed to be washed and reused over an extended lifespan before being composted at end of life, reducing single-use plastic consumption in food storage and other household/personal-hygiene applications. The company also runs an affiliate marketing program to extend distribution reach. As a founder-led direct-to-consumer brand, its scale is smaller than mainstream food-storage brands but it has built a recognisable niche within the North American zero-waste/plastic-free product market.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

etee is a credible, founder-led Canadian example of reusable-product design substituting for single-use plastic in food storage and household use, with an authentic origin story and a decade of continuous operation. Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation is as a niche consumer-products case study rather than an infrastructure or systems-level reuse model; no independently verified impact data (units sold, plastic displaced) was found to support a higher priority rating at this time.
