Organisation Overview

Loopipak is a Belgian brand of Graine de Malice SRL that designs, manufactures and rents or sells reusable, trackable packaging for industrial and e-commerce logistics — replacing single-use cardboard boxes, stretch film, and protective wrapping with durable crates, covers and custom-built containers. A distinguishing feature is that its products are made from upcycled advertising banners and other end-of-life materials, and manufactured/repaired in sheltered workshops that provide stable employment to people with disabilities or those on professional reintegration pathways, combining circular-economy and social-inclusion objectives.

Business Model

Loopipak sells and rents standardised and custom-made reusable packaging — crates, protective covers, and specialty containers for bulky or precious goods — to industrial and e-commerce clients seeking to replace single-use stretch film, bags, and cardboard boxes in their logistics flows (returns, handling, storage). Customers can choose to buy or rent depending on their operational needs, and Loopipak positions its reusables as becoming more cost-effective than disposable alternatives after just a few use cycles. Products are manufactured locally in Belgium, partly from upcycled advertising banners and other repurposed materials, and production/repair work is carried out in sheltered workshops ("ateliers protégés"), giving the business a dual circular-economy and social-enterprise character. A deposit-management system built on Odoo (with partial Shopify integration) supports tracking of packaging as it moves through client supply chains. Loopipak emphasizes local production and regional industrial partnerships to minimise transport distances and reinforce a local circular economy in Belgium.

Industrial and e-commerce businesses in Belgium and the surrounding region seeking to replace single-use stretch film, plastic bags, and cardboard packaging in logistics and returns flows (specific named clients not published).

Partnerships

Sheltered workshops ("ateliers protégés") for manufacturing and repair, providing employment to people with disabilities or in professional reintegration; regional industrial partners in Belgium for local material sourcing and production. Specific named commercial partners are not publicly available.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Loopipak is a credible, operational B2B reusable-packaging provider with a genuine buy-or-rent model, a digital deposit-tracking system, and an unusual and valuable social-inclusion dimension (manufacturing via sheltered workshops). Its combination of circular economy and social enterprise objectives, plus use of upcycled input materials, make it a distinctive case for the library. However, the absence of publicly disclosed founding year, named clients, or quantified impact metrics limits full verification and comparability against similar reusable-packaging operators.
