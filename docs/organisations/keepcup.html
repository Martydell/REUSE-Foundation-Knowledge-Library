KeepCup

Melbourne-founded creator of the world's first "barista-standard" reusable coffee cup, launched 2009 by siblings Abigail and Jamie Forsyth.

Organisation Overview

KeepCup was founded in 2009 in Melbourne by siblings Abigail and Jamie Forsyth, who previously ran Bluebag, a Melbourne CBD sandwich-shop chain founded in 1998. Observing the volume of disposable cups generated by their café customers, they designed what is described as the world's first "barista-standard" reusable coffee cup — engineered to work efficiently with espresso machines and café service — and sold the first units at an independent Melbourne market in 2009. The brand has since become one of the most recognisable reusable-cup brands globally, with 11-50 employees and international distribution.

Business Model

KeepCup manufactures and sells reusable cups (coffee, tea, cold beverages) designed to integrate smoothly into café/barista workflows, differentiating it from generic reusable cups not designed with hospitality-service efficiency in mind. It sells direct-to-consumer online and through retail/hospitality channels globally, promoting a "refill culture" in cafés by making reusable cups a practical daily-use option rather than a niche behaviour. Co-founder Abigail Forsyth received Australia's Member of the Order of Australia (OAM) honour in 2024 for her sustainability/waste-reduction contributions.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

KeepCup is one of the most well-established and globally recognised reusable-cup brands, credited with popularising the "barista-standard" reusable cup category and driving broader reuse-culture adoption in cafés during the 2010s. Its 15+ year operating history, founder recognition (OAM honour), and global brand reach make it a strong benchmark case for product-level reuse in the hospitality/consumer beverage space.
