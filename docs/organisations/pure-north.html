Organisation Overview

Pure North is Iceland's only plastic recycling plant, operating on geothermal steam and hydropower electricity in South Iceland. Established around 2015, it processes plastic waste — including most of Iceland's agricultural film plastic — into raw recycled material sold on to manufacturers, rather than directly producing finished reusable containers.

Business Model

Pure North collects plastic waste from households, businesses, and municipalities and mechanically recycles it using renewable geothermal and hydropower energy, selling the resulting recycled raw material to companies that manufacture new products. This avoids the need to ship Icelandic plastic waste to mainland Europe or Asia for processing.

Measurable Impact

A third-party Life Cycle Assessment found Pure North's process saves up to 1.52 tons CO2-eq per ton of plastic recycled, an estimated 81.2% lower carbon footprint than the average European recycling facility.
