Organisation Overview

Cokodeal is a Nigeria-headquartered B2B e-commerce marketplace that lets African traders, manufacturers, farmers and small businesses create self-managed online stores to market and export locally-produced goods — agricultural commodities, foodstuffs, textiles, crafts, solid minerals and similar categories — to buyers within Africa and internationally. Founded by Mike Dola, the platform describes its mission as connecting intra-African and Africa-to-global trade, targeting price disparities and limited market access faced by African producers and exporters. It is frequently described in Nigerian business press as an African e-commerce/export-trade platform.

Business Model

Cokodeal operates a subscription/listing-based B2B marketplace model: businesses and individual traders subscribe to create an online storefront, through which they list and promote goods for sale to buyers across Africa and globally. The platform's own materials frame its core offering as technical support, search-engine visibility, and business-listing tools to help subscribers "get more customers," alongside stated ambitions around trade financing ("The Commodity Bank") and a regional-agent network for real-time commodity information. Cokodeal's own "About" content explicitly frames its focus as export of locally-manufactured African goods and commodities (agriculture, textiles, minerals, crafts) — not on resale, refurbishment or circulation of used/secondhand goods.

Circular Economy Activities

No circular-economy or reuse activities from the fixed vocabulary (Reusable Packaging, Refill Systems, Deposit Return, Packaging-as-a-Service, Repair, Rental, Sharing, Reverse Logistics, Take-back, Industrial Reuse, Waste Reduction, Consumer Education, Digital Tracking, Material Recovery) were identified in any source reviewed. Cokodeal's own official materials (website, FAQ, "About Cokodeal" blog post) consistently describe it as a marketplace for new, locally-manufactured African commodities, foodstuffs and goods for export — with no mention of used, secondhand, or reuse-oriented trade in furniture, electronics or tools as stated in the seed data.

Products & Services

Self-managed online storefronts for African traders/manufacturers; marketplace listings for agricultural commodities, foodstuffs, textiles, crafts and solid minerals; business-to-business trade-connection tools; stated (but not independently confirmed as launched) ambitions around trade financing and a regional trade-agent network.

Customers

African commodity/goods producers, manufacturers, farmers and small businesses (as sellers/subscribers); international and intra-African wholesale buyers, including stated interest from "thousands of stores in the US, UK, EU, Asia markets" seeking African suppliers.

Partnerships

Not publicly available. Cokodeal's own materials reference government agencies and unnamed "organizations" as past collaborators in support of its trade-connection mission, but no specific named partner organisations could be independently verified.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available. Cokodeal's homepage claims it is "trusted by 20,000+ global businesses," but this is a self-reported marketing claim with no independent source, date, or methodology disclosed, so it is not presented here as a verified metric.

Technology

A web-based e-commerce marketplace platform with self-service store creation, business listings, and search-engine-optimisation tooling for sellers. No reuse-specific technology (e.g., tracking of used-goods condition, circular logistics) was identified.
