Organisation Overview

CupClub was the original brand name of what is now Clubzero, founded in 2018 by UCL alumnus Safia Qureshi with the goal of replacing single-use cup packaging with a reusable, tracked cup system for hot and cold beverages. Operating initially in London offices and later rolling out a retail network in Palo Alto, California, CupClub's operations were disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic; the company subsequently returned to London and rebranded as Clubzero, broadening its positioning beyond beverage cups specifically toward a wider "zero waste" reuse system.

Business Model

CupClub's original model was a daily reusable-packaging service for drinks, primarily targeting London offices: cups were tracked and returned through a managed logistics system rather than being customer-owned. The company raised a £460k pre-seed funding round early in its life and was accepted into R/GA London's IoT Venture programme, reflecting its technology-enabled approach to reusable-cup tracking. This service-based, tracked-reuse model carried forward into the Clubzero rebrand, which expanded the same underlying reuse-logistics approach to other reusable packaging (not just cups) and other institutional/retail settings (e.g. universities, Wimbledon/Barclays partnership) — see the Clubzero profile for details on the current, expanded operation.
