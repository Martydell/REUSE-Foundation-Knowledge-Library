Organisation Overview

The Atıksız Yaşam Dükkanı ("Zero Waste Life Shop") is a municipally-run retail initiative of Kadıköy Belediyesi (Kadıköy Municipality) in Istanbul, described in Turkish press and municipal sources as the country's first and only local-government zero-waste store. The first location opened on 5 June 2020 (World Environment Day) at the Feneryolu fixed market, with a second branch opening in 2022 at the Caddebostan Culture Centre (CKM). The shop sells unpackaged or minimally-packaged food, cleaning, kitchen and personal-care products, connecting customers directly with local producers, and includes a free water-refill station for reusable bottles.

Business Model

The Dükkan operates as a public-sector retail service rather than a commercial enterprise: it is directly run by Kadıköy Municipality as part of its environmental/sustainability programme, aiming to demonstrate and normalise package-free shopping rather than to generate profit. Customers bring their own reusable bags, sacks or jars to purchase products across categories including "Daily Life," "Food," "Cleaning," "Kitchen," and "Personal Care & Health" — items are sold unpackaged or in recyclable packaging by weight/volume. The municipality also uses digital invoicing instead of paper receipts to minimise waste, and the shop sources products from local and regional producers, giving it a dual purpose of waste reduction and local-economy support. As a municipal programme, its "revenue" model is functionally a public service subsidised by the local government budget rather than a for-profit retail operation, and its two locations (Feneryolu, Caddebostan) serve as demonstration sites intended to be replicated by other municipalities — the project has reportedly been cited as a good-practice example in European contexts.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems — package-free bulk shopping using customer-supplied containers/bags/jars
  • Waste Reduction — unpackaged/recyclable-packaging retail model; digital invoicing to cut paper waste
  • Consumer Education — public demonstration of package-free shopping; connecting consumers with local producers
  • Other — free public water-refill station for reusable bottles at store location(s)

Products & Services

Unpackaged or recyclable-packaged food products, cleaning products, kitchen items and personal-care/health products sourced from local producers; free drinking-water refill station.

Customers

Residents of Kadıköy district and visitors to Istanbul seeking package-free/zero-waste shopping; local producers supplying the shop's product range.

Partnerships

  • Local producers and artisans supplying unpackaged goods (unnamed specific businesses in sources reviewed)
  • Recognised as a good-practice example in European municipal sustainability circles (per cevremuhendisligi.org coverage), though the specific recognising body/programme is not named in detail in sources reviewed

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available — no independently audited figures for packaging waste avoided, customer volume, or water refilled were found in verified public sources; sources confirm two operating locations (Feneryolu since 2020, Caddebostan/CKM since 2022).

Technology

Digital invoicing system replacing paper receipts; water purification/refill station for reusable bottles. No app-based or digital container-tracking system identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

The Kadıköy Municipality Zero Waste Shop is a notable example of local government directly operating refill/reuse retail infrastructure rather than merely regulating or subsidising private operators — a model with strong replication potential for other municipalities. Its described status as Turkey's first such municipal programme, its multi-year track record (since 2020) and expansion to a second location support genuine, sustained operation rather than a one-off pilot. Public impact data remains limited, but the model itself — a municipality directly running package-free retail — is highly relevant to the REUSE Foundation's interest in scalable, institutionally-backed reuse systems.
