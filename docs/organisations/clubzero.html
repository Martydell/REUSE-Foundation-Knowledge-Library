Organisation Overview

Clubzero (formerly CupClub) is a UK-founded reuse-system company that provides returnable, serialised packaging — cups, containers and other food-service items — tracked via QR code, RFID or barcode, for the food & beverage sector. Founded by Safia Qureshi, the company began with reusable to-go cup infrastructure and has since expanded into a broader "Reuse System" covering on-trade (restaurants, stadiums), retail, and online-order channels. It positions itself as a "Reuse Scheme Administrator," handling packaging serialisation, washing/logistics coordination and impact reporting for client brands.

Business Model

Clubzero operates a Packaging-as-a-Service / reuse-infrastructure model aimed at businesses (B2B), not direct consumers. Client organisations — restaurants, caterers (e.g., Compass Group, Aramark, ISS), retailers, sports venues, and corporate offices — subscribe to Clubzero's returnable packaging system instead of using single-use disposables. Clubzero supplies durable, uniquely serialised containers, installs drop-off/return points, and coordinates a network of third-party washing and logistics partners to clean and redistribute packaging. Digital tracking (QR/RFID/barcode) records each container's rotation, enabling return-rate and environmental-impact reporting back to client brands. Revenue is generated through service contracts with venues and brands rather than by selling packaging outright. The company reports patents secured in the US and Singapore for aspects of its tracking/reuse system.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging
  • Deposit Return (serialised container return/tracking underpins its return-rate model)
  • Packaging-as-a-Service
  • Reverse Logistics (washing/logistics partner network for collection and redistribution)
  • Digital Tracking (QR code / RFID / barcode tracking of packaging rotations)
  • Consumer Education / Waste Reduction (impact reporting to partner organisations)

No evidence was found of repair, rental of non-packaging goods, or material-recovery/recycling activities; Clubzero's scope is specifically returnable food & beverage packaging infrastructure.

Products & Services

Returnable/reusable cups and food-service containers; drop-point and collection infrastructure; digital tracking and impact-reporting technology; managed washing and reverse-logistics coordination; deployment across on-trade (restaurants/cafes/stadiums), retail, and online-order/delivery channels.

Customers

B2B clients in food-service, hospitality, retail, corporate and sports/events sectors. Named clients/partners reported on Clubzero's own site include Compass Group, Aramark, ISS, Wimbledon (All England Lawn Tennis Club), Barclays, Nestlé, McDonald's, Expo City Dubai, and University College London (UCL) — the last confirmed via a UCL sustainability case study.

Partnerships

  • Wimbledon (All England Lawn Tennis Club) — returnable cup/packaging deployment at the Championships
  • Barclays — corporate deployment; Clubzero's site states Barclays saved 100,000 pieces of single-use plastic packaging from landfill through the partnership
  • Compass Group, Aramark, ISS — foodservice/catering partners
  • UCL (University College London) — returnable cups scheme, confirmed via UCL's own published case study
  • Innovate UK / UK Research & Innovation — Smart Sustainable Plastic Packaging Challenge grant (awarded 2023), which funded an independent Life Cycle Assessment
  • Giraffe Innovation Ltd — independent environmental consultancy that conducted Clubzero's 2024 Life Cycle Assessment

Measurable Impact

  • Independent Life Cycle Assessment conducted by Giraffe Innovation Ltd (published on Clubzero's site 15 August 2024, funded via an Innovate UK Smart Sustainable Plastic Packaging Challenge grant) found "up to 93% reduction in CO2 emissions" for Clubzero's returnable packaging compared with single-use alternatives. This corroborates the seed data's 93% CO2 claim, with a named third-party assessor and publication date. (Source: clubzero.co news post on the LCA.)
  • Clubzero's own site states 95–98% return rates and 3m+/3.3m+ packaging rotations delivered, and elsewhere reports having served 1.3 million+ orders, saving 2.6 million single-use items from landfill, though these are self-reported figures without an identified independent auditor.
  • The specific seed-data claim of "128,000+ single-use plastics saved (2023)" was not independently located in the sources checked; a related but distinct figure found is that Barclays specifically is credited with saving 100,000 pieces of single-use plastic packaging through its Clubzero partnership (self-reported, no date given). Treat the 128,000 figure as unverified.
  • All quantitative claims above (aside from the third-party-authored LCA) are company self-reported; no independently audited impact report was located.

Technology

Digital tracking of reusable packaging via QR code, RFID and barcode serialisation; an AI-driven system for managing container rotations, drop-point logistics and return-rate/impact reporting; patents reported secured in the US and Singapore covering aspects of the reuse-tracking system.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Clubzero is a well-documented, actively marketed B2B reuse-infrastructure provider with named enterprise partnerships (Wimbledon, Barclays, UCL, major catering groups) and a genuinely independent third-party Life Cycle Assessment substantiating its headline 93% CO2-reduction claim — a notably strong evidence base compared with many peers in this space. However, a significant, unresolved red flag emerged during verification: UK Companies House records for the entity behind the brand, Acet Circular Solutions Ltd, show what appears to be an active insolvency/liquidation case filed in 2026. This must be reconciled before the profile is used for outreach or partnership purposes — the company's live website and case studies suggest ongoing operations, but the legal-entity status raises real questions about current solvency that a public knowledge base should not gloss over.

  • The seed data's "93% CO2 reduction vs single-use" claim is verified, with a named independent assessor (Giraffe Innovation Ltd) and Innovate UK grant funding — this is stronger sourcing than typical for this dataset.
