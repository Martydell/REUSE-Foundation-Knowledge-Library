WaterKiosk Africa

Kenya-based operator of solar-powered water desalination kiosks providing off-grid African communities with refillable drinking water via reusable jerrycans.

Organisation Overview

WaterKiosk Africa installs, operates, and maintains solar-powered water desalination systems for off-grid communities across Africa, providing drinking, irrigation, sanitation, and fish-farm water. It works closely with Boreal Light GmbH, a German manufacturer of the underlying solar desalination technology, and emphasises women as kiosk operators.

Business Model

WaterKiosk Africa deploys solar desalination "kiosks" in off-grid communities, generating revenue by selling refilled drinking and utility water to local residents (who typically bring reusable jerrycans rather than buying bottled water), supplemented by community WiFi access sold at the kiosks and training delivered through its "WaterKiosk Academy." It partners with development agencies and local governments to fund and site installations.

Products & Services

Solar-powered desalinated drinking water; irrigation, sanitation, and fish-farm water; community WiFi access at kiosks; WaterKiosk Academy training programme.

Customers

Off-grid rural and peri-urban communities across Africa; local women trained and employed as kiosk operators.

Partnerships

GIZ, UNIDO, Boreal Light GmbH, Kenya Water Institute (KEWI), Bilal Muslim Mission of Kenya, Baringo County Government, among others.

Measurable Impact

States alignment with 7 UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs 2, 3, 6, 7, 8, 12, 13); claims CO2 emission reduction, local employment creation, and reduced plastic waste through reusable jerrycan refilling. No independently audited quantitative figures found.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

WaterKiosk Africa is a well-documented water-access and refill infrastructure operator with credible institutional partners (GIZ, UNIDO) and a clear reuse mechanism (reusable jerrycan refilling replacing bottled water). Strong candidate given verifiable partnerships, though quantified impact data is largely self-reported rather than independently audited.
