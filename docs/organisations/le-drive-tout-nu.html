Le Drive Tout Nu

The world's first zero-waste grocery "drive-through," delivering French organic groceries in reusable, deposit-based glass jars and fabric bags.

Organisation Overview

Le Drive Tout Nu was founded in 2018 by Pierre Géraud-Liria and Salomé Géraud, who conceived the idea in mid-2017 and opened what they describe as the world's first zero-waste "drive" (a French click-and-collect grocery format) in Beauzelle, near Toulouse, in December 2018. Products — including fruit and vegetables, dairy, meat, cosmetics and cleaning items — are supplied in reusable glass jars or fabric bags rather than disposable packaging. The concept has since expanded to additional sites in Toulouse, Lille and Bordeaux, and in 2025 began converting locations (e.g. Marquette-lez-Lille) into full physical supermarkets under the "Le Super tout nu" banner.

Business Model

Le Drive Tout Nu operates a click-and-collect ("drive") grocery model in which all products are pre-packaged by the company or its suppliers into reusable glass jars, fabric bags or other reusable containers, replacing conventional single-use grocery packaging entirely. Its distinguishing circular mechanism is a "reverse deposit": rather than charging a deposit at the point of sale, the company credits customers €0.10 per container returned, which can be redeemed against future purchases — lowering the upfront cost barrier compared with traditional deposit-return schemes. Customers can also return third-party glass jars (e.g. tomato sauce or jam jars) for reuse in the system. The company has grown through a franchise-style expansion model (listed on French franchise directories), opening additional drive locations across several cities, and has begun converting at least one drive location into a full-format zero-waste supermarket, indicating a scaling strategy beyond the original click-and-collect format.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Le Drive Tout Nu is a genuinely innovative reuse model — the reverse-deposit mechanism and full-catalogue reusable packaging (not just dry-goods bulk) distinguish it from typical bulk/refill stores, and its multi-city expansion plus 2025 supermarket conversion signal real commercial traction. It is one of the more structurally significant reuse operators among this batch, combining deposit-return, reverse logistics and reusable packaging into a single grocery format.
