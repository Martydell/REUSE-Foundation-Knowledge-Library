Organisation Overview

LifeBag is a manufacturer based in Almaty, Kazakhstan, producing reusable eco-bags made from spunbond, a non-woven polypropylene material. Its products — offered in t-shirt style, die-cut handle, and loop handle formats — are marketed as durable, reusable alternatives to single-use polyethylene bags, positioned as compliant with EU Directive 2004/12/EC on packaging and packaging waste. The company's stated mission, "Let's preserve nature together," reflects a focus on reducing plastic bag consumption in Central Asia. Public information about the company is limited to its own bilingual (Russian/English) website; no independent press coverage, financial data, or ownership records were found.

Business Model

LifeBag manufactures and sells reusable non-woven spunbond bags, likely on a B2B/wholesale basis to retailers and businesses seeking branded reusable-bag alternatives, alongside possible direct sales to environmentally conscious consumers, following the pattern common among regional spunbond bag manufacturers (e.g., in Ukraine, Uzbekistan). Its value proposition rests on spunbond's durability (bags reusable "hundreds of times") and faster environmental degradation than conventional polyethylene bags (marketed as roughly 1-2 years versus 100+ years), positioning the product as both a reusable substitute for single-use plastic bags and a lower-impact material choice compared to conventional plastic packaging. No information is publicly available on production volume, customer base, retail partnerships, or export markets.
