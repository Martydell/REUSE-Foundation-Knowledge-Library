Organisation Overview

ReTuna Återbruksgalleria is a municipally-owned shopping mall in Eskilstuna, Sweden, billed as the world's first recycling mall. Public donations of discarded items are sorted and redistributed to independent shops within the mall, which repair, upcycle, and resell them, alongside a café and educational programming.

Business Model

The public drops off unwanted items at on-site recycling containers/depots; these are sorted and distributed to independent tenant shops (furniture, vintage clothing, toys, construction materials, flowers, etc.), which repair or transform items before reselling them. ReTuna also generates revenue through guided tours, conferences, and educational visits, and participates in sustainability projects like "Waste to Value." It is owned and operated by the municipal utility Eskilstuna Energi och Miljö rather than a private company.

Products & Services

Repaired and upcycled furniture, clothing, toys, building materials, and household goods sold through independent mall shops; guided tours and educational visits; café.

Customers

General public shoppers in the Eskilstuna region; municipal and international visitors, students, and delegations on educational tours.

Partnerships

Owned/operated by Eskilstuna Energi och Miljö; participant in the "Waste to Value" sustainability project.

Measurable Impact

Generated SEK 11.7 million in sales from recycled products in 2018 (per third-party sources).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

An internationally recognised flagship model proving that a dedicated retail mall built entirely on reuse/repair/upcycling is commercially and civically viable, with strong municipal backing and documented revenue. A benchmark case study for the reuse sector.
