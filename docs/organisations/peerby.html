Organisation Overview

Peerby is an Amsterdam-based sharing platform connecting neighbors to borrow and lend household items such as tools, electronics, bikes, and camping gear. Founded in 2012 by Daan Weddepohl, it claims to be one of the first working goods-sharing platforms, now active in the Netherlands, Belgium, Germany, and the UK.

Business Model

Peerby operates a digital marketplace where users list items available to lend, free or for a fee, within their local neighborhood. Revenue comes from transaction/service fees and optional insurance add-ons. The platform reports it turned profitable around 2019 after revising its model, with subsequent strong revenue growth (reported 222% in 2021).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Peerby is a clear consumer-sharing-economy model that directly displaces new purchases through reuse of existing goods. Its scale within the Benelux region and multi-country reach make it a relevant reference case for sharing platforms, though impact data is largely self-reported. ★★★★ High
