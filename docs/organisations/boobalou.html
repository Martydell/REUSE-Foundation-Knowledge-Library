Organisation Overview

Boobalou is a UK e-commerce retailer founded in 2014 by Jo Mack, a work-at-home mother who started the business selling reusable cloth nappies and reusable menstrual products after struggling to find genuinely plastic-free alternatives for her family. The business grew from a single room to a garden cabin and then multiple storage units and warehouses in Arundel, Sussex, as demand increased (the company's own account attributes part of this growth to increased public interest following the "Blue Planet" documentary). Boobalou's success led founder Jo Mack to develop an own-brand product line, which grew into a related brand, ecoLiving, that manufactures and sells eco-friendly household and personal-care products alongside third-party eco brands.

Business Model

Boobalou operates as a B2C online marketplace/retailer for reusable and plastic-free lifestyle products, spanning reusable menstrual products (cups, cloth pads), reusable cloth nappies and eco baby items, eco home goods (household cleaners, reusable containers), plastic-free personal care (shampoo bars, deodorant, safety razors, reusable wipes) and general reusable lifestyle items (bottles, coffee cups, bags, straws). Revenue comes from retail sale of both third-party eco brands and, increasingly, own-manufactured products developed under the related ecoLiving brand as the company identified gaps in the market. The business also runs an "Eco Points Rewards" loyalty programme and customer-acquisition incentives (e.g., discount for newsletter sign-up).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Boobalou is a long-running (12-year), founder-led UK retailer with a credible origin story and consistent focus on reusable products across menstrual care, baby care, and household categories — a genuine and well-established reuse-adjacent retail business, evidenced by its organic growth into the ecoLiving own-brand manufacturing line. However, as a retailer/marketplace rather than a product innovator or system operator, and with no independently verifiable impact metrics, its relevance is best understood as an established distribution channel for reuse products rather than a reuse-system innovator in its own right.
