Fairphone

Modular, repairable smartphones designed to extend device lifespan and reduce e-waste.

Organisation Overview

Fairphone is an Amsterdam-based social enterprise founded in January 2013 by Bas van Abel, Tessa Wernink and Miquel Ballester, following two and a half years operating as an awareness campaign about conflict minerals and e-waste. It designs and sells modular smartphones engineered for easy repair, upgrade and disassembly, with a focus on longer device lifespans, ethically-sourced materials (including conflict-free minerals) and fair labour conditions in its supply chain. Fairphone became a certified B Corporation in April 2015 and has been widely cited (including by Ethical Consumer and the UNFCCC) as a leading example of right-to-repair design in consumer electronics.

Business Model

Fairphone designs, manufactures (via contract manufacturers) and sells smartphones directly and through carrier/retail partners, primarily in Europe. Its differentiation is a modular hardware design — user-replaceable battery, screen, camera and other components — paired with extended software support, intended to let a phone last significantly longer than typical smartphones (the company states extending a phone's life by two years cuts its CO2 footprint by roughly 30%). Revenue is generated through phone hardware sales and accessories/spare parts. Financially, the company scaled from €10.8M revenue in 2018 to a peak of €58.9M in 2022, faced a loss-making 2023 (revenue €54.8M, EBITDA -€14.3M), and returned to profitability in 2024 (revenue €54.4M, EBITDA +€1.7M) — reflecting the volatility typical of a scaling hardware social enterprise.

Products & Services

Modular smartphones (Fairphone 4, 5, and successors) with user-replaceable components and spare parts; software support commitments; consumer education on ethical sourcing and device longevity.

Customers

Individual consumers (primarily Europe); telecom carriers and retail partners distributing Fairphone devices; enterprise/business customers in some markets.

Partnerships

Certified B Corporation (B Lab); EcoVadis Platinum rating (2024, top 1% of 114,000+ evaluated firms); UNFCCC "Momentum for Change" recognition. Specific supply-chain and carrier partners not exhaustively verified in this research.

Measurable Impact

Revenue and EBITDA trend (company-reported, audited financial figures): €10.8M revenue (2018) to €58.9M (2022 peak); €54.8M revenue / -€14.3M EBITDA (2023); €54.4M revenue / +€1.7M EBITDA (2024). Company claims extending phone life by two years reduces CO2 emissions by ~30% (methodology/date not independently verified here).

Technology

Modular hardware architecture (user-replaceable battery, display, camera, and other modules); extended software update commitments to support longer device life.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Fairphone is one of the strongest, best-documented examples of repair-driven circularity in consumer electronics, with verifiable financials, B Corp certification, and a long track record (founded 2013) of right-to-repair advocacy and product design. It is a clear fit for the "Repair" category and should be considered an essential/flagship reference organisation for the REUSE Foundation's knowledge library.
