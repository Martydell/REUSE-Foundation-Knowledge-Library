Organisation Overview

Rituals is a Dutch luxury personal-care, cosmetics, and home-fragrance retailer founded in 2000 by Raymond Cloosterman, opening its first Amsterdam store the same year. It has grown into a global retail chain exceeding €1 billion in annual turnover, and introduced an in-store product-refill programme in 2017 as part of its sustainability strategy.

Business Model

Rituals sells premium personal-care, cosmetics, and home-fragrance products through its own retail stores and e-commerce channels globally, positioned in the "affordable luxury" and wellbeing segment. Its refill programme, launched in 2017, lets customers return empty containers to stores to be refilled with product at a lower cost than buying new packaging, supporting both a sustainability narrative and repeat in-store visits; the "Be The Change" campaign ties refill sales milestones to tree-planting commitments.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems (in-store product refill programme since 2017)
  • Reusable Packaging (refillable container lines)
  • Consumer Education (sustainability/refill campaigns, "Be The Change")
  • Waste Reduction (materials, energy, and water savings reported from refill uptake)

Products & Services

Premium cosmetics, personal-care, and home-fragrance products sold via retail stores and online, including a refillable product range and in-store refill stations.

Customers

Mass-premium retail consumers globally across Rituals' store network and e-commerce channels.

Partnerships

"Be The Change" campaign conducted in partnership with unspecified environmental organisations for tree-planting commitments tied to refill sales milestones.

Measurable Impact

Per Rituals' 2022 sustainability report, refill programme uptake that year saved 802,587 kg of materials, 32,871 GJ-eq of energy, 1,766 tons CO2-eq, and 19.3 million litres of water; the company reached a goal of selling 5 million refills tied to a 5-million-tree planting pledge.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Rituals is a large, financially substantial retailer with a genuine, multi-year in-store refill programme and independently reported impact figures, making it a strong mainstream example of refill-at-scale, though refill remains one strand of a broader conventional retail business. ★★★★ High
