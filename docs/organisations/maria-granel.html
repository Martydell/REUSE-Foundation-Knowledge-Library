Maria Granel

Portugal's first zero-waste, bulk-food and organic grocery store, pioneering the Bring-Your-Own-Container (BYOC) model in the national market.

Organisation Overview

Maria Granel is described as the first "zero waste store" in Portugal and among the pioneers of the bulk/package-free retail model in Europe. Founded by Eunice Maia and her husband, with the concept conceived in 2013 and the first store opened in 2015 in Lisbon, it sells organic and bulk products — grains, dried fruits, coffee, cleaning and personal-care items — dispensed by customers directly from bulk dispensers, evoking traditional neighbourhood groceries but with a modern, sustainability-driven model. It operates two physical stores (Alvalade and Campo de Ourique, Lisbon) plus an online shop with a curated product range.

Business Model

Maria Granel operates as a specialty bulk-goods grocery retailer, selling over 1,000 organic and low-waste products — food staples, dried goods, cleaning products and personal-care items — dispensed from individual bulk dispensers rather than pre-packaged units. Customers bring their own containers (BYOC) or purchase reusable jars, glass bottles or recycled-paper bags in-store, paying only for the quantity/weight of product taken, which eliminates most single-use primary packaging at the point of sale. Revenue is generated through retail sales across two Lisbon storefronts and an online shop (200+ products). The company curates its supplier base around organic certification, geographic proximity, fair-trade credentials (including Biopartenaire-certified suppliers), low-waste packaging practices, and support for carbon-neutral and socially inclusive producers, positioning supplier selection itself as part of its sustainability model. Beyond retail, Maria Granel runs consumer-facing education programmes (e.g., a "Z(h)ero" zero-waste programme, a "Pantry Makeover" service, and zero-waste school initiatives) that extend its business into sustainability consulting/education services.

Products & Services

Bulk organic food (grains, dried fruits, coffee, etc.), bulk cleaning and personal-care products, reusable glass bottles/jars and recycled-paper bags for purchase in-store, online shop with curated product range, plus consumer education services (Z(h)ero Program, Pantry Makeover, zero-waste school initiatives).

Customers

Retail consumers in Lisbon, Portugal, seeking organic, package-free and zero-waste grocery options, plus schools and households engaged through its educational programmes; some online customers across Portugal via its e-commerce shop.

Partnerships

Certified organic producers (prioritising Portuguese/national suppliers); fair-trade suppliers including Biopartenaire members; supplier network selected for proximity, low-waste packaging, and carbon-neutral practices. Recipient of the Terre de Femmes 2019 Prize from the Yves Rocher Foundation.

Measurable Impact

Company-reported: more than 1 million plastic bags diverted from landfills since inception (undated cumulative figure per company communications). Recognition: Terre de Femmes 2019 Prize (Yves Rocher Foundation). No independently audited, dated tonnage or unit metrics were found.

Technology

Not publicly available; no specific digital reuse-tracking (RFID/QR) system identified. The model relies on physical bulk dispensers and manual BYOC weighing at point of sale.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Maria Granel is a well-documented, award-recognised pioneer of the zero-waste/bulk-retail model in Portugal, credibly described across Portuguese press and sustainability directories as the country's first store of its kind. Its BYOC system, curated low-waste supplier network, and consumer-education programming make it a strong illustrative case of refill-system retail at the small/independent-business scale. The absence of independently audited impact metrics (relying on a single company-reported "1 million bags" figure) is the main gap for verification purposes.
