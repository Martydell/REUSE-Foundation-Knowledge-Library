Organisation Overview

The Bulk House is described in multiple independent sources (China Development Brief, Xinhua, Yicai Global) as mainland China's first social enterprise dedicated to a zero-waste lifestyle. Founded in 2018 by Chinese-British couple Carrie Yu and Joseph Harvey, it opened a physical shop in Beijing's Drum Tower (Gulou) area where customers could bring their own containers to refill shampoo, conditioner, soap, and other personal-care and household products, and buy reusable alternatives such as steel straws, cotton bags, and bamboo toothbrushes. The physical store closed in March 2019; the business pivoted to an online-only model, pop-up stores, lectures, and workshops, and per a November 2024 reference remained active in that form.

Business Model

The Bulk House began as a bricks-and-mortar refill shop in central Beijing, allowing customers to bring their own containers ("bring your own container") to purchase bulk quantities of shampoo, conditioner, soap, and cleaning products without single-use packaging, alongside a retail range of reusable items (steel straws, organic cotton handkerchiefs, cotton bags, bamboo toothbrushes). Regulatory constraints reportedly prevented it from obtaining certification to sell bulk food items. After closing its physical location in March 2019 — attributed to the challenges of running a niche zero-waste storefront in Beijing — the founders shifted the business online, selling reusable/plastic-free products via e-commerce while continuing to promote zero-waste practices through workshops, lectures, and pop-up events aimed at consumer education. The company is frequently cited in Chinese and international media as a pioneer and bellwether for the nascent zero-waste retail movement in mainland China.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

The Bulk House is a genuine and well-documented pioneer of refill retail in mainland China, notable for being cited as "the first such business" of its kind there by multiple independent outlets (Xinhua, China Development Brief, Yicai Global). However, its original in-store refill model — the part most relevant to a reuse knowledge library — ceased operating in 2019, and the business has since operated primarily as an online reusable-products retailer plus an educational/events platform. Its official website could not be reached during verification, and English-language coverage, while credible, is limited and largely from 2018–2020 with only sparse confirmation of continued activity through 2024. Historical/pioneer significance is high; current operational reuse infrastructure appears limited.
