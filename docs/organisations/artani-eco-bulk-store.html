Organisation Overview

Artani is a small, independently owned zero-waste bulk store based in Makassar, South Sulawesi, Indonesia — described in local press as the city's first store of its kind. Founded in May 2021 by Ria Lestari Baso, an agriculture graduate, the business was inspired by her own difficulty sourcing package-free baby food. Operating under the tagline "Beli secukupnya, pakai semuanya" ("Buy only what you need, use it all"), Artani sells agricultural, household and personal-care products by weight/volume without single-use plastic packaging, and has since expanded into a tea bar and creative workshop space.

Business Model

Artani operates a physical bulk/refill retail store in Makassar plus online sales via Tokopedia (an Indonesian e-commerce marketplace) and Instagram, with no independently owned e-commerce website identified. Customers bring their own containers or purchase reusable ones, buying dry goods (natural powder detergent) and personal-care items (wooden toothbrushes, Lerak-based natural soap and body care) measured to their own needs rather than in pre-packaged units, avoiding single-use plastic. The business has diversified revenue by adding a tea bar (serving herbal tea, described as Makassar's first of its kind) and hosting creative workshops. As a small independent retailer, no revenue, staff size, or investment information is publicly disclosed.

Products & Services

Package-free bulk agricultural and household products (measured by weight/volume); natural powder detergent; wooden toothbrushes; Lerak-based natural soap and body-care products; herbal tea bar service; creative workshops.

Customers

B2C — individual consumers in Makassar, South Sulawesi, both in-store and via online orders through Tokopedia and Instagram.

Partnerships

Reported collaboration with LindungiHutan (an Indonesian reforestation/environmental platform) on a mangrove-planting initiative in the Untia coastal area of Makassar (87 mangroves planted, per LindungiHutan's own coverage); this source could not be independently re-verified during this research pass due to an access error, so treat with moderate confidence.

Not publicly available as independently verified figures for waste diverted or plastic avoided; a secondary source (LindungiHutan) references 87 mangroves planted as part of a coastal-greening collaboration with Artani, but this could not be directly re-confirmed during verification (source returned an access error on re-fetch).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Artani is a credible grassroots example of package-free bulk retail in Indonesia, notable as reportedly the first such store in Makassar and documented by multiple independent local and regional media outlets (identitas Unhas, BaKTI, Makaraeng, MataKita). It is a small, founder-led business without scaled infrastructure, quantified impact data, or broader partnerships, making it primarily useful as a regional case study of grassroots refill retail in a Global South context rather than a strategically central organisation.
