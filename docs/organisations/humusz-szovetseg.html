Organisation Overview

Humusz Szövetség is a Hungarian environmental NGO alliance dedicated to waste prevention and the transition toward zero waste. Headquartered in Budapest ("Humusz Ház"), it grew out of a long-running waste working group of Hungarian environmental organisations. It is a member of Zero Waste Europe and is listed among RREUSE's network of organisations. Its work spans policy advocacy, public education, and coordination of a national network of grassroots zero-waste initiatives.

Business Model

As a non-profit alliance, Humusz Szövetség does not have a commercial "business model" — it is funded through donations, Hungary's 1% personal income tax designation scheme, grants, and volunteer contributions. Its core activity is running the "Nulla Hulladék Program" (Zero Waste Program), launched in 2008, which brings together more than 40 Hungarian organisations, and operating an associated "Zero Waste Network" (Nulla Hulladék Hálózat) of roughly 44 member organisations working on local waste reduction. It also delivers environmental education programmes for teachers and schools to embed zero-waste principles in curricula, publishes research/advocacy content on circular economy and waste policy, and supports community composting initiatives. It functions as a coordinating and advocacy body rather than a product or service provider.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Humusz Szövetség is a credible, long-standing Hungarian civil-society actor in waste prevention and zero-waste advocacy, with recognised affiliations (Zero Waste Europe, RREUSE) and a genuine multi-decade track record of coordinating national networks.
