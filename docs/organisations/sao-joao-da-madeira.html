Organisation Overview

São João da Madeira (pop. ~22,000, near Porto) is among the first Portuguese municipalities to pursue Zero Waste City certification under Mission Zero Academy / Zero Waste Europe. The town centre offers swap, refill, and repair services and workshops as part of a 2030 zero-waste roadmap.

Business Model

Municipal policy programme: door-to-door packaging/bio-waste collection, pay-as-you-throw incentives, and a zero-waste town centre hosting community swap/refill/repair services, aiming to cut waste to 100kg/person/year and lift recycling to 75% by 2030.

Municipal zero-waste programme: door-to-door collection, PAYT incentive scheme, swap/refill/repair town-centre services.

Partnerships

Mission Zero Academy (MiZA), Zero Waste Europe, ZERO (Portuguese NGO).

Measurable Impact

2030 targets: ≤410kg/person/year total waste, ≤100kg/person/year residual waste, 75% recyclables collection rate.
