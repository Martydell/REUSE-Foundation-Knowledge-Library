Bare Necessities

A Bengaluru-based, B Corp-certified zero-waste social enterprise selling personal- and home-care products in low-waste and refillable formats, with an in-house pay-by-weight refill counter at its headquarters.

Organisation Overview

Bare Necessities is a zero-waste social enterprise founded by Sahar Mansoor, a Cambridge-educated environmental policy specialist, who launched the brand in 2016 and formally registered the company in 2019. Headquartered in Bengaluru, it produces personal-care (soap bars, shampoo/conditioner bars, deodorants, bath salts), face and hair care, and home-care products in low-waste, often waterless and concentrated formats. It is described as India's first FMCG-sector B Corporation and is also PETA-certified. The company combines retail, corporate gifting and educational programming (workshops and online courses) around zero-waste living.

Business Model

Bare Necessities sells direct-to-consumer through its own website and via third-party retail partners (including Nykaa, Blinkit and Amazon India), alongside a network of stockists across India. At its Bengaluru headquarters, customers can refill select products by bringing their own containers or using glass jars provided in-store, paying by weight for the exact quantity taken, and receiving a store credit worth 10% of the order for their next purchase. This "Bare HQ" refill counter is the company's most direct reuse mechanism; broader retail sales rely on low-waste and waterless product formats (bars, powders, concentrates) rather than a nationwide refill network. The company also runs a corporate-gifting arm and sells workshops/educational content on zero-waste living, diversifying revenue beyond product sales.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Refill Systems — pay-by-weight in-person refill counter at its Bengaluru headquarters (bring-your-own-container or provided jars)
  • Waste Reduction — waterless/concentrated product formats marketed as reducing packaging, water and carbon impact versus conventional liquid products
  • Consumer Education — workshops, corporate sustainability programmes and online courses on zero-waste living
  • Take-back — a "recycle/refill" programme referenced on the company's website for returning packaging

A dedicated Delhi refill location referenced in seed data could not be independently confirmed; the verified in-person refill counter operates at the Bengaluru headquarters, with broader distribution via stockists nationwide.

Products & Services

Solid/bar-format soaps, shampoo and conditioner bars, deodorants, bath salts, lip balms, face masks, dishwash and handwash powders, reusable lifestyle items (bamboo toothbrushes, reusable straws, tumblers), curated and corporate gift hampers, and paid workshops/online courses on zero-waste living.

Customers

Individual retail consumers across India (online and via stockists/marketplaces), plus corporate clients purchasing sustainable gifting and workshop programmes.

Partnerships

Mason & Co (co-branded products); listed on third-party marketplaces Nykaa, Blinkit and Amazon India; B Lab (B Corp certifying body); PETA (cruelty-free certification). A specific "Delhi" retail partnership referenced in seed data was not independently confirmed.

Measurable Impact

Company-stated figures (as reported in press coverage, source and year not independently specified beyond the article itself): over 68 million units of plastic saved and approximately 155,000 kg of waste diverted. These are self-reported/press-reported figures rather than independently audited metrics, so should be treated with appropriate caution.

Technology

Standard e-commerce platform; no dedicated reuse-tracking app or digital deposit system was identified in public sources.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Bare Necessities is a credible, long-running (since 2016) Indian zero-waste social enterprise with independent third-party validation via B Corp and PETA certification, and a genuine in-person refill mechanism at its headquarters. However, its primary reuse lever at national scale is waste-reduced product formats (bars, powders) rather than a widespread refill-store network — the pay-by-weight refill counter is currently confined to one location. Self-reported impact figures (68 million plastic units, 155,000 kg waste) are notable in scale but not independently audited in sources reviewed. It is a relevant and well-regarded organisation for the library, particularly as an example of a B Corp-certified zero-waste enterprise in the Indian market.
