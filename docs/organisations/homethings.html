Homethings

Organisation Overview

Homethings is a UK direct-to-consumer cleaning products company founded by Matt Aubrey and Tim Keaveney. Its core innovation is concentrated cleaning "tabs" (for sprays, laundry, dishwashing and toilet cleaning) that customers dissolve in tap water inside reusable bottles, eliminating the need to ship and dispose of mostly-water single-use plastic bottles. The company is B Corp certified, gained national visibility via a 2021 Dragons' Den appearance, and has expanded from pure online sales into UK retail, including Waitrose.

Business Model

Homethings sells refill tablets designed to be activated with tap water inside reusable containers that customers buy once (starter kits with a reusable bottle) and then replenish via lower-cost, lightweight tablet refills purchased on a subscription or one-off basis direct-to-consumer through its website, with expansion into UK retail chains (Waitrose, Wholefoods, Booths). The pitch is economic and environmental: because ~95% of a typical cleaning spray's shipped weight/volume is water, shipping only the concentrated tablet cuts packaging, transport emissions and plastic waste substantially (the company cites 93% less CO2e for its tabs versus regular products). The company describes a community of 60,000+ "Refillas" (repeat customers), reflecting a subscription/repeat-purchase revenue model layered on top of one-time hardware (bottle) sales.

Products & Services

Concentrated cleaning tablets (multi-surface spray, laundry sheets/tabs, dishwasher tabs, toilet cleaning tablets "Loothings", washing-up liquid tabs), reusable aluminium spray bottles with interchangeable refill tabs, bamboo toilet roll, and plant-based biodegradable sponges ("Spongethings").

Customers

UK consumers purchasing direct via gethomethings.com (reported 60,000+ repeat customers, "Refillas"), plus retail shoppers at Waitrose, Wholefoods and Booths.

Partnerships

  • InnovateUK (grant funding for washing-up liquid product development)
  • Deborah Meaden and Peter Jones (investors, via BBC's Dragons' Den, 2021)

Measurable Impact

Company claims its cleaning tabs produce "93% less CO2e" than regular equivalent products (per company marketing materials); community size cited as 60,000+ repeat customers ("Refillas"). No independently audited lifecycle assessment or third-party verified plastic-waste-avoided figures were found.

Technology

Standard D2C e-commerce/subscription platform (gethomethings.com); reusable aluminium spray-bottle hardware with replaceable tablet cartridges; no bespoke IoT or digital tracking system identified.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Homethings is a clear, well-documented example of the "concentrate + reusable container" refill model applied to household cleaning, with credible B Corp certification, mainstream UK retail distribution, and public investor backing. Its environmental case (reduced water-weight shipping, reusable bottle hardware) is a strong, replicable template for other reuse brands, though its self-reported impact figures are not independently verified. It merits inclusion as a solid mid-market UK case study of packaging reuse via refill tablets.
