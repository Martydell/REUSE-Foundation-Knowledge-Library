Organisation Overview

Megloo is a reusable takeaway-container sharing service in Japan, operated by the company Kaman and launched as a pilot in November 2022, originating in Kamakura before expanding to Tokyo and other locations. Its name derives from the Japanese word for "circulation" (megaru/meguru). Customers order takeaway food at participating restaurants and food trucks, receive it in a durable reusable container tracked via the LINE messaging app, and return the container at the restaurant or a designated deposit point after use, where it is collected, cleaned, and redistributed — each container designed for roughly 100 reuse cycles before replacement. The initiative has been supported by the Alliance to End Plastic Waste (AEPW) and incubated through the Plug and Play programme.

Business Model

Megloo operates a borrow-and-return reusable-container-as-a-service model for the food service sector: participating restaurants, cafés and food trucks serve takeaway orders in Megloo's durable, lightweight polypropylene containers instead of single-use disposable packaging. Customers use Japan's dominant LINE messaging app to scan a QR code, record and confirm the number of containers borrowed, and show confirmation to store staff; after eating, containers are returned to the originating restaurant or to designated deposit/collection points, from where operator Kaman collects, professionally cleans, and redistributes them back into circulation for further use (targeting around 100 cycles per container before replacement). Revenue is generated through service fees charged to participating food-service venues for access to the reusable-container pool and associated collection/washing logistics, positioning Megloo as B2B reusable-packaging infrastructure for the restaurant and event-catering sector. The venture has been financially and programmatically supported by the Alliance to End Plastic Waste and incubated via the Plug and Play startup accelerator network.

Products & Services

Reusable polypropylene takeaway food containers provided on a borrow-and-return basis to restaurants, cafés, food trucks and event/stadium caterers; container collection, washing and redistribution logistics; LINE-app-based borrowing/tracking system with QR-code check-in.

Customers

Restaurants, cafés and food trucks in Japan (reported at 88 establishments across 9 cities, towns and stadiums as of a 2023 update); consumers ordering takeaway food at participating venues.

Partnerships

Kaman (operating company); Alliance to End Plastic Waste (AEPW) — funding/programme support; Plug and Play — startup incubation partner.

Measurable Impact

Per Alliance to End Plastic Waste reporting: over 3,100 container uses recorded between November 2022 and May 2023; a stated target to avoid an estimated 50 tonnes of virgin plastic by 2025; each container designed for approximately 100 reuse cycles before replacement; service reported to have reached 88 restaurants/food trucks across 9 cities, towns and stadiums.

Technology

LINE messaging app integration for order tracking, container-borrowing confirmation, and location/deposit-point information; QR-code-based check-in process at point of order.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Megloo is a well-documented, actively operating reusable-container sharing scheme for Japan's food-service sector, with credible third-party validation via the Alliance to End Plastic Waste and clear, dated usage metrics (3,100+ uses, 88 venues) demonstrating real-world traction beyond concept stage. Its integration with Japan's dominant LINE messaging platform for tracking is a practical, low-friction technology choice suited to the market. This makes it a strong, directly relevant example of reusable packaging-as-a-service infrastructure for the REUSE Foundation's library.
