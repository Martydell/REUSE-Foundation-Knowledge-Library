Solidarity Market Bonpland (Mercado Bonpland)

Buenos Aires solidarity-economy market founded 2003, selling bulk organic goods via popular assemblies and cooperatives.

Organisation Overview

Mercado Bonpland was founded in 2003 from a joint project between neighbourhood assemblies, cooperatives, NGOs and small producers in the wake of Argentina's economic crisis. Held Tuesdays, Fridays and Saturdays in Palermo Hollywood, it sells dried fruits, nuts, organic produce, cleaning products and artisanal goods.

Business Model

Solidarity-economy market model: small farmers and cooperative producers sell directly to consumers, emphasising agroecological production and self-managed economic practices rather than conventional retail margins; bulk dry goods (nuts etc.) are sold allowing customer refills, though a dedicated refill infrastructure is not separately documented.
