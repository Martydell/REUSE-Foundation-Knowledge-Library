Organisation Overview

CauliBox (legal name Cauli Ltd) is a UK reuse-system operator providing reusable food containers and coffee cups ("CauliCups") to workplace canteens, hospitals, and offices as a substitute for single-use packaging. Founded in 2019 and headquartered in London, the company describes itself as running the largest reuse system for foodservice in the UK. Customers borrow containers via a mobile app using QR-code tracking, use them, and return them to be washed and redistributed. Confirmed clients include NHS Barts Health Trust hospitals, BT offices, Norton Rose Fulbright, and the University of Worcester.

Business Model

CauliBox operates a B2B subscription/service model rather than selling containers outright. Food-service operators (workplace canteens, hospital catering, office restaurants) contract with Cauli to replace disposable containers and cups with a reusable, trackable alternative. End users ("Cauli users") borrow a container or cup via the Cauli App, which uses QR-code scanning to log each loan and return; users can earn "CauliCoins" as an incentive for returning items. Once returned, containers are collected and washed — either in-house or via partner washing services — before re-entering circulation. Cauli positions the offering as both a sustainability and cost play, citing potential client savings of up to 40% versus disposables and reporting client annual savings ranging from roughly £4,000 to £100,000 depending on site size. The CauliCup product, launched in 2022, extended the model from food containers to reusable, tech-enabled coffee cups aimed at eliminating disposable cups in office and hospital settings.

Customers

Reported client base: 50+ catering sites across the UK and over 10,000 active users, including Barts Health NHS Trust (five hospitals), BT offices, Norton Rose Fulbright, and University of Worcester. Initial CauliCup orders came from Barts Health NHS Trust and financial institutions in Canary Wharf.

Partnerships

Container washing is handled via in-house operations and partner washing services (specific partner names not confirmed in available sources); BM Caterers is referenced in connection with catering partnerships. No further named commercial or NGO partners were confirmed in public sources.

Measurable Impact

  • 1,600,900 pieces of single-use packaging diverted from landfill (cumulative figure, as stated on wearecauli.com; exact reporting date not specified)
  • Containers designed for reuse up to 400 times
  • Claimed CO2 footprint 98.7% lower than compostable clamshell boxes over 400 uses of a container (company claim, source: wearecauli.com)
  • Client cost savings of up to 40% versus disposable packaging; reported annual client savings of £4,000–£100,000 depending on site (company claim, source: wearecauli.com)

Technology

Mobile app ("Cauli App") using QR-code scanning to track container/cup borrowing and returns, plus a loyalty mechanism ("CauliCoins") to incentivize returns. No further technical detail on backend tracking infrastructure was available in public sources.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

CauliBox is a clear, well-documented example of a B2B reusable packaging system operator serving institutional foodservice clients (NHS trusts, corporate offices, universities) in the UK. Its deposit/return-and-wash model, QR-based digital tracking, and named clients make it a strong, verifiable case study for reuse-system design and institutional adoption. Public disclosure is moderate — company-reported impact figures exist but are not third-party audited, and funding/ownership detail is limited to aggregator sites (Crunchbase, PitchBook) rather than primary financial disclosures.
