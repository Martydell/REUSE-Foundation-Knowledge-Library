Organisation Overview

Precious Plastic Bangkok, founded in 2018, is a Thai chapter of the global open-source Precious Plastic recycling movement. It establishes community-based workspaces where local groups collect, clean, sort, and recycle plastic waste using low-cost, open-source machines, transferring recycling knowledge and technology to communities across Thailand.

Business Model

PPB operates as a community-led, non-profit-style project rather than a for-profit enterprise, funding machine kits (around US$2,350 each) for community workshops with support from partners such as the PepsiCo Foundation and the ECCA Family Foundation. Communities generate income by processing collected plastic into molds, sheets, or 3D-printed products.

Products & Services

Community plastic-recycling workshops, open-source recycling machines, and products made from recycled plastic (molds, sheets, 3D-printed items).

Customers

Local Thai communities and households; end products sold to local markets.

Partnerships

PepsiCo Foundation (machine kit funding for seven Thai communities); three-year partnership with ECCA Family Foundation.

Measurable Impact

Machine kits deployed to at least seven communities across Thailand (PepsiCo Foundation-funded, self-reported).
