Organisation Overview

Eco Blocks and Tiles is a Kenyan social enterprise, founded in 2016 by environmental scientist Hope Mwanake and co-founder Kevin Mureithi, that manufactures roofing tiles and other construction materials from recycled plastic and glass waste. Mwanake's experience running a waste-collection service in Gilgil, where she observed large volumes of discarded plastic bottles, buckets and jerrycans, motivated the venture. The company melts waste plastic (e.g. shampoo and vegetable-oil bottles) and combines it with crushed glass waste to produce roof tiles described as more durable, lighter and easier to install than conventional concrete or clay tiles, at comparable cost. Commercial production began in 2018, funded partly through crowdfunding and grants (e.g. from UK packaging firm Mondi and Netherlands-based VIA Water).

Business Model

Eco Blocks and Tiles operates a manufacturing business model that converts locally collected plastic and glass waste into building materials — principally roofing tiles, with additional block/paving products — sold to home builders and construction customers in Kenya. The company sources feedstock (waste plastic bottles/containers and glass) via its own or partner waste-collection channels, processes it in-house (melting plastic, mixing with crushed glass), and sells finished tiles/blocks at prices positioned to compete with standard clay or concrete alternatives while offering claimed durability and weight advantages. Growth has been funded through a mix of crowdfunding and grant funding from international sustainability-focused funders (Mondi, VIA Water), rather than conventional venture capital, consistent with its social-enterprise positioning. This is fundamentally an industrial/material-reuse (upcycling) manufacturing business rather than a reuse-of-packaging or refill model — it converts post-consumer waste into new, long-life durable products rather than enabling repeated reuse of the same packaging.

Products & Services

Roofing tiles and building blocks manufactured from recycled plastic and glass waste, marketed to residential and construction customers in Kenya as a durable, lightweight, cost-competitive alternative to concrete/clay materials.

Customers

Home builders, construction firms, and residential customers in Kenya seeking affordable, durable roofing/building materials.

Partnerships

Grant/funding support from Mondi (UK-based global packaging company) and VIA Water (Netherlands-based water-innovation fund); crowdfunding campaigns for growth capital. Featured by UNEP's Young Champions of the Earth programme and Global Citizen.

Technology

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Eco Blocks and Tiles is a well-documented, internationally recognised (UNEP Young Champions, Global Citizen) social enterprise converting plastic and glass waste into durable construction materials — a genuine example of industrial material reuse/upcycling in the circular economy. However, as the seed data itself flags, this is materials upcycling for construction rather than packaging reuse, refill, or deposit-return, placing it adjacent to but outside the REUSE Foundation's core packaging-reuse focus; it remains relevant as an industrial-reuse case study.
