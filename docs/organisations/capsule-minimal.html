Organisation Overview

Capsule Minimal is an Israeli startup that develops water-soluble "Active Capsules™" — concentrated dry-form personal care and household product formulas that, when dropped into a reusable bottle with tap water, dissolve into a finished product such as shampoo, body wash, hand soap, toothpaste, mouthwash, or household cleaner. The company was co-founded by Yael Goethe (a biotechnology engineer and CEO) and Ami Krupik (Chairman), and developed through the I4Valley Karmiel Incubator for Small Industry, which is backed by corporations including Keter and supported by the Israel Innovation Authority (IIA). The company has received IIA grants reported at 4.3 million shekels and was building a factory in Israel as of the most recent coverage reviewed. It was selected as one of 30 Israeli companies represented at the COP28 climate summit in Dubai (December 2023).

Business Model

Capsule Minimal's core proposition is eliminating the water content of personal-care and household products from packaging, shipping, and storage — the company notes that such products are typically 80–90% water — by supplying only the concentrated active-ingredient capsule, which the end user combines with tap water in a reusable bottle at the point of use. The company operates a B2B/B2B2C model, partnering with brands and OEMs (original equipment manufacturers) to supply its capsule technology and formulations rather than necessarily selling a consumer-facing Capsule Minimal-branded product itself; its website describes a "Buy Now, Invest Later" approach allowing partner brands to trial the product via samples before committing to larger-scale production. The underlying technology is protected by multiple patents covering microencapsulation, precision dosing, controlled release, and an activation mechanism the company terms "chemo-mechanical coding." Public sources do not name specific commercial brand/OEM partners.

Products & Services

Water-soluble "Active Capsules™" across face care, body care, hair care, and mouth care categories (facial cleansers, hand soaps, body washes, specialty foams, shampoos, conditioners, toothpaste, mouthwash); reusable bottles; B2B/OEM formulation and technology licensing services for partner brands.

Technology

Multi-patented microencapsulation technology enabling precision dosing and controlled release of active ingredients; an activation mechanism described by the company as "chemo-mechanical coding," designed to trigger dissolution/activation only under intended conditions (e.g., combination with water in the reusable bottle).

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Capsule Minimal is an early-stage, technology-driven Israeli startup with a genuine reuse/refill mechanism (a reusable bottle refilled with water-soluble concentrate capsules) and credible third-party validation via Israel Innovation Authority grant funding and selection to represent Israel at COP28. However, public information is limited — no confirmed founding year, no named commercial partners, no impact data, and no confirmed HQ address beyond its association with the Karmiel incubator program. As a pre-scale B2B/OEM technology supplier rather than a consumer-facing brand, its current market footprint could not be verified, though the underlying concept is well aligned with reuse/refill principles.
