Organisation Overview

Ethique is a New Zealand-founded personal care brand created by Brianne West in Christchurch in 2012 while she was a science undergraduate. The company makes concentrated solid bars — shampoo, conditioner, body wash, cleanser, deodorant and household bars — designed to replace liquid products sold in plastic bottles. Ethique is a certified B Corporation and Living Wage employer, and is majority-owned by US investment firm Bansk Group since 2020, though it retains its New Zealand brand identity and manufacturing base.

Business Model

Ethique sells direct-to-consumer online and through roughly 4,500 retail stockists across around 24 countries (including major US retailers such as Target under Bansk Group ownership). Its core model is "waterless"/solid format: eliminating the plastic bottle entirely by concentrating formulas into bars, which also reduces shipping weight and volume compared to liquid equivalents. Revenue comes from bar and packaging-free product sales at consumer and wholesale price points. The company reinvests in R&D for new solid formats (e.g. household and haircare) and funds a charitable giving programme (2% of sales) supporting conservation, animal welfare and environmental causes. Since the 2020 majority acquisition by Bansk Group, the brand has pursued more aggressive US retail expansion while retaining its sustainability positioning and NZ manufacturing/founding story.

Products & Services

Solid bars for shampoo, conditioner, body wash, facial cleansing, deodorant, and household/dish cleaning; associated tins and accessories to store bars package-free.

Customers

Individual consumers (DTC and retail); stockists including pharmacies, health stores, and major mass-market retailers such as Target (US).

Partnerships

Certified B Corporation (B Lab); Living Wage Employer (NZ). Majority investor: Bansk Group (US private equity). No further named commercial partnerships independently verified.

Measurable Impact

Company states its products have "prevented the manufacture and disposal of more than 11 million plastic containers worldwide," with a stated goal of 500 million by 2030 — self-reported by the company, exact reporting date not specified in available sources; no independently audited figure located.

Technology

No proprietary digital tracking technology identified; core innovation is product formulation (solid/concentrated format) rather than software or hardware.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Ethique is a well-documented, globally-distributed example of packaging elimination through product reformulation rather than a reuse-logistics model per se — it avoids packaging rather than reusing it. It is relevant to the REUSE Foundation's remit primarily as a waste-reduction/consumer-education case study. Its acquisition by a US private equity group (Bansk Group) in 2020 is a material governance change from its founder-led NZ origins, worth noting for anyone citing it as an independent B Corp example.
