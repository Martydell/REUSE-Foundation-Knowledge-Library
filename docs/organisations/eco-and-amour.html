Organisation Overview

eco+amour is a Canadian sustainable-living beauty and home-care refillery, opened in August 2018 by co-founders Sarah Marcus, Laura Craig and Julianne Robicheau. Originally based at 30 Bertrand Ave, Scarborough (Toronto), it was described in contemporary press as a "concept shop" combining green beauty retail with a full-service refill model for bath, body and home products, letting customers bring their own clean containers and pay by weight. Secondary sources indicate the original Scarborough pop-up has since closed, with the brand more recently associated with a Muskoka, Ontario storefront, though current operating status could not be fully confirmed as part of this research.

Business Model

eco+amour's core model is a "bring your own container, pay by weight" bulk refill retail format for bath, body and home-care products (soaps, lotions, cleaning products and similar), supplemented by pre-packaged eco-friendly goods from partner brands sold in-store and online. Rather than a deposit-return system with owned/tracked containers, the model relies on customers reusing their own containers indefinitely, which removes packaging waste at the point of sale without requiring reverse logistics. The business also curated and retailed third-party sustainable beauty and lifestyle brands alongside its own refill offering, positioning itself as a broader "sustainable living" boutique rather than a single-category refill station. Its footprint has evolved from an original Toronto/Scarborough retail location to (per limited recent secondary evidence) a Muskoka, Ontario presence, suggesting the business may have downsized, relocated, or shifted to a seasonal/regional format.

Products & Services

Bulk refill of bath, body and home-care products (soaps, lotions, cleaning products) by weight using customer-supplied containers; retail of pre-packaged sustainable beauty and lifestyle products from partner brands.

Customers

Environmentally conscious consumers in the Toronto/Ontario area seeking low-waste beauty, body-care and home-care shopping.

Partnerships

Retail partnerships with sustainable brands including Graydon Skincare, Siima Handmade, and The Urbivore Sisters (per 2019 press coverage). No other formal industry or NGO partnerships confirmed.

Measurable Impact

Not publicly available (company materials cite industry-wide statistics — e.g., "120 billion units of cosmetics packaging produced globally per year" and "less than 11% of plastics recycled in Canada" — but no company-specific, dated impact metrics were found).

Technology

No digital tracking, app, or deposit-return technology identified; standard in-store refill dispensing plus e-commerce storefront.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

eco+amour is a legitimate, founder-led refill retail case study for beauty/home-care products with several years of operating history and named founders, but current operating status is uncertain: its official domain now redirects to an unrelated third-party site, and secondary evidence suggests its original Toronto location has closed in favour of a smaller Muskoka presence. This significantly limits its current value as an active reference case pending direct confirmation of present-day operations.

Verification Notes

Seed description (Canada, bulk refillery + container reuse for home/body care) is directionally correct but the seed's claim of a "container return system" was not confirmed — the model is customer-supplied reusable containers, not a deposit/return scheme with company-owned containers. Founding year: cosmeticsdesign.com (contemporary Sept 2018 trade coverage) states the shop opened August 2018; Toronto Guardian's own 2019 piece was previously read as implying a 2019 founding, but the earlier trade press date is treated as more reliable. IMPORTANT: the seed website https://ecoandamour.com/ currently 301-redirects to an unrelated third-party domain ("linklist.bio/zeus88"), suggesting the domain has lapsed or been repurposed; this could not be resolved within research scope, and current live operating status/location(s) should be treated as unconfirmed.
