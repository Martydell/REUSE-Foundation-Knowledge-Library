Uzaje

French industrial washing-center operator for reusable food packaging — now ceasing operations.

Primary Category Industrial Reuse

Organisation Overview

Uzaje operated high-efficiency industrial washing centers in France, collecting, cleaning, and redelivering reusable food packaging for caterers, restaurants, and retailers. Since founding in 2019, it built out multiple sites before entering liquidation and ceasing all operations in May 2026.

Business Model

B2B reuse-logistics operator: collected dirty reusable containers from food-service and retail clients, washed them at scale in dedicated industrial centers, and redelivered clean packaging while pooling logistics across clients to reduce transport. Raised €4 million in 2022 to accelerate national rollout, but could not reach a sustainable economic model amid slower-than-expected market development.

Circular Economy Activities

  • Reusable Packaging
  • Reverse Logistics

Products & Services

Industrial container washing, collection and redelivery logistics, reuse-system implementation consulting for caterers, restaurants, agro-industry, and retail (historic; service discontinued).

Customers

Formerly served caterers, restaurants, agro-industry businesses, and food/non-food retailers across France.

Partnerships

Ideel Garden (cited joint impact figures); operated washing centers in Neuilly-sur-Marne and Avignon.

Measurable Impact

Company reported washing over 20 million reusable containers and creating 50+ jobs across four sites over its operating history; in H1 2022 alone, 66,500 containers were cleaned at its Avignon site, preventing 1,709 kg of waste.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Uzaje was a significant industrial-scale reuse-washing infrastructure provider and a European Reusable Award winner, but its 2026 closure is an important cautionary case on the economic fragility of reuse-logistics infrastructure businesses. Retain as a historical/lessons-learned entry rather than an active partner. Priority: ★★ Low
