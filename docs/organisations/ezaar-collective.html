Organisation Overview

Ezaar Collective is a small social enterprise based in Assam, India (the brand explicitly states "WE ARE PROUDLY INDIAN"), founded by entrepreneur Barsha Sharma. It upcycles discarded fabric waste sourced from handloom manufacturers into lifestyle accessories — pouches, bags, wallets, home décor, stationery and other utility products — under a "Waste to Wealth"/"Dream Stitch" positioning. The company has gained some regional media attention, including a feature on Jio Hotstar, and describes empowering women artisans as a core part of its mission alongside environmental sustainability.

Business Model

Ezaar Collective operates a direct-to-consumer e-commerce model (Shopify-based storefront) selling handcrafted, upcycled textile products made from fabric waste otherwise discarded by handloom manufacturers in Assam. Products are low-cost utility and lifestyle items (e.g. patchwork pouches around Rs. 150) rather than premium fashion, positioning the brand as accessible sustainable fashion/accessories rather than a high-end label. The stated model combines three pillars: collaboration with women artisans, craftsmanship, and environmental responsibility — i.e. it is as much a social/artisan-employment enterprise as a materials-reuse one. Revenue comes from direct product sales; the company registers as an LLP (Limited Liability Partnership) rather than a larger corporate structure, consistent with a small/independent scale.
