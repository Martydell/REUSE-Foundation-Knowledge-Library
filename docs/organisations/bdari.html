Organisation Overview

BDARI is a Malaysian household-goods brand launched in August 2020, evolving out of an earlier household-essentials business, AZ Homefix PLT, which had been operating in household products since early in the COVID-19 pandemic. BDARI's core product is a range of designer stainless-steel tiffin carriers (multi-tier food containers), sold as a sustainable alternative to single-use plastic bags and containers for meals, gifting and events, alongside a broader catalogue of reusable food containers, bottles, bags and cutlery. The company positions itself around reducing single-use plastic waste in Malaysia and promoting a "bring your own container" (BYOC) lifestyle.

Business Model

BDARI operates primarily as an e-commerce retailer (via its own Shopify-based website) with an accompanying physical retail presence, described as having "over 10 nationwide points of sale" in Malaysia. It sells stainless-steel tiffin carriers in three tiers — Standard (SUS410 steel), Premium (SUS304 steel), and an insulated Deluxe variant that keeps food warm for up to five hours — alongside customisation options (personalised text, names, or designs) for individual and bulk/corporate orders. The company targets three customer segments in its own marketing: working professionals seeking sustainable packed-lunch solutions, female workers, and households with children. Revenue is generated through direct product sales, customisation/personalisation upsells, and bulk corporate or event orders; no subscription, deposit-return, or rental mechanism was identified — customers purchase and own the tiffin carriers outright rather than participating in a shared/returnable system.

BDARI sells durable goods for individual ownership rather than operating a shared reuse, refill, or take-back system; no evidence of deposit-return, rental, or reverse-logistics infrastructure was found.

Products & Services

Stainless-steel tiffin carriers (Standard, Premium and insulated Deluxe variants) in a range of designs, plus reusable food containers, water bottles, bags and cutlery; personalisation/customisation services for individual and bulk/corporate orders.

Customers

Individual Malaysian consumers (particularly working professionals and households), and corporate/bulk buyers purchasing customised tiffin carriers for gifting or events.

Measurable Impact

Company-stated figures reported in search-indexed content (source not independently verified beyond aggregated search results, year not specified): more than 90,000 tiffin carriers sold, with a claim of potentially saving 52 million single-use plastic bags. These figures could not be traced to a specific dated report or press release during this research and should be treated as unverified company claims.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

BDARI is a small Malaysian retailer whose core product — durable, reusable stainless-steel tiffin carriers — has an indirect but genuine relevance to reuse and single-use-plastic reduction, even though the business model is conventional retail (sell-and-own) rather than a structured reuse system such as refill, deposit-return, or rental. Its claimed sales volume (90,000+ units) suggests meaningful market reach within Malaysia if accurate, but this figure is unverified. Given the absence of a closed-loop or shared-use mechanism, and limited independent public documentation, its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's core focus areas is moderate.
