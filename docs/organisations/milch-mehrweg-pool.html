Milch Mehrweg Pool (MMP e.V.)

German non-profit association operating a nationwide standardized glass-bottle pooling system for milk, yoghurt, and other food products.

Organisation Overview

The Milch Mehrweg Pool traces back to a reusable glass-bottle pooling system established by the German dairy industry in the 1980s for milk bottles and yoghurt jars. The original pool was disbanded in 2008 but was revived in 2022 under the name "Mach Mehrweg Pool" (MMP e.V.), continuing the tradition with standardized reusable glass containers now open to a broader range of food products beyond dairy. The pool coordinates manufacturers, retailers, and logistics providers around shared, standardized reusable packaging formats and crates, enabling economies of scale for reuse systems. In 2023, MMP e.V. received the European Refillable Award — notable as the first such award given to a reuse pool outside the beverage sector.

Business Model

MMP e.V. operates as a member-funded, non-profit pooling association rather than a commercial packaging supplier. Its core function is to standardize reusable glass bottle and jar formats (currently four formats, each with associated transport crates) so that multiple dairies, food producers, retailers, and logistics companies can share a single pool of reusable containers instead of each maintaining proprietary packaging. This pooling reduces the barriers to entry for smaller producers wanting to use reusable glass packaging, since they do not need to invest in bottle washing, crate logistics, or exclusive container design — they instead plug into the shared system. Bottles in the pool are reused for an estimated average of about 50 cycles before being retired. Revenue/funding comes from member fees and pool participation charges from dairies, bottlers, and retailers using the system, rather than product sales to end consumers.

Products & Services

Standardized reusable glass bottle and jar formats (four formats) for milk, yoghurt, and other food products; associated standardized transport crates; pool management and logistics coordination services for member dairies, bottlers, retailers, and logistics companies.

Customers

Dairy producers, food and beverage bottlers, retailers, and logistics/transport companies across Germany participating in the shared reusable packaging pool (not a direct-to-consumer service).

Partnerships

Member network of German dairy and food producers, bottlers, and logistics providers (specific company members not itemized in public sources). Recognized by the European Refillable Award (2023).

Measurable Impact

Each bottle in the MMP pool is reused for an average of approximately 50 cycles (MMP-reported figure). Received the European Refillable Award 2023, cited as the first reuse pool outside the beverage sector to receive this recognition. No aggregate annual bottle-volume or emissions-reduction figures are publicly available.

Technology

Standardized pool logistics and crate/bottle format management; no advanced digital tracking platform identified in public sources.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

MMP e.V. is a structurally important example of an industrial-scale reuse pooling system — the kind of shared infrastructure (standardized formats, shared washing/logistics, low barriers to entry for small producers) that reuse advocates often cite as a model for scaling deposit-return and reusable packaging beyond single-company programs. Its multi-decade history, revival after a 2008 disbandment, and 2023 European Refillable Award give it strong external validation, even though granular impact metrics (total volume, member count) are not publicly disclosed. Priority: ★★★★ High.
