Organisation Overview

EcoTrace is a Kenyan refill startup founded by Donatus Njoroge, who previously headed Innovations, Intellectual Property, and Community Engagement at Mount Kenya University (MKU), where he ran incubation programmes supporting over 1,000 entrepreneurs. EcoTrace delivers refills of everyday consumer necessities — hygiene products, detergent, and cooking oil — via mobile and stationary refill stations, eliminating single-use plastic packaging and lowering costs for low-income customers. The company has been recognised in innovation/climate challenge circles, including a MIT Solve 2024 Global Climate Challenge solution listing.

Business Model

EcoTrace operates a "win-win" refill model serving four stakeholder groups simultaneously: low-income consumers (who get reduced-cost access to hygiene and household staples), retail partners (who host refill stations and gain foot traffic), FMCG/consumer goods companies (who reduce packaging costs and reach underserved markets), and environmental goals (plastic waste reduction). Revenue is generated through product refill sales, sustainability consultation services for partner brands/retailers, and refill station installation and customisation for third parties. The company positions itself as reimagining retail distribution infrastructure for daily necessities rather than solely operating its own retail outlets, embedding refill stations within existing retail partner locations across its network.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

EcoTrace addresses a genuinely under-served segment of the refill economy: low-income consumers in emerging markets, where affordability rather than premium eco-positioning drives adoption. Its multi-stakeholder distribution model (retail partner network plus consultation/installation services) could offer a scalable template for refill infrastructure in similar markets. However, verifiable independent data is scarce — impact figures are small-scale and self-reported, and the company's public footprint (single website, limited press) suggests an early-stage venture. Worth monitoring as an emerging-market refill case study but not yet demonstrating proven scale.
