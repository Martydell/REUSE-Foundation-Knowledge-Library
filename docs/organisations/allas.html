Organisation Overview

Allas is described by its parent program as "the first returnable packaging service for out-of-home food and drinks in Indonesia." It provides reusable silicone food containers and stainless-steel drink containers to restaurants and cafés in Jakarta for dine-in and delivery orders, then collects, washes, sanitises and redistributes the containers in a closed loop. Allas was developed and is administered through Zero Waste Living Lab, a venture-building program run by Enviu (an international entrepreneurial NGO focused on systemic environmental and social challenges), rather than existing as a standalone independently-founded company.

Business Model

Allas operates a B2B2C returnable-packaging model: it partners with restaurants and cafés in Jakarta, supplying them with reusable containers (silicone for food, stainless steel for drinks) as an alternative to single-use packaging for both dine-in and delivery/takeaway orders. Consumers register as subscribers via the Allas website, confirm registration through WhatsApp, and pay a membership fee to receive a user ID enabling them to use the packaging system. Used containers are returned either via arranged pickup or at designated drop-off points, after which Allas washes and re-circulates them. As of the most recent verified figures, the venture had partnered with roughly 20 restaurant and café establishments in Jakarta and served more than 700 active subscribers, with a reported reduction of 762 kg of single-use plastic waste. The venture is run under Enviu's Zero Waste Living Lab program (alongside related ventures such as Alner, formerly Koinpack, a reusable-packaging platform for FMCG), with support from development-sector partners rather than functioning as an independently financed startup; it is explicitly described as being in a "pilot phase," indicating it has not yet scaled to full commercial operation.

Products & Services

Reusable food containers (silicone) and drink containers (stainless steel) supplied to F&B partners for dine-in and delivery use; a subscription-based collection, washing and redistribution service for consumers and restaurant partners.

Customers

Restaurants and cafés in Jakarta seeking single-use-packaging alternatives for delivery and dine-in orders, and individual consumers who subscribe to use the returnable packaging when ordering food and drinks.

REUSE Foundation Assessment

Allas is a genuine, verifiable reusable-packaging operator addressing food-delivery and dine-in single-use waste in Jakarta, with credible NGO/development-sector backing (Enviu's Zero Waste Living Lab, GIZ, GIDKP) and specific, if modest, reported impact figures (762 kg plastic reduced, 20 F&B partners, 700+ subscribers). Its relevance to the REUSE Foundation's mission is direct — it is precisely the kind of returnable-packaging-as-a-service model the library aims to document — but its scale remains small and it is explicitly framed as a "pilot," and it does not appear to be an independently incorporated company with its own governance and financial disclosures, which limits assessment of its long-term viability. The seed tracker's suggested LinkedIn URL (allas-returnable) is a legitimate but secondary source; the venture's primary web presence is allas.id and the Enviu/Zero Waste Living Lab program pages.
